# Talent Market And Competitive Intelligence

Talent decisions do not happen in a vacuum. The external labor market, what capabilities are available, what competitors pay, how the organization is perceived, and how skills are evolving, shapes what is possible. The agent must understand that workforce decisions made without market context produce uncompetitive offers, unfilled roles, and misaligned strategies, while well-informed decisions enable faster hiring, better retention, and stronger positioning. Talent market intelligence is not a one-time research project; it is an ongoing practice of monitoring the external environment to inform workforce strategy, compensation, hiring, and development.

Use this skill when gathering or analyzing talent market intelligence. The goal is to make the agent ground workforce decisions in current, relevant market understanding.

## Core Rules

### Monitor Talent Availability For Critical Capabilities

Understanding whether the talent you need is available, and where, is foundational to workforce planning and hiring.

- Identify the critical capabilities the organization needs, now and in the forecast period. See the workforce planning skill for detailed guidance.
- Research the availability of those capabilities in relevant labor markets: how many qualified candidates exist, where they are concentrated, and what the trends are.
- Distinguish abundant capabilities, which can be hired readily, from scarce capabilities, which require development, relocation, or alternative strategies.
- Monitor changes in availability, such as emerging skills, declining skills, and talent migration patterns.
- Use availability data to inform location strategy, hiring timelines, and development investment.

### Benchmark Compensation Against The Market

Compensation must be competitive to attract and retain talent. Benchmarking against the market is essential.

- Participate in compensation surveys or use market data to benchmark roles against the relevant market. See the market benchmarking skill for detailed guidance.
- Define the relevant market for each role: it may be local, regional, national, or specialized, depending on the capability and competition.
- Monitor not just base salary but total compensation, including bonus, equity, and benefits, as the mix varies by role and market.
- Track compensation trends, such as inflation in specific capabilities, to adjust strategy proactively.
- Use benchmarking to inform pay structure, offer strategy, and retention focus.

### Understand Competitor Hiring And Talent Strategy

Competitors' talent strategies reveal where the market is heading and where your talent is at risk.

- Monitor competitor hiring: what roles they are hiring, at what volume, and in what locations.
- Identify where your talent is being recruited from and to, to understand flight risk and competitive pressure.
- Analyze competitor job postings for insights into their strategy, required skills, and positioning.
- Track competitor compensation and benefits changes, which signal market shifts.
- Use competitive intelligence to anticipate retention threats and adjust strategy.

### Assess Employer Brand And Market Position

How the organization is perceived in the talent market affects its ability to attract and retain talent.

- Understand how the organization is perceived by candidates and employees, through surveys, reviews, and feedback.
- Benchmark employer brand against competitors, identifying strengths to leverage and weaknesses to address.
- Monitor online reputation, including review sites and social media, for talent-relevant sentiment.
- Connect employer brand to hiring outcomes, recognizing that brand affects offer acceptance and recruiting efficiency.
- See the engagement and culture skills for internal factors that shape external reputation.

### Track Skills And Capability Trends

The capabilities the organization needs are evolving. Track trends to anticipate future needs and development investment.

- Monitor emerging skills in the industry, such as new technologies, methods, or regulatory requirements.
- Track declining skills, which may warrant reduced investment or transition planning.
- Identify adjacent skills that can be developed from existing capabilities, informing internal mobility and development strategy. See the career pathing skill for detailed guidance.
- Use trend data to inform development investment, hiring focus, and workforce planning.

### Analyze Talent Flow And Pipelines

Understanding where talent comes from and goes to informs sourcing and retention strategy.

- Analyze the sources of successful hires: which channels, schools, companies, or regions produce talent that stays and performs.
- Analyze where departing employees go, to understand competitive pressure and retention drivers.
- Identify talent pipelines that can be built, such as university partnerships, apprenticeship programs, or community relationships.
- Use flow analysis to focus sourcing investment on high-return channels.

### Integrate Intelligence Into Workforce Decisions

Intelligence is valuable only if it informs decisions. Build processes to connect market data to workforce actions.

- Incorporate market data into workforce planning, to ensure plans are feasible. See the workforce planning skill for detailed guidance.
- Incorporate compensation benchmarks into pay structure and offer decisions. See the compensation skills for detailed guidance.
- Incorporate availability and trend data into hiring timelines and sourcing strategy.
- Incorporate employer brand assessment into recruiting and retention strategy.
- Make intelligence accessible to decision-makers, not buried in reports.

### Maintain Ongoing Monitoring, Not One-Time Research

The talent market changes continuously. One-time research quickly becomes outdated.

- Establish regular monitoring of key metrics: availability, compensation trends, competitor activity, and brand sentiment.
- Set up alerts or periodic reviews for significant changes.
- Update intelligence at least quarterly, and immediately for significant market events.
- Build intelligence gathering into ongoing processes, such as recruiting debriefs and exit interviews, rather than relying solely on external research.

## Common Traps

### Decisions Without Market Context

Making compensation, hiring, or planning decisions without market data, producing uncompetitive offers and unfilled roles. Ground decisions in market intelligence.

### Wrong Market Definition

Benchmarking against the wrong market, such as local when the relevant market is national, producing misleading data. Define the relevant market for each role.

### One-Time Research

Conducting market research once and relying on it as conditions change, using outdated data. Monitor continuously.

### Intelligence Without Action

Gathering market data that sits in reports without informing decisions, wasting the investment. Integrate intelligence into decisions.

### Ignoring Competitor Talent Strategy

Failing to monitor competitor hiring and compensation, being surprised by retention threats and market shifts. Monitor competitors actively.

### Stale Employer Brand Understanding

Assuming the organization's reputation without monitoring, missing shifts in perception. Assess brand regularly.
