# Town Hall And Skip-Level Meeting Facilitation

Town halls and skip-level meetings are forums where leadership and employees meet directly, bypassing the managerial layers that normally mediate communication. Their potential value is high: they surface what layers filter out, build connection between leadership and workforce, and demonstrate that voice reaches the top. Their potential to damage is equally high: a forum where questions are screened, dissent is punished, and commitments are made and forgotten teaches the workforce that the forums are performative and that honest voice is unwelcome, producing cynicism deeper than the absence of the forums would. The recurring failure is to design these forums as broadcasts — leadership presenting, questions managed, dissent avoided — and to call the result engagement. A second failure is to lack follow-through, so commitments made in the forum evaporate and the forum's credibility erodes with each unkept promise. This skill covers the design and facilitation of town halls and skip-levels as genuine two-way forums whose value depends on safety, authenticity, and follow-through.

Use this skill when designing or facilitating town halls or skip-level meetings, preparing leaders to participate genuinely, building the follow-through process, or diagnosing why existing forums produce passive or cynical participation. The goal is to make the agent design forums that surface what layers filter and build trust, not forums that broadcast and perform.

## Core Rules

### Design For Two-Way Exchange, Not Broadcast

The defining choice is whether the forum is a two-way exchange or a broadcast with a question period attached. Broadcasts with questions attached produce passive audiences and managed engagement; genuine two-way forums surface what leadership needs to hear.

- Balance presentation with genuine dialogue, because a forum dominated by leadership presentation is a broadcast regardless of the question period that follows.
- Allocate substantial time to employee questions and discussion, because the value of the forum is in what employees raise, not in what leadership presents.
- Resist the urge to fill the agenda with announcements that could be emailed, because using live forum time for one-way information crowds out the two-way exchange that justifies the forum.
- Design the format to invite, not withstand, employee input, because a format built to control input produces controlled input.

### Prepare Leaders To Engage Genuinely

The leader's behavior determines whether the forum is genuine. A leader who is scripted, defensive, or performative produces a forum that employees read as theater; a leader who is present, candid, and willing to engage produces a forum that builds trust.

- Prepare leaders to engage authentically rather than to perform, because employees detect performance instantly and trust the forum accordingly.
- Coach leaders to answer candidly, including acknowledging what they do not know or cannot share, because candor builds trust and evasion destroys it.
- Prepare leaders to receive challenge without defensiveness, because a leader who punishes a hard question in the forum teaches the workforce never to ask one again.
- Recognize that the leader's response to the hardest question defines the forum's safety more than any design choice, because the workforce watches the hard cases.

### Protect Psychological Safety For Dissent

A forum is only as valuable as the dissent it can surface. If employees learn that hard questions carry cost, the forum produces only safe questions and leadership hears only what it wants to hear.

- Make space for hard questions and receive them without penalty, because the willingness to engage dissent is what makes the forum a source of truth rather than a source of affirmation.
- Avoid screening questions to remove the difficult ones, because screened forums produce the appearance of openness without the substance, and employees detect the screening.
- Protect employees who raise concerns from retaliation, direct or indirect, because a single retaliation teaches the entire workforce that dissent is unsafe.
- Use multiple channels for questions (live, written, anonymous) so that employees who cannot speak publicly can still raise what they need to, because not everyone can voice concern in a room.

### Make Skip-Levels Safe And Substantive

Skip-level meetings — where a leader meets with employees below their direct reports — surface what the intervening layers filter, but only if employees trust that what they share will not be relayed in identifying ways to their manager.

- Establish and communicate confidentiality norms for skip-levels, especially that individual comments will not be attributed to the manager, because fear of attribution suppresses the candor skip-levels exist to surface.
- Use skip-levels to listen, not to evaluate or relay, because a skip-level used as a performance channel destroys its safety and its purpose.
- Aggregate themes rather than reporting individual comments upward, because themes preserve safety while individual attribution breaches it.
- Ensure managers understand that skip-levels are not a channel to surveil their teams through the leader, because a skip-level used as a surveillance instrument is corrupted at its purpose.

### Build Follow-Through Into The Design

The forum's credibility is built or destroyed by follow-through. Commitments made in the forum that are kept build trust; commitments that evaporate destroy it faster than the forum ever built it.

- Capture commitments and questions raised in the forum, with owners and timelines, because untracked commitments are forgotten commitments.
- Report back on commitments in subsequent forums, because visible follow-through is what makes the next forum honest.
- Be honest about what cannot be done and why, because a forum that only makes commitments it keeps and explains the ones it cannot is more credible than one that promises everything and delivers little.
- Treat the forum as part of a continuous loop, not a standalone event, because a forum without follow-through is a forum whose commitments are performative.

### Choose Cadence And Format To Fit Purpose

The cadence and format of the forum should fit its purpose and the organization's reality. Too infrequent and the forum loses relevance; too frequent and it becomes ritual without substance.

- Choose a cadence that allows meaningful update and follow-through between forums, because a forum faster than follow-through produces repetition without progress.
- Adapt format to purpose: town halls for broad organizational connection, skip-levels for filtered-signal surfacing, small-group sessions for depth, because one format cannot serve all purposes.
- Vary the forum to reach different audiences and levels, because a forum that reaches only the same audience captures only the same perspective.
- Use remote and hybrid formats deliberately to include distributed workforces, because excluding distributed employees from voice forums signals their lesser standing.

### Connect Forum Input To Decisions

A forum whose input never influences decisions teaches that the forum is consultative theater. Connecting forum input to actual decisions demonstrates that voice matters and sustains participation.

- Bring forum input into decision processes where it is relevant, because visible influence on decisions is the strongest evidence that voice is valued.
- Cite forum input when explaining decisions, because connecting the decision to what was heard closes the loop visibly.
- Be honest when decisions go against the forum's input, and explain why, because honesty about disagreement preserves credibility where false agreement would destroy it.
- Avoid using the forum to ratify decisions already made, because a forum used for ratification rather than input is read as manipulation and breeds cynicism.

## Common Traps

### The Broadcast With A Question Period

The town hall is structured as a leadership presentation followed by a short, managed question period, and the format is defended as engagement because questions are taken. The trap is that a forum dominated by presentation with a controlled question period is a broadcast regardless of the questions attached, because the design communicates that leadership's message is the substance and employee input is the courtesy, and employees respond with the passive engagement the format invites. The forum produces affirmation rather than insight, because the format is built to withstand input rather than to invite it, and leadership concludes that the workforce has no concerns when the format has ensured that concerns are not raised. Designing for genuine two-way exchange, with substantial time for employee-driven dialogue, is what makes the forum a source of truth, because the value of a live forum is the exchange that cannot happen by email, and a forum that does not exchange is an email delivered in person.

### Scripted And Defensive Leadership

The leader arrives with a scripted presentation and rehearsed answers, responds to hard questions with defensiveness or talking points, and treats challenge as an attack to be parried rather than a signal to be engaged. The trap is that employees detect performance and evasion instantly, and the forum's credibility collapses regardless of how polished the presentation was, because trust is built by authenticity and destroyed by its appearance. A leader who punishes a hard question — through visible irritation, a dismissive answer, or subsequent retaliation against the asker — teaches the entire workforce in a single moment that dissent is unsafe, and the forum produces only safe questions forever after. Preparing leaders to engage genuinely, to answer candidly including what they do not know, and to receive challenge without defensiveness is what makes the forum build trust, because the leader's response to the hardest question defines the forum's safety for every future question.

### Screened Questions

The forum collects questions in advance and screens out the difficult or embarrassing ones, presenting only the questions leadership is comfortable answering, and the workforce observes that the hard questions never surface. The trap is that screened forums produce the appearance of openness without the substance, and employees detect the screening — they know what was asked and they notice what was not answered — so the forum reads as managed engagement rather than genuine listening, and the questions that leadership most needs to hear are precisely the ones the screening removes. A forum that cannot receive the difficult question is a forum that cannot hear the truth, because the difficult questions are where the real signal sits, and a screening process optimized for comfort produces a forum optimized for ignorance. Allowing hard questions and engaging them without penalty is what makes the forum a source of truth, because the forum's value is in what it can surface, not in what it can avoid.

### The Commitment That Evaporates

A leader, in the moment of the forum, makes a commitment in response to a concern — to look into an issue, to address a gap, to change a practice — and the commitment is sincere in the moment and forgotten afterward, with no follow-through and no report-back. The trap is that evaporating commitments destroy the forum's credibility faster than the forum ever built it, because each unkept promise teaches the workforce that the forum's words are performative, and the willingness to raise concerns in future forums declines as the evidence accumulates that concerns raised produce no action. The credibility of the forum is built by follow-through, not by the forum itself, and a forum whose commitments evaporate is a forum whose future input will be hollow. Capturing commitments with owners and timelines, and reporting back on them in subsequent forums, is what sustains credibility, because a forum is a loop, and a loop that does not close teaches that speaking is pointless.

### The Skip-Level Used As Surveillance

A leader holds skip-levels with a manager's team and reports back to the manager what individual employees said, in identifying detail, and the manager then consequences the employees who raised concerns. The trap is that a skip-level used as a surveillance channel is corrupted at its purpose, because skip-levels exist to surface what the intervening manager filters, and relaying identifying comments to that manager ensures that nothing the manager would filter will ever be surfaced again. The retaliation, direct or indirect, teaches the entire team that skip-levels are unsafe, and the forum that was meant to bypass the manager becomes an extension of the manager's surveillance, producing only sanitized input that confirms what the manager wants to hear. Aggregating themes rather than attributing comments, and treating skip-levels as a listening channel never to be relayed in identifying form, is what preserves their safety, because a skip-level that is not safe is a skip-level that hears only what is safe to say.

### The Forum That Ratifies

Leadership has made a decision and uses the forum to present it as if it were open to input, taking questions that frame the decision as already settled and treating dissent as delay. The trap is that a forum used to ratify decisions already made is read as manipulation, because employees can distinguish genuine consultation from performed consultation, and the discovery that the forum was theater breeds cynicism toward every future forum, including those that are genuine. The forum used for ratification destroys the forum's value for consultation, because once the workforce has learned that some forums are rigged, all forums are suspect, and the input that genuine forums need is suppressed by the suspicion that the input is unwelcome. Bringing forum input into decisions before they are made, or being honest that a decision is settled and the forum is for understanding rather than influencing, is what preserves the forum's integrity, because honesty about the forum's role sustains trust where false consultation would destroy it.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Is the forum designed for genuine two-way exchange with substantial time for employee-driven dialogue, rather than a broadcast with a question period attached?
- [ ] Are leaders prepared to engage authentically, answer candidly including what they do not know, and receive challenge without defensiveness?
- [ ] Is psychological safety for dissent protected, with hard questions engaged rather than screened, and no retaliation for those who raise concerns?
- [ ] Are skip-levels conducted with confidentiality norms that prevent identifying relay to managers, aggregating themes rather than individual comments?
- [ ] Is follow-through built into the design, with commitments captured with owners and timelines and reported back in subsequent forums?
- [ ] Are cadence and format chosen to fit purpose and audience, including remote and hybrid formats that include distributed employees?
- [ ] Is forum input connected to decisions where relevant, with honesty about decisions that go against the input and clear explanation of why?
- [ ] Is the forum's role honest — consultation where input is sought, information where decisions are settled — rather than using forums to ratify decisions already made?
