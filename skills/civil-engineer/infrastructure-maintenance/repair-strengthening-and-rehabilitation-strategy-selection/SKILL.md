# Repair, Strengthening, and Rehabilitation Strategy Selection

Repair, strengthening, and rehabilitation strategy selection is the engineering of the choice and the design of the intervention that restores the deteriorated or the under-capacity structure to the required capacity and the service life. The repair addresses the deterioration (the corrosion, the cracking, the section loss), the strengthening addresses the capacity deficiency (the increased load, the code update, the design deficiency), and the rehabilitation addresses the serviceability and the function (the deck overlay, the joint replacement). The harm this skill prevents is a repair that is incompatible with the cause (the patch over the active corrosion that spalls again, the FRP over the ongoing settlement that delaminates), a strengthening that does not achieve the capacity gain (the insufficient FRP area, the unbonded overlay), or a strategy that ignores the remaining service life and the life-cycle cost (the expensive repair on the structure near the end of the life). Because the repair and the strengthening are the high-cost and the safety-critical, the strategy must be matched to the cause, the condition, and the capacity, and verified for the durability and the life-cycle.

## Core Rules

### Base the Strategy on the Condition Assessment and the Cause Diagnosis

The strategy selection must be based on the condition assessment (the distress, the in-place properties, the capacity) and the cause diagnosis (the corrosion, the overload, the settlement, the design deficiency), because the strategy must address the cause, not just the symptom. Review the condition assessment report, the capacity evaluation, and the cause diagnosis, and select the strategy that addresses the specific deterioration and the deficiency. A strategy selected without the condition and the cause is the generic, and it will fail, because the cause was not addressed.

### Match the Repair Material to the Substrate and the Exposure

The repair material (the concrete patch, the mortar, the epoxy, the FRP) must be compatible with the substrate (the bond, the modulus, the shrinkage) and the exposure (the freeze-thaw, the chemical, the temperature). The concrete patch must bond to the substrate (the surface preparation, the bonding agent), match the modulus (the compatibility), and resist the shrinkage (the low-shrinkage mix). The FRP must bond to the substrate (the surface preparation, the resin), and resist the exposure (the UV, the temperature, the moisture). A material incompatible with the substrate or the exposure will debond or degrade, and the repair fails, because the compatibility was not verified.

### Select the Strengthening Method for the Deficiency and the Capacity Gain

The strengthening method (the FRP wrap, the section enlargement, the external post-tensioning, the steel plate, the near-surface-mounted reinforcement) must be selected for the specific deficiency (the flexural, the shear, the axial, the seismic) and the required capacity gain. The FRP wrap is suited to the confinement and the shear strengthening, with the limited flexural gain; the section enlargement is suited to the flexural and the axial gain, with the significant dimension change; the external post-tensioning is suited to the large flexural gain, with the anchorage and the corrosion considerations. Select the method that achieves the required gain with the constructability and the durability, and verify the gain with the calculation and the test, because the method that does not achieve the gain leaves the structure under-capacity.

### Address the Active Cause Before the Repair

The active cause (the ongoing corrosion, the continuing settlement, the repeated overload) must be addressed before or with the repair, because the repair over the active cause will fail. The corrosion must be stopped (the cathodic protection, the chloride extraction, the waterproofing) before the patch, because the patch over the active corrosion spalls again. The settlement must be stabilised (the underpinning, the grouting) before the crack repair, because the continuing settlement reopens the crack. A repair that addresses the symptom without the active cause fails, because the cause continues and the distress returns.

### Verify the Capacity Gain With the Calculation and the Detailing

The capacity gain from the strengthening must be verified with the calculation (the strain compatibility, the debonding, the failure mode) and the detailing (the anchorage, the lap, the termination). For the FRP, verify the strain at the concrete crushing (the FRP does not reach the ultimate), the debonding at the termination (the anchorage), and the failure mode (the brittle versus the ductile). For the section enlargement, verify the bond (the shear transfer), the reinforcement development, and the composite action. A strengthening that is not verified for the capacity gain and the failure mode may not achieve the gain or may fail the brittle, because the calculation and the detailing were not performed.

### Consider the Constructability and the Access

The repair and the strengthening must be constructible (the access, the traffic, the sequence, the weather) and the constructability must be in the selection. The FRP can be applied in the confined space and the limited access, but it requires the surface preparation and the temperature; the section enlargement requires the formwork and the significant dimension change; the external post-tensioning requires the anchorage and the access to the ends. Select the method that is the constructible for the site, because the method that is not constructible is the failed project, regardless of the capacity.

### Evaluate the Life-Cycle Cost and the Remaining Service Life

The strategy must be evaluated on the life-cycle cost (the initial, the maintenance, the future repair) and the remaining service life, because the strategy must be the cost-effective over the life. Compare the repair (the lower initial, the shorter life) with the strengthening (the higher initial, the longer life) and the replacement (the highest initial, the full life), and select the strategy that is the life-cycle cost-effective for the remaining service life. A repair that is the low initial but the high maintenance may be the higher life-cycle cost than the strengthening, and the expensive repair on the structure near the end of the life is the wasted resource, because the life-cycle was not the basis.

### Provide the Quality Control and the Verification

The repair and the strengthening require the quality control (the material, the surface preparation, the application) and the verification (the bond test, the pull-off, the inspection), because the repair's performance depends on the installation. Specify the surface preparation (the ICRI profile, the cleanliness), the material (the mix, the cure), the application (the temperature, the thickness), and the verification (the bond test, the inspection), because the repair without the quality control is the failed installation. A repair that is not quality-controlled and verified may debond or underperform, because the installation was not verified.

## Common Traps

### The Repair Incompatible With The Cause

The repair addresses the symptom (the spall, the crack) without the cause (the active corrosion, the ongoing settlement), and the distress returns. The trap is that the repair is the performed, while the cause is the active, and the repair fails. The false signal is the repaired surface; the harm is the recurring distress, because the cause was not addressed.

### The Material Incompatible With The Substrate

The repair material is incompatible with the substrate (the modulus, the shrinkage, the bond), and the repair debonds or cracks. The trap is that the material is the specified, while the compatibility is the not verified, and the repair fails. The false signal is the applied material; the harm is the debonding, because the compatibility was not in the selection.

### The Strengthening Without The Capacity Verification

The strengthening is installed without the verification of the capacity gain (the strain, the debonding, the failure mode), and the structure remains the under-capacity. The trap is that the strengthening is the installed, while the gain is the not verified, and the structure is the still under-capacity. The false signal is the installed strengthening; the harm is the under-capacity structure, because the calculation was not performed.

### The Strategy Without The Life-Cycle

The strategy is selected for the low initial cost, without the life-cycle, and the repair requires the frequent maintenance and the recurring cost. The trap is that the low initial is the attractive, while the life-cycle is the higher, and the strategy is the not cost-effective. The false signal is the low initial cost; the harm is the higher life-cycle cost, because the maintenance and the future repair were not in the evaluation.

### The Method Not Constructible

The strengthening method is selected without the constructability (the access, the traffic, the sequence), and the method is the not installable, and the project fails. The trap is that the method is the designed, while the constructability is the not considered, and the method is the not buildable. The false signal is the designed strengthening; the harm is the failed project, because the constructability was not in the selection.

### The Repair Without The Quality Control

The repair is installed without the quality control (the surface preparation, the bond test), and the repair debonds or underperforms. The trap is that the repair is the installed, while the quality is the not controlled, and the repair fails. The false signal is the installed repair; the harm is the failed repair, because the installation was not verified.

## Self-Check

- Is the strategy based on the condition assessment and the cause diagnosis, and does it address the cause, not just the symptom?
- Is the repair material compatible with the substrate (the bond, the modulus, the shrinkage) and the exposure (the freeze-thaw, the chemical, the temperature)?
- Is the strengthening method selected for the specific deficiency (the flexural, the shear, the seismic) and the required capacity gain?
- Is the active cause (the corrosion, the settlement) addressed before or with the repair, so the cause does not continue?
- Is the capacity gain verified with the calculation (the strain compatibility, the debonding, the failure mode) and the detailing (the anchorage, the lap)?
- Is the constructability (the access, the traffic, the sequence, the weather) considered in the selection, and is the method buildable?
- Is the strategy evaluated on the life-cycle cost and the remaining service life, and is the cost-effective strategy selected?
- Is the quality control (the surface preparation, the material, the application) and the verification (the bond test, the inspection) specified, and the repair verified?
