# Bridge and Pavement Inspection and Management

Bridge and pavement inspection and management is the systematic assessment of the transportation infrastructure's condition and the planning of the maintenance, the preservation, and the rehabilitation based on the assessment. The bridge (the National Bridge Inspection Standards, the NBIS) and the pavement (the pavement management system, the PMS) are the high-value, the long-life assets that the public depends on, and the inspection and the management are the means to maintain the safety and the serviceability over the life. The harm this skill prevents is an inspection that misses the critical defect (the section loss in the primary member, the delamination in the deck, the fatigue crack in the steel), a condition rating that is the inconsistent or the unsupported (the asset mis-prioritised, the critical defect deferred), or a management plan that optimises the short-term cost and triggers the expensive reconstruction (the deferred preservation that allows the rapid deterioration). Because the bridge and the pavement are the safety-critical and the public-trust assets, the inspection must be the qualified, the thorough, and the documented, and the management must be the data-driven and the life-cycle.

## Core Rules

### Plan the Inspection to the Standard and the Component

The inspection plan must comply with the governing standard (the NBIS for the bridges, the AASHTO Manual for the bridge evaluation, the state PMS for the pavement) and address the specific structure and the component. For the bridge, define the inspection type (the routine, the in-depth, the fracture-critical, the underwater, the special), the frequency (the 24-month routine, the 12-month for the poor condition, the special as needed), the components (the deck, the superstructure, the substructure, the culvert), and the access (the snooper, the lift, the rope, the underwater). For the pavement, define the survey method (the manual, the automated, the walking or the windshield), the distress type (the cracking, the rutting, the roughness, the raveling), and the frequency. A plan that does not address the standard, the component, and the access will miss the critical defect, because the inspection was not scoped to the requirement.

### Use the Qualified Inspector and the Documented Method

The inspection must be performed by the qualified personnel (the team leader with the NBIS certification for the bridge, the trained rater for the pavement) and the documented method (the procedure, the equipment, the safety). The inspector's qualification and the method's consistency are the foundation of the reliable data, because the unqualified inspector or the inconsistent method produces the unreliable rating, and the management decision is the unsupported. Verify the qualification, the equipment (the calipers, the crack gauge, the chain drag, the automated vehicle), and the safety (the traffic control, the fall protection), and document the inspection, because the data and the decision depend on the inspector and the method.

### Document the Distress With the Location, the Severity, and the Extent

The distress must be documented with the location (the member, the span, the station), the severity (the section loss percentage, the crack width, the rut depth), and the extent (the linear metres, the area, the number), because the rating and the priority depend on these. For the bridge, document the corrosion (the section loss, the pack rust), the cracking (the fatigue, the shear, the flexural), the delamination and the spalling (the deck, the substructure), the scour (the footing, the pile), the settlement, and the bearing and the joint condition. For the pavement, document the cracking (the alligator, the longitudinal, the transverse, the block), the rutting, the roughness (the IRI), the raveling, and the patching. A distress documented without the location, the severity, and the extent is the unverifiable, and the rating and the priority are the unsupported.

### Assign the Condition Rating With the Consistency and the Evidence

The condition rating (the NBI 0-9 for the bridge, the PCR or the condition index for the pavement) must be assigned with the consistency (the same rating for the same condition) and the evidence (the documented distress that supports the rating). Use the rating manual (the FHWA Recording and Coding Guide, the state PMS manual), and assign the rating based on the documented distress, the severity, and the extent. The rating drives the priority and the funding, and the inconsistent or the unsupported rating mis-allocates the resource and the mis-prioritises the asset. A rating assigned without the manual and the evidence is the subjective, and the data is the unreliable for the management.

### Identify the Critical Defect and the Action

The critical defect (the section loss in the primary member, the scour at the footing, the fatigue crack in the fracture-critical member, the punch-through in the deck) must be identified and the action (the repair, the posting, the closure, the monitoring) specified, because the critical defect is the safety-critical and the immediate. Identify the critical defect in the inspection, and specify the action with the urgency (the immediate, the programmed), because the critical defect that is deferred may fail. The inspection that documents the distress but does not identify the critical defect and the action is the incomplete, because the safety-critical finding was not flagged.

### Prioritise the Maintenance and the Rehabilitation With the Life-Cycle

The maintenance and the rehabilitation plan must prioritise the assets and the treatments based on the condition, the deterioration rate, the remaining service life, and the life-cycle cost. Use the management system (the BMS, the PMS) to model the deterioration, to predict the future condition, and to optimise the treatment (the preservation, the repair, the rehabilitation, the reconstruction) over the analysis period. Apply the preservation (the crack seal, the chip seal, the deck seal) before the rapid deterioration, because the preservation at the good condition is the cost-effective, and the deferred preservation triggers the expensive rehabilitation or the reconstruction. A plan that optimises the short-term cost (the worst-first) and defers the preservation allows the rapid deterioration and the expensive reconstruction, because the life-cycle was not the basis.

### Verify the Scour and the Underwater Condition for the Water Crossing

For the bridge over the water, the scour (the local and the contraction scour at the pier and the abutment) and the underwater condition (the footing, the pile, the streambed) must be assessed, because the scour is the leading cause of the bridge failure. Perform the underwater inspection (the 60-month frequency, the 24-month for the scour-critical), assess the scour potential (the hydraulic analysis, the HEC-18), and the scour criticality (the NBI Item 113). Identify the scour-critical bridge and the plan of action (the monitoring, the countermeasure, the closure), because the scour-critical bridge without the plan of action is the unmanaged risk.

## Common Traps

### The Critical Defect Missed

The inspection documents the general condition but misses the critical defect (the section loss in the primary member, the fatigue crack in the steel, the scour at the footing), and the structure continues in the unsafe condition. The trap is that the general inspection is the performed, while the critical defect is the hidden or the overlooked, and the structure is the unsafe. The false signal is the completed inspection; the harm is the undetected critical defect, because the inspection did not focus on the safety-critical elements.

### The Rating Inconsistent Or Unsupported

The condition rating is assigned without the manual and the evidence, and the rating is the inconsistent (the same condition rated differently by the different inspector) or the unsupported (the rating without the documented distress). The trap is that the rating is the assigned, while the basis is the subjective, and the data is the unreliable. The false signal is the precise rating; the harm is the mis-allocated resource and the mis-prioritised asset, because the rating was the inconsistent or the unsupported.

### The Distress Without The Location, Severity, And Extent

The distress is documented without the location, the severity, and the extent, and the rating and the priority are the unverifiable. The trap is that the distress is the noted, while the specifics are the missing, and the data is the unverifiable. The false signal is the documented distress; the harm is the unsupported rating, because the specifics were not recorded.

### The Preservation Deferred

The management plan prioritises the worst-first (the reconstruction of the poor asset) and defers the preservation (the crack seal of the fair asset), and the fair asset deteriorates to the poor, and the reconstruction multiplies. The trap is that the worst-first is the intuitive, while the preservation is the cost-effective, and the deferred preservation allows the rapid deterioration. The false signal is the addressed worst; the harm is the multiplied reconstruction, because the life-cycle was not the basis.

### The Scour Not Assessed

The bridge over the water is inspected for the superstructure and the deck, but the scour and the underwater condition are not assessed, and the scour-critical bridge is the unidentified. The trap is that the above-water inspection is the performed, while the scour (the leading cause of the bridge failure) is the not assessed, and the scour-critical bridge is the unmanaged. The false signal is the inspected bridge; the harm is the undetected scour vulnerability, because the underwater and the scour were not in the inspection.

### The Unqualified Inspector

The inspection is performed by the unqualified personnel, and the data is the unreliable, because the inspector did not have the certification or the training. The trap is that the inspection is the performed, while the inspector is the unqualified, and the data is the unreliable. The false signal is the completed inspection; the harm is the unreliable data, because the inspector was not qualified.

## Self-Check

- Does the inspection plan comply with the governing standard (the NBIS, the PMS), and address the specific structure, the component, the type, the frequency, and the access?
- Is the inspection performed by the qualified personnel, with the documented method and the safety?
- Is the distress documented with the location, the severity, and the extent, and the evidence (the photos, the measurements)?
- Is the condition rating assigned with the consistency (the manual) and the evidence (the documented distress)?
- Is the critical defect (the section loss, the scour, the fatigue crack) identified, and the action (the repair, the posting, the closure, the monitoring) specified with the urgency?
- Is the maintenance and the rehabilitation plan prioritised with the life-cycle (the BMS, the PMS), and the preservation applied before the rapid deterioration?
- For the water crossing, is the scour and the underwater condition assessed, and the scour-critical bridge identified with the plan of action?
- Is the inspection documented, with the data entered into the management system, and the report that supports the rating and the recommendation?
