# Peer Review and Independent Verification

Peer review and independent verification is the engineering practice of the independent check of the civil or the structural design by the qualified reviewer, to confirm the code compliance, the analysis correctness, the constructability, and the standard of care. The peer review is the standard of care for the complex, the novel, or the high-risk project (the long-span, the seismic, the high-rise, the special structure), and the independent verification is the means to catch the error that the original designer missed. The harm this skill prevents is a design that is the error-laden and unverified (the capacity error, the load omission, the connection failure), a peer review that is the superficial or the non-independent (the review that does not catch the error), or a review whose findings are the ignored or the unresolved (the error that proceeds to the construction). Because the civil engineering error is the catastrophic and the irreversible (the collapse, the casualty), the peer review must be the independent, the qualified, and the thorough, and the findings must be the resolved and the documented.

## Core Rules

### Establish the Need and the Scope of the Review

The need for the peer review (the project's complexity, the novelty, the risk, the code or the authority requirement) and the scope (the design discipline, the element, the limit state) must be established before the review. The long-span, the seismic, the high-rise, the special structure, and the code-required (the tall building, the seismic design category D and above) trigger the peer review. Define the scope (the structural, the geotechnical, the wind tunnel, the seismic), the deliverables (the review report, the findings), and the timing (the design development, the construction document). A review without the established need and the scope is the unfocused, because the review was not scoped to the risk.

### Select the Independent and the Qualified Reviewer

The reviewer must be the independent (the no involvement in the design, the no conflict of interest) and the qualified (the experience in the project type, the license, the expertise). The independence is the critical, because the reviewer who is the involved or the conflicted is the biased, and the review is the not credible. The qualification is the critical, because the reviewer who is the not experienced in the project type is the not competent to review. Verify the independence (the disclosure, the contract), and the qualification (the resume, the reference), and document. A review by the non-independent or the unqualified reviewer is the not credible, because the independence and the qualification were not verified.

### Review the Analysis, the Loads, and the Code Compliance

The review must cover the analysis (the model, the assumptions, the boundary conditions), the loads (the dead, the live, the wind, the seismic, the combinations), and the code compliance (the IBC, the ASCE 7, the ACI, the AISC). Verify the analysis model (the geometry, the property, the load, the result), with the independent check (the hand calculation, the simplified model, the second software). Verify the loads (the magnitude, the distribution, the combination), against the code and the project criteria. Verify the code compliance (the strength, the serviceability, the detailing), against the governing code. A review that does not independently check the analysis, the loads, and the code is the superficial, because the core of the design was not verified.

### Review the Drawings, the Details, and the Constructability

The review must cover the drawings (the design, the dimension, the consistency), the details (the connection, the reinforcement, the weld), and the constructability (the sequence, the access, the temporary). Verify the drawings against the calculations (the member size, the reinforcement, the connection), and the consistency (the plan, the section, the detail). Verify the details (the connection capacity, the reinforcement development, the weld), and the constructability (the sequence, the temporary works, the access). A review that does not cover the drawings, the details, and the constructability misses the error that the calculation does not show, because the drawing and the detail were not verified.

### Review the Special and the High-Risk Elements

The special and the high-risk elements (the transfer girder, the long-span, the connection, the seismic system, the foundation) require the focused review, because the failure of these is the catastrophic. Identify the special and the high-risk elements, and review them with the depth (the independent analysis, the detail check, the alternative). A review that treats the special element the same as the standard misses the high-risk error, because the focus was not on the critical.

### Document the Review and the Findings

The review must be documented: the scope, the reviewer, the method, the findings (the issue, the severity, the recommendation), and the resolution. The review report is the evidence of the standard of care and the basis of the resolution, and it must be the clear, the complete, and the retained. Document the findings with the severity (the critical, the major, the minor), and the recommendation (the redesign, the clarification, the acceptance). A review without the documentation is the unverifiable, and the findings are the unresolvable, because the report was not produced.

### Resolve the Findings and Verify the Closure

The findings must be resolved (the redesign, the clarification, the acceptance), and the resolution must be verified (the reviewer confirms the closure). The resolution is the critical, because the unresolved finding is the error that proceeds to the construction. Track the findings to the closure, and the reviewer verifies the resolution, and the documentation is updated. A review with the findings that are the not resolved is the incomplete, because the error was not corrected.

## Common Traps

### The Review Not Independent

The reviewer is the involved in the design or the conflicted (the same firm, the personal relationship), and the review is the biased, and the error is the not caught. The trap is that the review is the performed, while the reviewer is the not independent, and the review is the not credible. The false signal is the completed review; the harm is the missed error, because the independence was not verified.

### The Review Not Qualified

The reviewer is the not experienced in the project type (the long-span, the seismic), and the review is the not competent, and the error is the not caught. The trap is that the review is the performed, while the reviewer is the not qualified, and the review is the not competent. The false signal is the completed review; the harm is the missed error, because the qualification was not verified.

### The Review Superficial

The review covers the drawings and the general, but not the analysis, the loads, and the code, and the core error is the not caught. The trap is that the review is the performed, while the core is the not checked, and the error is the not caught. The false signal is the completed review; the harm is the missed error, because the analysis and the code were not verified.

### The Special Element Not Focused

The review treats the special element (the transfer girder, the connection) the same as the standard, and the high-risk error is the not caught. The trap is that the review is the performed, while the special is the not focused, and the high-risk error is the not caught. The false signal is the completed review; the harm is the missed high-risk error, because the focus was not on the critical.

### The Findings Not Documented

The review is the performed, but the findings are the not documented, and the resolution is the unverifiable. The trap is that the review is the performed, while the findings are the not documented, and the resolution is the unverifiable. The false signal is the completed review; the harm is the unverifiable resolution, because the report was not produced.

### The Findings Not Resolved

The findings are the documented, but the resolution is the not performed or the not verified, and the error proceeds to the construction. The trap is that the findings are the documented, while the resolution is the not, and the error proceeds. The false signal is the documented findings; the harm is the error in the construction, because the findings were not resolved.

## Self-Check

- Is the need for the peer review established (the complexity, the novelty, the risk, the code requirement), and the scope defined (the discipline, the element, the timing)?
- Is the reviewer independent (the no involvement, the no conflict) and qualified (the experience, the license, the expertise), and the independence and the qualification verified?
- Does the review cover the analysis (the model, the assumptions, the independent check), the loads (the magnitude, the combination), and the code compliance (the strength, the serviceability, the detailing)?
- Does the review cover the drawings (the design, the consistency), the details (the connection, the reinforcement, the weld), and the constructability (the sequence, the access)?
- Are the special and the high-risk elements (the transfer, the long-span, the connection, the seismic) reviewed with the depth and the focus?
- Is the review documented (the scope, the method, the findings, the severity, the recommendation), and the report retained?
- Are the findings resolved (the redesign, the clarification, the acceptance), and the resolution verified by the reviewer?
- Are the critical findings (the capacity error, the load omission) resolved before the construction, and the closure documented?
