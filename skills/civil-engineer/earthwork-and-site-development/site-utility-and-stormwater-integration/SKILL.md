# Site Utility and Stormwater Integration

Site utility and stormwater integration is the engineering of the underground and the surface water systems for the development: the water supply (the domestic and the fire), the sanitary sewer, the storm drain, and the stormwater management (the detention, the water quality, the infiltration). The utilities are the gravity and the pressure systems that must fit within the site, coordinate with the grading and the foundations, and connect to the municipal system. The harm this skill prevents is a storm drain that is the under-capacity and floods (the property damage and the safety), a sewer that is the flat and the clogs (the backup and the health), a water main that is the under-pressure or the under-fire-flow (the inadequate fire protection), or a utility layout that conflicts (the crossing, the clearance, the repair). Because the utilities are the underground, the expensive to relocate, and the safety-critical (the fire flow, the sewage backup), the design must be against the demand, the hydraulics, and the coordination, with the verified capacity and the conflict-free layout.

## Core Rules

### Establish the Demand and the Design Flow

The utility design begins with the demand and the design flow: the water demand (the domestic average and peak, the fire flow), the sanitary flow (the population equivalent, the peak factor, the infiltration), and the storm flow (the rational method or the SCS, for the design storm). Establish the design flow for each system, with the code (the fire flow per the IBC and the fire marshal, the sewer per the state or the local), and the future (the growth, the connection). A utility designed without the demand and the design flow is the under- or the over-sized, because the flow was not established.

### Size the Pipes and the Channels for the Capacity and the Velocity

The pipes and the channels must be sized for the capacity (the design flow at the acceptable hydraulic grade) and the velocity (the self-cleaning, the minimum 0.6 to 0.9 m/s for the sewer and the storm; the non-erosive, the maximum 2.5 to 3 m/s for the storm). Size the gravity pipe (the Manning equation, the partial flow, the slope), with the minimum slope (the sewer, commonly 0.5 to 1 percent) and the minimum diameter (the sewer 200 mm, the storm 300 mm). Size the pressure pipe (the water, the Hazen-Williams), with the pressure (the minimum 140 kPa residual at the max day plus the fire flow) and the velocity (the maximum 1.5 to 2.5 m/s). A pipe sized without the capacity and the velocity is the under-capacity or the clogging, because the hydraulics were not verified.

### Set the Inverts and the Hydraulic Grade

The inverts (the inside bottom of the pipe) and the hydraulic grade (the water surface in the pipe and the structure) must be set for the gravity flow and the cover. Set the inverts for the slope (the minimum, the design), the cover (the minimum for the frost and the load, commonly 0.9 to 1.2 metres), and the hydraulic grade (the below the ground and the below the floor of the building, for the sewer and the storm). For the pressure system (the water), set the hydraulic grade (the pressure) for the minimum residual at the critical point (the highest and the farthest). A system with the inverts and the hydraulic grade that are the wrong (the flat, the shallow, the above the floor) fails the flow or the backup, because the hydraulics were not set.

### Coordinate With the Grading and the Structures

The utilities must be coordinated with the grading (the inverts follow the grade, the structures at the surface) and the structures (the building connection, the foundation clearance). Set the structure (the manhole, the inlet, the vault) at the grade, with the rim and the invert, and the connection (the building sewer, the building storm) at the correct elevation. Coordinate with the foundation (the clearance, the trenching), and the basement (the backup protection, the sump pump). A utility that is the uncoordinated with the grading and the structure has the shallow cover, the conflicting structure, or the backup, because the coordination was not performed.

### Resolve the Conflicts and the Crossings

The utilities (the water, the sewer, the storm, the gas, the electric, the communication) must be laid out to avoid the conflicts (the crossing, the parallel) and to provide the clearance (the vertical and the horizontal). Resolve the conflicts (the relocation, the adjustment), and provide the clearance (the water above the sewer, commonly 0.3 metres vertical separation), and the casing (the water through the casing at the sewer crossing). A utility layout with the conflicts and the inadequate clearance has the maintenance problem and the contamination risk, because the conflicts were not resolved.

### Integrate the Stormwater Management

The stormwater management (the detention, the water quality, the infiltration) must be integrated with the storm drain, with the flow routed through the management facility before the discharge. Size the management facility (the detention volume, the water quality volume, the infiltration area), and the outlet (the orifice, the weir, the control structure), and connect to the storm drain and the receiving water. Coordinate the grading (the facility at the low point, the overflow), and the maintenance access. A storm drain that bypasses the management facility (the direct discharge) violates the permit, because the management was not integrated.

### Connect to the Municipal System and Verify the Capacity

The site utilities connect to the municipal system (the water main, the sewer main, the storm outfall), and the connection must be verified for the capacity and the approval. Verify the municipal system's capacity (the water pressure and the fire flow, the sewer capacity, the storm outfall capacity), and the connection (the tap, the manhole, the outfall structure), and the approval (the utility, the permit). A connection to the municipal system without the capacity verification may overload the system (the sewer surcharge, the storm flooding), because the capacity was not checked.

## Common Traps

### The Pipe Under-Capacity

The pipe is sized for the small storm or the low flow, and the pipe is the under-capacity at the design event, and the system floods or surcharges. The trap is that the pipe is the sized for the common event, while the design event is the larger, and the pipe is the under-capacity. The false signal is the installed pipe; the harm is the flooding or the surcharge, because the design flow was not the basis.

### The Velocity Too Low

The pipe slope is the flat, and the velocity is the below the self-cleaning, and the sediment settles and the pipe clogs. The trap is that the slope is the flat for the cover, while the velocity is the below the self-cleaning, and the pipe clogs. The false signal is the flowing pipe; the harm is the clogging and the backup, because the velocity was not verified.

### The Invert Too Shallow Or The Hydraulic Grade Too High

The invert is the shallow (the inadequate cover, the frost), or the hydraulic grade is the above the floor (the backup risk), and the system fails. The trap is that the invert is the set for the grade, while the cover and the hydraulic grade are the not checked, and the system is the shallow or the backup-prone. The false signal is the set invert; the harm is the freezing or the backup, because the cover and the hydraulic grade were not verified.

### The Utility Conflict Unresolved

The utilities are laid out without the conflict resolution, and the water and the sewer cross without the clearance, and the maintenance and the contamination risk arise. The trap is that the layout is the drawn, while the conflicts are the not resolved, and the clearance is the inadequate. The false signal is the completed layout; the harm is the conflict and the contamination, because the clearance was not provided.

### The Storm Drain Bypassing The Management

The storm drain discharges directly to the outfall, bypassing the detention or the water quality facility, and the permit is violated. The trap is that the storm drain is the routed for the convenience, while the management is the bypassed, and the discharge violates the permit. The false signal is the connected storm drain; the harm is the permit violation, because the management was not integrated.

### The Municipal Capacity Not Verified

The site utility connects to the municipal system without the capacity verification, and the municipal system is the overloaded (the sewer surcharge, the storm flooding). The trap is that the connection is the made, while the capacity is the not verified, and the municipal system is the overloaded. The false signal is the approved connection; the harm is the overload, because the capacity was not checked.

## Self-Check

- Are the demand and the design flow established for each system (the water domestic and fire, the sewer peak and infiltration, the storm design storm)?
- Are the pipes and the channels sized for the capacity and the velocity (the self-cleaning minimum, the non-erosive maximum), with the minimum slope and diameter?
- Are the inverts and the hydraulic grade set for the gravity flow, the cover (the frost, the load), and the below-floor (the backup protection)?
- Are the utilities coordinated with the grading (the structures at the surface) and the structures (the building connection, the foundation clearance)?
- Are the conflicts resolved and the clearance provided (the water above the sewer, the casing at the crossing)?
- Is the stormwater management integrated, with the flow routed through the detention, the water quality, and the infiltration before the discharge?
- Is the connection to the municipal system verified for the capacity (the water pressure and fire flow, the sewer capacity, the storm outfall), and the approval obtained?
- Are the minimum cover and the frost depth provided for all the gravity and the pressure systems?
