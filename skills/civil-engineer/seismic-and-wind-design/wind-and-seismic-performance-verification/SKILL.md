# Wind And Seismic Performance Verification

Performance verification is the act of confirming, by computation and check, that the building's lateral system meets the code's drift, stability, and separation limits, and where the project demands, the performance-based objectives for a higher hazard or a stricter damage state, and it is the final gate before the design is issued, where the assumptions and the analysis are tested against the acceptance criteria. Agents who run the analysis and report that it "works" miss that the verification requires checking every story against the drift limit, every adjacent structure against the pounding gap, every slender frame against the stability coefficient, and where the project is performance-based, every component against its plastic rotation or deformation acceptance. This skill covers the judgment exercised while verifying wind and seismic performance, with the goal that the building meets every applicable limit, the separation and the stability are confirmed, and the performance, code or enhanced, is demonstrated by the analysis.

## Core Rules

### Check Story Drift Against The Code Limit At Every Story

Story drift, the relative horizontal displacement of adjacent floors, is the governing criterion for both wind and seismic performance, and it must be checked at every story against the code's limit, which depends on the seismic design category and the occupancy. Compute the drift under the design wind and the design seismic, using the amplified inelastic drift for seismic, with the deflection amplification factor, because the elastic drift under-predicts the real movement. Check every story, not just the maximum, because a single story exceeding the limit, a soft story, is a violation and a weakness, and the drift must be within the limit at every level. Confirm the drift limit is appropriate for the structure's non-structural components, because a tighter limit may be required for brittle cladding or sensitive equipment. The drift check is the lateral system's primary verification, and it must be done at every story with the amplified value.

### Verify Stability Under P-Delta At Every Story

The building's stability under the combined lateral displacement and the gravity load, the P-delta effect, must be verified at every story, because a story that meets the drift limit can still be unstable if the second-order amplification is large. Compute the stability coefficient at every story, using the story shear, the story drift, the vertical load, and the story height, and compare it to the code's limit, because a coefficient above the limit indicates instability that cannot be solved by strength alone and requires additional stiffness. Where the coefficient is between the threshold and the limit, apply the amplification to the drift and the forces, because the second-order effect is significant and must be included. Where the coefficient exceeds the limit, redesign the lateral system for additional stiffness, because stability is a stiffness problem, and adding strength does not stabilize an unstable frame. The stability check is the lateral system's interaction with gravity, and it must be done at every story.

### Confirm Seismic Separation To Prevent Pounding

Adjacent structures, the building and its neighbor, or two wings of the same building separated by a joint, must be separated by a gap that prevents pounding under the design seismic, because the collision of two swaying structures causes localized catastrophic damage. Compute the required separation as the square root of the sum of the squares of the two structures' inelastic drifts, or the sum, per the code, accounting for both structures' movement toward each other. Confirm the provided gap exceeds the required, at every floor and every point of proximity, because the gap varies with the floor and the proximity, and a gap adequate at the ground may be inadequate at the upper floors. Where the gap cannot be provided, consider the pounding and its consequences explicitly, which is rarely feasible, or modify the structure to reduce its drift. The separation check is the building's interaction with its neighbors, and it must be confirmed at every point of proximity.

### Check The Torsional Response And Its Amplification

The building's torsional response, the rotation due to the offset between the center of mass and the center of rigidity, must be checked, and where the torsional irregularity trigger is exceeded, the amplified accidental torsion must be applied. Compute the torsional irregularity indicator, the ratio of the maximum to the average drift at the building's edges, and compare it to the code's trigger, because a building that exceeds the trigger has a torsional irregularity that requires the amplified accidental torsion and may face additional penalties. Apply the amplified accidental eccentricity, where the trigger is exceeded, to the design, because the torsion increases the demand on the perimeter elements and they must be designed for it. Check the extreme torsional irregularity, which may be prohibited in high seismic categories, because a building with extreme torsion cannot be permitted and the layout must be revised. The torsional check is the lateral system's response to plan asymmetry, and it must be done with the amplification where triggered.

### Perform Nonlinear Analysis For Performance-Based Design

Where the project's performance objectives exceed the code's life safety, for critical facilities, tall buildings, or base-isolated structures, a nonlinear analysis is required to verify the performance at the design and the maximum-considered hazard. Perform the nonlinear response history analysis, with the matched or scaled ground motions, modeling the nonlinear behavior of the fuses and the elastic behavior of the protected elements, because the linear analysis cannot capture the ductility demand and the plastic rotation that the performance verification requires. Check the components' acceptance criteria, the plastic rotation or the deformation, against the code's or the project's limits for the performance level, because the performance is verified component by component, not by a global force check. Evaluate the global response, the base shear, the drift, and the stability, against the performance objectives, because the global indicators confirm the system's behavior. The nonlinear analysis is the tool for performance beyond the code, and it must be performed with the rigor the performance demands.

### Verify Non-Structural Components And Cladding Performance

The building's performance includes the non-structural components, the cladding, the partitions, the ceilings, and the equipment, which are damaged by the drift and the acceleration, and their performance must be verified against the project's damage objectives. Check the cladding and the partitions for the drift they can tolerate, comparing the design drift to the component's accommodation, because a cladding designed for a smaller drift than the building experiences cracks or falls. Design the cladding's connections for the seismic accelerations, with the component force amplification, because the acceleration at the upper floors exceeds the ground acceleration and the connection must resist it. Check the equipment and the systems, the HVAC, the piping, and the electrical, for their seismic anchorage and their performance, because their failure can disable a building that is otherwise structurally sound. The non-structural verification is part of the building's performance, and it must be done alongside the structural verification.

### Document The Performance Verification For Review And Record

The performance verification must be documented, with the drift at every story, the stability coefficient, the torsion check, the separation, and where applicable, the nonlinear results and the component acceptances, because the verification is the proof that the building meets the criteria, and it must be reviewable and archival. Document the acceptance criteria, the code limits and the project objectives, and the analysis results against them, with the margin or the satisfaction noted, because a verification without the criteria is a set of numbers without judgment. Document the analysis method, the model, the loads, and the assumptions, so that the verification can be reproduced and re-checked. The performance verification documentation is the design's evidence of adequacy, and it must be as complete as the design itself, because the verification is what the reviewer and the future engineer rely on.

## Common Traps

### The Drift Checked At The Maximum Story Only

The engineer checks the story drift at the story with the maximum value and confirms it is within the limit, without checking every story. The mechanism is that the drift varies over the building's height, and a story other than the maximum can exceed the limit if the limit is tighter at that story or if the drift profile is irregular, and checking only the maximum misses a story that violates the limit. The false signal is that the maximum drift "is within the limit." The harm is that an intermediate story exceeds the limit, a soft story or an irregular profile, and the violation is missed, allowing a weak story into the building. The drift must be checked at every story against the applicable limit, because the limit applies at every level, and a single violation is a failure.

### P-Delta Checked Globally, Not By Story

The engineer checks the P-delta stability for the building as a whole, or at the maximum drift story, without checking the stability coefficient at every story. The mechanism is that the stability coefficient depends on the story shear, drift, and vertical load, and it varies by story, and a story with a high vertical load and a moderate drift can have a critical coefficient even if it is not the maximum drift story, and checking globally or at one story misses the critical one. The false signal is that the building "is stable." The harm is that an intermediate story is unstable, the second-order amplification causes it to drift excessively or collapse, and the instability is missed because the coefficient was not checked there. The stability coefficient must be checked at every story, because the stability is a story-by-story property, and the critical story may not be the one with the maximum drift.

### The Separation Sized For One Structure's Drift

The engineer sizes the seismic separation to the building's own drift, without accounting for the neighbor's drift toward it, and the gap is half of what is needed. The mechanism is that pounding occurs when two structures move toward each other, and the required separation is the combination of both structures' drifts, typically the square root of the sum of the squares, and sizing for one structure's drift alone under-provides the gap by a large factor. The false signal is that a gap "is provided." The harm is that the two structures collide in the earthquake, the pounding damages both at the collision point, and the damage can be catastrophic if the collision is at a column or a floor level, because the gap was sized for one drift, not two. The separation must be sized for the combination of both structures' inelastic drifts, because pounding is a two-structure event.

### Torsional Irregularity Trigger Exceeded, Amplification Not Applied

The engineer computes the torsional irregularity indicator and finds it exceeds the trigger, but does not apply the amplified accidental torsion, treating the indicator as information rather than a design requirement. The mechanism is that the torsional irregularity trigger, when exceeded, requires the amplified accidental eccentricity to account for the increased torsional demand and the uncertainty in the center of mass and rigidity, and omitting the amplification under-designs the perimeter elements that the torsion loads. The false signal is that the torsion "is computed." The harm is that the perimeter elements, the edge walls or frames, are under-designed for the amplified torsion, and they fail in the earthquake, because the amplification that addressed the irregularity was not applied. The amplified accidental torsion must be applied where the trigger is exceeded, because the trigger is a design requirement, not a notation.

### Nonlinear Analysis Run, Acceptance Criteria Not Checked

The engineer performs the nonlinear response history analysis and reports the base shear and the drift, without checking the individual components' plastic rotations or deformations against the acceptance criteria. The mechanism is that the nonlinear analysis's purpose is to verify the component-level performance, the plastic rotation in the hinges and the deformation in the braces, against the acceptance limits for the performance level, and reporting only the global response does not verify the components, and a component can exceed its acceptance even if the global response looks reasonable. The false signal is that the analysis "ran and the building stood." The harm is that a component exceeds its plastic rotation capacity, fails in the earthquake, and the global response that looked acceptable masked the local failure, because the acceptance criteria were not checked component by component. The nonlinear analysis must include the component acceptance check, with the plastic rotation or deformation compared to the limit, because the performance is verified at the component level.

### Non-Structural Components Not Verified

The engineer verifies the structural lateral system's drift and stability, without checking the cladding, the partitions, or the equipment for the drift and the acceleration they will experience. The mechanism is that the non-structural components are damaged by the drift and the acceleration, and their failure, the cracked cladding, the fallen ceiling, or the displaced equipment, can injure occupants and disable the building, even if the structure is sound, and the verification that omits them misses a major performance failure. The false signal is that the structure "meets the drift limit." The harm is that the cladding cracks and falls, the partitions damage, the equipment shifts, and the building is unusable after a moderate earthquake, because the non-structural components were not verified for the drift and the acceleration. The non-structural components must be verified, with the cladding and partitions checked for the drift and the equipment anchored for the acceleration, because the building's performance includes its non-structural systems.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Is the story drift checked at every story against the applicable limit, using the amplified inelastic drift for seismic and the appropriate limit for the occupancy and the non-structural components?
- [ ] Is the P-delta stability coefficient checked at every story, with the amplification applied where it is between the threshold and the limit, and the system stiffened where it exceeds the limit?
- [ ] Is the seismic separation to adjacent structures confirmed at every point of proximity, sized for the combination of both structures' inelastic drifts?
- [ ] Is the torsional irregularity indicator checked, and where the trigger is exceeded, is the amplified accidental torsion applied, with the extreme torsion's prohibition respected?
- [ ] For performance-based projects, is the nonlinear response history analysis performed with matched ground motions, and are the components' plastic rotations or deformations checked against the acceptance criteria?
- [ ] Are the non-structural components, cladding, partitions, ceilings, and equipment, verified for the drift and the acceleration they will experience?
- [ ] Is the performance verification documented with the criteria, the analysis method, the results, and the satisfaction, for review and archival?
- [ ] Has the verification confirmed that the building meets every applicable code limit and every project-specific performance objective before the design is issued?
