# Structural Steel Welding and Bolting Inspection

Structural steel connection inspection is a domain where the connection is the weak link in the load path, where a weld or a bolt that looks good on the surface can hide a lack-of-fusion, a crack, or an under-tension that reduces the capacity to a fraction of the design, and where the inspection tasks — verifying the weld procedure and the welder qualification, confirming the bolt pretension, and running the nondestructive testing (NDT) — must be performed to the applicable standards before the connection is accepted. The recurring failure is to accept a weld on visual appearance alone, to verify bolt pretension by feel or by the absence of complaint rather than by the specified method, to apply NDT selectively or not at all, and to treat a weld defect or a loose bolt as a minor cosmetic issue rather than a capacity reduction. The engineer's job is to verify the welding and bolting against AWS D1.1 and the AISC Specification, to apply the NDT where it is required, and to decide on acceptance, repair, or rejection based on the defect's effect on the connection capacity. This skill covers the judgment used to inspect structural steel welding and bolting, with the overriding principle that the connection capacity depends on what is inside the weld and the bolt, not on the surface appearance.

## Core Rules

### Verify the Welding Procedure, the Welder Qualification, and the Consumables

A sound weld requires a qualified Welding Procedure Specification (WPS) supported by a Procedure Qualification Record (PQR), a welder qualified for the procedure and the position, and consumables (electrode, flux, shielding gas) that match the procedure and the base metal. Verify the WPS and the PQR before welding begins, confirm the welder's qualification covers the position and the process, and confirm the consumables on site match the WPS. A weld made with an unqualified procedure, an unqualified welder, or the wrong consumable may look sound but lack the required mechanical properties, and it must be rejected regardless of appearance. The procedure, the qualification, and the consumables are the prerequisites for a sound weld.

### Apply the AWS D1.1 Visual Inspection Criteria to Every Weld

Visual inspection is the first and primary inspection for every weld, performed to the AWS D1.1 criteria, and it checks the weld size, the length, the profile, the undercut, the porosity, the overlap, the cracks, and the alignment against the code limits. Perform the visual inspection with the weld clean (slag removed, spatter cleaned), measure the weld size with a weld gauge, and compare every observable feature to the criteria. A weld that fails visual inspection — excessive undercut, undersized, cracked, excessive porosity — is rejected or repaired before any further NDT, because the visual defects often indicate deeper problems. Visual inspection is not optional or cursory; it is the baseline, and it must be performed and documented for every weld.

### Apply NDT (UT, MT, PT, RT) Where Required by the Code or the Design

Nondestructive testing reveals the internal defects — lack of fusion, incomplete penetration, internal porosity, cracks — that visual inspection cannot see, and it is required by AWS D1.1 for certain welds (complete-joint-penetration groove welds in tension, seismic welds in special moment frames) and by the design for critical connections. Apply the NDT method appropriate to the defect and the joint: ultrasonic testing (UT) for internal discontinuities in thicker materials, magnetic particle testing (MT) for surface and near-surface cracks in ferromagnetic materials, dye penetrant testing (PT) for surface cracks in any material, radiographic testing (RT) for internal defects in a full penetration weld. Specify the extent of NDT (100 percent for critical welds, a percentage for routine) and apply it to the welds where it is required, not selectively or by convenience. A critical weld without NDT is an unverified connection.

### Verify the Bolt Pretension by the Specified Method

Bolted connections that require pretension — slip-critical connections, connections subject to fatigue or load reversal, and the bolted joints in seismic force-resisting systems — must be pretensioned by a specified method: turn-of-nut, calibrated wrench, direct tension indicator (DTI) washers, or tension-control (TC) bolts with the torque-notch sheared off. Verify the pretension by the specified method, with the method demonstrated and calibrated, and do not accept a bolt as "tight" by feel or by the absence of complaint. The pretension is what makes a slip-critical connection slip-critical and a seismic connection reliable, and a bolt that is under-tensioned does not provide the capacity, regardless of how it looks. The verification method must match the specification.

### Confirm the Connection Type (Bearing vs Slip-Critical) and the Applicable Requirements

The connection type — bearing (the bolts bear on the connected material), slip-critical (the friction between the faying surfaces resists the load), or direct tension — sets the requirements: the bolt grade, the hole type, the faying surface preparation, the pretension, and the inspection. Confirm the connection type from the design drawings, verify the faying surface is prepared as required for slip-critical (unpainted, or painted with a qualified paint, and free of oil and dirt), and apply the inspection requirements for that type. A slip-critical connection with a painted or oily faying surface, or with no pretension, is a bearing connection in disguise and does not provide the slip resistance the design assumed. The connection type must be confirmed and its requirements met.

### Evaluate Weld Defects Against the Acceptance Criteria and the Connection Capacity

When a weld defect is found — by visual inspection or NDT — evaluate it against the AWS D1.1 acceptance criteria, which set the allowable size and type of defect by the weld and the loading. Some defects (cracks, lack of fusion) are rejectable regardless of size; others (porosity, undercut) are acceptable up to a limit. For a defect that exceeds the criteria, evaluate the repair (gouge out and re-weld), and for a defect that is near the limit, consider the effect on the connection capacity before deciding. Do not dismiss a defect as cosmetic without evaluating its effect, and do not repair a defect without a procedure that does not introduce new problems (a repair weld that induces a crack or a distortion). The defect evaluation must consider the capacity, not just the appearance.

### Coordinate the Inspection Sequence With the Construction Sequence

The inspection of welding and bolting must be coordinated with the construction sequence, because some inspections cannot be performed after the connection is enclosed or the structure is loaded. Perform the faying surface inspection for slip-critical connections before the bolts are tightened, perform the bolt pretension verification during the tightening, perform the visual weld inspection after the weld is complete and before it is painted or enclosed, and perform the NDT after the weld has cooled and within the specified window. Coordinate the inspection with the erector's sequence, document each inspection, and do not allow a connection to be enclosed or loaded before the inspection is complete. An inspection that is missed because the connection was enclosed is an inspection that cannot be recovered.

## Common Traps

### Weld Accepted on Visual Appearance Without NDT

The weld is accepted based on visual appearance — it looks uniform and full — without the NDT that would reveal the internal lack of fusion, incomplete penetration, or cracks. The mechanism is that a good-looking weld feels like a sound weld and NDT feels like extra scope. The false signal is a weld that "looks good." The harm is that the internal defects, which are common in complete-joint-penetration welds and in the root passes of groove welds, reduce the capacity to a fraction of the design, and the connection fails under load despite the sound appearance. NDT must be applied where required.

### Bolt Pretension Verified by Feel, Not by the Specified Method

The bolt pretension is verified by the inspector's feel or by the erector's statement that the bolts are tight, rather than by the specified method (turn-of-nut rotation, TC bolt spline shear, DTI protrusion, calibrated wrench torque). The mechanism is that a tight bolt feels tight and the method feels like procedure. The false signal is a bolt that "is tight." The harm is that the pretension is well below the required value, the slip-critical connection slips at a fraction of the design load, or the seismic connection does not develop the intended friction, and the connection fails in service. The pretension must be verified by the specified method.

### Slip-Critical Connection With Unprepared or Painted Faying Surfaces

The slip-critical connection is assembled with painted, oily, or dirty faying surfaces, or with the wrong paint, so the slip coefficient is far below the qualified value. The mechanism is that the faying surface is not inspected before bolting and the paint or oil is not noticed. The false signal is a connection that "is bolted up." The harm is that the friction the slip-critical design depends on is not present, the connection slips at a fraction of the design load, and the bolts go into bearing and possibly shear off or elongate the holes. The faying surface must be inspected and prepared before bolting.

### NDT Applied Selectively or Not at All

The NDT is applied to a few convenient welds, or omitted entirely, rather than to the welds where it is required (complete-joint-penetration tension welds, seismic welds, critical connections). The mechanism is that NDT is expensive and feels like it can be sampled. The false signal is an NDT report. The harm is that the critical welds that need NDT are not tested, the internal defects in those welds are not found, and the connections that carry the highest demand fail. NDT must be applied to the welds where it is required, at the specified extent.

### Weld Defect Dismissed as Cosmetic Without Capacity Evaluation

A weld defect — undercut, porosity, a small crack — is noticed and dismissed as cosmetic, without evaluating its effect on the connection capacity. The mechanism is that a small defect feels like a minor issue. The false signal is a defect that "is not structural." The harm is that the defect, which may exceed the acceptance criteria or may reduce the capacity at a critical location, is left in place, and the connection fails under load at the defect. Every defect must be evaluated against the acceptance criteria and the capacity.

### Unqualified WPS or Welder Not Caught Before Welding

The welder or the WPS is not qualified for the procedure, the position, or the process, and the welding proceeds, producing a weld that may lack the required properties. The mechanism is that the qualifications feel like paperwork and the welder feels experienced. The false signal is a welder who "can do the work." The harm is that the weld, made with an unqualified procedure or by an unqualified welder, may have internal defects or inadequate mechanical properties, and it must be rejected and re-done at significant cost. The qualifications must be verified before welding.

### Inspection Missed Because the Connection Was Enclosed

The inspection of the weld or the bolt is missed because the connection was enclosed (fireproofed, covered, or the next piece erected) before the inspector arrived. The mechanism is that the construction sequence moves fast and the inspection is not coordinated. The false signal is a connection that "is done." The harm is that the uninspected connection may have defects or under-tensioned bolts, and the inspection cannot be recovered without removing the enclosure or the adjacent piece. The inspection must be coordinated with the sequence and completed before the connection is enclosed.

## Self-Check

- Are the WPS, PQR, welder qualifications, and consumables verified to match the procedure, the position, the process, and the base metal before welding begins?
- Is the AWS D1.1 visual inspection performed and documented for every weld, with the weld size, profile, undercut, porosity, and cracks checked against the code criteria?
- Is NDT (UT, MT, PT, RT) applied to the welds where it is required by code or design (CJP tension welds, seismic welds, critical connections), at the specified extent, rather than selectively?
- Is the bolt pretension verified by the specified method (turn-of-nut, TC bolt, DTI, calibrated wrench), with the method demonstrated and calibrated, rather than by feel?
- Is the connection type (bearing, slip-critical, direct tension) confirmed from the design, with the faying surface prepared and the pretension applied for slip-critical connections?
- Are weld defects evaluated against the AWS D1.1 acceptance criteria and the connection capacity, with repair procedures that do not introduce new defects?
- Is the inspection sequence coordinated with the construction sequence, so that no connection is enclosed or loaded before the inspection is complete?
