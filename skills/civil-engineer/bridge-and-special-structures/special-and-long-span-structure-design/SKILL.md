# Special and Long-Span Structure Design

Special and long-span structure design is the engineering of the structures that fall outside the conventional building: the stadium and the long-span roof (the steel truss, the space frame, the tensile, the dome), the transmission and the communication tower, the industrial (the silo, the tank, the stack), the pedestrian bridge, and the other special structures. These structures have the non-standard loads (the wind on the open frame, the vortex shedding, the thermal), the non-standard systems (the tensile, the shell, the space frame), and the non-standard considerations (the buckling, the dynamics, the erection). The harm this skill prevents is a long-span roof that fails the wind or the ponding (the progressive collapse), a tower that fails the vortex shedding or the galloping (the fatigue or the dynamic), a tank that fails the buckling or the contents, or a structure that fails during the erection (the collapse before the completion). Because the special structures are the high-visibility, the high-uncertainty, and the often-non-standard, the design must be against the specific code (the ASCE 7, the ASCE for the steel stacks, the ACI for the silos, the AASHTO for the pedestrian bridge), the wind tunnel, and the non-linear analysis, with the conservative and the verified approach.

## Core Rules

### Identify the Applicable Code and the Non-Standard Provisions

The special structures have the specific codes and the non-standard provisions that differ from the building: the ASCE 7 for the loads (the open frame, the tank, the tower), the AISC for the steel, the ACI 313 for the silos, the ACI 350 for the environmental concrete, the AASHTO for the pedestrian bridge, the ASCE 7 and the TIA for the transmission tower. Identify the applicable code and the provisions, and do not default to the building code, because the special structure has the specific requirements (the wind, the dynamics, the buckling) that the building code does not address. A design with the wrong code misses the specific provisions, because the code was not identified.

### Select the Structural System for the Function and the Span

The structural system must be selected for the function (the stadium, the roof, the tower, the tank), the span, and the load. For the long-span roof, the steel truss (to 60 metres), the space frame (to 100 metres), the tensile (the cable and the membrane, to 200 metres), the dome (the 200 metres and above). For the tower, the lattice (the steel, the high), the the monopole (the lower). For the tank, the steel or the concrete, the above-ground or the underground. Select the system that is the efficient for the span and the load, and the constructible, and consider the maintenance and the inspectability.

### Analyse the Wind and the Non-Standard Loads

The wind and the non-standard loads are the dominant for the special structures, and they must be analysed with the appropriate method. For the wind, use the ASCE 7 (the open frame, the tank, the roof), the wind tunnel (the long-span, the unusual shape, the dynamic), and account for the across-wind (the vortex shedding, the galloping), the internal pressure, and the fatigue. For the thermal, account for the expansion and the contraction of the long span. For the contents (the silo, the tank), use the specific load (the ACI 313, the API), and the dynamic (the sloshing, the fluid-structure). A design with the simplified wind (the building method) for the open frame or the long-span underestimates the load, because the wind was not the appropriate.

### Evaluate the Dynamics and the Serviceability

The dynamics (the vibration, the vortex shedding, the flutter) and the serviceability (the deflection, the acceleration) are the critical for the special structures, and they must be evaluated. For the pedestrian bridge, evaluate the vertical and the lateral vibration (the AASHTO, the natural frequency, the acceleration), and the syncronised walking. For the long-span roof, evaluate the wind dynamics and the ponding. For the tower, evaluate the vortex shedding and the galloping. Verify the natural frequency, the acceleration, and the deflection against the human comfort and the function criteria, because the special structures are the dynamic-sensitive, and the dynamics are the serviceability and the safety issue. A design that checks the strength but not the dynamics may fail the vibration or the flutter, because the dynamics were not evaluated.

### Design for the Buckling and the Stability

The buckling and the stability are the critical for the slender and the shell structures (the dome, the tank, the tower, the arch), and they must be designed with the non-linear analysis. For the shell (the dome, the tank), evaluate the buckling (the eigenvalue, the non-linear, the imperfection), and the design against the knockdown factor. For the arch and the long-span, evaluate the in-plane and the out-of-plane buckling, and the bracing. For the tower, evaluate the global and the member buckling. A design that uses the linear analysis for the slender or the shell overestimates the capacity, because the buckling and the imperfection were not in the analysis.

### Detail the Connections and the Critical Elements

The connections and the critical elements (the cable anchorage, the tensile termination, the space frame node, the base of the tower) are the high-stress and the high-consequence, and they must be detailed with the care. Detail the cable anchorage (the socket, the fatigue, the corrosion), the tensile termination, the space frame node (the bolt, the weld, the eccentricity), and the tower base (the anchor bolt, the foundation). Verify the connection capacity (the strength, the fatigue, the ductility), and the inspection access, because the connection failure is the catastrophic, and the connection is the difficult to inspect.

### Plan the Erection and the Construction Sequence

The erection and the construction sequence are the critical for the special structures, because the structure is the unstable during the construction, and the load path changes. Plan the erection (the shoring, the temporary bracing, the cable tensioning, the segment construction), and verify the stability at each stage, and the load (the construction, the wind during the erection). Provide the erection procedure and the engineering, and the monitoring, because the structure that is the stable in the completed state may fail during the erection, because the temporary condition was not engineered.

### Address the Durability and the Maintenance

The special structures are the long-life and the difficult to access, and the durability and the maintenance must be addressed. For the steel, the corrosion protection (the coating, the galvanising, the weathering steel), and the inspection access. For the tensile, the cable and the membrane durability (the corrosion, the UV, the fatigue), and the replacement. For the concrete, the durability (the cover, the mix), and the crack control. Provide the access (the walkway, the lift, the rope access), and the maintenance plan, because the special structure without the maintenance deteriorates, because the access and the plan were not provided.

## Common Traps

### The Building Code Applied To The Special Structure

The building code (the IBC, the ASCE 7 for the buildings) is applied to the special structure (the tank, the tower, the silo), and the specific provisions (the wind, the contents, the buckling) are missed. The trap is that the building code is the familiar, while the special structure has the specific code, and the provisions are the not applied. The false signal is the code-compliant design; the harm is the missed provisions, because the wrong code was used.

### The Simplified Wind For The Dynamic Structure

The simplified wind (the ASCE 7 building method) is used for the long-span or the tower, and the across-wind (the vortex shedding, the galloping) and the dynamics are the not evaluated. The trap is that the simplified wind is the used, while the structure is the dynamic, and the across-wind and the dynamics are the not accounted. The false signal is the wind load calculated; the harm is the dynamic failure or the fatigue, because the wind was not the appropriate.

### The Dynamics Not Evaluated

The structure is designed for the strength, but the dynamics (the vibration, the flutter, the vortex shedding) are the not evaluated, and the structure fails the vibration or the flutter. The trap is that the strength is the checked, while the dynamics are the not, and the structure fails the serviceability or the dynamic. The false signal is the strong structure; the harm is the vibration or the flutter, because the dynamics were not evaluated.

### The Linear Analysis For The Slender Or The Shell

The linear analysis is used for the slender or the shell structure (the dome, the tank, the arch), and the buckling and the imperfection are the not accounted, and the capacity is the overestimated. The trap is that the linear is the used, while the structure is the slender or the shell, and the buckling is the not accounted. The false signal is the calculated capacity; the harm is the overestimated capacity, because the non-linear and the buckling were not in the analysis.

### The Erection Not Engineered

The structure is designed for the completed state, but the erection (the temporary condition, the shoring, the wind during the erection) is the not engineered, and the structure fails during the construction. The trap is that the completed is the designed, while the erection is the not, and the structure is the unstable during the construction. The false signal is the designed structure; the harm is the erection collapse, because the temporary condition was not engineered.

### The Connection Under-Detailed

The connection (the cable anchorage, the node, the base) is the under-detailed, and the connection fails the fatigue or the brittle. The trap is that the member is the designed, while the connection is the under-detailed, and the connection is the weak link. The false signal is the designed member; the harm is the connection failure, because the connection was not detailed.

## Self-Check

- Is the applicable code (the ASCE 7, the ACI 313, the AASHTO, the TIA) identified, and the non-standard provisions applied, and not the building code default?
- Is the structural system selected for the function, the span, and the load, with the maintenance and the inspectability?
- Is the wind analysed with the appropriate method (the ASCE 7, the wind tunnel), and the across-wind (the vortex shedding, the galloping), the internal pressure, and the fatigue accounted?
- Are the dynamics (the vibration, the flutter, the acceleration) evaluated, and the serviceability (the deflection, the acceleration) verified against the human comfort and the function?
- Is the buckling and the stability (the non-linear, the imperfection, the knockdown) evaluated for the slender and the shell, and not the linear analysis?
- Are the connections and the critical elements (the cable anchorage, the node, the base) detailed for the strength, the fatigue, and the ductility, with the inspection access?
- Is the erection and the construction sequence engineered, with the stability at each stage, the temporary bracing, and the wind during the erection?
- Is the durability (the corrosion protection, the membrane, the cover) and the maintenance (the access, the plan) addressed, for the long-life and the difficult access?
