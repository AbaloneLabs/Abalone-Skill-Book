# Medical Gas System Testing and Certification to NFPA 99

A medical gas system carries life-support gases directly to patients, and NFPA 99 imposes a sequence of acceptance tests because the cost of contamination, misconnection, or particulate reaching a patient is injury or death. The judgment problem is that the tests are interdependent and order-dependent (you cannot meaningfully test a system that was brazed without a nitrogen purge, or certify outlets before the cross-connection test proves no gas goes to the wrong port), and the work must be done by ASSE 6010 (installers) and verified by ASSE 6030 (verifiers) with a documentation package the AHJ will audit. An installer who skips the purge, who pressure-tests with the wrong gas, or who certifies outlets without the cross-connection test delivers a system that can kill a patient on first use. This skill covers the testing, credentialing, documentation, and pre-patient-use verification decisions that make a medical gas system certifiable.

## Core Rules

### Use Only ASSE 6010/6020-Certified Installers and an ASSE 6030 Verifier

NFPA 99 requires that medical gas piping be installed by ASSE 6010-certified medical gas installers (ASSE 6020 for medical vacuum), brazed by certified brazers, and that the final system be verified by an independent, ASSE 6030-certified third-party verifier who did not install the system. The trap is using a general plumber or an uncertified crew for "just a small run," which voids the certification and introduces contamination risk. The disciplined rule is to confirm and document the certification of every person brazing or joining medical gas piping, to keep the verifier independent of the installer, and to record the certification numbers on the documentation package. Uncertified work cannot be certified after the fact; it must be redone.

### Braze With a Continuous Nitrogen Purge and Use Only Medical-Gas-Cleaned Materials

NFPA 99 requires that all medical gas brazed joints be made with a continuous nitrogen purge through the pipe during brazing to prevent internal oxidation (which produces flakes that reach patient outlets), and that all tubes, fittings, and valves be medical-gas-cleaned and oil-free (oxygen-clean for oxygen service). The trap is brazing without purge (producing black copper oxide scale inside the pipe) or using standard plumbing fittings that carry oil or debris. The disciplined rule is to set up the nitrogen purge before any brazing, to maintain flow during the braze and cool-down, to use only cleaned-and-capped medical gas components, and to keep pipe ends capped until jointing. A system brazed without purge will fail the particulate test and must be cleaned or replaced.

### Run the Tests in Order: Cross-Connection, Pressure, Purge, Particulate, Dewpoint

The NFPA 99 acceptance test sequence is interdependent. The cross-connection (cross-identification) test proves each outlet is connected to the correct gas and that no gas flows to the wrong outlet, using the test gas at each source; it must be done before any outlet is trusted. The pressure test proves the system holds at test pressure (commonly 150 percent of operating pressure, held for a documented time) with no leaks. The purge removes test gas and construction air. The particulate (particulate and microbial) test confirms the gas at outlets is clean. The medical air dewpoint test confirms medical air moisture content (commonly at or below the NFPA limit, e.g., a dewpoint low enough to inhibit bacterial growth). The disciplined rule is to run them in order, to document each, and to fail the system if any test fails rather than moving on.

### Verify the Documentation Package Is Complete Before Requesting AHJ Sign-Off

The certification documentation package is what the AHJ, the facility, and any future investigator rely on. It must include installer and verifier certifications, material certificates (cleaned for medical gas), the brazing purge procedure and log, the cross-connection test results for every outlet, the pressure test chart, the purge record, the particulate and dewpoint test results, the as-built drawings, and the outlet schedule. The trap is a package missing the brazing log or the material certificates, which makes the certification unverifiable. The disciplined rule is to assemble the complete package as the work proceeds (not reconstruct it at the end), to have the ASSE 6030 verifier sign each section, and to deliver the package to the facility and AHJ before the system is placed in service.

### Confirm System Verification and Source Readiness Before Any Patient Use

No medical gas system may supply patients until verification is complete and the sources (manifold, compressor, vacuum pump, medical air plant) are commissioned, alarms verified, and the facility has accepted the system. The trap is turning on outlets for a rushed patient move before the verifier has signed off. The disciplined rule is to treat the system as out of service (capped, labeled "do not use") until the verifier releases it, to confirm sources are online and alarms are active, and to brief clinical staff that the system is certified. A system used before verification is an uncontrolled hazard, and any adverse event becomes the installer's liability.

## Common Traps

### Using Uncertified Installers for a "Small Run"

A general plumbing crew runs a short oxygen extension without ASSE 6010 personnel. The trap is that the work looks identical but cannot be certified. The mechanism is that NFPA 99 requires certified installers for all medical gas work. The false signal is that "the pipe is connected." The harm is an uncertified, potentially contaminated line feeding a patient. The defense is to use only ASSE 6010/6020-certified personnel and to document their certification numbers.

### Brazing Without a Nitrogen Purge

Joints are brazed without purge to save time, leaving black copper oxide scale inside the pipe. The trap is that the scale is invisible until it reaches an outlet. The mechanism is that oxygen-free brazing requires a purge to prevent internal oxidation. The false signal is a joint that "looks good." The harm is particulate failing the test, or worse, reaching a patient if the test is skipped. The defense is a continuous nitrogen purge during brazing and cool-down, documented in the brazing log.

### Skipping or Reordering the Cross-Connection Test

The installer pressure-tests and certifies outlets without first proving each outlet receives only its correct gas. The trap is that a crossed line passes pressure but delivers the wrong gas. The mechanism is that the cross-connection test is the only proof of correct gas routing. The false signal is that "the system holds pressure." The harm is a patient receiving nitrous oxide at an oxygen outlet. The defense is to run the cross-connection test first, at every outlet, before any certification.

### A Documentation Package Missing the Brazing Log or Material Certificates

The package has the test results but omits the brazing purge log or the cleaned-for-medical-gas material certificates. The trap is that the certification is unverifiable without them. The mechanism is that the package must prove materials and methods, not just results. The false signal is a signed verifier report. The harm is AHJ rejection, or an un-defendable record after an adverse event. The defense is to assemble the complete package as work proceeds and have the verifier sign each section.

### Placing the System in Service Before Verification

Outlets are turned on for a patient move before the ASSE 6030 verifier releases the system. The trap is that an unverified system is an uncontrolled hazard. The mechanism is that NFPA 99 requires verification before patient use. The false signal is that "the gas is flowing." The harm is patient exposure to a contaminated or misconnected system. The defense is to keep the system capped and labeled out of service until the verifier releases it and sources and alarms are confirmed.

## Self-Check

- Were all installers and brazers ASSE 6010/6020-certified, and is the verifier an independent ASSE 6030-certified third party, with certification numbers recorded?
- Was every brazed joint made with a continuous nitrogen purge during brazing and cool-down, and is the purge documented in the brazing log?
- Are all tubes, fittings, and valves medical-gas-cleaned (oxygen-clean for oxygen service), with material certificates on file?
- Was the cross-connection test run first, at every outlet, proving each outlet receives only its correct gas with no cross-flow?
- Did the pressure test hold at the required test pressure (commonly 150 percent of operating) for the documented duration with no leaks?
- Were the purge, particulate, and medical air dewpoint tests run in sequence after the pressure test, with results documented and within NFPA 99 limits?
- Does the documentation package include installer/verifier certifications, material certificates, brazing purge log, cross-connection results, pressure chart, purge record, particulate and dewpoint results, as-builts, and outlet schedule?
- Was the complete package assembled as work proceeded and signed by the ASSE 6030 verifier, rather than reconstructed at the end?
- Was the system kept capped and labeled out of service until the verifier released it, with sources online and alarms verified?
- Did I brief clinical staff that the system is certified and confirm no patient use occurred before verification was complete?
