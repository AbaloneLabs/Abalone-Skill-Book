# Unit Publicity Strategy And Press Asset Planning

Unit publicity is the capture, clearance, and supply of the stills, video, and behind-the-scenes material that the entire later campaign depends on, and it is usually planned while the final marketing position, the sales agent's needs, and the platform's asset specs are still unknown. The agent is typically asked for a strategy before the constraints are visible: the cast's press availability may be uncertain, the shoot days that offer usable BTS material may be limited, and the rights to third-party material in the frame may not be cleared. The task is not to staff a publicist; it is to decide what assets must exist by the end of production, who can legally use them, and how they will reach every downstream buyer, platform, and journalist on time.

Unit publicity also converts advice into irreversible capture windows. A stills photographer who is not scheduled on the right day, a BTS crew that misses the climactic sequence, or a talent release that does not cover marketing use cannot be fixed in post; the asset simply does not exist, and the campaign must work around the gap. Use this skill to slow the decision down enough to expose hidden constraints, then make the recommendation staged, documented, and appropriately conservative when capture windows, talent clearance, asset sufficiency, and downstream delivery are involved.

## Core Rules

### Name The Audiences, The Calendar, And The Asset Map
Start by stating exactly who the assets must serve, on what calendar, and what specific assets each audience needs. Audiences typically include the sales agent and distributors, festivals, the platform or broadcaster, press and junket, social and digital, and the film's own website. Many weak publicity answers skip this mapping and collect generic BTS material. The correct plan works backwards from each audience's deliverable list and deadline, so that production captures exactly what the campaign will later demand.

### Capture Against The Shoot, Not The Wish List
Publicity capture is constrained by the production schedule, not by the marketing wish list. The agent should map the shoot's critical, visually rich, and emotionally representative days, and schedule the stills photographer and BTS crew to be present on those days, with the AD's cooperation and the cast's awareness. Capture that misses the signature sequences, the key cast interactions, or the production-design highlights produces a campaign that looks generic or that must be padded with frame grabs.

### Clear Talent, Music, And Third-Party Material For Marketing Use
Every asset must be cleared for the use it will actually see. Cast and crew appearances in BTS and stills require marketing and publicity rights in their deal or release; music audible in BTS video requires clearance or removal; visible third-party artwork, logos, signage, and trademarks require clearance or avoidance. The agent should ensure capture is planned around cleared material, and that releases are collected during production, because clearing after wrap is slower, costlier, and sometimes impossible.

### Define Ownership, Exclusivity, And Embargo Rules
Publicity assets are governed by ownership and exclusivity rules that must be set during production. The agent should clarify who owns the stills and BTS, who controls exclusives and embargoes, what may be released and when, and how sales-agent, distributor, and platform windows interact. Loose ownership and embargo rules lead to leaked exclusives, duplicated effort, and conflicts between distributors who believe they own the same asset.

### Match Quantity And Quality To Downstream Specs
Each downstream audience has its own asset specs: resolution, format, aspect ratio, duration, caption and credit requirements, and language. The agent should capture at a resolution and quality that serves the most demanding deliverable, slate the assets with accurate captions and credit lines, and avoid both undersupplying, which forces the campaign to reuse a few images to exhaustion, and oversupplying, which buries the best material. A curated, well-captioned library is more useful than a vast unsorted dump.

### Build A Living Asset Library With Version Control
Publicity material is used and reused across a long campaign, and a disorganized library wastes the capture. The agent should specify a single living library with naming, captioning, credit, clearance, and embargo metadata, version control for edited variants, and clear access for each downstream party. The library should be auditable so that any asset's clearance status can be confirmed before release.

### Stage The Plan And Preserve The Evidence Trail
Prefer a staged path: map audiences and the calendar, schedule capture against the shoot, collect clearances and releases, define ownership and embargoes, curate and caption the library, and supply each audience to spec. Record the asset map, the shoot-day capture schedule, the talent and third-party clearances, the ownership and embargo rules, the library metadata, and the downstream delivery log. If the recommendation cannot say what should be recorded, it has not defined the publicity plan clearly enough.

## Common Traps

### Collecting Generic BTS Without An Asset Map
Capturing whatever happens on set, without mapping what each downstream audience needs, produces a library that is large but useless at deadline.

### Missing The Signature Shoot Days
A stills or BTS crew not scheduled on the climactic or visually defining days cannot capture them later; the campaign must then improvise with weaker material.

### Assuming Cast And Music Are Cleared For Marketing
Marketing and publicity rights are separate from performance rights; uncleared cast, music, or third-party material can make otherwise excellent assets unusable.

### Loose Ownership And Exclusivity Rules
Without clear ownership, embargo, and exclusivity rules, assets leak, distributors clash, and exclusives lose their value.

### Oversupplying An Uncaptioned Dump
A huge library without captions, credits, and clearance metadata buries the best material and forces downstream users to guess at what they may release.

### Undersupplying And Reusing To Exhaustion
Too few strong assets force the campaign to repeat the same images until the audience and the press tire of them.

### Presenting Clearance And Contract Advice As Publicity Judgment
Talent releases, music clearance, and exclusivity contracts are deal- and jurisdiction-specific. The trap is approving a capture plan when the right next step is a clearance coordinator, legal, or the sales agent.

### Hiding Uncertainty Behind A Confident Tone
Publicity decisions often need action under uncertainty, but uncertainty should be named. State what is known, what is inferred, what must be verified, and what would change the recommendation.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Is the exact publicity plan tied to specific audiences, a marketing and sales calendar, and a per-audience asset map rather than generic BTS collection?
- [ ] Is capture scheduled against the shoot's critical, visually rich, and representative days, with AD cooperation and cast awareness?
- [ ] Are talent, music, and visible third-party material cleared for the marketing and publicity uses they will actually see, with releases collected during production?
- [ ] Are ownership, exclusivity, embargo, and sales-agent or distributor window rules defined before assets are released?
- [ ] Is capture matched to the most demanding downstream spec, with curated, accurately captioned and credited assets rather than an unsorted dump?
- [ ] Is there a single living asset library with naming, caption, credit, clearance, and embargo metadata, version control, and auditable access?
- [ ] Is the plan staged, with audience mapping, capture scheduling, clearance collection, ownership rules, library curation, and downstream supply completed in sequence?
- [ ] Are the asset map, shoot-day capture schedule, clearances and releases, ownership and embargo rules, library metadata, and delivery log recorded?
- [ ] Does the output distinguish immediate production-capture steps from staged downstream supply across the campaign?
- [ ] Are clearance, legal, and sales-agent referral boundaries explicit where the local answer changes the recommendation?
