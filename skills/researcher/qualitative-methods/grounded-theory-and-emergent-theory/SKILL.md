# Grounded Theory And Emergent Theory

Grounded theory is a systematic methodology for developing theory that emerges from data, rather than applying or testing a theory imported from outside. Its central discipline is the delay of theorizing until the data have been worked with closely, and the constant comparison of incident to incident, code to code, and category to category until a core category and its relationships take shape. Agents often call any inductive qualitative coding "grounded theory," but grounded theory is a demanding, iterative method with its own logic of sampling, coding, memoing, and integration. The judgment problem is to choose a coherent variant, to genuinely let categories emerge rather than smuggling in a preformed theory, and to know when integration is complete. A study that codes inductively once and stops is not grounded theory; a study that claims emergence while forcing data into a borrowed framework betrays the method's core commitment.

Use this skill when designing or conducting a grounded theory study. The goal is to prevent the agent from mislabeling ordinary thematic analysis as grounded theory, from forcing data into a predetermined theory, and from stopping integration before a coherent theoretical account has emerged. The agent has freedom in variant and focus, but the iterative logic of constant comparison, theoretical sampling, and memoing must be sustained.

## Core Rules

### Choose A Coherent Variant And Follow Its Logic

Grounded theory has distinct variants whose assumptions and procedures differ. Mixing them unaware produces incoherent method.

Choose by:

- Glaserian: emergent, rigorous delay of the literature, forced fit to a core category;
- Straussian: more prescriptive coding phases (open, axial, selective) and a coding paradigm;
- constructivist: co-construction of meaning, attention to situated action and researcher position;
- following one variant's procedures consistently rather than cherry-picking.

The variants disagree on the role of the literature, the coding paradigm, and the nature of the resulting theory. Picking procedures from each without reconciliation produces a method that satisfies none.

### Delay The Literature And Let Categories Emerge

Grounded theory's distinctive discipline is theoretical sensitivity without theoretical imposition. The researcher enters with awareness but not a framework to confirm.

Practice by:

- delaying deep engagement with the specific literature until categories begin to form;
- bracketing preconceived concepts rather than forcing them onto data;
- using the constant comparison of data to let categories arise;
- justifying later theoretical sampling by emergent categories, not by prior interest.

Emergence is not naivety. It is the disciplined refusal to decide the answer before the data have spoken, combined with enough theoretical sensitivity to recognize a category when it appears.

### Use Constant Comparison As The Engine Of Analysis

Constant comparison is the analytic heart of grounded theory. Without it, coding collapses into mere labeling.

Use by:

- comparing incident to incident within a code;
- comparing code to code to distinguish and refine categories;
- comparing category to category to find relationships and a core category;
- comparing emerging categories back to the literature at the integration stage.

Each new datum is compared against what has already been coded, so that categories are continuously refined, merged, split, or abandoned rather than fixed once.

### Code In Iterative Phases Appropriate To The Variant

Grounded theory coding is staged and developmental, moving from open description toward theoretical integration.

Code by:

- open or initial coding that fractures the data into many close codes;
- axial or focused coding that groups codes into categories and identifies properties and dimensions;
- selective or theoretical coding that integrates categories around a core category;
- the coding paradigm (conditions, actions, interactions, consequences) where the variant uses it.

The phases are iterative, not strictly linear. The researcher moves back to open coding as new data demand it, especially under theoretical sampling.

### Sample Theoretically And Pursue Saturation

Theoretical sampling is the defining sampling logic of grounded theory. Sampling follows the emerging theory, not a fixed plan.

Sample by:

- collecting data to develop and refine specific emerging categories;
- deciding the next case based on what the analysis needs to understand;
- pursuing variation, density, and boundaries of categories;
- stopping when categories are saturated: no new properties, dimensions, or relationships emerge.

Theoretical sampling is not possible if all data are collected before analysis begins. The method requires interleaving collection and analysis, which has practical and ethical implications that must be planned.

### Write Analytic Memos Throughout

Memos are where theory is built. A grounded theory study without sustained memoing produces codes but no theory.

Write by:

- memos on each category's properties, dimensions, and conditions;
- memos on relationships between categories and candidate core categories;
- memos that track the researcher's shifting theoretical thinking over time;
- memos that move from descriptive to increasingly abstract, theoretical writing.

Memos are the bridge between coded data and the final theory. They are also the audit trail that demonstrates how the theory emerged.

### Integrate Around A Core Category

Grounded theory aims for an integrated theoretical account, not a list of themes. Integration is the deliverable.

Integrate by:

- identifying a core category that centralizes the analysis;
- relating other categories to it as conditions, strategies, contexts, or consequences;
- writing the theory as a coherent explanatory account, not a thematic summary;
- checking that the theory accounts for variation and negative cases.

A study that reports well-developed categories but never integrates them has produced a taxonomy, not a grounded theory. The integration is what makes it theory.

### Report The Emergent Theory And Its Grounding

The reader must be able to see how the theory emerged from the data and assess its credibility.

Report by:

- the variant used and its key procedural choices;
- how theoretical sampling and saturation were operationalized;
- the core category and the structure of the theory;
- how the emergent theory relates to, extends, or challenges existing literature.

Grounded theory contributes by offering a transferable middle-range theory, not by claiming statistical generality. The report must make the theory's scope and grounding visible.

## Common Traps

### Mislabeling Thematic Analysis As Grounded Theory

Coding inductively once and reporting themes is thematic analysis, not grounded theory, and overstates the methodological contribution.

### Forcing Data Into A Borrowed Theory

Claiming emergence while actually fitting data to a pre-existing framework betrays the method's central commitment and produces confirmation.

### Mixing Variants Unawares

Cherry-picking procedures from Glaserian, Straussian, and constructivist variants without reconciliation produces an incoherent method.

### Collecting All Data Before Analysis

Theoretical sampling is impossible if all data are collected up front, collapsing the iterative logic that defines the method.

### Stopping At Categories Without Integration

Reporting developed categories but no core category and integrated theory yields a taxonomy, not a grounded theory.

### Premature Saturation Claims

Declaring saturation after a few interviews, without dense category development, underbuilds the theory and overstates its rigor.

### Ignoring The Literature's Later Role

Either ignoring the literature entirely at integration, or engaging it too early and imposing it, both distort the emergent theory.

## Self-Check

- Is a coherent grounded theory variant chosen and followed consistently rather than mixed unaware?
- Are preconceived frameworks bracketed so that categories genuinely emerge from constant comparison?
- Is constant comparison used as the engine, comparing incident, code, and category throughout?
- Are coding phases (open, focused/axial, theoretical) iterative and appropriate to the variant?
- Is sampling theoretical, following the emerging theory, with a credible saturation account?
- Are analytic memos written throughout to bridge coded data and the emergent theory?
- Is the analysis integrated around a core category rather than left as a list of themes?
- Does the emergent theory account for variation and negative cases, not just confirming data?
- Does the report make the theory's emergence, grounding, and scope visible to the reader?
- Is the contribution framed as a transferable middle-range theory rather than statistical generalization?
