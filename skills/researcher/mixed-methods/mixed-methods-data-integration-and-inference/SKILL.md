# Mixed Methods Data Integration And Inference

Integration is where mixed-methods research succeeds or fails, and it is the hardest part to do well. Once the strands are collected and analyzed separately, they must be brought together, and the act of bringing them together must produce something more than the sum of the parts. Agents often present the qualitative and quantitative findings side by side, note that they are broadly consistent, and move to conclusions. This is display, not integration. Genuine integration uses techniques like joint displays or data transformation to make the strands interact, examines both convergence and divergence as informative, and draws meta-inferences that neither strand could support alone. The judgment problem is to integrate in a way that produces combined insight, to handle divergence as data rather than explaining it away, and to draw meta-inferences that are warranted by the integrated evidence.

Use this skill when integrating mixed-methods data or drawing conclusions from integrated findings. The goal is to prevent the agent from side-by-side display mistaken for integration, from smoothing over divergence, and from meta-inferences that outrun the evidence.

## Core Rules

### Use Structured Techniques For Integration

Integration should use deliberate techniques that make the strands interact, not rely on narrative assertion.

Use by:

- joint displays that array qualitative and quantitative findings together;
- data transformation, such as quantitizing qualitative or qualitizing quantitative data;
- connecting results through sequential design links;
- explicit matrices that map convergence, divergence, and expansion.

Narrative claims that the strands are consistent, without a structured integration, do not fulfill the mixed-methods promise. Techniques make the integration visible and inspectable.

### Examine Convergence And Divergence As Equally Informative

Both agreement and disagreement between strands are analytically valuable.

Examine by:

- convergence strengthening confidence in a finding;
- divergence treated as data requiring explanation;
- exploring why strands diverge;
- using divergence to refine constructs or surface context.

Smoothing divergence to claim consistency hides the most informative findings. Divergence often reveals that a construct means different things at different levels or in different groups.

### Draw Meta-Inferences From The Integration

The point of mixed methods is meta-inferences that neither strand could support alone.

Draw by:

- inferences that emerge from the combination;
- how qualitative depth explains quantitative patterns;
- how quantitative breadth contextualizes qualitative insight;
- the added value of integration over either strand alone.

If the conclusions could have been drawn from one strand alone, the integration added nothing. Meta-inferences must be genuinely integrative.

### Handle Data Transformation Carefully

Transforming qualitative data to numbers (quantitizing) or quantitative to narrative (qualitizing) is powerful but assumption-laden.

Handle by:

- the rationale and method for transformation;
- the assumptions embedded in converting forms;
- the validity of the transformed data;
- the limits of what transformed data can claim.

Quantitizing qualitative themes treats them as counts, which may strip meaning; qualitizing numbers may over-interpret. Transformation must be done with awareness of what is gained and lost.

### Resolve Or Explain Divergent Findings

When strands diverge, the divergence must be addressed, not ignored.

Resolve by:

- investigating whether the divergence reflects method effects;
- considering whether constructs differ across levels of analysis;
- examining whether the qualitative reveals context the quantitative misses;
- reporting the resolution or the irreducible tension.

Divergence that is ignored undermines the credibility of both strands. Divergence that is investigated often produces the study's most valuable insight.

### Connect Strands Through The Design Logic

In sequential designs, the connection between strands is itself a form of integration.

Connect by:

- using quantitative results to shape qualitative sampling or questions;
- using qualitative findings to develop quantitative instruments;
- explicit links between the strands' phases;
- documentation of how one strand informed the other.

In sequential designs, the connection is where integration happens. If the second strand does not actually build on the first, the sequence is decorative.

### Ensure Meta-Inferences Are Warranted

Conclusions must be supported by the integrated evidence, not asserted.

Ensure by:

- tracing each meta-inference to the integrated data;
- acknowledging where one strand is stronger than the other;
- hedging where evidence is thin or strands conflict;
- not letting the stronger strand dominate the inference unduly.

A meta-inference that rests mainly on one strand, with the other used for decoration, is not genuinely integrative. The warrant must come from the combination.

### Report The Integration And Its Products Transparently

The reader must see how integration was done and what it produced.

Report by:

- the technique used and why;
- joint displays or matrices showing the integration;
- the convergences, divergences, and their handling;
- the meta-inferences and their evidential basis.

A report that asserts integration without showing it cannot be assessed. Transparency about the integration process and products is essential.

## Common Traps

### Side-By-Side Display Mistaken For Integration

Presenting strands in parallel and noting consistency is display, not integration.

### Smoothing Over Divergence

Explaining away disagreement between strands hides the most analytically valuable findings.

### Meta-Inferences From One Strand Alone

Conclusions that could be drawn from one strand, with the other decorative, do not fulfill mixed methods.

### Careless Data Transformation

Quantitizing or qualitizing without attention to assumptions and validity produces misleading integrated data.

### Ignored Divergence

Divergence left unaddressed undermines the credibility of both strands and the integration.

### Disconnected Sequential Strands

A second strand that does not build on the first makes the sequence decorative rather than integrative.

### Unwarranted Meta-Inferences

Conclusions that outrun the integrated evidence overstate what the study established.

## Self-Check

- Are structured integration techniques (joint displays, transformation, matrices) used rather than narrative assertion?
- Are convergence and divergence both examined, with divergence treated as data?
- Do meta-inferences emerge from the combination in a way neither strand could achieve alone?
- Is data transformation, if used, handled with attention to rationale, assumptions, and validity?
- Are divergent findings investigated and resolved or honestly reported as irreducible?
- In sequential designs, are strands genuinely connected, with one building on the other?
- Are meta-inferences warranted by the integrated evidence, not dominated by one strand?
- Is the integration process and its products reported transparently, with joint displays where appropriate?
- Are the limits and tensions of the integration acknowledged?
- Does the study produce combined insight that justifies the mixed-methods design?
