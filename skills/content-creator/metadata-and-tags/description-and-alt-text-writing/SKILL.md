# Description And Alt Text Writing

Descriptions and alt text are the written layer that lets both machines and humans understand content that is otherwise visual or audio. The judgment problem is that creators treat this layer as an afterthought, dumping leftover keywords or leaving fields blank, when in fact it does two critical jobs at once. It feeds discovery systems that decide who sees the content, and it serves real people, including those with disabilities, who depend on text to access images, video, and audio.

The harm is dual. For discovery, thin or stuffed descriptions leave content invisible to search and recommendation, or they attract the wrong audience through misleading text. For accessibility, missing alt text and captions exclude a meaningful share of the audience entirely, and increasingly violate platform and legal expectations. The trap is assuming descriptions are only for the algorithm, which produces keyword-stuffed text no human can read, or assuming they are only for humans, which ignores the discovery benefit. This skill helps the agent write descriptive text that serves both goals honestly, leads with value, and treats accessibility as core rather than optional.

## Core Rules

### Lead The Description With Value, Not Keywords

The first lines of a description are what humans see before truncation and what systems weight most heavily. Leading with keyword soup repels humans while barely helping discovery.

Lead well by:

- opening with a clear sentence on what the content offers;
- putting the most important information in the first two lines;
- writing naturally with relevant terms integrated, not stuffed;
- reserving deeper detail, links, and timestamps for lower in the description;
- treating the opening as a pitch, not a tag dump.

A value-first opening serves the click decision and the relevance signal simultaneously. A keyword-first opening fails both.

### Write Alt Text That Conveys Meaning, Not Appearance

Alt text exists to convey what the image means in context, not to narrate pixels. Good alt text lets someone who cannot see the image still understand the content and intent.

Write meaningful alt text by:

- describing the purpose the image serves in the content;
- including relevant context, not just literal objects;
- keeping it concise, usually under 125 characters where possible;
- avoiding redundant phrases like image of or picture of;
- handling decorative images with empty or minimal alt text appropriately.

Alt text that merely lists objects without meaning leaves the audience uninformed. Alt text that explains significance makes the content fully accessible.

### Treat Captions And Transcripts As Core, Not Optional

Captions and transcripts serve deaf and hard-of-hearing audiences, people watching without sound, and discovery systems that index spoken content. They are infrastructure, not extras.

Provide captions and transcripts by:

- ensuring accurate, synchronized captions on all video content;
- offering full transcripts for audio and long video where useful;
- editing auto-generated captions for accuracy and readability;
- preserving speaker identity and meaningful non-speech audio;
- not relying on auto-captions without review.

Auto-captions are a starting point, not a finished product. Their errors can distort meaning and exclude the audience they are meant to serve.

### Integrate Keywords Naturally, Never Stuffed

Descriptions and alt text can carry relevant terms, but only when they read naturally. Forced keyword repetition signals spam to both humans and platforms.

Integrate naturally by:

- using relevant terms where they fit the sentence;
- writing for a human reader first;
- avoiding unnatural repetition of the same phrase;
- not hiding keyword lists in descriptions or alt text;
- letting relevance emerge from honest description.

A readable description with natural terms outperforms a stuffed one that triggers spam filters and repels readers.

### Match Description Structure To The Platform

Each platform truncates descriptions differently, weights different fields, and has different norms. One description shape does not fit all.

Adapt structure by:

- front-loading value where truncation is aggressive;
- using timestamps and chapters where the platform supports them;
- placing links and calls to action where they will be seen;
- respecting character limits on each platform;
- aligning tone with platform expectations.

A YouTube description, an Instagram caption, and a blog meta description serve the same goal through different structures.

### Write For Accessibility As A Default, Not A Feature

Accessibility text is not an enhancement for some users. It is part of making content complete. Treat it as a default deliverable, not an optional add-on.

Default to accessibility by:

- writing alt text for every meaningful image;
- providing captions for every video with speech;
- adding descriptive metadata for audio where supported;
- checking color contrast and text legibility in visuals;
- not leaving accessibility fields empty when they exist.

Accessible content reaches more people and often ranks better. Inaccessible content excludes audiences and increasingly carries compliance risk.

### Keep Descriptions Honest And Aligned To Content

Descriptions that overpromise attract viewers who bounce, which hurts retention and future reach more than honest text would. Alignment protects both trust and performance.

Keep honest by:

- describing what the content actually delivers;
- avoiding clickbait claims the content cannot support;
- setting accurate expectations for length, depth, and tone;
- updating descriptions when content meaningfully changes;
- not using misleading framing to chase clicks.

Honest descriptions build the trust that compounds. Misleading descriptions produce spikes followed by suppression.

### Maintain Descriptive Text Over Time

Evergreen content keeps earning discovery long after publication, but descriptions and alt text can go stale as language, links, and context shift.

Maintain by:

- reviewing descriptions on evergreen content periodically;
- refreshing links and timestamps that have changed;
- updating alt text when images are replaced;
- refining terms as audience language evolves;
- checking that captions remain accurate after edits.

Stale descriptive text lets evergreen content fade. Maintenance keeps it findable and accessible.

## Common Traps

### Keyword-Stuffed Descriptions

Unnatural keyword repetition repels human readers and increasingly triggers spam penalties that suppress the content.

### Decorative-Style Alt Text That Lists Objects

Alt text that merely enumerates visible objects without meaning leaves audiences who depend on it uninformed about the image's purpose.

### Relying On Unedited Auto-Captions

Auto-generated captions contain errors that distort meaning and exclude the audiences captions are meant to serve.

### Front-Loading Keywords Over Value

Opening a description with keyword soup instead of a value statement loses the human reader and underuses the most visible, most weighted lines.

### Copy-Pasting One Description Across Platforms

Ignoring each platform's truncation, fields, and norms underperforms tailored description structure.

### Leaving Accessibility Fields Empty

Blank alt text and missing captions exclude real users and forgo the discovery benefits accessible content earns.

### Overpromising In Descriptions

Clickbait descriptions that the content cannot deliver attract bounces that damage retention and suppress future reach.

## Self-Check

Before approving descriptions and alt text, verify:

- Each description opens with a clear value statement before any keywords or links.
- Alt text conveys the meaning and purpose of images, not just a list of objects.
- Captions and transcripts are accurate, synchronized, and reviewed rather than left as raw auto-generated output.
- Keywords are integrated naturally with no stuffing, hidden text, or spam patterns.
- Description structure is adapted to each platform's truncation, fields, and norms.
- Accessibility text is treated as a default deliverable, with no meaningful image or video left without support.
- Descriptions honestly match what the content delivers, with no clickbait overpromising.
- Timestamps, links, and calls to action are placed where they will actually be seen.
- Evergreen content has a plan to refresh descriptions, links, and alt text over time.
- The descriptive text serves both human readers and discovery systems without sacrificing either.
