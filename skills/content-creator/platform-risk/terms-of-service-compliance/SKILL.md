# Terms Of Service Compliance

Platform terms of service are the rules under which a creator is allowed to exist on that platform, and they are written to favor the platform, not the creator. The judgment problem is that terms are treated as fine print to ignore, when they define what content, monetization, and growth tactics are permitted, and violating them, even unknowingly, can demonetize, suspend, or terminate a channel that took years to build. Worse, creators often copy tactics from other creators without realizing those tactics violate terms, and enforcement is uneven, so a tactic can seem safe right up until the platform decides to enforce.

The harm is asymmetric and unpredictable. A growth tactic that violates terms can produce a spike followed by a strike. Monetization methods the platform prohibits, such as certain paid placements or incentive schemes, can forfeit monetization entirely. Automated enforcement acts on patterns, so a creator can be penalized for behavior that was common and tolerated until it was not. Terms also change, so compliance is not a one-time check but ongoing monitoring. This skill helps the agent read terms as risk rules, evaluate tactics before use, distinguish permitted from prohibited behavior, and treat compliance as a continuous obligation rather than a hurdle cleared once.

## Core Rules

### Read Terms As Risk Rules That Favor The Platform

Platform terms are drafted to protect the platform and are enforced at its discretion. Read them to understand what the platform can restrict, not what is fair to the creator.

- recognize terms give the platform broad discretion to restrict or terminate;
- identify the clauses that most affect the creator's content and monetization;
- understand that enforcement is uneven and can change without notice;
- never assume a tactic is permitted merely because others use it.

### Check Tactics Against Terms Before Using Them

Growth, engagement, and monetization tactics should be checked against terms before deployment, because the penalty for violation often exceeds the benefit.

- evaluate any growth tactic, automation, or incentive against the terms;
- be cautious with sub-farming, engagement loops, and paid-view schemes;
- confirm monetization methods are permitted, including paid placements and affiliate structures;
- reject tactics whose upside is short-term and whose downside is account loss.

### Distinguish Permitted From Prohibited Monetization

Platforms specify which monetization methods are allowed, and methods that circumvent the platform's own monetization, or that fail disclosure, often violate terms.

- confirm whether direct sponsorships require platform disclosure or tools;
- check whether external links and affiliate structures are permitted;
- avoid schemes that pay for views, clicks, or installs in prohibited ways;
- understand rules around gating, paywalls, and external commerce.

### Treat Automated Enforcement As Real Enforcement

Much enforcement is automated and acts on patterns, not intent. A creator can be penalized for behavior that triggers automated systems even if a human would consider it acceptable.

- avoid patterns that resemble spam, bot, or manipulation behavior;
- be cautious with rapid, repetitive, or bulk actions that trigger automation;
- understand that mass posting, mass messaging, and aggressive outreach flag accounts;
- design tactics to look legitimate to automated systems as well as humans.

### Monitor For Term Changes Continuously

Platforms update their terms, and a tactic that complied yesterday may violate tomorrow. Compliance is ongoing, not a one-time check.

- subscribe to or periodically review platform policy updates;
- re-check tactics when terms change, not only when problems appear;
- track which tactics depend on current term interpretations;
- treat major platform announcements as prompts to review compliance.

### Keep Disclosure And Transparency Aligned With Terms

Terms often require specific disclosures for paid content, and failure to use platform-native disclosure tools can itself be a violation separate from legal disclosure duties.

- use platform-native disclosure tools and features where required;
- follow the platform's specific disclosure format, not just legal minimums;
- disclose relationships the platform requires even beyond legal duties;
- keep disclosure practices current as platform rules evolve.

### Document Compliance Decisions And Rationale

When a tactic is questioned later, documentation of the compliance reasoning helps. Keep records of what was checked and why a tactic was considered compliant.

- record which terms were checked before adopting a tactic;
- note the interpretation that supported the decision;
- keep records dated so they reflect the terms at the time;
- revisit documentation when terms change.

### Escalate Ambiguity Conservatively

When terms are ambiguous or silent on a tactic, the conservative choice is to avoid it or seek clarification, because the platform enforces its own interpretation in disputes.

- avoid tactics whose compliance depends on a generous reading of terms;
- seek platform clarification or partner support for valuable but ambiguous tactics;
- prefer clearly permitted alternatives over borderline ones;
- weigh the cost of losing the tactic against the cost of losing the account.

## Common Traps

### Copying Tactics From Other Creators

Tactics that violate terms can spread widely before enforcement, and copying them imports the risk without the awareness.

### Treating Terms As Fine Print

Ignoring terms until enforcement means the creator learns the rules only after losing monetization or the account.

### Monetization Methods That Circumvent The Platform

Schemes that route around platform monetization or disclosure often violate terms and forfeit eligibility.

### Triggering Automated Enforcement Unknowingly

Patterns that look like spam or manipulation to automated systems draw penalties even when intent is legitimate.

### Assuming Past Tolerance Means Permission

Platforms tolerate behavior until they enforce, so past non-enforcement is not evidence that a tactic is permitted.

### Ignoring Term Updates

Terms change, and a tactic that complied at adoption may violate after an update the creator did not read.

### Skipping Platform-Native Disclosure

Failing to use required platform disclosure tools is a separate violation from legal disclosure duties.

### Generous Readings Of Ambiguous Terms

Relying on a favorable interpretation of ambiguous terms exposes the creator to the platform's stricter reading in a dispute.

## Self-Check

- Have the platform terms been read as risk rules, identifying clauses that affect content and monetization?
- Was each growth, engagement, and monetization tactic checked against terms before use?
- Are monetization methods, including sponsorships and affiliate structures, confirmed as permitted?
- Could the tactic trigger automated enforcement patterns such as spam or manipulation flags?
- Is there a process to monitor term changes and re-check compliance when they occur?
- Are platform-native disclosure tools used where required, beyond legal minimums?
- Are compliance decisions and their rationale documented with dates?
- Has ambiguity been handled conservatively rather than through generous readings?
- Is the creator relying on any tactic whose permission depends on past tolerance rather than clear terms?
- Would the creator be able to explain and defend each tactic against the current terms?
