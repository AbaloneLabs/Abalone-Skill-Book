# Factory to Site Connection Detailing

The connection between the factory-built prefab components (the modules, the panels) and the site-built elements (the foundation, the core, the podium, the roof) is the most demanding detail of a prefab project, because it is where the high-precision factory work meets the lower-precision site work, and the tolerance, the interface, the seal, and the trade responsibility must all be resolved at that junction. The judgment problem is that the factory-to-site connection is often detailed as a generic joint, when in fact the site work is 5 to 10 times less precise than the factory work, and the connection must absorb the tolerance stack-up, manage the interface gap, and allocate the responsibility clearly, or the joint fails. A module set on a foundation that is 25mm out of level cannot connect; a panel-to-core gap of 50mm cannot be sealed; a trade gap between the factory and the site installer leaves the joint unowned. This skill covers the decisions that determine whether the factory-to-site connection works, including the tolerance management, the interface and the gap, the structural and the weather connection, and the trade and the responsibility allocation.

## Core Rules

### Manage the Tolerance Stack-Up Between the Factory Precision and the Site Imprecision

The tolerance stack-up (the accumulated deviation between the factory-built component and the site-built element) must be managed deliberately, because the factory work is precise (plus or minus 3 to 5mm) and the site work is imprecise (plus or minus 15 to 25mm or more), and the connection must absorb the difference or the components will not fit. The designer must define the tolerance for each element (the factory module, the site foundation, the site core), must design the connection to absorb the stack-up (the oversized base plate, the shim, the grout pad, the adjustable bracket), and must verify the absorbed tolerance covers the realistic site deviation. The trap is a connection detailed to the factory precision that cannot absorb the site deviation.

### Design the Interface Gap and the Adjustment for the Site Deviation

The interface gap (the space between the factory component and the site element) and the adjustment (the means to close or to absorb the gap) must be designed for the site deviation, because the gap is where the tolerance is absorbed and the adjustment is the mechanism, and an undersized gap or a missing adjustment stalls the installation. The designer must size the interface gap to accommodate the expected site deviation (often 25 to 50mm), must provide the adjustment (the shim, the threaded rod, the slotted hole, the grout), and must verify the gap and the adjustment allow the component to be set level, plumb, and aligned. The tradeoff is that the larger gap is easier to set but harder to seal; the resolution is the adjustable connection with the seal strategy.

### Detail the Structural Connection for the Load Transfer and the Ductility

The structural connection between the factory component and the site element must transfer the load (the gravity, the lateral, the uplift) and provide the ductility (the seismic, the wind), because the connection is the structural joint and the failure at the connection is the collapse risk. The designer must design the connection for the load transfer (the bolted or the welded joint, the bearing on the foundation, the shear key), must provide the ductility for the seismic and the wind (the ductile connection, the energy dissipation), and must verify the connection meets the structural code. The trap is a connection that transfers the gravity but not the lateral, or a rigid connection that fails in the seismic.

### Detail the Weather and the Air Seal for the Joint and the Gap

The weather and the air seal at the factory-to-site joint must be detailed for the water, the air, and the vapor, because the joint is the leak risk and the gap is the air-infiltration risk, and the unsealed joint leaks and the unsealed gap wastes the energy. The designer must detail the weather barrier (the membrane, the flashing, the sealant) continuously across the joint, must provide the air seal (the gasket, the backer rod and the sealant, the tape) at the gap, and must verify the seal accommodates the movement and the tolerance. The trap is a seal that does not accommodate the movement or the tolerance and fails.

### Allocate the Trade Responsibility and the Interface Clearly Between the Factory and the Site

The trade responsibility and the interface (who builds what, who connects what, who seals what) must be allocated clearly between the factory and the site, because the ambiguous interface is the gap that neither party owns, and the gap produces the defect and the dispute. The designer must define the scope boundary (the factory scope, the site scope), must define the connection responsibility (who sets the component, who makes the structural connection, who seals the joint), and must verify the interface is owned by one party with no gap. The trap is an interface left to "coordination" that no one owns.

## Common Traps

### A Connection Detailed to the Factory Precision That Cannot Absorb the Site Deviation

The connection is detailed to the factory precision (plus or minus 5mm), with no adjustment. The mechanism of the trap is that the site work (the foundation, the core) is 5 to 10 times less precise than the factory work, and a connection detailed to the factory precision cannot absorb the site deviation (the 25mm out-of-level, the 15mm out-of-alignment), so when the factory component is set on the site element, it does not fit, and the installation stalls, and the component must be shimmed, ground, or reworked on site, which negates the factory precision and the prefab efficiency. The false signal is the precision detail, which suggests quality but masks the tolerance failure. The harm is installation stalls, site rework, and lost efficiency. The defense is to manage the tolerance stack-up, size the interface gap, and provide the adjustment.

### An Undersized Interface Gap That Stalls the Installation

The interface gap between the factory component and the site element is sized at 10mm for a clean joint. The mechanism of the trap is that the site deviation (the 25mm out-of-level, the 15mm out-of-alignment) exceeds the 10mm gap, so the factory component cannot be set into the gap (it binds, it does not fit), and the installation stalls, and the gap must be enlarged on site (the grinding, the cutting) or the component must be modified, which delays the installation and damages the component. The false signal is the clean joint, which suggests a tight detail but masks the fit failure. The harm is installation stalls, component damage, and delay. The defense is to size the interface gap to accommodate the expected site deviation (25 to 50mm) and to provide the adjustment.

### A Structural Connection That Transfers the Gravity but Not the Lateral

The structural connection is designed for the gravity load (the module bearing on the foundation) but not for the lateral (the seismic, the wind). The mechanism of the trap is that the lateral load (the earthquake, the wind) must be transferred from the module to the foundation or the core, and a connection that only bears for the gravity has no lateral path, so in the seismic or the wind event the module slides or overturns, and the connection fails, and the building collapses or is severely damaged, which is a life-safety failure. The false signal is the gravity connection, which suggests a structural joint but masks the lateral failure. The harm is structural failure, collapse risk, and life-safety hazard. The defense is to detail the connection for the gravity, the lateral, and the uplift, and to verify the ductility.

### A Seal That Does Not Accommodate the Movement or the Tolerance and Fails

The weather seal at the joint is a rigid sealant bead that does not accommodate the movement. The mechanism of the trap is that the joint between the factory component and the site element moves (the thermal expansion, the seismic, the settlement) and the tolerance gap varies, and a rigid sealant cannot accommodate the movement or the variation, so the sealant cracks and peels, and the joint leaks the water and the air, and the leak damages the building and wastes the energy, and the repair is difficult because the joint is between the components. The false signal is the sealant presence, which suggests a sealed joint but masks the movement failure. The harm is leaks, energy loss, and difficult repair. The defense is to detail the weather and the air seal that accommodates the movement and the tolerance (the flexible membrane, the gasket, the backer rod and the sealant).

### An Interface Left to Coordination That No One Owns

The interface between the factory component and the site element is left to the field coordination, with no defined responsibility. The mechanism of the trap is that the interface (the connection, the seal, the gap) is the critical joint, and if no party owns it, then when the component is set, the interface is discovered to be incomplete (no connection, no seal, a gap), and neither the factory installer nor the site trade claims it, and the parties dispute the responsibility, and the joint is left incomplete or hastily patched, which produces the defect and the delay and the dispute. The false signal is the coordination, which suggests the parties will resolve it but masks the unowned gap. The harm is defects, delays, and disputes. The defense is to allocate the trade responsibility and the interface clearly, and to verify the interface is owned by one party.

## Self-Check

- Did I define the tolerance for each element (the factory module, the site foundation, the site core), and design the connection to absorb the stack-up (the oversized base plate, the shim, the grout, the adjustable bracket)?
- Did I size the interface gap to accommodate the expected site deviation (25 to 50mm), provide the adjustment (the shim, the threaded rod, the slotted hole), and verify the component can be set level, plumb, and aligned?
- Did I detail the structural connection for the gravity, the lateral, and the uplift, and provide the ductility for the seismic and the wind?
- Did I detail the weather and the air seal (the membrane, the flashing, the gasket, the sealant) continuously across the joint, and verify the seal accommodates the movement and the tolerance?
- Did I allocate the trade responsibility and the interface clearly (who sets, who connects, who seals), and verify the interface is owned by one party with no gap?
- Did I verify the connection absorbs the realistic site deviation, not the factory precision?
- Did I verify the seal is flexible and accommodates the movement, not a rigid bead?
