# Export License Application And Management

An export license is the authorization that permits a transaction otherwise prohibited, and managing it is an operational discipline, not a one-time filing. The process begins with jurisdiction determination, because applying under the wrong regime invalidates the effort: an item controlled by the ITAR cannot be authorized by an EAR license, and vice versa. It proceeds through classification, license-determination analysis, exception evaluation, application preparation, condition management, and recordkeeping. Each step generates evidence that must be preserved, and each license carries conditions that bind every subsequent shipment. A license mishandled, exceeded, or allowed to lapse becomes the basis for violations that are strict liability.

Use this skill before preparing a license application, determining EAR versus ITAR jurisdiction, evaluating license exceptions, applying license conditions, or advising on license recordkeeping and lifecycle management. The compliance officer must drive the process from jurisdiction through recordkeeping, treating each license as a controlled asset with binding terms.

## Core Rules

### Determine Jurisdiction Before Anything Else

The first decision is whether the item is subject to the EAR (Commerce/BIS) or the ITAR (State/DDTC). This determines the licensing authority, the application system, and the entire process.

- Review the USML for ITAR coverage; if covered, the item is ITAR and DDTC is the authority.
- If not USML, classify under the EAR CCL; BIS is the authority.
- For ambiguous items, a commodity jurisdiction request can resolve the question; treat conservatively pending resolution.
- The jurisdiction determination must be documented, because the licensing authority follows from it.

See the dedicated EAR and ITAR classification skills. Do not begin a license application until jurisdiction is settled.

### Classify And Determine The License Requirement

Within the correct regime, classify the item and determine whether a license is required.

- Under the EAR, assign the ECCN, read the reason for control and country chart intersection, and determine the license requirement.
- Under the ITAR, the USML category and the destination, end-use, and end-user drive the authorization type.
- Layer end-use, end-user, Entity List, and sanctions controls on top of the classification-based determination.
- Confirm whether the destination, end-use, or end-user triggers additional license requirements independent of classification.

### Evaluate License Exceptions Before Applying

Under the EAR, license exceptions may authorize the export without a license, subject to conditions.

- License Exception LVS permits limited value shipments of certain items, with value caps by ECCN and destination.
- License Exception GBS permits exports of certain items to Country Group B destinations.
- License Exception TSR permits technology and software exports to certain restricted destinations subject to conditions.
- License Exception STA permits certain strategic trade authorizations for validated parties.
- Each exception has item, destination, end-use, and end-user eligibility criteria, plus recordkeeping and reporting conditions.

Confirm the exception applies to the specific transaction, and document the basis and the conditions complied with. Misuse of an exception voids the authorization and creates liability. ITAR exemptions are narrower and must be confirmed element-by-element.

### Prepare The License Application With Complete Information

A license application must be complete and accurate. Incomplete or misleading applications can be rejected or, worse, can form the basis for misrepresentation liability.

- Under the EAR, file via the SNAP-R or current BIS electronic system.
- Under the ITAR, file the appropriate DSP form, TAA, or MLA via DDTC's system.
- Provide accurate item descriptions, classifications, quantities, values, and technical specifications.
- Identify all parties: applicant, ultimate consignee, end-user, intermediate consignees, freight forwarders.
- Provide the end-use and end-user details with supporting documentation.
- Address any red flags or sensitive aspects proactively.

Material misstatements in an application are independent violations. Accuracy is not optional.

### Manage License Conditions On Every Shipment

A license is not a blanket permission. It carries conditions that bind each authorized shipment.

- read the license conditions before first use and on each shipment;
- confirm each shipment falls within the license scope (items, quantities, parties, destinations, validity period);
- comply with reporting, recordkeeping, and post-shipment verification conditions;
- track license utilization against authorized quantities and values;
- refrain from shipping when a condition is not met, even if the license is otherwise valid.

Exceeding license scope, shipping after expiration, or ignoring conditions are violations.

### Track License Lifecycle And Validity

Licenses have validity periods and utilization limits. Manage them as controlled assets.

- track expiration dates and renew before lapse where ongoing authorization is needed;
- track cumulative quantities and values against authorized limits;
- maintain a license register with status, conditions, and utilization;
- retire licenses that are exhausted, expired, or no longer needed;
- reconcile actual shipments against license authorizations periodically.

### Maintain Recordkeeping For Each Authorization

Export records must be retained and producible. The retention period and scope differ by regime.

- EAR records: retain for a minimum of five years from the date of export or the date of the activity requiring a record.
- ITAR records: retain for a minimum of five years from the expiration of the license or authorization, or as specified.
- Records include the license, classification, application, party screening, end-use documentation, shipping records, and condition compliance evidence.
- Records must be producible to BIS, DDTC, or other authorities on request.

## Common Traps

### Applying Under The Wrong Regime

An EAR license cannot authorize an ITAR item. Jurisdiction must be settled before application.

### Skipping Exception Evaluation And Defaulting To A License

License exceptions may authorize the transaction with less burden. Failing to evaluate them wastes time and resources, though over-reliance without confirming conditions creates liability.

### Filing An Incomplete Or Inaccurate Application

Material misstatements in applications are independent violations. Accuracy and completeness are mandatory.

### Treating The License As A Blanket Permission

Licenses carry conditions. Shipping outside scope, after expiration, or in violation of conditions is a violation.

### Failing To Track Utilization Against Authorized Limits

Cumulative quantities and values must be tracked. Exceeding authorized limits invalidates the authorization.

### Ignoring End-Use, End-User, And Entity List Layers

A license based on classification does not resolve Entity List, end-use, or sanctions prohibitions. Layer all analyses.

### Letting Licenses Lapse Without Renewal

Validity periods are finite. Ongoing shipments require renewal before lapse.

### Inadequate Recordkeeping

Records must be retained for the required period and be producible. Missing records undermine the defense of any authorization.

## Self-Check

- Has jurisdiction been determined as EAR or ITAR and documented before any license application is prepared?
- Has the item been classified and the license requirement determined, with end-use, end-user, Entity List, and sanctions controls layered on the classification-based analysis?
- Have license exceptions been evaluated for the specific item, destination, end-use, and end-user, with eligibility and conditions documented?
- Is the license application complete and accurate, including item descriptions, classifications, quantities, values, parties, end-use, and supporting documentation?
- Are license conditions read and complied with on every shipment, including scope, parties, destinations, validity, reporting, and verification?
- Is license utilization tracked against authorized quantities, values, and validity periods in a license register?
- Are licenses renewed before lapse where ongoing authorization is needed, and retired when exhausted or no longer needed?
- Are actual shipments reconciled against license authorizations periodically?
- Are records retained for the required period (five years from export under EAR; five years from authorization expiration under ITAR) and producible on request?
- Are material misstatements in applications avoided by ensuring accuracy and completeness before filing?
