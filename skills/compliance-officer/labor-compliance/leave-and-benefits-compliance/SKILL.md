# Leave And Benefits Compliance

Leave and benefits compliance governs the employee's right to time away from work and access to health and welfare benefits. The defining feature is that leave entitlements come from overlapping federal, state, and local sources—FMLA, ADA, paid sick leave, paid family leave, military leave, pregnancy accommodation—each with different eligibility, duration, and job-protection rules. The central difficulty is that these entitlements interact and sometimes conflict, and an employer that applies one framework without considering the others will deny leave that is legally required or fail to restore the employee properly.

Use this skill before advising on leave policies, benefits eligibility, ACA compliance, COBRA administration, or leave coordination. The goal is to make the agent identify the applicable leave frameworks, the eligibility and duration rules, the job-protection and restoration duties, and the benefits obligations before concluding that a leave or benefits program is compliant.

## Core Rules

### Coordinate Overlapping Leave Frameworks

Leave entitlements overlap and must be coordinated, not applied in isolation.

Map and integrate:

- FMLA leave for serious health conditions, family care, and military exigencies;
- ADA leave as a reasonable accommodation, potentially beyond FMLA;
- paid sick leave under state and local mandates;
- paid family and medical leave under state insurance programs;
- pregnancy and childbirth leave and accommodation;
- military leave (USERRA) and reemployment rights;
- state-specific disability, bereavement, and domestic violence leave;
- workers' compensation leave coordination.

An employee's absence may be covered by multiple frameworks simultaneously. FMLA and ADA leave can run concurrently; paid sick leave may run concurrently with FMLA. The employer must apply the most protective combination.

### Apply FMLA Eligibility And Obligations Correctly

FMLA provides up to 12 weeks (or 26 weeks for military caregiver) of job-protected leave.

Control:

- employer coverage: 50 or more employees within 75 miles;
- employee eligibility: 12 months of service and 1,250 hours in the prior year;
- qualifying reasons: serious health condition, birth or adoption, family care, military exigency, military caregiver;
- leave accounting: 12 months, 1,250 hours, and the workweek calculation;
- intermittent and reduced-schedule leave for qualifying conditions;
- certification and recertification procedures;
- designation notice to the employee;
- job restoration to the same or equivalent position;
- maintenance of health benefits during leave.

FMLA has specific eligibility thresholds and procedures. Failure to designate leave properly, or to restore the employee, creates liability. Intermittent leave for chronic conditions is frequently mishandled.

### Provide ADA Leave As A Reasonable Accommodation

Leave beyond FMLA can be a required ADA accommodation.

Assess:

- additional leave beyond FMLA as a reasonable accommodation;
- interactive process to explore alternatives;
- undue hardship analysis for extended leave;
- modified or part-time schedules;
- reassignment to a vacant position as a last resort;
- documentation through medical inquiry;
- return-to-work and fitness-for-duty processes.

ADA leave is not capped at 12 weeks. An inflexible leave policy that terminates after a set period can violate the ADA if it denies reasonable accommodation without individualized assessment.

### Comply With Paid Sick And Family Leave Mandates

State and local laws require paid sick and family leave.

Implement:

- accrual rates and caps by jurisdiction;
- permissible uses including illness, family care, and safe leave (domestic violence);
- usage and anti-retaliation protections;
- payout or carryover rules;
- notice and posting requirements;
- recordkeeping of accrual and use;
- coordination with employer-provided PTO.

Paid sick leave is distinct from PTO and has specific uses and protections. Employees cannot be required to find coverage or disclose sensitive details beyond the need for the leave.

### Manage Vacation, PTO, And Final Pay

Vacation and PTO are governed by state law on accrual and payout.

Control:

- state rules on "use it or lose it" and caps;
- payout of accrued, unused PTO at termination;
- final paycheck timing and content by state;
- deductions from PTO banks;
- policy clarity on accrual, use, and forfeiture;
- coordination with leave laws.

In many states, accrued PTO is wages that must be paid at termination. "Use it or lose it" is restricted or prohibited in several jurisdictions.

### Administer Health Benefits Under ACA, ERISA, And COBRA

Health benefits carry multiple compliance obligations.

Cover:

- ACA employer shared responsibility (applicable large employers);
- ACA reporting (1095-C or 1095-B);
- ACA affordability and minimum value standards;
- ERISA plan documentation, summary plan descriptions, and Form 5500;
- fiduciary duties in plan administration;
- COBRA continuation coverage eligibility, notice, and election;
- HIPAA special enrollment rights;
- mental health parity and essential health benefits.

ACA, ERISA, and COBRA each impose distinct obligations. COBRA notices have strict timing, and missed notices are a common violation. ERISA fiduciary duty applies to plan asset management and vendor selection.

### Protect Military Leave And Reemployment Rights

USERRA protects service members' employment rights.

Implement:

- reemployment rights after military service;
- seniority and benefit accrual as if continuously employed;
- no discrimination based on service;
- health plan coverage during service and restoration;
- notice obligations of the employee;
- escalation of discharge or removal protections based on service length.

USERRA reemployment is a robust right. The employer must restore the service member to the position they would have held, with seniority.

### Prevent Leave-Related Retaliation

Retaliation for taking leave is independently unlawful.

Control:

- protected activity including leave requests and usage;
- adverse action awareness including subtle actions;
- performance evaluations that penalize leave;
- denial of opportunities or bonuses tied to attendance;
- documentation of legitimate reasons for actions affecting leave-takers;
- monitoring of treatment after leave.

Attendance-based policies that penalize leave-takers can constitute retaliation. Performance evaluations should account for leave periods.

## Common Traps

### Leave Frameworks Applied In Isolation

Applying FMLA without considering ADA, paid sick leave, or state family leave can deny required leave.

### Inflexible Leave Cap Violating ADA

A maximum leave policy that terminates without individualized assessment can deny reasonable accommodation.

### FMLA Designation Or Restoration Mishandled

Failure to designate leave or restore the employee to an equivalent position creates liability.

### Paid Sick Leave Treated As Ordinary PTO

Paid sick leave has specific uses, anti-retaliation protections, and rules distinct from discretionary PTO.

### COBRA Notice Timing Missed

COBRA election notices have strict deadlines, and late notices are a common violation.

### Attendance Policy Penalizing Leave-Takers

No-fault attendance policies that count protected leave as an occurrence can be retaliation.

## Self-Check

- Are leave frameworks coordinated including FMLA, ADA, paid sick leave, paid family leave, pregnancy accommodation, military leave, and workers' compensation?
- Is FMLA eligibility, designation, certification, intermittent leave, job restoration, and health benefit maintenance handled correctly?
- Is ADA leave provided as a reasonable accommodation through an interactive process, with undue hardship analysis and without inflexible caps?
- Are paid sick and family leave mandates followed including accrual, uses, anti-retaliation, payout, carryover, notice, and recordkeeping?
- Are vacation and PTO managed per state law including use-it-or-lose-it, termination payout, and final pay timing?
- Are health benefits administered under ACA shared responsibility, reporting, affordability, ERISA documentation, fiduciary duty, COBRA notice and election, and HIPAA special enrollment?
- Are military leave and USERRA reemployment rights protected including seniority, benefits, and anti-discrimination?
- Is leave-related retaliation prevented through protected activity identification, attendance policy review, and documentation?
- Is the interaction between frameworks assessed to apply the most protective combination?
- Are state and local leave and benefits rules applied in each jurisdiction?
