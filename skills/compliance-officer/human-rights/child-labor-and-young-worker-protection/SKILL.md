# Child Labor And Young Worker Protection

Child labor compliance requires distinguishing lawful youth employment from exploitative child labor, and the distinction turns on age, hazard, hours, and the worst forms standard. The defining difficulty is that the harm is irreversible, the legal standards differ sharply between developed and developing country regimes, and the presence of child labor in a supply chain can attach severe reputational and legal consequences even when the company lacked direct knowledge. Verification of age is harder than it appears in environments with weak identity documentation.

Use this skill before assessing child labor risk, setting age and work rules, verifying worker age, or responding to a child labor finding. The goal is to make the agent identify the applicable age and hazard standards, design age verification that works in the operating environment, distinguish lawful young worker employment from prohibited child labor, and meet remediation obligations when child labor is found. Child labor standards are jurisdiction-specific and findings should involve qualified counsel and child protection specialists.

## Core Rules

### Apply The Correct Minimum Age Standard

Minimum age depends on the jurisdiction, the type of work, and whether the work is hazardous.

Apply:

- the general minimum age, typically aligned with the age of completion of compulsory schooling and not below the international floor;
- a higher minimum age for hazardous work that could jeopardize health, safety, or morals;
- the distinction between light work permitted at a younger age and regular employment;
- the stricter of national law, international standards, and buyer or certification requirements;
- special rules for entertainment, family businesses, and apprenticeships where permitted.

Where national law sets a lower standard than international conventions, the higher standard generally applies for companies committed to international norms. The minimum age for hazardous work is higher and applies to a defined list of hazardous activities.

### Identify And Prohibit The Worst Forms Of Child Labor

The worst forms of child labor are prohibited absolutely and without exception.

Recognize:

- all forms of slavery and practices similar to slavery including debt bondage and serfdom;
- forced or compulsory recruitment of children for armed conflict;
- use of children for prostitution or pornographic performances;
- use of children for illicit activities including drug production and trafficking;
- work which by its nature or circumstances is likely to harm the health, safety, or morals of children.

The worst forms trigger immediate removal and remediation regardless of age thresholds that might otherwise apply to light work. There is no permissible form of these activities.

### Define And Prohibit Hazardous Work For Young Workers

Hazardous work is prohibited below a higher age threshold and is defined by the type of activity.

Cover hazardous work including:

- work underground or underwater;
- work with dangerous machinery, sharp tools, or moving parts;
- work with hazardous substances, chemicals, or biological agents;
- work in unhealthy environments exposing children to noise, vibration, temperature extremes, or radiation;
- work under particularly difficult conditions including long hours, night work, or confinement;
- work involving heavy load bearing unsuited to the worker's physical capacity.

The hazardous work list must reflect both the operating environment and applicable standards. Even where young workers may lawfully be employed, they may not be assigned to hazardous tasks.

### Verify Age Reliably In The Operating Environment

Age verification is the control that prevents child labor, and it must be robust to the local documentation reality.

Implement:

- collection of official identity documents at hiring;
- verification of document authenticity where feasible;
- cross-checking of age across multiple sources including education records;
- awareness of document fraud and weak birth registration in some regions;
- periodic re-verification where turnover and subcontracting are high;
- training of hiring personnel on age verification and red flags.

In environments with weak birth registration or common document fraud, document collection alone is insufficient. Triangulation across sources and vigilance for inconsistency are necessary.

### Control Hours, Conditions, And Night Work For Lawful Young Workers

Where young workers are lawfully employed, their hours and conditions are restricted.

Control:

- limits on working hours that do not interfere with education;
- prohibition or restriction of night work;
- rest breaks and rest periods adequate to age;
- prohibition of work during school hours where applicable;
- safe conditions and exclusion from hazardous tasks;
- protection from economic exploitation and work unsuited to age.

Lawful young worker employment is conditioned on these protections. Employment that interferes with education or exposes young workers to harm is prohibited even where the worker meets the minimum age.

### Remediate When Child Labor Is Found

When child labor is identified, the response must protect the child and address the root cause.

Remediate by:

- immediate removal of the child from harmful work, prioritizing the child's safety and welfare;
- engagement with the family and community to address the economic drivers;
- support for the child's education and transition, avoiding harm from sudden income loss;
- correction of the root cause in the supplier's or operation's practices;
- documentation of the finding and remediation;
- protection of the child and informants from retaliation.

Summary dismissal of a child worker without support can worsen harm by pushing the child into worse conditions. Remediation must be child-centered and address the economic drivers that caused the work.

### Map Risk Across Operations And Supply Chain

Child labor risk concentrates in specific sectors and supply chain tiers.

Map:

- agriculture including cocoa, cotton, coffee, sugar, and tobacco;
- mining including artisanal and small-scale mining of minerals;
- manufacturing including garments, bricks, and carpets;
- service sectors including domestic work and street vending;
- lower tiers and informal labor where oversight is weakest.

Risk is highest in agriculture and artisanal mining at the raw material tier, where traceability and verification are most difficult. Sector-specific risk knowledge is essential.

## Common Traps

### Applying The General Minimum Age To Hazardous Work

Hazardous work requires a higher age threshold and is prohibited below it.

### Document Collection Treated As Age Verification

In environments with weak registration and fraud, documents alone are insufficient.

### Lawful Young Worker Employment Without Hour And Condition Limits

Meeting the minimum age does not permit hazardous tasks, night work, or education interference.

### Summary Dismissal Without Remediation

Removing a child without support can push the child into worse conditions.

### Ignoring Raw Material And Lower-Tier Risk

The highest child labor risk sits in agriculture and artisanal mining at tiers with weak oversight.

## Self-Check

- Is the correct minimum age standard applied, taking the stricter of national law, international standards, and buyer requirements, with a higher threshold for hazardous work?
- Are the worst forms of child labor identified and prohibited absolutely including slavery, forced recruitment, commercial sexual exploitation, illicit activities, and hazardous work?
- Is hazardous work for young workers defined and prohibited, covering underground and underwater work, dangerous machinery, hazardous substances, unhealthy environments, difficult conditions, and heavy load bearing?
- Is age verified reliably through document collection, authenticity checks, source triangulation, periodic re-verification, and hiring personnel training appropriate to the operating environment?
- Are hours, conditions, night work, and education-interference controlled for lawfully employed young workers?
- When child labor is found, is remediation child-centered with immediate safe removal, family and community engagement, education support, root cause correction, documentation, and retaliation protection?
- Is risk mapped across sectors and supply chain tiers with attention to agriculture, artisanal mining, and lower tiers where oversight is weakest?
- Where a finding arises or age verification is uncertain, has the matter involved qualified counsel and child protection specialists, recognizing that child labor standards are jurisdiction-specific and this guidance is not legal advice?
