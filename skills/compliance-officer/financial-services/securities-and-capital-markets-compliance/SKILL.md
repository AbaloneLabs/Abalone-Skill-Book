# Securities And Capital Markets Compliance

Securities compliance governs how capital is raised, how public companies disclose information, how investment professionals behave, and how markets stay fair. The rules are designed around information asymmetry: investors rely on disclosure and fair dealing, and the system breaks when material information is hidden, misused, or selectively shared. Securities violations carry a distinctive severity profile because they can trigger personal liability for officers and directors, bar individuals from serving as officers or directors, rescind transactions, and impose civil and criminal penalties. The compliance challenge is that the same information can be routine in one context and material nonpublic information in another.

Use this skill before advising on a securities offering, reviewing public company disclosure controls, designing insider trading controls, evaluating broker-dealer or adviser conduct, planning a capital raise, or assessing token or digital asset obligations. The goal is to make the agent identify the actor's role, the applicable disclosure and conduct duties, the materiality of information, and the control environment before concluding compliance.

## Core Rules

### Identify The Actor And The Applicable Regime

Securities obligations depend on who the actor is and what they are doing. A company, its officers, a broker-dealer, an investment adviser, a fund, and an investor face different rules.

Map:

- issuer of securities and its reporting status (public, emerging growth, foreign private issuer, smaller reporting);
- officers, directors, and insiders with disclosure or trading duties;
- broker-dealer and its registration, supervision, and capital obligations;
- investment adviser and its fiduciary duty, custody, and compliance rule obligations;
- investment company and its restrictions on affiliated transactions and leverage;
- transfer agent obligations;
- exchanges and alternative trading systems;
- rating agencies;
- whether the actor is a control, affiliate, or underwriter.

Each role triggers specific registration, disclosure, conduct, books-and-records, and examination obligations.

### Control Material Nonpublic Information And Insider Trading

Insider trading liability arises when a person trades, tips, or causes another to trade on the basis of material nonpublic information in breach of a duty. The control environment must prevent misuse.

Implement:

- insider trading policy with defined blackout periods;
- preclearance for trades by insiders and covered employees;
- watch lists of working groups with MNPI access;
- information barriers between business units;
- training on tipping, trading, and causing others to trade;
- personal trading reporting and monitoring;
- controls on selective disclosure under Regulation FD;
- pre-announcement communication review for earnings and deals;
- tracking of gifts, political contributions, and outside business activities for investment personnel.

Materiality is judged by whether a reasonable investor would consider the information important. Do not assume technical or pipeline information is immaterial because it is not financial.

### Maintain Public Company Disclosure Controls

Public companies must maintain disclosure controls and procedures that gather, evaluate, and report material information accurately and on time. This is broader than financial controls.

Cover:

- quarterly and annual reporting accuracy and timeliness;
- earnings releases and guidance consistency;
- material agreement filings;
- risk factor currency and specificity;
- management discussion and analysis quality;
- non-GAAP measure compliance;
- cybersecurity incident disclosure obligations;
- events triggering current reporting (material contracts, executive changes, restatements);
- internal control over financial reporting and any material weakness;
- certifications by principal executive and financial officers.

Disclosure controls should identify who surfaces information, who decides materiality, who drafts, who reviews, and who certifies. A breakdown anywhere in that chain creates reporting risk.

### Structure Capital Raises To Fit An Exemption Or Registration

Offering securities requires either registration or a valid exemption. The choice determines disclosure, resale, and ongoing obligations.

For each raise, identify:

- whether the offering is registered or exempt;
- the specific exemption or registration statement;
- accredited investor verification where required;
- offering limits and integration with other raises;
- general solicitation rules if using certain exemptions;
- resale restrictions and holding periods;
- bad actor disqualification;
- Form D or other filing obligations;
- state blue-sky notice filings;
- integration analysis across concurrent offerings;
- disclosure adequacy for private placements.

A misstructured raise can rescind the offering, bar future reliance on exemptions, and create personal liability.

### Enforce Broker-Dealer And Adviser Conduct Rules

Intermediaries face detailed conduct, suitability, best-interest, supervision, and books-and-records rules.

For broker-dealers, assess:

- registration and licensing of the firm and individuals;
- best interest and suitability obligations for recommendations;
- supervision and reasonable basis for products;
- capital and customer protection rules;
- trade reporting and best execution;
- conflicts disclosure and mitigation;
- anti-money laundering program;
- customer communication review;
- books and records retention.

For investment advisers, assess:

- fiduciary duty including duty of care and loyalty;
- compliance rule written program;
- custody and safeguarding of client assets;
- conflicts disclosure;
- allocation and trade practices;
- advertising and marketing rule compliance;
- recordkeeping;
- valuation of hard-to-price assets.

The fiduciary standard for advisers is ongoing and fact-specific. A single disclosure does not cure an undisclosed conflict.

### Assess Digital Asset And Token Obligations

Tokens, stablecoins, and digital assets may be securities, commodities, or something else depending on the economic reality of the instrument.

Analyze:

- whether the asset is an investment contract under the Howey test or local equivalent;
- registration or exemption availability;
- disclosure and anti-fraud obligations even for unregistered assets;
- custody of digital assets and customer protection;
- market manipulation surveillance;
- transfer restrictions and accredited investor limits;
- tax and reporting consequences;
- cross-border treatment.

Do not assume a token is outside securities law because it is labeled a utility token. Analyze the economic substance.

### Monitor Market Conduct And Manipulation

Market integrity rules prohibit manipulation, spoofing, wash trades, and dissemination of false information.

Control:

- surveillance of trading for manipulative patterns;
- watch lists around deals and announcements;
- communication surveillance in trading and banking;
- controls on research and investment banking interactions;
- watch for front-running, painting the tape, and marking the close;
- analyst conflicts and research separation;
- whistle-blower protections and reporting.

## Common Traps

### Assuming Information Is Immaterial Because It Is Technical

Pipeline, clinical, engineering, or legal information can be highly material. Materiality turns on investor importance, not on whether the data looks financial.

### Disclosure Controls Limited To Accounting

Material nonfinancial events, cyber incidents, material agreements, and executive departures must also surface. Disclosure controls must reach beyond finance.

### Selective Disclosure To Analysts Or Large Investors

Sharing material information selectively without simultaneous broad disclosure can violate fair disclosure rules. Reg FD requires public disclosure or no disclosure.

### Treating Adviser Fiduciary Duty As A One-Time Disclosure

Fiduciary duty is ongoing and contextual. Conflicts must be identified, disclosed in a manner clients understand, and often mitigated, not merely listed.

### Capital Raise Without Integration Analysis

Multiple concurrent or close-in-time offerings can integrate and blow an exemption's offering limit. Analyze the six-month integration window.

### Token Treated As Outside Securities Law By Label

Labels do not control. Economic substance and the investment contract analysis determine whether securities law applies.

### Broker-Dealer Supervision Treated As A Paper Exercise

Supervision requires reasonable systems, follow-up on exceptions, and documented escalation. A written supervisory procedure without operation fails examination.

## Self-Check

- Is the actor's role (issuer, insider, broker-dealer, adviser, fund, transfer agent) identified with the matching registration, conduct, and reporting duties?
- Are insider trading controls, blackout periods, preclearance, watch lists, information barriers, Reg FD, and personal trading monitoring operational and documented?
- Do disclosure controls surface both financial and nonfinancial material information, with defined materiality, drafting, review, and certification chains?
- Is each capital raise mapped to a registration statement or specific exemption, with accredited verification, offering limits, integration, bad actor, and filing obligations checked?
- Are broker-dealer best-interest, supervision, capital, trade reporting, conflicts, AML, and records obligations addressed?
- Are investment adviser fiduciary duty, compliance rule program, custody, conflicts, allocation, advertising, and valuation obligations addressed?
- For digital assets, is the securities-law analysis based on economic substance and the investment contract test, not labels?
- Are market manipulation surveillance, communication review, research-banking separation, and whistle-blower protections in place?
- Is materiality assessed from the reasonable investor perspective, including technical, pipeline, legal, and cyber information?
- Are restatement, material weakness, cyber incident, and current-report triggers covered in disclosure readiness?
