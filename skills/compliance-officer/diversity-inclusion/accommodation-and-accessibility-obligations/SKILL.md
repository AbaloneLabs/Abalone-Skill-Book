# Accommodation And Accessibility Obligations

Accommodation compliance requires a fact-specific, interactive judgment that balances an individual's needs against an employer's operational constraints. The defining difficulty is that the duty is triggered by request or obvious need, the analysis is individualized rather than categorical, and the undue hardship defense turns on specific operational evidence that employers rarely document well. Treating accommodation as a checklist rather than an interactive, documented dialogue is the most common source of liability.

Use this skill before responding to an accommodation request, designing an accommodation process, evaluating undue hardship, or assessing accessibility obligations. The goal is to make the agent identify the triggering regime, run the interactive process, document the analysis, and recognize where the conclusion requires legal review. Accommodation rules are jurisdiction-dependent and contested cases should involve qualified counsel.

## Core Rules

### Identify The Triggering Accommodation Regime

Different needs trigger different regimes with different standards.

Map the applicable regime:

- disability accommodation under federal and state disability rights law;
- religious accommodation with its specific undue hardship standard;
- pregnancy, childbirth, and related condition accommodation under pregnancy fairness laws;
- lactation and nursing break obligations;
- medical and family leave as a form of accommodation;
- veteran and service member reemployment and accommodation rights.

Each regime has its own coverage, trigger, and hardship standard. A pregnancy accommodation request may invoke a lower hardship threshold than a disability request. The regime must be identified before the analysis begins.

### Run A Genuine Interactive Process

The interactive process is itself a legal duty, independent of the outcome.

Implement:

- prompt response to requests or obvious needs;
- individualized assessment of the employee's limitations and the essential functions of the job;
- exploration of possible accommodations without predetermining the answer;
- dialogue with the employee about effectiveness and preference;
- documentation of alternatives considered and reasons for acceptance or rejection;
- periodic review of effectiveness.

Failure to engage in the interactive process is a standalone violation even if no accommodation was ultimately possible. The process must be genuine, not a formality leading to a predetermined denial.

### Analyze Essential Functions Before Denying

Whether an employee can perform the essential functions determines whether an accommodation is reasonable.

Determine essential functions through:

- the employer's judgment and written job descriptions;
- the amount of time spent on the function;
- the consequences of not requiring the function;
- the terms of collective bargaining agreements;
- the work experience of past and current incumbents;
- whether the function is highly specialized.

Marginal functions can be reassigned. Essential functions cannot be eliminated as an accommodation, but the determination must be evidence-based and not a conclusory label. Over-broad essential function lists defeat accommodations that should be granted.

### Apply The Correct Undue Hardship Standard

Undue hardship is a defense that requires specific evidence, and the standard varies by regime.

Assess:

- for disability accommodation, significant difficulty or expense relative to the employer's resources and operation;
- for religious accommodation, the standard has shifted toward a substantial increased costs test in some jurisdictions;
- the cost of the accommodation relative to employer size and resources;
- the impact on operations, safety, and other employees;
- the availability of tax credits or external funding;
- whether the accommodation would be an undue hardship or merely an inconvenience.

Undue hardship is not a vague sense of disruption. It requires quantified evidence of cost or operational impact. The threshold is higher for large employers and lower for small ones, and the religious standard has become more demanding for employers to invoke.

### Consider Reassignment As A Last Resort

When no accommodation permits performance of the current job, reassignment to a vacant position may be required.

Address:

- reassignment to a vacant position the employee is qualified to perform;
- the vacancy requirement, meaning no displacement of other employees in most jurisdictions;
- search for vacancies at the same or equivalent level;
- seniority system interactions, which can limit but not always defeat reassignment;
- the distinction between reassignment as accommodation and competitive selection.

Reassignment is a last-resort accommodation and is frequently misunderstood. It is not guaranteed to a position the employee wants, but the duty to look for a vacancy is real.

### Meet Workplace And Digital Accessibility Duties

Accommodation extends to physical and digital accessibility.

Cover:

- physical accessibility of facilities under accessibility design standards;
- accessible application and hiring processes;
- accessible technology, software, and internal systems;
- accessible public-facing websites and digital services under applicable accessibility laws;
- effective communication including auxiliary aids and alternative formats;
- accessible meetings, training, and events.

Digital accessibility is an emerging enforcement area. Inaccessible hiring technology can screen out qualified candidates with disabilities and create liability before employment even begins.

### Document And Avoid Retaliation

The accommodation process must be documented and free from retaliation.

Maintain:

- records of requests, the interactive process, accommodations provided, and reasons for denial;
- confidentiality of medical and accommodation information;
- protection against retaliation for requesting accommodation or asserting rights;
- consistent application across similar requests.

Retaliation against an employee for seeking accommodation is a separate violation. Inconsistent treatment across similar requests evidences pretext.

## Common Traps

### Predetermined Denial Without A Genuine Process

Denying an accommodation without exploring alternatives is a standalone violation of the interactive process duty.

### Over-Broad Essential Function Labels

Labeling marginal tasks as essential to defeat an accommodation fails when the function is not truly essential.

### Conclusory Undue Hardship Without Evidence

Claiming disruption without quantified cost or operational evidence is not a valid undue hardship defense.

### Ignoring The Lower Pregnancy Accommodation Threshold

Pregnancy fairness laws often impose a lower hardship threshold, making denials harder to defend.

### Inaccessible Hiring Technology

Screening out candidates with disabilities through inaccessible application systems creates liability before employment begins.

## Self-Check

- Has the triggering accommodation regime been identified, recognizing that disability, religious, pregnancy, and lactation obligations have different standards?
- Is a genuine interactive process run with individualized assessment, exploration of alternatives, dialogue, and documentation, rather than a predetermined denial?
- Are essential functions determined through evidence rather than conclusory labels, with marginal functions distinguished from truly essential ones?
- Is undue hardship supported by quantified evidence of cost or operational impact under the correct regime-specific standard?
- Is reassignment to a vacant position considered as a last resort when current-job accommodation is not possible?
- Are physical and digital accessibility obligations met including facilities, hiring technology, internal systems, websites, and effective communication?
- Is the process documented, medical information kept confidential, and retaliation avoided, with consistent treatment across similar requests?
- Where the request is contested or the hardship defense is uncertain, has the matter been escalated to qualified employment counsel, recognizing that accommodation standards are jurisdiction-dependent and this guidance is not legal advice?
