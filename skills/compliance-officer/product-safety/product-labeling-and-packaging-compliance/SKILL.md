# Product Labeling And Packaging Compliance

Product labeling compliance governs the information that must appear on product labels and packaging. The defining feature is that labeling requirements vary by product category (food, drug, cosmetic, consumer product, textile) and jurisdiction, and the same label can be subject to multiple regimes simultaneously. The central difficulty is that label claims—health, nutritional, environmental, origin—each have specific substantiation and formatting requirements, and a claim that is accurate can still be non-compliant if it is not presented in the required manner.

Use this skill before advising on label content, claims, formatting, origin labeling, or packaging restrictions. The goal is to make the agent identify the applicable labeling regimes, the mandatory and optional label elements, the claim substantiation requirements, and the packaging rules before concluding that a product label is compliant.

## Core Rules

### Identify All Applicable Labeling Regimes

Labeling requirements depend on product category and jurisdiction.

Map:

- food labeling (nutrition facts, ingredient lists, allergens, claims);
- drug and OTC labeling (active ingredients, warnings, directions);
- cosmetic labeling (ingredients, warnings, net quantity);
- consumer product labeling (safety warnings, instructions, tracking labels);
- textile, wool, and fur labeling (fiber content, origin);
- pesticide labeling (active ingredients, directions, warnings);
- jurisdiction-specific requirements (federal, state, international);
- the interaction between regimes for multi-category products.

A product can be subject to multiple labeling regimes (e.g., a cosmetic that makes drug claims becomes a drug). Each regime has mandatory elements, claims rules, and formatting requirements. The strictest applicable requirement governs.

### Comply With Mandatory Label Elements

Mandatory label elements vary by product category.

Cover:

- statement of identity (product name);
- net quantity of contents;
- ingredient list in descending order of predominance;
- allergen declarations for food;
- manufacturer, packer, or distributor name and address;
- country of origin;
- nutrition facts for food;
- warning and caution statements;
- directions for safe use;
- tracking labels for children's products;
- placement, type size, and formatting requirements.

Mandatory elements must be present, correctly formatted, and properly placed. Type size, prominence, and contrast matter. Allergen declarations have specific formatting. Nutrition facts have a prescribed format.

### Substantiate And Format Label Claims

Label claims require substantiation and specific formatting.

Control:

- health claims (requiring significant scientific agreement or authorization);
- nutrient content claims (defined terms like "low fat," "high in");
- structure/function claims for dietary supplements and foods;
- "natural," "healthy," and other implied claims;
- organic claims (requiring certification);
- non-GMO and other absence claims;
- environmental and green claims;
- the substantiation required for each claim type;
- the formatting and disclaimer requirements.

Claim types have different substantiation thresholds. Health claims require the highest level of evidence. Nutrient content claims must use defined terms. Structure/function claims require substantiation and a disclaimer. Organic claims require certification.

### Manage Country-Of-Origin Labeling

Origin labeling requirements vary by product and jurisdiction.

Cover:

- "Made in USA" and country-of-origin claims;
- the "all or virtually all" standard for unqualified US origin claims;
- qualified origin claims ("assembled in USA from imported parts");
- textile, wool, and fur origin requirements;
- food country-of-origin labeling;
- customs marking requirements for imported products;
- the distinction between origin for labeling and origin for customs purposes.

Unqualified "Made in USA" claims require that the product is all or virtually all made in the US. Qualified claims must be clear about the extent of domestic processing. Origin for labeling may differ from origin for customs (tariff) purposes.

### Comply With Food And Beverage Labeling Requirements

Food labeling has specific, detailed requirements.

Implement:

- nutrition facts panel format and content;
- serving size and servings per container;
- ingredient list with allergens;
- nutrient content and health claims;
- front-of-pack labeling where required;
- bioengineered food disclosure;
- restaurant and vending machine labeling;
- infant formula and special dietary requirements.

Food labeling is highly regulated with prescribed formats. Allergen declarations must highlight major allergens. Bioengineered food disclosure is mandatory. Front-of-pack labeling requirements are expanding.

### Address Packaging Material Restrictions And Labeling

Packaging materials face restrictions and labeling requirements.

Cover:

- restrictions on specific materials (lead, cadmium, phthalates, PFAS);
- food contact substance compliance for packaging;
- recycling labeling and resin identification codes;
- toxic packaging restrictions (model toxics in packaging legislation);
- packaging material restrictions by jurisdiction;
- the distinction between product and packaging claims;
- minimum recycled content requirements.

Packaging that contacts food must comply with food contact substance rules. Toxic packaging laws restrict heavy metals and specific substances. Recycling labels must be accurate and not misleading. Packaging restrictions vary by jurisdiction.

### Manage International Labeling Compliance

International labeling requirements differ from domestic.

Cover:

- multilingual labeling requirements;
- metric measurement requirements;
- international nutrition labeling formats;
- ingredient restrictions and naming conventions;
- warning label requirements by jurisdiction;
- the effect of trade agreements on labeling;
- coordination of labels across markets.

Products sold internationally must meet each market's labeling requirements, often in the local language. Measurement units, ingredient names, and warning requirements differ. Trade agreements may affect labeling requirements.

### Ensure Label Accuracy And Consistency

Labels must be accurate and consistent with the product.

Control:

- verification that label matches product formulation;
- update of labels when formulations change;
- consistency between label claims and substantiation files;
- review of labels by regulatory and legal functions;
- tracking of label versions and changes;
- management of multiple label versions for different markets;
- the risk of outdated labels on retail shelves.

Labels must match the actual product. Formulation changes require label updates. Outdated labels in the market are violations. Label review should include regulatory, legal, and marketing functions.

## Common Traps

### Accurate Claim In Non-Compliant Format

A claim that is substantively accurate but not in the required format is non-compliant.

### Product Reclassified By Claims (Cosmetic Becoming Drug)

Making drug claims on a cosmetic subjects it to drug labeling requirements.

### Unqualified "Made In USA" Without "All Or Virtually All" Support

Overstating domestic content is deceptive and a violation.

### Organic Claim Without Certification

Organic claims require certification from an accredited body.

### Label Not Updated After Formulation Change

Outdated labels that do not match the current formulation are violations.

### Packaging Material Violating Toxic Packaging Or Food Contact Rules

Packaging materials must comply with restrictions independent of the product.

## Self-Check

- Are all applicable labeling regimes identified by product category and jurisdiction including food, drug, cosmetic, consumer product, textile, pesticide, and international?
- Are mandatory label elements present including identity, net quantity, ingredients, allergens, manufacturer, origin, nutrition facts, warnings, directions, tracking labels, and formatting?
- Are label claims substantiated and formatted correctly for health, nutrient content, structure/function, natural, organic, non-GMO, and environmental claims?
- Is country-of-origin labeling compliant with the "all or virtually all" standard, qualified claims, textile and food requirements, and customs marking?
- Are food and beverage labeling requirements met including nutrition facts, serving size, ingredients, allergens, claims, front-of-pack, bioengineered disclosure, and special dietary?
- Are packaging material restrictions and labeling complied with including substance restrictions, food contact, recycling labels, toxic packaging, and recycled content?
- Is international labeling compliance managed with multilingual, metric, format, ingredient, warning, trade agreement, and cross-market coordination?
- Is label accuracy and consistency maintained through formulation matching, update on change, substantiation files, cross-functional review, version tracking, and outdated label management?
- Is the distinction between product category labeling regimes understood when claims reclassify products?
- Are stricter state and international requirements applied where applicable?
