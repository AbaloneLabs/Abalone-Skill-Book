# Nuclear And Critical Infrastructure Security

Nuclear facilities, radioactive materials, and designated critical infrastructure carry security obligations that exceed ordinary industrial safety. The defining feature is that these assets are potential targets for sabotage, theft, and misuse, and the rules impose mandatory physical protection, cyber defense, personnel screening, and reporting duties designed to prevent catastrophic harm. Compliance failure is not measured only in fines; it can mean loss of license, facility shutdown, government intervention, and severe public safety consequences. The central difficulty is that security requirements are often classified or sensitive, and a company may not fully know its obligations until it engages with the relevant government authority.

Use this skill before advising on nuclear licensing, radioactive materials handling, critical infrastructure protection, physical and cyber security programs, or supply chain security for critical systems. The goal is to make the agent identify the asset's regulatory status, the mandatory security standards, the personnel and access controls, and the reporting and coordination duties before concluding compliance.

## Core Rules

### Secure The Right License And Authorization For Nuclear And Radiological Activity

Nuclear and radiological activities require specific licenses tied to the material, activity, and facility.

For nuclear and radioactive materials:

- identify the material type, quantity, and enrichment level;
- determine the license class required for possession, use, transport, or disposal;
- obtain construction permit, operating license, or materials license as applicable;
- meet financial assurance and decommissioning funding requirements;
- maintain qualified personnel and radiation safety officer;
- comply with radiation protection standards and dose limits;
- address import, export, and international safeguards obligations;
- renew licenses and meet license renewal conditions.

Operating without the correct license or outside license conditions is a serious violation. Enriched and special nuclear materials carry the strictest controls.

### Implement Mandatory Physical Security

Nuclear facilities and certain radiological materials must meet mandatory physical protection standards.

Assess:

- physical barrier and access control requirements;
- intrusion detection and assessment systems;
- armed response and contingency response force;
- target sets analysis and vital area determination;
- background investigation and access authorization for personnel;
- protection against design basis threat;
- transportation security for high-risk shipments;
- coordination with law enforcement and emergency response;
- security event reporting and response.

Physical security standards are tied to the assessed threat. The design basis threat defines the adversary capabilities the system must defeat.

### Defend Against Cyber Attacks On Critical Systems

Critical infrastructure cyber security standards are mandatory and increasingly stringent.

Implement:

- identification of critical digital assets and critical cyber assets;
- security controls for access, authentication, encryption, and monitoring;
- network architecture separating control systems from enterprise networks;
- patch and vulnerability management for operational technology;
- supply chain risk management for hardware and software;
- incident detection and response for operational technology;
- workforce cyber security training and awareness;
- coordination with government cyber authorities and information sharing;
- standards specific to the sector such as electric reliability cyber standards.

Operational technology and industrial control systems have different security constraints than enterprise IT, and compromises can cause physical harm.

### Screen Personnel And Control Access

Access to nuclear and critical infrastructure requires personnel screening and ongoing evaluation.

Cover:

- background investigations for access authorization;
- fitness-for-duty and behavioral observation programs;
- trustworthiness determination for unescorted access;
- insider threat detection and mitigation;
- access badge and key management;
- escort and visitor controls;
- reporting of adverse information and access termination;
- periodic reinvestigation and continuous evaluation.

Insider threat is a primary concern for nuclear and critical infrastructure. Personnel reliability programs must be active, not nominal.

### Protect Critical Infrastructure Supply Chains

Critical infrastructure supply chains are a recognized attack vector and subject to risk management requirements.

Assess:

- vendor and supplier risk assessment for critical components;
- provenance and authenticity of hardware and software;
- restrictions on prohibited vendors and equipment from certain origins;
- software bill of materials and component inventory;
- secure development and procurement standards;
- testing and verification of acquired systems;
- lifecycle management and patch support commitments;
- contingency for supplier failure or compromise.

Supply chain compromises can introduce vulnerabilities that persist undetected. Provenance and vendor restrictions are increasingly mandated.

### Meet Reporting And Coordination Duties

Nuclear and critical infrastructure operators must report incidents and coordinate with government.

Implement:

- prompt reporting of security events, cyber incidents, and safeguards events;
- reporting of lost or stolen radioactive materials;
- reporting of unauthorized access or insider concerns;
- coordination with the regulator, law enforcement, and intelligence community;
- participation in sector information sharing and analysis centers;
- emergency classification and notification procedures;
- records of security events and corrective actions.

Reporting duties are often triggered by thresholds and timelines that run regardless of confirmation. Delayed reporting compounds exposure.

### Address Safeguards And International Obligations

Nuclear materials are subject to international safeguards and nonproliferation obligations.

Cover:

- material control and accounting for special nuclear material;
- international safeguards agreements and inspections;
- export control and nuclear nonproliferation commitments;
- additional protocol declarations where applicable;
- reporting of inventory and material balance;
- physical protection under international conventions.

Safeguards accounting must reconcile material to detect diversion. Discrepancies trigger investigation and international concern.

### Manage Government Security Clearances And Classified Information

Some critical infrastructure work involves classified information and government security clearances.

Assess:

- facility security clearance and safeguarding capability;
- personnel security clearances and continuous evaluation;
- classified information handling, storage, and transmission;
- safeguarding of safeguards information and other sensitive categories;
- reporting of security violations and foreign contacts;
- compliance with government security directives and guidance.

Handling classified or sensitive information requires a cleared facility and cleared personnel with strict handling controls. Mishandling can trigger criminal investigation.

## Common Traps

### Operating Outside License Conditions

Even a licensed facility violates if it operates outside the specific license terms for material, activity, or location.

### Physical Security Below The Design Basis Threat

Security must defeat the assessed threat. Systems designed for a lower threat fail the standard.

### Cyber Security Applied To IT But Not Operational Technology

Industrial control systems have different constraints. Applying enterprise IT controls without adaptation leaves operational technology exposed.

### Insider Threat Program That Is Nominal

Personnel reliability requires active monitoring, not just initial screening. Insiders are a primary risk vector.

### Supply Chain Provenance Ignored

Components of unknown provenance can carry embedded vulnerabilities. Vendor restrictions and authenticity matter.

### Reporting Delayed Pending Confirmation

Many reporting duties run from the event, not from confirmation. Waiting for certainty can miss deadlines.

### Classified Information Handled Without Proper Clearance

Handling classified information requires cleared facility and personnel. Unauthorized possession is a serious violation.

## Self-Check

- Is the correct license or authorization secured for the material, activity, and facility, with financial assurance, qualified personnel, and renewal conditions met?
- Are mandatory physical protection standards met for barriers, access control, intrusion detection, response force, vital areas, design basis threat, and transportation?
- Are cyber security controls applied to critical digital and cyber assets, with network separation, patch management, supply chain risk, and operational technology protection?
- Are personnel screening, fitness-for-duty, insider threat detection, access management, and adverse information reporting implemented?
- Are supply chain risks assessed for vendor, provenance, prohibited equipment, software bill of materials, testing, and lifecycle?
- are reporting and coordination duties met for security events, cyber incidents, lost materials, unauthorized access, and emergency classification?
- Are material control and accounting, international safeguards, export control, and nonproliferation obligations addressed?
- Are government security clearances, facility clearance, classified information handling, and sensitive category safeguarding compliant?
- Is the distinction between enterprise IT and operational technology security reflected in the cyber program?
- Are reporting timelines triggered by events rather than confirmation?
