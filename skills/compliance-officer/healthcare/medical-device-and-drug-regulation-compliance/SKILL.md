# Medical Device And Drug Regulation Compliance

Drugs, biologics, and medical devices are governed by a lifecycle regulatory regime that controls development, approval, manufacturing, labeling, marketing, and post-market conduct. The defining feature is that the regulator's authority continues after the product reaches the market: labeling must stay accurate, manufacturing must meet quality standards, adverse events must be reported, promotion must match approved uses, and recalls must be executed. Compliance failure can mean product seizure, injunction, withdrawal of approval, criminal prosecution, and patient harm. The central difficulty is that scientific, commercial, and regulatory judgments are intertwined, and a commercial decision made without regulatory input can create enforcement exposure.

Use this skill before advising on a drug or device approval, reviewing a clinical trial program, assessing manufacturing quality, evaluating promotional materials, or planning a recall. The goal is to make the agent identify the product classification, the approval pathway, the quality system, the labeling boundaries, and the post-market duties before concluding compliance.

## Core Rules

### Classify The Product And Select The Regulatory Pathway

The regulatory pathway depends on product classification, which determines the approval process and ongoing obligations.

Classify:

- drug, biologic, or device, and within devices the risk class (low, moderate, high) determining controls;
- combination products and the lead center;
- whether the product is a medical device versus a wellness or general wellness product outside regulation;
- software as a medical device and its risk classification;
- in vitro diagnostics and their classification;
- whether a product qualifies for an exemption or is subject to full approval or clearance.

Misclassification is a foundational error. A product marketed as wellness that crosses into diagnosis or treatment can become an unapproved device.

### Build The Clinical Trial Program On Ethical And Regulatory Foundations

Clinical investigations must protect participants and generate reliable data.

Cover:

- investigational application and clearance or approval before human use;
- institutional review board or ethics committee approval and ongoing oversight;
- informed consent process and documentation;
- good clinical practice compliance;
- investigator qualifications and financial disclosure;
- monitoring and auditing of trial sites;
- adverse event reporting during trials;
- data integrity and source documentation;
- registration of applicable trials on public registries;
- protection of vulnerable populations and pediatric subjects.

Informed consent is not a form but a process. Data integrity failures can invalidate an entire program.

### Implement Good Manufacturing Practices

Quality systems govern how drugs and devices are designed, produced, tested, stored, and distributed.

Assess:

- design controls for devices and process validation for drugs;
- quality management system and quality unit independence;
- supplier and component controls;
- production and process controls;
- laboratory controls and release testing;
- packaging, labeling, and storage;
- records and batch documentation;
- change control and change notification duties;
- corrective and preventive action system;
- internal and supplier audits;
- serialization and traceability where required;
- sterile and aseptic processing controls where applicable.

A quality system is only as strong as its documentation and its independence from production pressure. Quality cannot be inspected in after the fact.

### Keep Labeling Within Approved Boundaries

Labeling defines the lawful scope of promotion. Promoting outside approved uses is a core enforcement risk.

Control:

- consistency of promotional materials with approved labeling;
- prohibition on promoting off-label uses while allowing truthful, non-misleading scientific exchange under defined limits;
- fair balance of efficacy and risk information;
- substantiation for comparative and superiority claims;
- consistency of advertising with labeling in regulated jurisdictions;
- controls on sales aids, websites, social media, and congress materials;
- review of speaker programs and third-party promotion;
- reprints and dissemination of scientific literature within regulatory limits.

The line between permissible scientific exchange and promotional off-label use is fact-specific and must be controlled by clear procedures and training.

### Manage Post-Market Surveillance And Adverse Event Reporting

Regulatory duties continue after approval. The company must monitor and report.

Implement:

- adverse event collection, evaluation, and reporting within deadlines;
- periodic safety reports;
- medical device reporting for deaths, serious injuries, and malfunctions;
- trend analysis and signal detection;
- post-approval studies where required;
- risk evaluation and mitigation strategies where imposed;
- field corrections and removals tracking;
- complaint handling and investigation;
- vigilance reporting in applicable jurisdictions.

Failure to report known adverse events is a serious violation and can lead to criminal liability for responsible individuals.

### Execute Recalls And Field Actions Correctly

When a product is defective or risky, the company must act promptly and transparently.

Cover:

- recall classification and health hazard evaluation;
- notification to the regulator and, where required, the public;
- communication to customers and patients;
- retrieval, correction, or destruction logistics;
- effectiveness checks confirming recall reach;
- accounting of product retrieved and corrected;
- root-cause analysis and corrective action;
- reporting of corrections and removals.

A recall that is announced but not verified for effectiveness is incomplete. Hiding a known defect to avoid a recall creates severe exposure.

### Control Interactions With Healthcare Professionals

Relationships with healthcare professionals create anti-kickback and transparency exposure.

Manage:

- consulting and advisory arrangements with fair market value and documented need;
- research funding and grants with independence;
- speaker programs with legitimate purpose and audience;
- meals, travel, and hospitality within limits;
- samples tracking and distribution controls;
- transparency reporting of payments to physicians and teaching hospitals;
- anti-kickback statute and similar anti-bribery analysis for healthcare professionals;
- controls on inducements that could influence prescribing.

Transparency reporting requires accurate data capture. Anti-kickback analysis turns on whether one purpose of a transfer is to induce referrals or business.

### Address Combination Products And Digital Health

Combination products and digital health add complexity across centers and software lifecycle rules.

Assess:

- lead center assignment for combination products;
- software lifecycle and change management for software as a medical device;
- interoperability and cybersecurity for connected devices;
- algorithm updates and whether they require new clearance or approval;
- real-world data and evidence use in regulatory submissions;
- artificial intelligence and machine learning enabled device guidance.

Software changes that affect safety or effectiveness may require regulatory action before deployment.

## Common Traps

### Wellness Claim That Drifts Into Medical Claim

Marketing language that implies diagnosis, treatment, prevention, or cure can convert a wellness product into a regulated device. Review claims against the intended use standard.

### Off-Label Promotion Disguised As Education

Scientific exchange has limits. Structuring education to steer prescribers toward off-label use is promotion and creates enforcement risk.

### Quality System Pressured By Production Deadlines

Quality cannot be subordinated to shipment goals. Design controls, validation, and release testing must operate independently.

### Adverse Event Reporting Delayed

Deadlines are short and strict. A known serious adverse event that is not reported within the deadline is a violation regardless of intent.

### Recall Treated As Optional Or Quiet

Voluntary actions must still be reported and effective. Concealing a defect to avoid visibility compounds exposure.

### Transparency Reporting With Poor Data Capture

Payment data must be captured accurately and reported. Inaccurate or incomplete reporting creates its own enforcement risk.

### Software Update Deployed Without Regulatory Analysis

A change to a regulated device's software may require new clearance. Deploying without analysis can mean marketing an unapproved device.

## Self-Check

- Is the product classified correctly as drug, biologic, device, combination, or wellness, with the risk class and approval or clearance pathway identified?
- Does the clinical trial program rest on regulatory clearance, IRB approval, informed consent, good clinical practice, monitoring, adverse event reporting, and data integrity?
- Does the quality system cover design controls, process validation, supplier controls, laboratory controls, change control, CAPA, audits, and serialization where required?
- Are promotional materials consistent with approved labeling, with fair balance, substantiation, and controls on off-label promotion and scientific exchange?
- Are adverse event reporting, periodic safety reports, device reporting, trend analysis, post-approval studies, REMS, and complaint handling operational within deadlines?
- Are recalls and field actions classified, notified, communicated, verified for effectiveness, and supported by root-cause corrective action?
- Are interactions with healthcare professionals controlled for fair market value, legitimate purpose, samples, transparency reporting, and anti-kickback risk?
- Are combination products assigned a lead center, and are digital health software changes analyzed for regulatory impact before deployment?
- Is the intended use monitored so that wellness claims do not drift into regulated medical claims?
- Are post-market duties treated as continuous, not completed at approval?
