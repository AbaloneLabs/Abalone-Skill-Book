# Visa Sponsorship And Nonimmigrant Compliance

Sponsoring nonimmigrant workers creates ongoing compliance obligations that extend well beyond the initial petition approval. The defining difficulty is that sponsorship carries wage, working conditions, notice, and recordkeeping duties that continue throughout employment, and violations can create liability for the employer, harm to the sponsored worker, and consequences for future sponsorship ability. The obligations differ by visa category, and treating all nonimmigrant workers the same is a frequent source of error.

Use this skill before sponsoring nonimmigrant workers, managing ongoing sponsorship obligations, handling transfers or terminations, or responding to sponsorship audits. The goal is to make the agent identify the category-specific obligations, meet wage and notice requirements, maintain required records, and manage termination and enforcement risk. Sponsorship rules are federal and category-specific, and audits and contested matters should involve qualified immigration counsel.

## Core Rules

### Identify The Category-Specific Obligations

Each nonimmigrant category has distinct requirements that must be applied to the specific worker.

Identify obligations by category:

- specialty occupation visas requiring a bachelor's degree or equivalent in a specialty field, with prevailing wage and labor condition application duties;
- treaty visas based on nationality and treaty qualifications;
- intra-company transferee visas for multinational executives, managers, and specialized knowledge employees;
- seasonal and temporary worker visas with prevailing wage, housing, and recruitment obligations;
- extraordinary ability and other specialized categories.

The obligations attach to the category, and a worker's change of status or concurrent employment can change the applicable requirements. The labor condition application regime for specialty occupation visas is the most procedurally intensive and the most frequently audited.

### Meet Prevailing Wage Requirements

Prevailing wage obligations ensure sponsored workers are not paid below the market rate, and they are strictly enforced.

Comply by:

- obtaining the applicable prevailing wage determination or using an approved survey;
- paying the higher of the prevailing wage or the actual wage paid to similar employees;
- paying the required wage level for the position and experience;
- maintaining documentation of the wage basis;
- adjusting for changes in duties or location;
- paying the required wage throughout employment, not only at initial hire.

Paying below the prevailing or actual wage is a core violation that can trigger back wages, penalties, and disqualification from future sponsorship. The actual wage comparison requires analysis of what the employer pays similar employees, which must be documented.

### Maintain Public Access Files And Attestations

Specialty occupation sponsorship requires a public access file with specific attestations.

Maintain:

- the labor condition application and supporting documentation;
- the prevailing wage determination or survey;
- the actual wage calculation methodology;
- the notice of filing posted at the worksite;
- documentation of the strike or lockout attestation;
- records accessible for inspection within the required timeframe.

The public access file must be available for inspection and must contain the required attestations and supporting documents. Missing or incomplete files are a common audit finding. The notice of filing must be posted at the worksite or provided electronically where permitted.

### Provide Required Working Conditions And Notices

Sponsorship carries conditions on working conditions and notice to the workforce.

Provide:

- notice of filing at the worksite on or before the filing date;
- working conditions that do not adversely affect similarly employed workers;
- no displacement of similarly employed workers within defined periods;
- no placement of the sponsored worker at a third-party worksite without meeting specific conditions;
- benefits offered on the same basis as similar employees.

The non-displacement and no-adverse-effect conditions protect the existing workforce. Benefits must be offered on the same basis, and discrepancies in benefits between sponsored and similar workers are a violation.

### Handle Transfers And Portability Correctly

Nonimmigrant workers may change employers under specific portability rules.

Handle:

- timely filing of new petitions to enable portability;
- verification of employment authorization during the transition;
- coordination of start dates with authorization validity;
- withdrawal of petitions upon termination;
- management of the gap between authorization and petition decisions.

Portability allows a worker to begin new employment upon filing of a non-frivolous petition in certain categories, but the employer must verify authorization and manage the timing. Failure to withdraw a petition upon termination can create ongoing wage obligations.

### Manage Termination And Return Transportation

Termination of a sponsored worker triggers specific obligations.

Manage:

- notification to the relevant authority of termination where required;
- offering return transportation home where the category requires it;
- coordination of the last day of authorized work;
- management of any grace period for the worker to seek new sponsorship;
- documentation of the termination and the basis.

Several nonimmigrant categories require the employer to offer return transportation to the worker's home country upon involuntary termination before the end of the authorized period. Failure to offer return transportation is a violation and can create ongoing obligations.

### Prepare For Worksite Enforcement And Audits

Sponsorship employers face worksite inspections and audits of their sponsorship files.

Prepare by:

- conducting self-audits of public access files and wage compliance;
- maintaining organized, retrievable records;
- designating response personnel and counsel;
- establishing protocols for inspection reception;
- training on what to produce and what requires review;
- planning for the consequences of identified violations.

Self-audits identify and allow correction of file and wage issues before an enforcement action. Inspections can be unannounced and broad in scope, covering not only sponsorship files but overall employment eligibility verification.

### Avoid Discrimination In Sponsorship Decisions

Sponsorship decisions must not become a vehicle for discrimination.

Avoid:

- citizenship status discrimination beyond permitted preferences;
- national origin discrimination in sponsorship and assignment;
- disparate treatment of sponsored workers in pay, conditions, or benefits;
- retaliation against workers who assert sponsorship-related rights.

While employers may have lawful preferences for citizens or certain statuses in limited circumstances, sponsorship processes must not mask national origin or citizenship status discrimination. Sponsored workers must receive equal pay, conditions, and benefits subject to the specific category requirements.

## Common Traps

### Paying Below The Prevailing Or Actual Wage

Sub-prevailing or sub-actual wage is a core violation triggering back wages and disqualification.

### Missing Or Incomplete Public Access Files

Files must contain the attestations and supporting documents and be inspection-ready.

### Benefits Disparity Between Sponsored And Similar Workers

Discrepancies in benefits violate the equal conditions requirement.

### Failure To Withdraw Petitions Upon Termination

Unwithdrawn petitions can create ongoing wage obligations.

### Missing Return Transportation Offers Where Required

Several categories require return transportation offers upon involuntary termination.

## Self-Check

- Are category-specific obligations identified and applied to each sponsored worker based on visa category?
- Are prevailing wage requirements met by paying the higher of prevailing or actual wage, with documentation, adjustment for changes, and payment throughout employment?
- Are public access files maintained with the labor condition application, prevailing wage basis, actual wage methodology, notice of filing, strike attestation, and inspection-ready records?
- Are working conditions and notices provided including worksite notice, no adverse effect, no displacement, third-party placement conditions, and equal benefits?
- Are transfers and portability handled with timely filing, authorization verification, start date coordination, petition withdrawal on termination, and gap management?
- Are termination obligations met including authority notification, return transportation offers where required, last authorized day coordination, grace period management, and documentation?
- Is the organization prepared for worksite enforcement through self-audits, organized records, designated counsel, reception protocols, and consequence planning?
- Is discrimination in sponsorship decisions avoided, and where audits or enforcement arise, has qualified immigration counsel been involved, recognizing that sponsorship rules are federal and category-specific and this guidance is not legal advice?
