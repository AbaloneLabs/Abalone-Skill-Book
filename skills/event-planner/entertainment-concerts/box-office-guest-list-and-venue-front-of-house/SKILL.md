# Box Office Guest List And Venue Front Of House

Concert audience experience begins before the first note. Box office, guest list, will call, door timing, security screening, accessibility, bars, merchandise, restrooms, and crowd entry determine whether the room is ready for the show. Agents often focus backstage and forget that a slow front door can delay the opener, anger VIPs, and create safety issues. This skill helps coordinate front-of-house as part of production, not a separate venue afterthought.

## Core Rules

### Reconcile ticketing and box office data

Confirm ticket platform, capacity, ticket types, holds, comps, artist allotment, promoter holds, sponsor tickets, VIP packages, ADA seats, guest list, will call, refunds, upgrades, and walk-up sales. Box office should know which lists are final and who can authorize changes.

Do not let guest list changes arrive through scattered texts. Use one controlled channel and cutoff time, with an escalation owner for true last-minute changes.

### Define door timing and show timing relationship

Doors should align with security capacity, box office staffing, bar readiness, merch setup, room turnover, soundcheck completion, VIP entry, and support act start. If doors open late, the rest of the show may compress or curfew may be at risk.

Production, venue manager, promoter, security, and box office should share status before doors and before show start. A start decision should consider audience ingress, not only stage readiness.

### Manage will call and VIP pickup

Will call needs ID rules, pickup windows, guest list categories, problem resolution, artist guests, media credentials, sponsor packets, VIP upgrades, and lost ticket process. VIP guests may need separate check-in or escort.

Avoid embarrassing guests by making them argue at the window. If a name is missing, staff need a resolution path that does not block the line.

### Coordinate security screening and entry policy

Bag policy, prohibited items, weapons policy, camera rules, reentry, age restrictions, alcohol wristbands, ID checks, and search procedures must be communicated before arrival and enforced consistently. Screening should not create unsafe crowd buildup outside.

Security needs briefing on artist photo policy, VIP routes, accessible entry, intoxication, medical issues, and escalation. Door staff should know when to involve security versus box office.

### Protect accessibility and guest services

Accessible entry, seating, companion seats, viewing platforms, restrooms, elevator access, service animals, interpreters, captions if provided, and early entry for mobility needs should be planned. ADA or accessibility tickets are not sufficient without operational support.

Guest services should know how to answer common questions: restrooms, water, medical, lost items, set times, exits, merch, and transportation after the show.

### Plan bar, merch, restroom, and circulation lines

Bars and merch can generate revenue but also block movement. Place lines away from exits, stairs, accessible routes, and emergency paths. Ensure payment devices, cash policy, inventory, age checks, and staffing are ready.

Restroom lines affect guest satisfaction and safety. If facilities are limited, use signage, ushers, and timing awareness to reduce crush before headliner.

### Handle comps and capacity discipline

Guest lists and comps can quietly exceed capacity or violate fire code if not controlled. Holds should be released at defined times. Track scanned entries, no-shows, and walk-up capacity.

Do not let VIP pressure override capacity, age, or safety rules. Overselling damages the venue and promoter.

### Close front-of-house after the show

Post-show includes lost and found, refunds or disputes, incident logs, bar and merch close, guest egress, rideshare pickup, security sweep, accessibility exit, and box office settlement. Audience exit can be as important as entry.

Document issues for future shows: slow scanners, confusing policy, underused entrance, bar bottleneck, or guest list errors.

### Keep front-of-house informed during the show

FOH staff should know set times, expected encore, severe weather instructions, medical escalation, door policy after headliner starts, reentry decisions, and any artist-requested changes. Guests ask ushers and box office staff first, so those teams need current answers.

Use a simple update channel rather than forcing FOH to infer changes from stage noise or social media.

## Common Traps

- Treating box office as separate from show timing.
- Allowing guest list changes through multiple informal channels.
- Opening doors before security, bar, merch, accessibility, or box office is ready.
- Providing no resolution path for missing will-call names.
- Communicating prohibited items only at the door.
- Letting screening lines build unsafely outside; selling ADA or accessible tickets without entry, seating, and restroom support
- Placing merch or bar lines across exits and accessible paths; letting comps and holds exceed capacity discipline
- Leaving ushers, box office, and guest services unaware of set time changes, reentry decisions, and incident instructions; ignoring post-show egress, lost items, disputes, and FOH lessons learned

## Self-Check

- Are ticketing, capacity, holds, comps, guest list, VIP, ADA, refunds, upgrades, and walk-up rules reconciled?
- Is there one guest list change channel, cutoff, and escalation owner?
- Do door timing and show timing account for security, box office, bars, merch, VIP, and audience ingress?
- Are will call, ID, missing names, media, sponsor packets, VIP pickup, and lost tickets handled?
- Are bag, camera, weapons, reentry, age, alcohol, and screening policies communicated and enforceable?
- Does accessibility cover entry, seating, companion seats, restrooms, service animals, elevators, and exit?
- Are bar, merch, restroom, and circulation lines placed safely and staffed?
- Is capacity protected through scanned counts, hold release, and comp discipline?
- Are FOH teams updated on set times, reentry, medical, weather, incidents, and guest answers during the show?
- Are post-show egress, disputes, lost items, settlement, and incident logs closed out?
