# Product Samples Security And Post Launch Followup

Launch value often depends on what happens after the reveal: samples reach the right people, embargoes hold, review units return or convert, sales follows up, press receives assets, and product feedback reaches the launch team. Samples and launch kits can also leak confidential information, create inventory shrink, or produce poor reviews if they are incomplete. The agent should plan sample security and follow-up as part of the event, not as cleanup.

## Core Rules

### Treat samples as controlled inventory

Track sample purpose, owner, SKU, serial number, quantity, condition, recipient, embargo status, return requirement, and replacement value. Review units, prototypes, beta devices, regulated products, luxury goods, and limited launch kits should not be handed out casually. Use sign-out, shipping tracking, and receipt confirmation.

Define who can approve sample distribution. Sales, PR, executives, influencers, partners, and VIP hosts may all ask for product. Without control, high-priority reviewers or customers may receive incomplete or late samples while low-value giveaways consume inventory.

### Protect embargoes and confidential materials

Embargoed samples need recipient agreements, clear dates and time zones, review guidance, do-not-publish rules, photo restrictions, and support contacts. Packaging, labels, Wi-Fi names, app screens, and instructions can leak product names or features. Control what vendors and couriers can see.

If samples are prototypes or pre-release software, define security rules: no teardown, no resale, no public benchmarking, no screenshots, no third-party sharing, or return by a deadline. Coordinate with legal and PR.

### Make samples usable before they leave

A reviewer or customer sample should include correct accessories, chargers, instructions, login credentials, firmware, support contact, known limitations, return label if needed, and safety information. Test kits before distribution. A dead battery, missing cable, wrong region plug, or broken account can become negative coverage.

For food, cosmetics, medical, regulated, or child-related products, check labeling, safety, expiration, allergen, batch, and compliance requirements. Launch excitement does not override product rules.

### Connect follow-up to launch objectives

After the event, route leads, meetings, demos, trial requests, press questions, analyst follow-ups, influencer posts, sample feedback, and customer issues. Define owners and response time. The first 48 hours after launch often determine whether interest converts.

Follow-up should be segmented. Press need assets and answers; enterprise prospects need sales contact; developers need docs and support; influencers need posting rules; customers need purchase or migration path; executives need performance reporting.

### Measure beyond attendance

Track sample delivery, embargo compliance, review quality, lead conversion, pipeline, trial activation, support tickets, social sentiment, press pickup, partner actions, sales meetings, and product feedback. Feed issues back to product, support, sales, and communications.

If something was miscommunicated at launch, correct it quickly. Post-launch silence lets confusion become narrative.

### Build post-launch issue triage

Launches create immediate issues: broken signup links, sample defects, pricing confusion, bad reviews, embargo violations, support spikes, social backlash, press corrections, demo bugs, and sales routing failures. Define a triage room or channel with owners from product, support, PR, sales, legal, operations, and events. Decide severity levels and response times before the event starts.

The first public problems should not be discovered by one person refreshing social media. Monitor registration, ecommerce, app downloads, trial activation, press coverage, influencer posts, sample tracking, support tickets, and partner channels.

### Control sample return, disposal, and secondary market risk

Review units and prototypes may need return, wipe, destruction, resale prohibition, or inventory reconciliation. If samples can enter secondary markets, track serials and enforce agreements. For regulated or safety-sensitive products, sample disposal may require specific handling.

### Separate feedback from noise

Create a method for product feedback to reach the right team with source, severity, and evidence. Do not let anecdotal VIP comments override broader launch data without context.

## Common Traps
- Treating samples as swag rather than controlled assets.
- Failing to track serials, recipients, return status, and embargo obligations.
- Letting executives or VIP teams bypass sample allocation rules.
- Shipping review units that are untested, incomplete, uncharged, or missing access credentials.
- Exposing product names or features through packaging, labels, apps, or vendor instructions.
- Forgetting regulated labeling, safety, allergen, expiration, or regional compliance.
- Capturing leads at the event but not assigning follow-up owners.
- Measuring launch success only by attendance, applause, or social impressions.
- Having no triage channel for broken links, sample defects, support spikes, bad reviews, or embargo violations.
- Forgetting sample return, wipe, destruction, secondary market, and regulated disposal obligations.
- Letting anecdotal comments override structured launch feedback.

## Self-Check

- Are samples tracked by owner, SKU, serial, quantity, condition, recipient, purpose, embargo, return, and value?
- Is sample approval authority clear across PR, sales, executives, partners, influencers, and VIP requests?
- Are embargo agreements, time zones, publication rules, photo restrictions, support contacts, and legal limits clear?
- Are prototype, beta, teardown, resale, screenshot, sharing, and return restrictions documented where needed?
- Have samples been tested for accessories, instructions, credentials, region fit, firmware, safety, and known limitations?
- Are regulated product labeling, safety, expiration, allergen, batch, and compliance requirements met?
- Are press, sales, developer, influencer, customer, partner, and executive follow-ups assigned with response times?
- Are sample performance, reviews, leads, trials, support issues, press, sentiment, and product feedback measured after launch?
- Is a cross-functional post-launch triage channel ready with severity, owner, response time, and monitoring sources?
- Are sample return, wiping, destruction, resale prevention, and disposal requirements controlled?
- Does product feedback include source, severity, evidence, and routing rather than unstructured anecdotes?
