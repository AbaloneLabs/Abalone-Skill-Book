# Revision And Fact Checking In Longform

A longform draft is not a finished story; it is the raw material from which a finished story is built through revision and verification. The harm this skill prevents is the longform published with structural or factual weaknesses that revision should have caught: the beautiful sentence that misstates a fact, the dramatic restructuring that distorts the timeline, the narrative polish that erodes verifiability, or the fact-check treated as a formality rather than a substantive process. Revision and fact-checking are where longform either earns its authority or loses it. Strong revision improves structure, pace, and clarity without compromising accuracy; strong fact-checking tests every claim against the reporting and forces honest reconciliation of narrative ambition with verified reality.

Use this skill when revising any longform draft and when conducting or preparing for fact-checking. Consult editors throughout revision, and for investigations or legally sensitive longform, ensure rigorous independent fact-checking and legal review before publication.

## Core Rules

### Revise for structure and clarity before line editing
Longform revision proceeds in layers. Begin with structure: does the overall shape work, does the opening earn its place, does the pace sustain, do the threads converge, does the resolution land? Structural problems cannot be fixed at the sentence level, and polishing sentences in a structurally flawed piece wastes effort. Move from structure through paragraph and section refinement to line editing only after the larger shape is sound. This sequencing respects the dependency: a beautiful sentence in the wrong place serves no one.

### Preserve accuracy through every revision
Each revision, restructuring, condensing, dramatizing, polishing, risks introducing inaccuracy. A condensed timeline may imply causation the full version did not; a dramatized scene may add detail the reporting did not establish; a polished sentence may sharpen a claim beyond what the evidence supports. After every substantive revision, re-check the affected material against the reporting to ensure accuracy survived the change. Treat revision as a process that can erode as well as improve, and guard the factual basis throughout.

### Maintain a verification trail linking claims to evidence
For fact-checking to work, every factual claim in the longform must be traceable to its evidence basis: the document, interview, observation, or data source that supports it. Maintain this trail as you revise, so that the fact-checker can test each claim against its basis. A claim that cannot be traced is a claim that cannot be verified, and unverified claims in longform are vulnerabilities. The verification trail is the infrastructure that makes rigorous fact-checking possible; without it, fact-checking becomes guesswork.

### Treat fact-checking as substantive, not ceremonial
Fact-checking is not a final proofreading step; it is a substantive independent verification of every claim, quote, name, date, figure, and characterization against the reporting and against external sources where relevant. A rigorous fact-checker reads the draft against the reporting notes, flags every discrepancy, queries every unsupported claim, and forces reconciliation. Treat the fact-checker as a collaborator in accuracy, give them the time and access they need, and respond to queries with evidence rather than defensiveness. A ceremonial fact-check provides false assurance; a substantive one catches real errors.

### Reconcile narrative ambition with verified reality
Longform often seeks narrative effects, dramatic scenes, smooth flow, vivid detail, that can pull against strict verifiability. Where the narrative ambition outruns what the reporting established, reconcile honestly: either trim the ambition to match the evidence, or do more reporting to earn the effect. Do not resolve the gap by inventing, compositing, or overstating. The longform's authority depends on readers trusting that the vividness reflects verified reporting, and a single discovered fabrication or composite destroys trust across the whole piece and the outlet.

### Handle quotes with special care through revision
Quotes are vulnerable in revision: condensing can change meaning, cleaning can misrepresent speech, partial quotes can imply more verbatim fidelity than they deliver, and moving quotes between contexts can alter their significance. After any revision touching quotes, re-check each against the source material to confirm fidelity and context. Follow the outlet's policy on quote cleaning, disclose material alterations, and never present paraphrase as direct quotation. Quotes carry special weight with readers; preserve that weight through honest handling.

### Use editor feedback to strengthen, not flatten
Editor feedback on longform aims to strengthen the piece, but uncritical acceptance can flatten distinctive voice or compromise accuracy in service of clarity. Engage editor feedback substantively: understand the underlying concern, consider multiple solutions, and push back where a suggested change would weaken accuracy or distort the writer's voice. The editor-writer collaboration produces the best longform when both engage critically rather than one deferring to the other. A writer who accepts every suggestion uncritically and an editor who imposes changes without dialogue both produce weaker work.

### Build in time for the revision and verification process
Revision and fact-checking take substantial time, often more than anticipated, and longform published without adequate time for these stages carries avoidable risk. Plan the timeline to include structural revision, line editing, fact-checking, reconciliation of queries, legal review where relevant, and final polishing. Compressing these stages under deadline pressure produces longform with weaknesses that more time would have caught. Treat the revision and verification timeline as essential, not optional, and push back on deadlines that would force shortcuts.

## Common Traps

### Line editing before structural revision
Polishing sentences in a structurally flawed piece wastes effort and masks problems. The trap is that line editing feels productive, but it cannot fix structural weakness.

### Accuracy eroded through revision
Restructuring, condensing, or dramatizing introducing inaccuracy. The trap is that revision improves some elements while eroding others, and the erosion is easy to miss.

### No verification trail
Claims unlinked to evidence cannot be fact-checked, leaving vulnerabilities. The trap is that the trail feels like overhead, but its absence makes fact-checking guesswork.

### Ceremonial fact-checking
Treating fact-check as a formality rather than substantive verification provides false assurance. The trap is that the formality feels sufficient, but it catches nothing.

### Narrative ambition outrunning evidence
Dramatic effects unsupported by reporting resolved through invention or overstatement. The trap is that the effect is compelling, but it breaches the verification compact.

### Quote distortion through revision
Condensing, cleaning, or moving quotes changing meaning or context. The trap is that the revised quote reads better, but it may no longer be faithful.

### Uncritical acceptance of editor feedback
Deferring to suggestions without critical engagement flattens voice and can compromise accuracy. The trap is that acceptance feels collaborative, but uncritical deference weakens the work.

### Compressed revision timeline
Insufficient time for revision and fact-checking leaving avoidable weaknesses. The trap is that the deadline feels urgent, but shortcuts produce vulnerable longform.

## Self-Check

- Does revision proceed from structure through section to line, with structural problems addressed before sentence-level polishing?
- Is accuracy preserved through every revision, with affected material re-checked against the reporting after substantive changes?
- Is there a verification trail linking every factual claim to its evidence basis, enabling rigorous fact-checking?
- Is fact-checking treated as substantive independent verification rather than a ceremonial final step?
- Where narrative ambition outruns verified reality, is it reconciled through trimming ambition or additional reporting rather than invention?
- Are quotes re-checked against source material after any revision touching them, with cleaning disclosed and paraphrase distinguished from direct quotation?
- Is editor feedback engaged with critically to strengthen rather than flatten, with pushback where changes would weaken accuracy or voice?
- Is adequate time built in for revision, fact-checking, reconciliation, legal review where relevant, and final polishing, with editors involved throughout and rigorous independent review ensured for investigations or legally sensitive longform?
