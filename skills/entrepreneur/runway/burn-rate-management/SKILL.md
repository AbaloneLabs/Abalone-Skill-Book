# Burn Rate Management

Burn rate is the rate at which a venture consumes cash each month. It is not inherently good or bad; the right burn depends on the venture's stage, evidence, runway, and goals. Burning too slowly can starve growth and lose a window; burning too fast can end the venture before it proves anything. Burn management is the discipline of matching spending to evidence and runway, cutting spend that does not produce learning or progress, and preserving the option to continue when assumptions fail. The danger is that spending feels like progress while quietly shortening the runway.

Use this skill before answering broad questions such as "how do I manage my burn rate", "am I spending too much", "what should I cut", or "how should an entrepreneur control cash outflow". The goal is to prevent the agent from treating spending as inherently virtuous and from letting burn drift upward without a return.

## Core Rules

### Match Burn To Stage And Evidence

The right burn depends on what the venture is trying to prove. Early, unvalidated ventures should burn slowly, spending only on the cheapest tests of the riskiest assumptions. Ventures with proven demand and a clear path can justify higher burn to capture the opportunity. Match the spending level to the evidence supporting it.

High burn before proof is a bet that the assumptions are right; if they are wrong, the runway is gone.

### Tie Spending To Learning Or Progress

Every dollar burned should buy either learning, progress toward a milestone, or capacity needed to deliver. Spending that does none of these is drift. Review each major spend category and ask what it produces.

Cut or defer spend that produces neither learning nor progress toward the current milestone.

### Distinguish Productive From Cosmetic Spend

Some spend advances the venture: building the product, reaching customers, delivering value. Other spend creates the feeling of a real business without advancing it: premature branding, unnecessary office space, over-customized tools, vanity marketing. Be honest about which is which.

Cosmetic spend feels productive and is not; it must be identified and deferred.

### Control Fixed Commitments

Fixed commitments, leases, salaries, long contracts, and subscriptions, lock in burn and reduce flexibility. Before taking on a fixed commitment, ask whether it can be deferred, rented, outsourced, or scaled gradually. Early ventures benefit from keeping commitments variable until evidence justifies fixing them.

Each new fixed commitment shortens the runway that flexibility could have protected.

### Review Burn Regularly And Adjust

Burn drifts upward as small additions accumulate. Review actual burn against plan regularly, identify where spend is growing without return, and adjust. Burn management is an ongoing discipline, not a one-time setup.

Track the trend of burn over time, not just the current level.

### Preserve Optionality Until Evidence Improves

Optionality, the ability to cut, defer, pivot, or wait, is valuable when evidence is still weak. Spending that removes optionality, irreversible commitments, long contracts, heavy fixed costs, raises the risk if assumptions fail. Preserve optionality until the evidence justifies committing.

### Plan Burn Around Milestones And Runway

Burn should be planned so that the runway reaches a defined milestone with margin to spare. If current burn will exhaust runway before the milestone, either reduce burn, accelerate the milestone, or secure more runway. Burn without a milestone view is spending blind.

### Include Founder Compensation Honestly

Founders sometimes omit their own compensation to make burn look lower, but the founder's real financial needs do not disappear. Include honest founder compensation in burn, and plan for when it must rise, because a founder who cannot sustain personal finances will be forced out of the venture.

### Acknowledge Personal Risk And Professional Advice

Burn decisions carry high stakes for venture survival and founder personal risk. Tax, employment, and contract implications of spending and cuts may require professional advice. This skill does not replace that counsel.

## Common Traps

### Spending Feels Like Progress

Activity and spending create the feeling of momentum while the venture learns nothing.

### Burn Drifts Up Unnoticed

Small additions accumulate; without regular review, burn creeps past plan.

### Fixed Commitments Too Early

Locking in costs before evidence justifies them removes flexibility when assumptions fail.

### Cosmetic Spend Mistaken For Productive

Branding, offices, and vanity marketing feel real but do not advance the venture.

### Omitting Founder Compensation

Excluding founder pay hides real burn and sets up personal financial failure.

### Burn Without A Milestone

Spending without a milestone to reach burns runway without direction.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Burn level matches the venture's stage and the evidence supporting the spend.
- [ ] Each major spend category is tied to learning or progress toward a milestone.
- [ ] Cosmetic spend is distinguished from productive spend and deferred where possible.
- [ ] Fixed commitments are minimized until evidence justifies them.
- [ ] Burn is reviewed regularly against plan and adjusted when it drifts.
- [ ] Optionality is preserved until evidence improves.
- [ ] Burn is planned so runway reaches the next milestone with margin.
- [ ] Founder compensation is included honestly in burn calculations.
- [ ] Tax, employment, and contract implications of spending and cuts are considered.
- [ ] The conclusion avoids treating spending as inherently virtuous without return on learning or progress.
