# Trade Secrets And NDA

Trade secrets can be among a startup's most durable advantages, yet they depend entirely on reasonable secrecy measures, and they are lost the moment secrecy is abandoned. Non-disclosure agreements are a tool to support secrecy, but they are widely over-used and poorly understood: founders demand NDAs before basic conversations, which signals naivety, while neglecting the internal safeguards, access controls, marking, and agreements, that actually preserve trade secret rights. Sound practice is to understand what trade secrets require, when NDAs help, and when they hinder.

Use this skill before answering broad questions such as "should I use an NDA", "how do I protect my trade secrets", "is my idea protected by an NDA", "when should I ask for a non-disclosure agreement", or "how should an entrepreneur handle confidentiality". The goal is to prevent the agent from over-relying on NDAs as a substitute for real secrecy measures and from misunderstanding what trade secret protection actually requires.

## Core Rules

### Understand What Trade Secret Protection Requires

Trade secret protection requires that information has independent economic value from not being generally known, and that the holder takes reasonable measures to keep it secret. The protection lasts as long as secrecy is maintained and can be indefinite, but it is destroyed the moment secrecy is lost. Reasonable measures, not mere intent, are what the law rewards.

### Implement Real Secrecy Safeguards

Real trade secret protection comes from internal safeguards: limiting access to those who need it, marking documents as confidential, using password protection and access logs, and binding employees and contractors through agreements. These measures, not NDAs with outsiders, are what establish reasonable secrecy. A venture that signs NDAs but has no internal controls has weak protection.

### Use NDAs Where They Genuinely Help

NDAs are valuable when sharing genuinely confidential information with a counterparty who could otherwise use or disclose it, such as a potential partner, manufacturer, or acquirer conducting due diligence. In these contexts, a tailored NDA creates a contractual obligation that supports trade secret status and provides a remedy. Use NDAs where disclosure is necessary and the information is genuinely protectable.

### Do Not Over-Use NDAs In Early Conversations

Demanding an NDA before a first meeting with investors, advisors, or casual contacts signals inexperience and often shuts down the conversation, because investors routinely see many similar ideas and will not accept blanket confidentiality. Ideas alone are rarely as valuable as execution, and over-using NDAs damages credibility. Reserve NDAs for genuine, necessary disclosures of protectable information.

### Do Not Confuse NDAs With Trade Secret Protection

An NDA is a contract that creates obligations between parties; it does not, by itself, make information a trade secret or prevent the world from using it if it becomes public through other means. NDAs support trade secret status by showing reasonable measures, but they are not a substitute for the underlying safeguards. Understand the distinction.

### Recognize That Ideas Are Hard To Protect

Raw ideas, without embodiment or execution, are difficult to protect through trade secrets or NDAs, because they are easily replicated once described and hard to prove as misappropriated. The execution, the data, the processes, and the know-how are usually more protectable than the high-level idea. Focus protection on the substantive, specific assets.

### Tailor The NDA To The Context

A one-size-fits-all NDA is often too broad or too narrow. The terms, duration, permitted uses, and residual knowledge clauses should fit the context: a manufacturing discussion differs from an investor pitch differs from a contractor engagement. Tailoring increases the chance the NDA is signed and that it actually protects.

### Plan For The Departure Of People

Trade secrets leave with people. When employees or contractors depart, their agreements, return of materials, and reminders of obligations matter. Plan for departures, because trade secret disputes frequently arise when former team members join competitors. Forethought here is far cheaper than litigation.

### Acknowledge Personal Risk And Professional Advice

Trade secret protection and NDAs involve jurisdiction-specific legal requirements. Use qualified counsel to draft agreements and design safeguards. This skill provides strategic framing only and does not replace professional legal advice.

## Common Traps

### NDA As A Substitute For Internal Safeguards

Signing NDAs while neglecting access controls and marking provides weak protection.

### Over-Using NDAs In Early Conversations

Demanding NDAs before investor meetings signals naivety and shuts down dialogue.

### Confusing NDAs With Trade Secret Status

A contract between parties does not protect information once it becomes public.

### Protecting Raw Ideas Instead Of Execution

High-level ideas are hard to protect; specific processes, data, and know-how are stronger.

### One-Size-Fits-All NDAs

Generic agreements are often rejected or fail to fit the actual disclosure.

### Neglecting Departure Safeguards

Trade secrets walk out with departing team members, creating disputes that foresight could prevent.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Internal secrecy safeguards, access control, marking, and agreements, are in place.
- [ ] NDAs are used where genuinely necessary, not demanded in early conversations.
- [ ] The distinction between NDAs and trade secret protection is understood.
- [ ] Protection focuses on substantive assets, not raw ideas.
- [ ] NDAs are tailored to the specific disclosure context.
- [ ] Departures of employees and contractors are planned for with agreements and safeguards.
- [ ] The venture's approach to confidentiality signals professionalism, not naivety.
- [ ] Trade secret status is supported by documented reasonable measures.
- [ ] Over-use of NDAs is avoided where it would damage credibility.
- [ ] Agreements and safeguards are flagged for qualified legal counsel.
