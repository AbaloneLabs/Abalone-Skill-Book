# Culture And Values Early

Culture is how a team actually behaves when no one is watching; values are the principles that shape those behaviors. In an early team, culture forms whether the founder shapes it or not, because the founder's behavior, decisions, and tolerance set the norms that the team absorbs. The question is not whether to have culture but whether it is shaped deliberately or left to accident. Early culture is also sticky: the norms established in the first few hires become the default that later hires inherit and reinforce. Shaping culture early, authentically, and in alignment with the venture's strategy is far easier than reforming a culture that formed badly.

Use this skill before answering broad questions such as "how do I build company culture", "what values should my startup have", "how do I set the tone early", or "how should an entrepreneur shape culture". The goal is to prevent the agent from equating culture with perks or slogans and from treating values as decoration rather than as decision-shaping principles.

## Core Rules

### Recognize That Culture Forms Whether Shaped Or Not

Culture is not optional; it emerges from the founder's behavior, decisions, and tolerance. If the founder does not shape it deliberately, it forms from accident and drift, often in directions the founder did not intend. Recognize that culture is always being created, and choose to shape it rather than let it form by default.

### Define Values That Shape Decisions, Not Slogans

Values are useful only if they shape decisions and behavior, not if they are slogans on a wall. Define values that are specific enough to guide choices: how the team makes tradeoffs, how it treats customers and each other, what it rewards and what it will not tolerate. Vague aspirational values, "excellence", "integrity", do not guide decisions; concrete, behavior-defining values do.

### Model The Culture As The Founder

The founder's behavior sets the culture more than any statement. If the founder values transparency but acts secretively, the team learns secrecy. If the founder values quality but ships carelessly under pressure, the team learns that quality is rhetorical. Model the culture the venture needs, because the team will follow behavior, not words.

### Hire And Reward For The Culture

Culture is reinforced by who is hired, promoted, and rewarded, and eroded by who is tolerated despite violating norms. Hire for alignment with the intended culture, recognize and reward behaviors that embody it, and address behaviors that violate it, even when the violator is otherwise valuable. Tolerance of cultural violation signals that the values are not real.

### Align Culture With Strategy

Culture should support the venture's strategy and stage. A venture competing on speed needs a culture that rewards decisiveness and iteration; one competing on trust and compliance needs a culture that rewards rigor and care. Align culture with what the venture must do well, because a culture misaligned with strategy undermines execution.

### Establish Norms Early, Because They Stick

The norms established in the first hires become the default that later hires inherit. Establish the important norms early, how decisions are made, how conflict is handled, how customers are treated, how feedback is given, because changing a norm later is far harder than setting it well at the start. Early culture is disproportionately consequential.

### Keep Culture Authentic, Not Performative

Culture that is authentic, reflecting how the team genuinely works and what it genuinely believes, builds trust and cohesion. Culture that is performative, aspirational statements that do not match reality, breeds cynicism and erodes credibility. Be honest about the culture the venture actually has, and shape it from there, rather than claiming a culture that does not exist.

### Balance Culture With Needed Diversity

Strong culture can become conformity if it screens out different perspectives and backgrounds. Balance cultural alignment with the diversity of thought, experience, and perspective the venture needs to avoid groupthink and to serve a broad market. Culture should define how the team works together, not require everyone to think the same.

### Evolve Culture As The Venture Grows

Culture that fits a team of five may not fit a team of fifty. As the venture grows, some norms must be formalized, some must evolve, and some must be deliberately preserved. Evolve culture intentionally as the venture scales, rather than letting it drift or calcify.

### Acknowledge Personal Risk And Professional Advice

Culture and values shape employment practices with legal implications, including discrimination and labor law, that require professional advice. This skill does not replace that counsel.

## Common Traps

### Culture Equated With Perks

Free food and ping-pong tables are not culture; behavior and norms are.

### Slogan Values

Aspirational words that do not shape decisions are decoration.

### Founder Behavior Contradicting Stated Values

The team follows behavior, not words, learning the real culture.

### Tolerance Of Cultural Violation

Keeping a valuable violator signals that values are not real.

### Performative Culture

Claiming a culture that does not match reality breeds cynicism.

### Culture As Conformity

Strong culture that screens out diversity creates groupthink.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Culture is recognized as forming whether shaped or not, and shaped deliberately.
- [ ] Values are specific enough to shape decisions and behavior, not slogans.
- [ ] The founder models the culture through behavior, not only statements.
- [ ] Hiring, rewarding, and accountability reinforce the intended culture.
- [ ] Culture is aligned with the venture's strategy and stage.
- [ ] Key norms are established early, recognizing their stickiness.
- [ ] Culture is authentic, reflecting reality, not performative aspiration.
- [ ] Cultural alignment is balanced with needed diversity of perspective.
- [ ] Culture is evolved intentionally as the venture grows.
- [ ] Employment-practice and labor-law implications are flagged for professional legal advice.
