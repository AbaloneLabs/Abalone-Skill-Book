# Cap Table Management

The capitalization table is the definitive record of who owns the venture and in what proportions, and it grows more complex with every grant, round, convertible instrument, and advisor addition. A clean, accurate cap table is essential for fundraising, hiring, exits, and founder trust, yet cap tables are often neglected, maintained in spreadsheets that drift from reality, or cobbled together from incomplete records. Sound cap table management is the discipline of maintaining an accurate, current, and investor-ready record of ownership from the earliest grants onward.

Use this skill before answering broad questions such as "how do I manage my cap table", "what is a cap table", "how does dilution work", "what should my cap table look like for investors", or "how should an entrepreneur handle the cap table". The goal is to prevent the agent from treating the cap table as a minor administrative task and from letting it drift into a source of confusion and dispute.

## Core Rules

### Maintain An Accurate, Current Record From The Start

Begin the cap table at formation and keep it current with every grant, transfer, and round. An accurate record built from the start is far easier to maintain than one reconstructed later from incomplete records. Neglect compounds, because each undocumented change makes the next reconstruction harder.

### Track All Forms Of Ownership And Convertible Interstruments

The cap table must capture all forms of ownership and conversion: common stock, preferred stock, options, warrants, restricted stock, and convertible instruments such as SAFEs and convertible notes. Each carries different rights and dilution effects. Omitting convertible instruments understates future dilution and misleads founders and investors.

### Model Dilution From Future Rounds And The Option Pool

A cap table that shows only current ownership misleads, because future rounds and option pool top-ups will dilute founders. Model dilution under plausible scenarios, so founders understand how their ownership changes with fundraising. Seeing future dilution prevents surprises and supports fundraising decisions.

### Reconcile The Cap Table With Legal Documents Regularly

The cap table must match the underlying legal documents, board approvals, and stock ledgers. Regular reconciliation catches errors before they become disputes or due diligence problems. A cap table that does not match the legal record is a red flag for investors and a source of conflict.

### Keep It Clean And Investor-Ready

Investors will scrutinize the cap table, and a messy, incomplete, or disputed table delays or derails deals. Keep the table clean, with clear labels, consistent formatting, and supporting documentation, so it is ready to share at any time. Investor-readiness is a continuous discipline, not a pre-round scramble.

### Document Every Change With Board Approval

Every grant, transfer, and issuance should be documented with board approval and proper filings. Undocumented changes create disputes and can invalidate grants. Documentation is the legal backbone of an accurate cap table.

### Use Proper Tools Or Professional Services

Spreadsheets work for the earliest stages but become error-prone as complexity grows. Use dedicated cap table software or professional services as complexity increases, because spreadsheet errors create real ownership disputes. The cost of proper tools is small relative to the cost of a cap table error.

### Understand The Rights Attached To Each Security

Different securities carry different rights: liquidation preferences, anti-dilution, voting, and information rights. The cap table should track not only percentages but the rights attached to each class, because these rights determine how proceeds are distributed and how decisions are made. Percentages alone obscure the economic reality.

### Plan The Option Pool And Its Refresh

The option pool and its planned refresh affect founder dilution and investor negotiation. Understand how the pool is sized, where it sits on the cap table, pre- or post-money, and how refreshes are negotiated. Pool planning is a strategic cap table decision, not just an administrative one.

### Acknowledge Personal Risk And Professional Advice

Cap table management involves legal, tax, and ownership consequences. Use qualified legal counsel and, as complexity grows, professional cap table services. This skill provides strategic framing only and does not replace professional legal and financial advice.

## Common Traps

### Neglected Spreadsheets

Cap tables that drift from reality become sources of confusion and dispute.

### Omitting Convertible Instruments

SAFEs and notes understated in the table mislead about future dilution.

### Ignoring Future Dilution

Founders are surprised by ownership after rounds they did not model.

### Table-Document Mismatch

A cap table that does not match legal records is a due diligence red flag.

### Undocumented Changes

Grants without board approval create disputes and can invalidate grants.

### Spreadsheet Errors At Scale

Manual errors in complex tables create real ownership disputes.
