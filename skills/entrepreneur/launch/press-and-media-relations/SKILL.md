# Press And Media Relations

Press coverage can lend a young company credibility, reach, and legitimacy that paid marketing cannot buy, but it is one of the most misunderstood growth levers, because teams confuse coverage with results and chase press for its own sake rather than for the strategic value it can create. A press hit that produces a spike of traffic and nothing more is a vanity outcome; coverage that is timed to a strategic moment, targeted to the right outlets, and supported by a funnel that captures the attention produces durable value. The judgment problem is recognizing what is genuinely newsworthy, building real relationships with journalists rather than spamming pitches, and integrating press into a broader strategy rather than treating it as a standalone spike. Agents tend to fail by pitching non-stories, by mass-emailing journalists, and by failing to build the funnel that converts coverage into retained users.

Use this skill when planning or executing press and media relations for a startup. The goal is coverage that serves a strategic purpose and produces lasting credibility, not fleeting attention.

## Core Rules

### Recognize What Is Genuinely Newsworthy

Journalists cover news, not promotions, and the most common failure is pitching something that is not news. A product launch, a funding round, a major milestone, or a genuinely novel insight can be newsworthy; a feature update or a marketing campaign usually is not.

Assess newsworthiness by:

- asking whether the story is timely, novel, and relevant to the journalist's audience;
- distinguishing a real announcement from a marketing message dressed as news;
- finding the angle that makes the story interesting beyond the company itself;
- being honest about whether a journalist would cover this if they did not know the founder.

A pitch for a non-story wastes the journalist's time and damages the relationship. Pitch only what is genuinely news.

### Build Relationships With Journalists Before You Need Them

Press relationships are built over time, not transactionally. A founder who engages with a journalist's work, offers useful expertise, and builds rapport before needing coverage receives a hearing when the announcement comes. A founder who appears only with a pitch is ignored.

Build relationships by:

- identifying the journalists who cover the company's space and audience;
- engaging with their work thoughtfully, without asking for anything;
- offering expertise and access as a source, building a track record of usefulness;
- reaching out with the pitch only when there is real news, leveraging established trust.

Relationships are the currency of press. Invest in them before you need to spend them.

### Craft Pitches That Respect The Journalist's Time And Beat

A pitch is a first impression, and a bad one, generic, long, off-beat, or promotional, ends the conversation. A good pitch is concise, relevant to the journalist's beat, and clear about why the story matters now.

Craft pitches by:

- keeping the pitch concise, leading with the news and the angle;
- targeting the pitch to the specific journalist's beat and past coverage;
- explaining why the story matters to their readers, not just to the company;
- including the essentials, facts, data, assets, and access, without overwhelming.

A pitch that respects the journalist's time and relevance earns a reading. One that is generic or self-serving is deleted.

### Time Announcements To Strategic Moments

Press coverage has the most impact when timed to a strategic moment, such as a launch, a funding round, or a major milestone that the coverage amplifies. Coverage timed randomly, or timed to the company's convenience rather than to news value, underperforms.

Time strategically by:

- aligning coverage with moments when attention converts to action, such as a product launch;
- coordinating press with other channels, such as a Product Hunt launch or a funding announcement;
- avoiding periods when the news will be buried, such as major events or holidays;
- using embargoes to coordinate multiple outlets for maximum impact.

Press is a force multiplier, and it multiplies most when aligned with a moment that benefits from amplification.

### Prepare For Interviews And Manage The Message

An interview is not a casual conversation; it is a managed exchange in which the founder represents the company. Preparation ensures the message is clear, the pitfalls are avoided, and the coverage reflects the intended narrative.

Prepare by:

- defining the key messages and the supporting points in advance;
- anticipating difficult questions and preparing honest, bridging answers;
- using bridges to return to the key messages without evading;
- avoiding speculation, off-the-record slips, and unforced errors.

A prepared founder produces clear, quotable coverage. An unprepared one produces confusion or damaging quotes.

### Use Embargoes To Coordinate Coverage

An embargo, an agreement that coverage will be held until a specified time, allows a company to offer the story to multiple outlets simultaneously, producing coordinated coverage that amplifies impact. Embargoes must be used carefully, because breaking one damages trust.

Use embargoes by:

- offering the story to targeted outlets under embargo, with the news and assets in advance;
- setting a coordinated release time that maximizes impact;
- respecting the embargo scrupulously, because a break damages all relationships;
- giving exclusive angles to select outlets to deepen the coverage.

Coordinated embargoed coverage produces a wave of attention that isolated stories cannot. Use it for major announcements.

### Build The Funnel That Converts Coverage To Retained Users

Press coverage produces a spike of traffic, and without a funnel to capture and retain it, the spike fades and leaves nothing behind. The coverage is the beginning, not the outcome.

Build the funnel by:

- ensuring the landing page and onboarding are ready to convert press traffic;
- capturing emails from visitors who are not ready to sign up;
- having a reason for users to return, such as a sequence or ongoing value;
- measuring the conversion from press visit to retained user.

Coverage without a funnel is attention that evaporates. Design the capture and retention alongside the press strategy.

### Integrate Press Into A Broader Strategy

Press is most valuable when integrated with other channels and moments, not when treated as a standalone spike. A funding announcement amplified by press, reinforced by community, and supported by product momentum produces compounding effect.

Integrate by:

- sequencing press with product launches, community activation, and sales outreach;
- using coverage as credibility in sales, partnerships, and recruiting;
- repurposing coverage across channels to extend its life;
- treating press as one lever in a coordinated strategy, not the whole strategy.

Press amplifies a strategy; it is not a strategy. Integrate it to maximize its value.

## Common Traps

### Pitching Non-Stories

Journalists cover news, not promotions. Pitch only what is genuinely newsworthy.

### Mass-Emailing Journalists

Generic pitches damage relationships. Target pitches to specific journalists and beats.

### Transactional Relationships

Relationships built only when needed fail. Invest in them before you need coverage.

### Poorly Timed Coverage

Coverage timed randomly underperforms. Align it with strategic moments.

### Unprepared Interviews

Unprepared founders produce confusing or damaging coverage. Prepare messages and answers.

### Broken Embargoes

Breaking an embargo damages trust with all outlets. Respect them scrupulously.

### Coverage Without A Funnel

A traffic spike that fades leaves nothing. Build capture and retention alongside press.

### Press As A Standalone Strategy

Press amplifies a strategy; it is not one. Integrate it with other channels.

## Self-Check

- [ ] The story being pitched is genuinely newsworthy, timely, novel, and relevant to the journalist's audience.
- [ ] Relationships with relevant journalists are built before coverage is needed.
- [ ] Pitches are concise, targeted to the journalist's beat, and clear about why the story matters.
- [ ] Announcements are timed to strategic moments that amplify their impact.
- [ ] Founders are prepared for interviews, with key messages and anticipated difficult questions.
- [ ] Embargoes are used to coordinate coverage and are respected scrupulously.
- [ ] A funnel is built to convert press traffic into retained users, not just a spike.
- [ ] Press is integrated with product launches, community, and sales, not treated as standalone.
- [ ] Coverage is repurposed across channels to extend its life and credibility.
- [ ] The press strategy serves a strategic purpose, rather than chasing coverage for its own sake.
