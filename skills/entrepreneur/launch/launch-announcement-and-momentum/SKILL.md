# Launch Announcement And Momentum

A launch announcement is not the launch itself, and a spike of attention is not momentum. Many founders conflate announcing the product with having launched it, and confuse a brief burst of press, social buzz, or launch-day traffic with durable market presence. A well-designed launch announcement creates initial awareness and channels it into a sustained momentum engine: a sequence of coordinated moves across press, community, content, product, and outreach that turns a one-time spike into ongoing discovery, conversion, and word of mouth. The discipline is to plan the announcement as the start of a campaign, not as the finish line, and to build the mechanisms that capture and compound the attention it generates.

Use this skill before answering broad questions such as "how should I announce my launch", "how do I get press for my startup", "what should I do for launch day", "how do I create momentum after launch", or "how should an entrepreneur plan a launch announcement". The goal is to prevent the agent from treating the announcement as a one-shot event or from generating attention that has nowhere to go and no plan to sustain it.

## Core Rules

### Distinguish The Announcement From The Launch

The launch is the venture becoming ready to serve real customers; the announcement is the communication that tells the world. A venture can launch without a loud announcement, and an announcement without launch readiness wastes the attention it generates. Confirm operational readiness before driving a public spike, because traffic to a product that cannot deliver, onboard, or support converts poorly and damages reputation.

### Define The Announcement's Goal Beyond Awareness

Awareness is not a goal; it is a means. Define what the announcement should produce: signups, pilot customers, press credibility, investor interest, community formation, app downloads, or a specific number of qualified conversations. A concrete goal shapes the channels, messaging, and calls to action, and makes success measurable. An announcement without a defined conversion goal produces noise without outcomes.

### Build A Capture Mechanism Before Driving Traffic

Before generating attention, build the mechanism that captures it: a landing page with a clear call to action, an onboarding flow that converts, a way to follow up, and analytics that attribute signups to sources. Driving traffic to a leaky funnel wastes the spike. The capture mechanism is what turns a launch-day audience into a retained base; without it, the attention arrives and leaves.

### Sequence Channels Into A Coordinated Campaign

A strong launch sequences channels over days and weeks rather than firing everything at once: an exclusive or embargoed story to seed press, coordinated posts and community engagement, partner and customer amplification, content and SEO that compounds, and paid amplification of what performs. Sequencing lets each channel build on the last and sustains attention beyond a single day. A simultaneous blast produces a sharp spike that collapses within 48 hours.

### Craft A Narrative, Not Just A Feature List

Press, customers, and communities respond to stories, not feature inventories. Craft a narrative: the problem, why it matters now, why this team, what is different, and what the launch means for the audience. A clear, human, surprising, or contrarian narrative travels; a generic product description does not. Tailor the narrative to each audience, press, customers, and community, while keeping a consistent core.

### Prepare Assets And Spokespeople In Advance

Launch momentum dies waiting for unprepared materials. Prepare in advance: press kit, screenshots and demo, founder bios, FAQ, talking points, customer quotes, and ready spokespeople who can do interviews and answer questions. Slow or inconsistent responses during the spike squander the window when attention is highest. Readiness lets the team move at the speed of the news cycle.

### Engage Communities Authentically, Not Transactionally

Communities, forums, and niche audiences often drive more durable launch impact than broad press, but only when engaged authentically. Participate where the audience already gathers, contribute value, and share the launch in a way that respects community norms. Transactional drops, spammy posts, or treating communities as free distribution channels generate backlash. Genuine engagement earns word of mouth that outlasts the announcement.

### Plan The Post-Launch Momentum Engine

The announcement is the start, not the end. Plan what sustains momentum after the spike: a content cadence, community building, customer storytelling, product updates, ongoing outreach, and amplification of early wins. Most launches fail not because launch day went badly but because nothing followed. Design the weeks after launch as deliberately as the day of, because momentum is built by sustained effort, not a single event.

### Measure And Adapt Based On What Performs

Instrument the launch so each channel's contribution to signups, conversion, and retention is visible, then adapt in real time: amplify what works, drop what does not, and follow the audience's actual response rather than the planned script. Launches rarely go exactly as planned, and the teams that adapt to live signal outperform those that execute a fixed plan regardless of results.

### Manage Expectations About The Spike

Be honest that most launch announcements produce a brief spike, not a permanent step-change in traffic. The value of the spike is the initial audience it delivers to the capture and momentum mechanisms; the durable growth comes from what those mechanisms do with it. Founders who expect the spike to sustain itself without follow-on effort are disappointed and often misdiagnose a strong launch as a failure.

### Acknowledge Personal Risk And Professional Advice

Launch announcements can attract scrutiny, competitive attention, and sometimes legal or reputational risk in claims made publicly. Ensure claims are accurate and defensible, and where the launch involves regulated claims, trademarks, or significant public exposure, seek marketing, legal, and PR counsel. This skill provides framing only and does not replace professional communications or legal advice.

## Common Traps

### Announcing Before Launch Readiness

Driving traffic to a product that cannot deliver, onboard, or support wastes the spike and harms reputation.

### Awareness Without A Conversion Goal

Generating attention with no defined outcome produces noise without measurable business results.

### No Capture Mechanism

Driving traffic to a leaky funnel lets the launch audience arrive and leave without converting.

### Simultaneous Channel Blast

Firing all channels at once produces a sharp spike that collapses within days instead of sustained momentum.

### Feature List Instead Of Narrative

Press and communities share stories; product descriptions do not travel.

### Unprepared Assets And Spokespeople

Slow, inconsistent responses during the attention window squander the highest-value days.

### Transactional Community Engagement

Treating communities as free distribution generates backlash and destroys goodwill.

### Expecting The Spike To Sustain Itself

The announcement delivers an initial audience; durable growth requires a planned momentum engine, not a one-time event.

## Self-Check

- [ ] The announcement is distinguished from launch readiness, and operational readiness is confirmed before driving a public spike.
- [ ] The announcement has a concrete goal beyond awareness, with measurable conversion outcomes.
- [ ] A capture mechanism, landing page, onboarding, follow-up, and analytics, is built before traffic is driven.
- [ ] Channels are sequenced into a coordinated campaign that sustains attention beyond launch day.
- [ ] A narrative, not just a feature list, is crafted and tailored to each audience.
- [ ] Assets and spokespeople are prepared in advance to move at the speed of the news cycle.
- [ ] Communities are engaged authentically, respecting norms rather than treated as transactional distribution.
- [ ] A post-launch momentum engine is planned as deliberately as launch day.
- [ ] The launch is instrumented, and the plan adapts to real-time performance signal.
- [ ] Expectations about the spike are realistic, with durable growth attributed to the momentum engine.
- [ ] Public claims are accurate and defensible, with professional counsel engaged where exposure or regulation warrants.
