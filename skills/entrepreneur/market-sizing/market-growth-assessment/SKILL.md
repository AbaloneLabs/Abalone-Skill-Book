# Market Growth Assessment

Market size tells you how big the opportunity is today; market growth tells you whether it will still exist, expand, or collapse tomorrow. Many failed startups entered large markets that were already eroding, and many successful ones entered small markets that were about to expand rapidly. Growth assessment is the discipline of reading the direction, velocity, and durability of change in a market, and judging whether the timing favors a new entrant. The danger is reading a trend line as destiny rather than as a fragile, reversible signal.

Use this skill before answering broad questions such as "is this market growing", "is now the right time to enter", "how do I assess market trends for my startup", or "how should an entrepreneur judge market timing". The goal is to prevent the agent from endorsing a market because of a single upward chart or dismissing one because of a single downward year.

## Core Rules

### Distinguish Direction, Velocity, And Durability

A growing market is not a single fact; it has three dimensions that matter independently.

- direction: is the market expanding, flat, or contracting;
- velocity: how fast, and is that speed accelerating or decelerating;
- durability: is the driver structural and lasting, or cyclical and temporary.

A fast-growing market driven by a temporary subsidy can collapse when the subsidy ends. A slow-growing market driven by demographic shift may be far more durable.

### Identify The Underlying Driver Of Growth

Growth is a symptom; the cause is what you must understand. Name the force creating the change.

Common drivers:

- regulatory change opening or closing a market;
- technology cost curves crossing a threshold;
- demographic or workforce shifts;
- behavior change from a crisis or cultural shift;
- platform or ecosystem emergence;
- price drops in an enabling input;
- consolidation or fragmentation of an industry.

Assess whether the driver is strengthening, weakening, or exhausted.

### Separate Headline Growth From Segment Growth

An aggregate market can grow while the specific segment you target shrinks, and vice versa. Always map growth at the level of your actual segment, geography, and price tier.

A rising tide in the industry does not lift a segment that is being disrupted beneath it.

### Check Whether Growth Is Real Spend Or Activity

Some markets show exploding activity but flat or declining spend, because prices collapse faster than volume rises. Assess both volume and value, not only one.

A market with a million new free users and shrinking revenue per user may be growing in usage but contracting in monetizable opportunity.

### Assess Whether Growth Is Already Priced In

A visibly fast-growing market attracts capital and competitors. By the time growth is obvious, incumbents and well-funded entrants may already be capturing it, raising the cost of entry and lowering the share available to a newcomer.

Ask how many others have noticed, how fast they are moving, and what durable advantage you would have against them.

### Evaluate Timing Relative To Adoption Curve

Entering too early means educating a market that is not ready, burning runway on behavior change. Entering too late means fighting entrenched incumbents for scraps. Assess where the market sits on its adoption curve and whether the timing favors a new entrant.

Consider:

- is the enabling technology mature enough to deliver;
- is customer awareness and budget emerging;
- are distribution channels open to newcomers;
- is regulation settled enough to operate.

### Look For Counter-Signals And Reversals

Trends reverse. Look for early signs that growth is stalling: slowing repeat purchase, rising churn, declining average spend, competitor consolidation, regulatory backlash, or substitute adoption.

A market that grew for five years can plateau in the sixth. Plan for the possibility that the trend bends.

### Distinguish Structural Growth From Cyclical Bounce

Some growth is structural and will persist; some is a cyclical rebound that will revert. Confusing the two leads to over-investment at the peak. Examine whether the growth would survive a downturn.

### Acknowledge Timing Risk And Personal Risk

Market timing is one of the hardest entrepreneurial judgments, and being early is financially similar to being wrong. Founders must weigh the personal runway cost of waiting for a market to mature. This skill does not replace professional advice for markets with regulatory or financial sensitivity.

## Common Traps

### Extrapolating A Straight Line

Past growth rates rarely continue indefinitely; curves flatten, saturate, or reverse.

### Confusing Activity With Spend

Viral usage growth can mask a market that cannot be monetized at scale.

### Chasing Obvious Growth

By the time growth is headline news, the entry cost has usually risen and the available share has fallen.

### Ignoring Segment-Level Reality

Industry growth can hide segment shrinkage that dooms a focused entrant.

### Treating Temporary Drivers As Permanent

Subsidy, crisis, or fad-driven growth can vanish when the driver does.

### Entering Too Early Or Too Late

Both errors are costly; timing judgment must be explicit, not assumed.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Direction, velocity, and durability of growth are assessed as separate dimensions.
- [ ] The underlying driver of growth is named and judged as strengthening, weakening, or exhausted.
- [ ] Growth is evaluated at the segment, geography, and price-tier level, not only in aggregate.
- [ ] Both volume and spend are examined, since activity growth can hide value contraction.
- [ ] Whether growth is already priced in by competitors and capital is considered.
- [ ] Timing relative to the adoption curve is assessed, including technology, awareness, channel, and regulatory readiness.
- [ ] Counter-signals and possible reversals are actively sought, not ignored.
- [ ] Structural growth is distinguished from cyclical bounce.
- [ ] The personal runway cost of being early is acknowledged.
- [ ] The conclusion avoids endorsing timing based on a single trend chart without driver and durability analysis.
