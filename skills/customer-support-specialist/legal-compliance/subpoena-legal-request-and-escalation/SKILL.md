# Subpoena Legal Request And Escalation

Legal requests often arrive through ordinary support channels, but frontline support usually is not authorized to evaluate them, disclose data, or negotiate deadlines. A well-meaning agent can create legal, privacy, or safety risk by answering casually, forwarding data, or promising action. This skill helps the agent recognize legal requests, preserve the record, and route them without giving legal advice or unauthorized disclosures.

## Core Rules

### Recognize legal form and legal intent

Look for subpoenas, warrants, court orders, attorney letters, regulator inquiries, discovery demands, preservation requests, police requests, requests for user data, demands to remove content, or statements such as "my lawyer will contact you." Some requests are formal legal process; others are threats or customer complaints.

Do not decide validity from appearance. Route anything plausibly legal to the approved legal process.

### Do not disclose data or confirm account facts

Before legal review and proper verification, do not provide customer data, account existence, order history, IP logs, communications, identity details, employee information, internal notes, or retention details. Even confirming that data exists can be sensitive.

Use approved acknowledgement language and routing instructions rather than answering the substance.

### Preserve the request exactly

Save the original message, attachment, sender, channel, timestamp, jurisdiction indicators, requested deadline, affected account identifiers, and any documents. Do not edit or summarize away key details before legal sees them. Use restricted storage if documents contain personal data or confidential information.

The legal team needs the original material, not only the agent's interpretation.

### Avoid legal advice and interpretation

Support should not tell the requester whether a subpoena is valid, what law applies, whether the company will comply, whether a customer has rights, or what legal strategy to use. If a customer asks what they should do, direct them to appropriate legal resources or the company's approved process without advising.

Accuracy in legal matters requires qualified review.

### Follow preservation and retention paths

Some legal requests ask the company to preserve data or suspend deletion. Others may conflict with a customer's deletion request. Route promptly to legal or compliance so holds, retention, or privacy conflicts are handled correctly.

Do not manually delete, modify, or export data after receiving a possible preservation demand unless legal authorizes the action.

### Handle law enforcement and emergency requests carefully

Law enforcement, emergency disclosure, imminent harm, child safety, fraud, and abuse cases may have special intake paths and urgency. Escalate through legal, trust and safety, security, or emergency procedures as defined by policy.

Do not use normal customer support judgment to decide emergency disclosure.

### Keep customer communication narrow

If the requester is a customer threatening legal action, acknowledge the concern, provide the appropriate legal or complaint channel if approved, and continue normal support only where safe. Do not argue legal positions, admit liability, or promise settlement, refund, deletion, or data production.

Separate the support issue from the legal process.

### Document routing and restrictions

Record that legal request material was received, where it was routed, what customer-facing response was sent, what actions are paused, and any deadline or risk. Avoid copying legal documents or sensitive data into broad notes.

Future agents need to know not to proceed casually.

### Watch for multi-party confidentiality conflicts

Legal requests often involve someone other than the requester: another customer, an employee, a minor, a business account, a former spouse, a contractor, a law enforcement agency, or a regulator. Support should not notify, disclose to, or discuss the request with another party unless the approved legal process says to do so.

If a customer asks whether the company received legal process about them, route that question rather than confirming or denying casually. Notice, gag restrictions, preservation duties, and confidentiality rules may be in tension.

## Common Traps

- Treating an attorney letter or subpoena-like message as an ordinary customer complaint.
- Deciding that a legal request is invalid because it looks informal.
- Confirming account facts or data existence before legal review.
- Forwarding customer data, logs, communications, or internal notes directly to a requester.
- Summarizing a legal request so loosely that deadlines, jurisdiction, or scope are lost; giving advice about rights, compliance, subpoena validity, or legal strategy
- Deleting or exporting data after a preservation demand without legal instruction; handling law enforcement or emergency requests through normal support queues
- Arguing with a customer about legal threats; failing to document routing, restrictions, and paused actions
- Not recognizing that notifying another customer or account owner may itself be restricted; confirming or denying receipt of legal process without legal approval

## Self-Check

- Was the request checked for subpoena, warrant, court order, attorney letter, regulator inquiry, discovery, preservation, police, user data, removal demand, or legal threat signals?
- Was the request routed to the approved legal, compliance, law enforcement, trust and safety, or emergency process?
- Did the response avoid disclosing data, account existence, logs, communications, employee information, internal notes, and retention details?
- Was the original request preserved with sender, channel, timestamp, deadline, jurisdiction signal, account identifiers, and attachments?
- Are sensitive legal documents stored or referenced in restricted locations?
- Does the response avoid legal advice, validity assessment, rights interpretation, and legal strategy?
- Were preservation, deletion, retention, export, and privacy conflicts paused or routed before action?
- Are law enforcement, imminent harm, child safety, fraud, and abuse requests escalated through special paths where required?
- Is normal support separated from the legal process without admitting liability or promising settlement?; are routing, response, paused actions, deadlines, and restrictions documented for future agents?
- Were multi-party confidentiality conflicts checked before notifying, disclosing to, or discussing the request with anyone else?; if someone asks whether legal process exists, is the answer routed or limited to approved language?
