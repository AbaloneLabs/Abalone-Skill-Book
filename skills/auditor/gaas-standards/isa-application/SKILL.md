# ISA Application

The International Standards on Auditing are a coherent, principle-based framework, and that is both their strength and the reason they are applied superficially. Because the standards are structured and familiar, auditors often reduce them to a set of section references and checklists, confirming that a procedure was performed without confirming that it satisfied the underlying requirement in the specific circumstances. ISA compliance is not about citing the right paragraph; it is about whether the engagement actually achieved the standard's objective, whether the risk responses were genuinely proportionate to the assessed risks, and whether the documentation would let an experienced auditor with no prior involvement conclude that the work was sufficient. The recurring failure is treating the standards as a compliance overlay rather than the operating logic of the audit.

Use this skill when applying ISAs, planning or performing an ISA-compliant engagement, mapping work to specific ISA requirements, or evaluating whether completed work meets ISA standards. The goal is to apply the standards as operative requirements that shape the audit, not as references bolted on at the end.

## Core Rules

### Anchor Every Phase To The Relevant ISA Objective

Each ISA states an objective, and the requirements exist to achieve it. Work that does not advance the objective is not compliance, even if it cites the standard. Read the objective before performing or evaluating any phase.

Anchor work by:

- identifying the governing ISAs for each phase, such as ISA 200, 315, 330, 500, 700;
- reading the objective of each standard before designing procedures;
- confirming each procedure responds to a specific requirement and risk;
- avoiding rote procedures copied from templates without a risk linkage;
- testing whether the objective was achieved, not merely that the section was addressed.

A workpaper that says the requirement was met without showing how the objective was achieved does not satisfy the standard.

### Apply The ISA Risk Model Fully

The ISA framework is built on risk assessment driving response. ISA 315, 320, 330, and 700 form a chain in which assessed risks must drive materiality, planned procedures, and the opinion. A break in the chain invalidates the rest.

Apply the chain by:

- performing risk assessment under ISA 315 to identify and assess risks of material misstatement at the financial statement and assertion levels;
- setting materiality under ISA 320 in the context of those risks;
- designing and performing further audit procedures under ISA 330 that specifically respond to the assessed risks;
- evaluating misstatements and sufficiency of evidence before forming the opinion under ISA 700;
- documenting the link from each significant risk to the procedures that address it.

A risk identified at planning that has no corresponding procedure is a gap; a procedure with no linked risk is untargeted effort.

### Meet The Documentation Requirements Deliberately

ISA 230 sets documentation requirements that are stricter than many auditors assume. Documentation must enable an experienced auditor with no prior connection to understand the work performed, the evidence obtained, and the conclusions reached.

Ensure documentation includes:

- the nature, timing, and extent of procedures performed;
- the results and evidence obtained, including who performed and reviewed the work;
- significant findings and the professional judgments made;
- the identified risks and how each was addressed;
- exceptions or unusual matters and how they were resolved;
- the basis for significant judgments, such as materiality and going concern;
- completion sign-off and the date of the audit report.

Documentation prepared after the fieldwork, or that cannot be understood without oral explanation, fails the ISA 230 test.

### Address The High-Emphasis ISA Topics Specifically

Certain ISAs are high-emphasis because they address the areas where audits most often fail. Each requires explicit, topic-specific work, not a generic reference.

Address specifically:

- ISA 240 fraud, including the brainstorming session, fraud risk factors, and mandatory procedures;
- ISA 570 going concern, including management's assessment, the auditor's evaluation, and events or conditions indicating doubt;
- ISA 550 related parties, including identification, significant transactions, and disclosure;
- ISA 540 accounting estimates, including the point estimate, the range, and management bias;
- ISA 600 group audits, including the group engagement partner's responsibility and component work;
- ISA 560 subsequent events, both adjusting and non-adjusting, up to the report date;
- ISA 260 communication with those charged with governance.

Confirm that each topic has dedicated procedures proportionate to the assessed risk, not a passing mention.

### Satisfy The Evidence And Sufficiency Requirements

ISA 500 governs audit evidence, and ISA 330 requires the auditor to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence before forming an opinion. Sufficiency and appropriateness are judgments tied to risk.

Evaluate evidence by:

- confirming the relevance and reliability of each evidence type obtained;
- obtaining more persuasive evidence for higher-risk assertions;
- considering whether external and independent evidence supports significant balances;
- testing the completeness and accuracy of information produced by the entity when used;
- corroborating inquiry with other procedures, since inquiry alone is rarely sufficient;
- assessing whether the aggregate evidence supports each assertion at the chosen risk level.

If the evidence is insufficient for a material assertion, the response is more work or a modified opinion, not a conclusion of comfort.

### Handle Estimates And Fair Values With ISA 540 Rigor

Estimates and fair values are inherently judgmental and are a recurring source of misstatement. ISA 540 requires specific procedures beyond those for deterministic balances.

Apply ISA 540 by:

- understanding management's process for developing the estimate, including the method and assumptions;
- testing the data, assumptions, and model management used;
- developing a point estimate or range to compare to management's figure;
- assessing indicators of management bias across estimates and over time;
- evaluating the adequacy of disclosure of estimation uncertainty;
- considering the work of a management expert and testing it where relied upon.

An estimate accepted at management's figure without an independent range or assessment of bias fails ISA 540.

### Discharge Group Audit Responsibilities Under ISA 600

Group audits distribute work across components, and ISA 600 places responsibility on the group engagement partner for the opinion on the group financial statements. The work cannot be delegated in substance.

Discharge ISA 600 by:

- determining significant components and the work needed on each;
- setting materiality for the group and for components, including threshold materiality;
- deciding on the type of component work, full audit, specified procedures, or analytical review;
- understanding the component auditor's competence and independence where used;
- involving the component auditor and evaluating their findings;
- consolidating significant risks and ensuring they are addressed at group level;
- evaluating the sufficiency of evidence across the group before forming the opinion.

A group audit that relies on component work without evaluating its sufficiency does not meet ISA 600.

### Reconcile ISA With Local Requirements And Differences

Many jurisdictions adopt ISAs with modifications, carve-outs, or additional local requirements. Applying a generic ISA without checking local overlays produces non-compliant work.

Reconcile by:

- confirming the version of the ISAs effective in the jurisdiction at the report date;
- identifying local additions, such as mandatory key audit matters or specific reporting formats;
- checking for differences where the entity reports under more than one framework;
- documenting any local requirement that goes beyond the baseline ISA;
- ensuring the audit report conforms to the jurisdictional template, not the generic ISA model.

A clean ISA application that ignores a local overlay is still a compliance failure.

### Form The Opinion And Report Under ISA 700 And 705

The opinion is the output of the entire framework, and ISA 700, 705, and 706 govern its form and content. The opinion must follow the evidence, not precede it.

Confirm the report:

- states the opinion clearly, whether unmodified, qualified, adverse, or disclaimed;
- identifies the applicable framework and confirms it is acceptable;
- includes the basis for opinion and, where required, key audit matters;
- adds an emphasis of matter or other-matter paragraph only where ISA 706 criteria are met;
- is signed, dated, and addresses the appropriate addressees;
- reflects any modification consistently with the evidence and conclusions documented.

An opinion that is not supported by the documented evidence, or that omits a required modification, is the most serious ISA failure.

## Common Traps

### Citing The Standard Without Achieving Its Objective

Naming the ISA in a workpaper is not compliance. The work must actually achieve the standard's objective in the specific circumstances.

### Breaking The Risk-Response Chain

Risk assessment under ISA 315 that does not drive procedures under ISA 330 leaves the audit unresponsive to the risks it identified, invalidating the opinion logic.

### Treating Documentation As An Afterthought

Notes written after the fieldwork, or that require oral explanation to understand, fail ISA 230. Documentation must be contemporaneous and self-explanatory.

### Generic Treatment Of High-Emphasis Topics

Fraud, going concern, related parties, and estimates need dedicated, risk-specific procedures. A passing reference does not satisfy ISA 240, 570, 550, or 540.

### Accepting Inquiry As Sufficient Evidence

Inquiry alone rarely provides sufficient appropriate evidence under ISA 500. It must be corroborated by other procedures for material assertions.

### Delegating Group Responsibility Without Evaluation

Relying on component auditors under ISA 600 without evaluating their competence, independence, and findings leaves the group opinion unsupported.

### Ignoring Local ISA Modifications

Applying baseline ISAs without checking jurisdictional additions or report-format requirements produces non-compliant work even when the substance is sound.

### Forming The Opinion Before The Evidence Supports It

The opinion under ISA 700 and 705 must follow the evidence. Drafting an unmodified opinion while material work is incomplete is a fundamental failure.

## Self-Check

- Is each audit phase anchored to the objective of its governing ISA, with work that demonstrably advances that objective?
- Does the risk-response chain run intact from ISA 315 risk assessment through ISA 320 materiality and ISA 330 procedures to the ISA 700 opinion?
- Does the documentation meet ISA 230, enabling an experienced auditor with no prior involvement to understand the work, evidence, and conclusions?
- Are high-emphasis topics, fraud, going concern, related parties, estimates, group audits, and subsequent events, addressed with dedicated risk-specific procedures?
- Is the audit evidence sufficient and appropriate for each material assertion, with inquiry corroborated rather than relied on alone?
- Are estimates and fair values tested under ISA 540 with an independent point estimate or range and an assessment of management bias?
- Are group audit responsibilities under ISA 600 discharged, with significant components identified and component work evaluated for sufficiency?
- Has the applicable ISA version and any local modification or reporting overlay been confirmed for the jurisdiction and report date?
- Is the audit report formed under ISA 700, 705, and 706 consistent with the documented evidence, with modifications where required?
- Could an independent reviewer conclude from the file that each ISA requirement was met in substance, not merely cited?
