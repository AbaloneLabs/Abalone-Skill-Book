# PCAOB Standards For Issuers

Audits of issuers, public companies registered with the SEC, are governed by the standards of the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB), alongside SEC requirements. PCAOB standards impose requirements that go beyond general GAAS, particularly in areas such as internal control over financial reporting, audit committee communications, and reporting. The recurring failure is applying general GAAS, or AICPA standards, to an issuer audit without recognizing where PCAOB standards impose additional or different requirements, or treating PCAOB compliance as a matter of form without understanding the substantive obligations. PCAOB standards, backed by inspection and enforcement, demand a higher level of rigor and documentation in specific areas, and the auditor must apply the correct framework and meet its specific requirements.

Use this skill when applying PCAOB standards to an issuer audit, when determining which standards govern specific areas, and when navigating the relationship between PCAOB standards and SEC requirements. The goal is an audit that applies the correct PCAOB standards and meets their specific requirements, alongside applicable SEC requirements.

## Core Rules

### Confirm The Engagement Is An Issuer Audit Governed By PCAOB Standards

The first step is to confirm that the engagement is an audit of an issuer, which triggers PCAOB standards. Issuers are entities registered with the SEC under Section 12 or that have filed a registration statement. Confirming the status determines the applicable framework.

Confirm by:

- determining whether the entity is an issuer registered with the SEC;
- identifying whether the engagement is an integrated audit of both financial statements and internal control, or a financial statement audit only;
- recognizing that broker-dealers have specific PCAOB requirements that differ from operating issuers;
- documenting the basis for applying PCAOB standards.

Applying the wrong framework, such as AICPA standards to an issuer audit, can result in significant compliance failures.

### Apply The Integrated Audit Framework Where Required

For most issuers, the audit is an integrated audit of both the financial statements and internal control over financial reporting (ICFR), under PCAOB AS 2201. The integrated audit imposes specific requirements, including separate opinions on the financial statements and on ICFR.

Apply by:

- planning and performing the financial statement audit and the ICFR audit in an integrated manner, leveraging work across both;
- issuing separate opinions on the financial statements and on ICFR, as required;
- applying the specific AS 2201 requirements for ICFR, including risk assessment, control testing, and evaluation of deficiencies;
- coordinating the two audits to avoid redundant work while meeting both sets of requirements.

Treating the ICFR audit as an add-on, rather than an integrated component, misses efficiency and risks gaps.

### Navigate The Relationship With SEC Requirements

PCAOB standards do not operate in isolation. SEC requirements, including Regulation S-X and specific rules, impose additional obligations on issuer audits. The auditor must navigate both frameworks.

Navigate by:

- identifying the SEC requirements that apply to the engagement, including those in Regulation S-X;
- applying PCAOB standards for the conduct of the audit and SEC requirements for reporting and disclosure;
- recognizing where SEC requirements impose specific obligations, such as reporting on consistency and on supplementary information;
- coordinating the two frameworks to ensure both are met.

Overlooking SEC requirements, while applying PCAOB standards, leaves the audit non-compliant with the issuer's regulatory framework.

### Apply PCAOB-Specific Communication Requirements

PCAOB standards impose specific communication requirements with audit committees, beyond general GAAS. These include communications about the audit's scope, significant findings, and the auditor's responsibilities.

Apply by:

- communicating, at or near the beginning of the audit, the scope and timing of the audit and significant risks;
- communicating significant findings, including significant deficiencies and material weaknesses in internal control;
- communicating matters required by PCAOB standards, such as accounting policies, estimates, and significant unusual transactions;
- documenting the communications and the audit committee's response.

Generic communications, without the PCAOB-specific content, fail the standard's requirements.

### Apply PCAOB Reporting Requirements

PCAOB reporting requirements differ from general GAAS in specific respects, including the form of the auditor's report, the opinion on ICFR, and the communication of critical audit matters (CAMs) for certain engagements.

Apply by:

- using the PCAOB-prescribed form of the auditor's report, including the opinion on the financial statements and, where applicable, on ICFR;
- communicating critical audit matters, where required, that meet the CAM criteria;
- applying the specific PCAOB requirements for emphasis-of-matter, consistency, and other reporting matters;
- ensuring the report meets PCAOB form and content requirements, not merely general GAAS.

Using a general GAAS report for an issuer audit, or omitting required PCAOB elements, is a reporting failure.

### Meet PCAOB Documentation Requirements

PCAOB standards, reinforced by inspection, impose rigorous documentation requirements. Documentation must be sufficient to support the auditor's conclusions and to demonstrate compliance with the standards.

Meet by:

- documenting the audit work performed, the evidence obtained, and the conclusions reached, at a level that supports the report;
- completing the documentation within the required timeframe, typically 45 days after the report;
- ensuring documentation reflects the specific PCAOB requirements, including for ICFR, fraud, and communications;
- preparing documentation that can withstand PCAOB inspection, with clear support for significant judgments.

Documentation that is incomplete, late, or that does not support significant judgments will not withstand PCAOB inspection.

### Prepare For PCAOB Inspection

PCAOB-registered firms are subject to inspection, which reviews engagements for compliance with standards and for audit quality. The auditor must perform and document the audit with inspection in mind.

Prepare by:

- performing and documenting the audit to meet PCAOB standards, not merely the firm's internal standards;
- ensuring significant judgments, especially in areas of PCAOB focus such as revenue, estimates, and ICFR, are well-supported;
- maintaining documentation that is complete, timely, and reviewable by an independent inspector;
- addressing prior inspection findings in the firm's methodology and in current engagements.

An audit performed without inspection in mind may meet internal standards but fail PCAOB review.

## Common Traps

### Applying General GAAS Or AICPA Standards To An Issuer Audit

Issuer audits require PCAOB standards. Applying general GAAS leaves significant requirements unmet.

### Treating The ICFR Audit As An Add-On

The ICFR audit is integrated with the financial statement audit. Treating it as an add-on misses efficiency and risks gaps.

### Overlooking SEC Requirements

SEC requirements operate alongside PCAOB standards. Overlooking them leaves the audit non-compliant.

### Using Generic Communications Without PCAOB-Specific Content

PCAOB communication requirements are specific. Generic communications fail them.

### Using A General GAAS Report Form

PCAOB report form and content requirements are specific. A general GAAS report fails them.

### Incomplete Or Late Documentation

PCAOB documentation requirements are rigorous and time-bound. Incomplete or late documentation will not withstand inspection.

### Performing Without Inspection In Mind

PCAOB inspection reviews engagements for compliance. Performing without inspection in mind risks failure.

## Self-Check

- Is it confirmed that the engagement is an issuer audit governed by PCAOB standards, with the basis documented?
- Where required, is the integrated audit framework applied, with separate opinions on financial statements and ICFR?
- Is the relationship with SEC requirements navigated, with both PCAOB and SEC obligations met?
- Are PCAOB-specific communication requirements with the audit committee applied, including scope, timing, and significant findings?
- Are PCAOB reporting requirements applied, including the prescribed report form, ICFR opinion, and CAMs where required?
- Are documentation requirements met, with complete, timely, and inspection-ready support for significant judgments?
- Is the audit performed and documented with PCAOB inspection in mind, particularly in areas of focus?
- Could an independent reviewer confirm that PCAOB standards are applied correctly and that the audit would withstand inspection?
- Is the application free of applying general GAAS, treating ICFR as an add-on, or overlooking SEC requirements?
- Are communications, reporting, and documentation PCAOB-compliant, not merely general GAAS-compliant?
