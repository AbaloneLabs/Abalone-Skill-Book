# Management Override Risk

Management override of controls is the risk that the people who design and oversee controls can also bypass them. It is present on every audit, because management's authority is precisely what allows them to instruct, pressure, or circumvent the staff who operate controls. This is why override cannot be addressed by relying on the controls themselves. Agents frequently test controls, find them operating, and conclude override is covered, when in fact override bypasses those controls by definition. The response to override must be specific: journal entry testing, estimate bias review, and scrutiny of unusual transactions. Without these, the audit has a hole exactly where the most damaging fraud tends to occur.

Use this skill when designing the response to management override, when testing journal entries, when reviewing estimates for bias, and when examining significant unusual transactions. The goal is override-specific procedures that do not rely on the controls being overridden.

## Core Rules

### Treat Override As A Pervasive Risk On Every Audit

Management override is not a risk that arises only when controls are weak. It arises because management can direct the staff who operate controls, reconfigure automated controls, or post entries outside the normal process. Treat it as present on every engagement.

Implications of pervasiveness:

- override cannot be eliminated by stronger controls, because management controls the controls;
- override testing is mandatory regardless of the control risk assessment;
- override can affect any account, but most commonly revenue, expenses, estimates, and reserves;
- override risk rises with earnings pressure, covenant pressure, and management compensation tied to results.

Document the override assessment on every engagement, and design the override response independently of the controls-reliance conclusion.

### Test Journal Entries With Override In Mind

Journal entry testing is the primary procedure for detecting override, because override is often effected through manual or top-side entries that bypass the normal approval process. The testing must be designed to find override, not to confirm that entries were posted correctly.

Design journal entry testing to address:

- completeness of the population, including all entry types, sources, and approval paths;
- the risk that entries are posted outside normal business hours, near period end, or by senior personnel;
- entries with round numbers, unusual account combinations, or vague descriptions;
- entries to revenue, reserves, estimates, or accounts management has incentive to manipulate;
- top-side entries posted at consolidated level without subsidiary support;
- entries that reverse shortly after period end;
- entries by users who do not normally post, or with elevated privileges.

Determine the population completely, then select entries using risk criteria and an element of unpredictability. Document the selection criteria and the entries examined.

### Review Accounting Estimates For Bias And Override

Estimates are a common override pathway because management can influence them within a wide range while appearing to follow a process. Override of estimates is harder to detect than override of entries because the manipulation is embedded in assumptions.

For each significant estimate, assess:

- whether assumptions are consistent with external evidence and market data;
- whether there is a pattern of bias, such as estimates consistently favoring earnings;
- the look-back at prior-period estimates, comparing prior estimates to actual outcomes for systematic bias;
- whether the estimate changed near period end without a clear trigger;
- whether the estimate sits at an aggressive end of a reasonable range;
- whether management's process is documented and followed, or adjusted after the fact;
- the sensitivity of the estimate to assumption changes.

Where bias is indicated, develop an independent range or use a specialist, and challenge management's point estimate.

### Examine Significant Unusual Transactions For Substance Over Form

Override is often effected through transactions that are structured to achieve an accounting result while differing in substance. These transactions may be real but designed to manipulate.

For significant unusual transactions, examine:

- the business rationale, and whether it makes economic sense apart from the accounting effect;
- whether the substance differs from the form, such as a sale that is effectively financing;
- whether the transaction is with a related party or an entity with opaque ownership;
- whether the timing, near period end, suggests a motivation to affect results;
- whether the accounting treatment reflects the substance or only the form;
- whether side agreements, commitments, or contingencies exist that change the economics;
- whether the transaction reverses or unwinds shortly after period end.

Document the examination and the conclusion on substance. Transactions that lack a coherent business rationale warrant heightened skepticism and possible escalation.

### Ensure Completeness Of The Journal Entry Population

Override testing fails silently if the population is incomplete. An entry posted through an untested path or an unextracted system will not appear in the testing and will not be caught.

Verify population completeness by:

- obtaining the full journal entry extract from the general ledger, including all entry types and sources;
- reconciling the extract to the trial balance and to the general ledger control totals;
- including manual, system, top-side, consolidating, and adjusting entries;
- confirming the extract covers the full period and the post-period-end adjustment window;
- identifying and testing any separate or offline entry paths, such as spreadsheet postings or subsidiary-to-parent entries;
- obtaining IT confirmation that no alternative posting paths exist.

If completeness cannot be established, document the limitation and consider its effect on the override conclusion.

### Incorporate Unpredictability Into Override Procedures

Management who understands the audit procedures can conceal override to avoid detection. Override responses should include an element of unpredictability.

Ways to introduce unpredictability:

- vary the selection criteria for journal entries from prior years;
- test entries from periods or accounts not previously examined;
- perform procedures at unexpected times or locations;
- select entries using criteria management cannot easily predict;
- include surprise procedures such as unannounced bank confirmations or vendor verifications;
- test populations management may not expect to be tested.

Document the unpredictable element so the response is clearly differentiated from routine testing.

### Evaluate The Control Environment For Override Conditions

While override cannot be addressed by relying on controls, the control environment signals whether override is more or less likely. A weak environment raises override risk and warrants more extensive override procedures.

Assess:

- whether tone at the top supports integrity or pressures results;
- whether there is a dominant leader who can direct accounting without challenge;
- whether internal audit or compliance functions are independent and active;
- whether prior-period adjustments, restatements, or disputes suggest override has occurred;
- whether governance, including the audit committee, provides effective oversight;
- whether there is high turnover in finance or internal oversight roles.

Use the environment assessment to calibrate the depth and extent of override procedures.

## Common Traps

### Concluding Override Is Covered Because Controls Operate

Override bypasses controls by definition. Testing controls and finding them operating does not address override; it addresses ordinary control risk. Override needs its own procedures.

### Incomplete Journal Entry Population

If the entry extract excludes top-side, consolidating, or offline entries, override testing misses the most likely override pathway. Completeness of the population is the foundation.

### Testing Entries For Accuracy Instead Of Override

Confirming that entries were posted correctly is not override testing. Override testing looks for entries that are suspicious by source, timing, amount, or description.

### Ignoring Estimate Bias

Estimates are a primary override pathway. Reviewing the estimate process without looking back for bias or challenging aggressive assumptions misses override embedded in assumptions.

### Accepting The Form Of Unusual Transactions Without Examining Substance

A transaction may be real and documented but structured to manipulate. Examining form without substance allows override through designed transactions to pass undetected.

### Predictable Procedures Management Can Anticipate

If the override procedures are identical each year, management can conceal override to avoid them. Unpredictability is a deliberate part of the design.

### Dismissing Override Risk Because Management Appears Cooperative

Cooperative demeanor is not evidence of integrity. Override risk is present regardless of demeanor and must be addressed with procedures, not impressions.

## Self-Check

- Is management override treated as a pervasive risk on every audit, addressed with procedures independent of the controls-reliance conclusion?
- Is journal entry testing designed to find override, with risk-based selection of suspicious entries by source, timing, amount, description, and user?
- Is the journal entry population complete, reconciled to the ledger, and confirmed to include all entry types including top-side and consolidating entries?
- Are significant estimates reviewed for bias, including look-back at prior estimates, consistency with external evidence, and sensitivity to assumptions?
- Are significant unusual transactions examined for substance over form, business rationale, related-party involvement, and period-end timing?
- Does the override response include an element of unpredictability so management cannot easily anticipate the testing?
- Is the control environment assessed for override conditions, with the depth of override procedures calibrated to the environment?
- Are convenient or self-serving explanations corroborated rather than accepted, with skepticism elevated throughout override testing?
