# PCAOB Requirements

The PCAOB auditing standards govern the audits of issuers (public companies) and broker-dealers, and they layer obligations on top of the AICPA foundation: specific independence and tax-services rules, engagement quality review, audit committee pre-approval, Form AP transparency reporting, and the communication of critical audit matters. Agents frequently treat a public-company audit as a private-company audit with a different opinion paragraph, which misses the PCAOB-specific obligations that are independently enforced through inspections. The harm is an inspection finding, a deficient audit report, or an independence breach that traces to a rule the agent never applied.

Use this skill when auditing an issuer under PCAOB standards, when evaluating independence and permitted services, when structuring engagement quality review and pre-approval, when preparing Form AP, and when identifying and communicating CAMs and going concern. The goal is an audit that satisfies each applicable AS and produces a report that meets the PCAOB's transparency and communication requirements.

## Core Rules

### Apply The Relevant AS Throughout The Engagement

PCAOB auditing standards (the AS series) are organized by topic and must be applied throughout the engagement, from planning through reporting. Agents must identify which AS provisions apply to the engagement's risks and assertions, and apply them specifically rather than treating them as background. Inspection findings frequently trace to an AS requirement that was never performed.

Apply the AS framework:

- planning and supervision (risk assessment, materiality, the audit team's competence);
- the auditor's reports on the financial statements and on ICFR, including the form of opinion;
- the standards on internal control relevant to an integrated audit;
- substantive procedures, including the response to assessed risks;
- the standards on using the work of others, specialists, and internal audit;
- the standards on related parties, subsequent events, and going concern;
- reporting and communication requirements, including CAMs and critical findings.

### Maintain Independence Under The PCAOB Rules

PCAOB independence rules are stricter than the general AICPA framework and include specific prohibitions and conditions on non-audit services, particularly tax services, and on certain relationships. An independence breach can disqualify the firm and the audit. Agents must evaluate independence before accepting and throughout the engagement, and document the analysis.

Confirm independence:

- no prohibited non-audit services are provided to the issuer or its affiliates;
- tax services comply with the PCAOB conditions (pre-approval, no aggressive tax positions, no contingent fees);
- no contingent fee arrangements exist;
- no prohibited employment or loan relationships with the issuer;
- the firm and covered persons have no disqualifying financial interests;
- audit committee pre-approval was obtained for permitted services within the de minimis and policy limits;
- changes in relationships during the engagement are evaluated for independence effect.

### Obtain Audit Committee Pre-Approval Properly

All audit and permitted non-audit services must be pre-approved by the audit committee (or its delegate under specified conditions), and the pre-approval must be substantive, not a rubber stamp. Agents must confirm that pre-approval was obtained before the service began and that the process met the PCAOB conditions.

Confirm pre-approval:

- the audit committee (or delegate) pre-approved each service before it began;
- the pre-approval was specific as to the particular service, not a blanket approval;
- any delegation to the chair followed the required procedures and full-committee reporting;
- non-audit services fall within the permitted categories and the de minimis exception conditions;
- the policies and procedures for pre-approval are documented and followed;
- the engagement letter and any non-audit engagement letters are consistent with what was pre-approved.

### Perform Engagement Quality Review

For audits of issuers, an engagement quality review (EQR) is required, and the engagement quality reviewer must be objective, competent, and involved at the key judgment points. The EQR is not a final read-through; it is a substantive review of significant judgments and conclusions. Agents must structure the EQR to add real assurance, not just a signature.

Structure the EQR to:

- confirm the reviewer's objectivity and competence, and absence of involvement in the engagement;
- involve the reviewer at key points (risk assessment, significant judgments, the proposed opinion);
- ensure the reviewer evaluates the significant judgments and the sufficiency of evidence;
- document the reviewer's evaluations, discussions, and conclusions;
- confirm the reviewer's approval or disapproval of the issuance of the report;
- address disagreements between the engagement team and the reviewer before reporting.

### Handle Related Party Transactions And Significant Unusual Transactions

PCAOB standards require specific procedures over related party transactions and significant unusual transactions, which are areas of elevated fraud and misstatement risk. Agents must perform the required procedures rather than relying on management's representation of completeness. These transactions are a recurring inspection focus because they are where concealment occurs.

Perform procedures to:

- obtain an understanding of the entity's processes for identifying, approving, and accounting for related party transactions;
- test the completeness of identified related party relationships and transactions;
- evaluate the business rationale of significant unusual transactions for indications of fraud;
- assess whether related party and unusual transactions are properly disclosed;
- consider whether transactions indicate undisclosed relationships or risks;
- evaluate the accounting for significant unusual transactions against the applicable framework.

### Identify And Communicate Critical Audit Matters

Critical audit matters (CAMs) are matters arising from the audit that were communicated to the audit committee, that relate to accounts or disclosures that are material, and that involved especially challenging, subjective, or complex auditor judgment. CAMs must be communicated in the auditor's report with specific content. Agents must apply the CAM criteria deliberately, because over- or under-inclusion both create reporting deficiencies.

For each potential CAM, evaluate and document:

- whether the matter was communicated to the audit committee;
- whether it relates to accounts or disclosures that are material to the financial statements;
- whether it involved especially challenging, subjective, or complex auditor judgment;
- the principal considerations that led to the CAM determination;
- how the CAM was addressed in the audit;
- the specific language to be included in the auditor's report;
- consistency with how the matter is described in the financial statements and MD&A.

### Prepare Form AP Accurately

Form AP discloses, for each issuer audit report, the engagement partner and other accounting firms and individuals who participated, and whether the firm took responsibility for the work of an unregistered firm. Form AP is a transparency requirement, and errors or omissions are filing deficiencies. Agents must prepare it from accurate engagement records.

Confirm Form AP:

- identifies the correct engagement partner;
- discloses other accounting firms and individuals who participated, with the extent of their work;
- states whether the firm took responsibility for the work of an unregistered firm and the basis;
- is filed within the required timeframe after the auditor's report;
- is consistent with the engagement records and the report;
- is prepared for each report subject to the requirement.

### Evaluate Going Concern Under The Applicable Standard

The auditor must evaluate whether there is substantial doubt about the entity's ability to continue as a going concern for a reasonable period, and report in accordance with the applicable standard. Agents must perform the evaluation based on the conditions and management's plans, and report the conclusion correctly. The going concern evaluation is a frequent area of inspection focus.

Perform and report going concern:

- evaluate conditions and events that raise substantial doubt;
- consider management's plans to mitigate and whether they are feasible;
- conclude whether substantial doubt exists after considering management's plans;
- report the conclusion in the appropriate form (substantial doubt remains, or substantial doubt alleviated);
- ensure consistency with the financial statement disclosures on going concern;
- consider the interaction with subsequent events and the period of evaluation.

### Communicate With The Audit Committee As Required

PCAOB standards require specific communications to the audit committee, including the overall audit strategy, significant risks, significant accounting policies, the form and content of the auditor's report, and critical matters. Agents must make these communications substantively and document them, not merely check that a meeting occurred.

Communicate to the audit committee:

- the overall audit strategy, scope, and timing;
- significant risks identified and the planned response;
- significant accounting policies and practices, and any significant changes;
- the basis for conclusions on significant judgments and estimates;
- the form and content of the auditor's report, including any CAMs;
- disagreements with management, fraud, and illegal acts identified;
- the auditor's evaluation of going concern and consistency with disclosures.

## Common Traps

### Treating PCAOB As AICPA Plus An Opinion Paragraph

The AS series imposes obligations beyond the AICPA foundation. Assuming the underlying audit satisfies PCAOB because the report language is changed leads to missed requirements and inspection findings.

### Independence Breach Through Tax Services

Tax services are permitted only under strict conditions. Providing a prohibited tax service, or one without proper pre-approval, impairs independence.

### Rubber-Stamp Pre-Approval

Pre-approval that is blanket, retroactive, or delegated improperly does not satisfy the PCAOB conditions and taints the services.

### EQR As A Final Read-Through

An engagement quality reviewer who is not involved at key judgment points, or who merely signs at the end, provides no real assurance and is an inspection target.

### Over- Or Under-Including CAMs

Failing to apply the CAM criteria, or including boilerplate CAMs to avoid judgment, produces a report that does not meet the transparency purpose.

### Form AP Errors And Late Filing

Misidentifying the engagement partner, omitting participating firms, or filing late are transparency failures that are easily detected in inspection.

### Going Concern Reported Inconsistently With Disclosures

A going concern conclusion in the auditor's report that does not match the financial statement disclosures creates confusion and a reporting deficiency.

### Audit Committee Communications As A Checklist and ignoring The Inspection Cycle

Going through required communications without substantive content, or without documentation, fails the communication standard and is an inspection focus.

PCAOB inspections focus on recurring deficiency areas (revenue, estimates, ICFR, CAMs, going concern). Designing the audit without awareness of inspection focus areas invites findings that could have been anticipated.

## Self-Check

- Are the applicable PCAOB AS provisions identified and applied throughout the engagement, from planning through reporting?
- Has independence been evaluated against the PCAOB rules, including the tax-services conditions, contingent fees, and prohibited relationships?
- Was audit committee pre-approval obtained for all audit and permitted non-audit services, specific to the service, before it began?
- Is the engagement quality reviewer objective, competent, involved at key judgment points, and documented?
- Has each potential CAM been evaluated against the criteria, with principal considerations, audit response, and report language documented?
- Is Form AP accurate as to the engagement partner and participating firms, and filed within the required timeframe?
- Has going concern been evaluated based on conditions and management's plans, and reported consistently with the financial statement disclosures?
- Are the required audit committee communications made substantively and documented, including strategy, risks, significant policies, and the report form?
- Are disagreements with management, fraud, and illegal acts communicated to the audit committee and documented?
- Is the auditor's report consistent across the opinion, CAMs, going concern, and the underlying financial statement disclosures?
