# Skepticism Documentation

Professional skepticism applied only in the auditor's mind cannot be reviewed, challenged, or relied upon. Documentation is what makes skepticism visible. A workpaper that records a conclusion without showing the questioning, alternatives, contradictory indicators, and reasoning that preceded it does not evidence skepticism, regardless of how much skepticism was actually applied. The documentation must allow a reviewer who was not present to see that the auditor questioned, considered alternatives, pursued contradictions, and reached the conclusion through a supported process rather than by accepting the first plausible answer. Skepticism documentation is not about adding volume; it is about capturing the judgment path so that the conclusion is defensible and the skepticism is demonstrable.

Use this skill when documenting skeptical judgments, recording the resolution of contradictory indicators, capturing consultations and reasoning, or reviewing whether workpapers evidence skepticism. The goal is to ensure the file demonstrates the skeptical process, not merely the result.

## Core Rules

### Document The Process, Not Only The Conclusion

The conclusion is the endpoint; skepticism lives in the process that reached it. A workpaper that states the conclusion and cites the supporting evidence, without showing the questioning and alternatives considered, could have been produced with or without skepticism. The documentation must capture how the auditor got there.

Capture the process by recording:

- the initial question or risk that prompted the procedure;
- the explanations or representations considered, including management's;
- the alternative conclusions evaluated and why they were rejected;
- the contradictory indicators identified and how each was resolved;
- the additional evidence sought in response to the indicators;
- the reasoning that links the evidence to the final conclusion.

A reviewer reading the workpaper should be able to reconstruct the auditor's thinking, not merely verify that the conclusion matches the cited evidence.

### Record Contradictory Indicators And Their Resolution

Contradictory indicators are among the most important products of skeptical audit work, and their resolution is where skepticism is most visible. Failing to document them leaves the file appearing as if no contradictions arose, which is rarely true and which provides no evidence that they were pursued.

For each contradictory indicator, document:

- what the indicator was, specifically, with reference to the evidence or circumstance;
- when and how it was identified;
- what the auditor did in response, including additional procedures or inquiry;
- what explanation or evidence resolved the indicator, or why it remained unresolved;
- the conclusion reached about the indicator and its effect on the overall conclusion.

An indicator dismissed as immaterial or a one-off without documented reasoning is not resolution. The file must show why the indicator was pursued, set aside, or escalated.

### Capture The Basis For Accepting Or Rejecting Explanations

Management offers explanations throughout an audit. The auditor accepts some and rejects others, and the basis for each significant decision must be documented. A workpaper that records that an explanation was accepted, without the reasoning, provides no evidence that the acceptance was skeptical.

For significant explanations, document:

- the explanation offered, in management's terms;
- what independent evidence the auditor sought to test it;
- whether the evidence supported, contradicted, or failed to address the explanation;
- the auditor's conclusion on whether to accept, partially accept, or reject the explanation;
- the reasoning, including why alternative explanations were less supported.

Acceptance based on plausibility alone, without documented testing, does not evidence skepticism. The file should show that the explanation was corroborated before it was relied upon.

### Document Independent Expectations And Comparisons

In judgment areas such as estimates, fair value, and analytical procedures, skepticism is demonstrated through the development of an independent expectation or range and its comparison to management's figure. Documenting only management's number, and the auditor's agreement with it, shows no independent challenge.

Document independent analysis by recording:

- the expectation or range the auditor developed, and its basis;
- the assumptions, data, and method used to develop it;
- the comparison to management's figure and the magnitude of any difference;
- the assessment of whether management's figure is at an aggressive or unsupported end of the range;
- the conclusion on whether management's figure is reasonable, and the reasoning.

An independent expectation that was never documented cannot be distinguished from no independent expectation at all. The workpaper must show the challenge occurred.

### Record Consultations And Their Outcomes

Consultation on difficult judgments is a key mechanism for bringing additional skepticism and expertise to bear. The fact and outcome of consultations must be documented so that the reasoning in the file reflects the combined judgment, not only the original auditor's view.

Document consultations by recording:

- the issue consulted on, and why consultation was sought;
- who was consulted, and their relevant expertise and independence;
- the advice or conclusion reached;
- how the consultation shaped the final conclusion;
- any remaining disagreement and its resolution.

A consultation that occurred but was not documented cannot be verified and may as well not have happened for evidentiary purposes. Capture the consultation and its effect.

### Make The Documentation Reviewable And Timely

Skepticism documentation must be created while the judgment is fresh and structured so a reviewer can follow it. Documentation reconstructed long after the work, from memory, loses specificity and credibility. Documentation that is disorganized or buried cannot serve its review function.

Ensure reviewability by:

- preparing documentation promptly, while the reasoning and context are current;
- structuring workpapers so the judgment path is clear, with indicators, alternatives, and reasoning visible;
- cross-referencing related procedures so a reviewer can navigate the evidence;
- avoiding conclusory statements that assert skepticism without showing it;
- using language that distinguishes what was tested from what was assumed.

Timely, well-structured documentation allows a reviewer to confirm skepticism was applied; late, disorganized, or conclusory documentation does not, regardless of the underlying effort.

### Document Escalation And Resistance

When skepticism leads to concerns that meet resistance, restricted access, or possible fraud, the escalation itself must be documented. The file must show what was escalated, to whom, the response, and the effect on the conclusion.

Document escalation by recording:

- the concern that prompted escalation, with the evidence basis;
- who was notified, including the engagement partner, governance, or regulators;
- the response received and any actions taken;
- whether the concern was resolved or remained outstanding;
- the effect on the engagement's scope, conclusion, or continuation.

Escalation that is not documented cannot be relied upon and leaves the auditor exposed if the concern later proves significant. The record protects both the engagement and the auditor.

## Common Traps

### Conclusion-Only Documentation

A workpaper that records the answer without the questioning, alternatives, and reasoning shows no skepticism. Document the process.

### Undocumented Contradictory Indicators

Indicators noted mentally but not recorded leave the file appearing contradiction-free. Document each indicator and its resolution.

### Acceptance Without Documented Testing

Recording that an explanation was accepted, without the corroboration performed, provides no evidence of skepticism. Show the testing.

### No Independent Expectation Recorded

Agreeing with management's figure without documenting an independent expectation or range shows no challenge. Record the expectation and comparison.

### Late Reconstruction

Documenting from memory long after the work loses specificity and credibility. Document while fresh.

### Consultations Not Recorded

A consultation that is not documented cannot be verified. Capture the issue, the consultant, the advice, and the effect.

### Conclusory Language

Statements asserting that skepticism was applied, without showing how, are not evidence. Show the reasoning, do not merely assert it.

## Self-Check

- Does each significant workpaper document the process, the question, alternatives, indicators, reasoning, and not only the conclusion?
- Are contradictory indicators specifically recorded, with the response, resolution, and effect on the conclusion documented for each?
- For significant management explanations, does the file show the corroboration performed and the basis for accepting or rejecting?
- In estimate and judgment areas, is an independent expectation or range documented, with the comparison to management's figure and the reasoning for the conclusion?
- Are consultations on difficult judgments recorded, including the issue, consultant, advice, and effect on the conclusion?
- Is documentation prepared timely while context is fresh, and structured so a reviewer can follow the judgment path?
- Are escalations, including restricted access, possible fraud, and governance notifications, documented with the response and effect?
- Does the documentation distinguish what was tested from what was assumed, avoiding conclusory assertions of skepticism?
- Could a reviewer who was not present reconstruct the auditor's thinking and confirm that skepticism was applied?
- Does the file demonstrate skepticism through shown reasoning rather than merely asserted diligence?
