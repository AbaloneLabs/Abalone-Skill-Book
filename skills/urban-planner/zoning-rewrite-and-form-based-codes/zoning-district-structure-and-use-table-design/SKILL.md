# Zoning District Structure and Use Table Design

The district structure and the use table are the heart of the zoning ordinance, because they define what can go where, and the structure and the table determine whether the ordinance is clear, usable, and aligned with the community's vision. The judgment problem is that the district structure is often outdated (the too many districts, the unclear purposes), the use table is often confusing (the P, the SE, the X, the blank, the footnote), and the permissions are often misaligned (the prohibited uses that should be allowed, the conditional uses that should be permitted), and these failures produce the ordinance that is hard to use and that does not implement the plan. A zoning rewrite that is designed well (the clear districts, the usable table, the aligned permissions) produces the ordinance that works, while a rewrite that is designed poorly (the confusing districts, the cryptic table, the misaligned permissions) fails. This skill covers the district structure and the use table decisions that determine whether the rewritten ordinance is clear and effective. It complements the broader zoning-rewrite skill by focusing on the district and the use table dimensions.

## Core Rules

### Design the District Structure with the Clear Purposes

The district structure should have the clear purposes, because the districts are the building blocks of the ordinance, and the purposes explain why each district exists and what it is for, and the clear purposes guide the administration and the interpretation. The rule is to design the district structure with the clear purposes, using the district inventory (the existing districts, the overlap, the gaps), the district consolidation (the merge of the redundant, the split of the overbroad), the purpose statements (the clear and the specific intent for each district), and the hierarchy (the residential, the commercial, the industrial, the mixed-use, the special). The district inventory should assess the existing districts, because the rewrite should address the problems, and the inventory identifies them. The district consolidation should merge the redundant districts and split the overbroad, because the too many districts confuse, and the consolidation simplifies. The purpose statements should be clear and specific, because the purpose guides the interpretation, and the vague purpose ("to promote the welfare") does not. The hierarchy should organize the districts by the category, because the organization aids the navigation, and the hierarchy structures it.

### Organize the Use Table for the Clarity and the Usability

The use table should be organized for the clarity and the usability, because the table is the most-used part of the ordinance, and the confusing table frustrates the users, and the clear table enables them. The rule is to organize the use table, using the use categories (the broad categories with the sub-uses), the permission symbols (the P, the SE, the X, the clear legend), the use definitions (the defined terms for the ambiguous uses), and the format (the readable layout, the cross-references). The use categories should group the related uses, because the categorical organization aids the navigation, and the flat list of the hundreds of uses confuses. The permission symbols should be clear and consistent (the P for the permitted, the SE for the special exception, the X for the prohibited), because the symbols are the table's language, and the clear symbols communicate. The use definitions should define the ambiguous terms, because the undefined use is the ambiguous use, and the definition clarifies it. The format should be readable and the cross-referenced, because the dense and the un-cross-referenced table is the hard-to-use table, and the readable format enables it.

### Calibrate the Use Permissions to the Plan and the Context

The use permissions should be calibrated to the comprehensive plan and the district context, because the permissions implement the plan, and the context determines the appropriate use, and the miscalibrated permissions undermine the plan. The rule is to calibrate the permissions, using the plan alignment (the permissions that implement the plan's land use vision), the context analysis (the district character, the adjacent uses, the infrastructure), the modern use patterns (the live-work, the micro-units, the coworking, the mixed-use), and the compatibility (the use that fits the district, the use that requires the conditions). The plan alignment should ensure that the permissions implement the plan, because the zoning implements the plan, and the misalignment undermines it. The context analysis should assess the district character and the adjacent uses, because the use must fit the context, and the analysis guides it. The modern use patterns should be addressed, because the old ordinance prohibits the new uses (the live-work, the coworking), and the rewrite should allow them. The compatibility should be assessed for each use, because the incompatible use harms the district, and the compatibility assessment guides the permission (the permitted, the conditional, the prohibited).

### Establish the Conditional Use Standards and the Process

The conditional uses should have the clear standards and the process, because the conditional use is the use that may be compatible with the conditions, and the standards and the process determine the compatibility, and the unclear standards and the process produce the inconsistent decisions. The rule is to establish the conditional use standards and the process, using the use-specific standards (the standards for each conditional use), the general criteria (the compatibility, the public welfare, the site suitability), the process (the application, the notice, the hearing, the decision), and the findings (the specific findings for the approval). The use-specific standards should address the impacts of each use, because the conditional use has the specific impacts, and the standards mitigate them. The general criteria should require the compatibility and the public welfare, because the conditional use must be compatible, and the criteria ensure it. The process should be clear and the consistent, because the process protects the rights, and the clear process enables it. The findings should support the decision, because the findings document the basis, and the unsupported decision is the overturned decision.

### Restructure for the Clarity and the Modernization

The ordinance should be restructured for the clarity and the modernization, because the old ordinance is the confusing and the outdated, and the restructuring produces the clear and the modern ordinance, and the clarity and the modernization improve the usability. The rule is to restructure for the clarity and the modernization, using the organization (the logical structure, the clear numbering), the plain language (the readable, the no legalese), the graphics (the diagrams, the tables, the illustrations), and the modernization (the new uses, the new standards, the new processes). The organization should be logical and the numbered, because the disorganized ordinance is the hard-to-use ordinance, and the organization enables it. The plain language should replace the legalese, because the legalese confuses, and the plain language clarifies. The graphics should illustrate the standards, because the text alone is the ambiguous, and the graphics clarify. The modernization should address the new uses and the standards, because the old ordinance is outdated, and the modernization updates it.

## Common Traps

### Too Many Districts, Overlap and Confusion

The too many districts are retained, and the overlap and the confusion persist, because the consolidation was not conducted. The trap is that the too many districts produce the confusion. The mechanism is that the consolidation was not conducted, and the existing districts were retained. The false signal is that "we have the districts for every context." The harm is that the districts overlap, the users are confused, the administration is inconsistent, and the consolidation that should simplify the structure does not.

### Use Table Cryptic, Hard to Use

The use table is cryptic (the symbols without the legend, the footnotes without the explanation), and it is hard to use, because the table was not organized for the clarity. The trap is that the cryptic table frustrates the users. The mechanism is that the table was not organized, and the existing format was retained. The false signal is that "the table has all the uses." The harm is that the table is confusing, the users cannot find the permission, the administration is inconsistent, and the clarity that should enable the use does not.

### Permissions Misaligned with the Plan

The permissions are misaligned with the plan (the prohibited uses that the plan encourages, the permitted uses that the plan discourages), because the plan alignment was not conducted. The trap is that the misaligned permissions undermine the plan. The mechanism is that the plan alignment was not conducted, and the existing permissions were retained. The false signal is that "we rewrote the ordinance." The harm is that the permissions do not implement the plan, the vision is not achieved, the rewrite is wasted, and the alignment that should implement the plan does not.

### Conditional Use Standards Vague

The conditional use standards are vague (the "compatible," the "no undue impact"), and the decisions are inconsistent, because the specific standards were not established. The trap is that the vague standards produce the inconsistent decisions. The mechanism is that the specific standards were not established, and the general language was used. The false signal is that "we have conditional use criteria." The harm is that the standards are vague, the decisions are inconsistent, the appeals overturn, and the standards that should guide the decisions do not.

### Modernization Skipped, Old Patterns Retained

The modernization is skipped, and the old use patterns are retained, because the modern uses were not addressed. The trap is that the skipped modernization produces the outdated ordinance. The mechanism is that the modern uses were not addressed, and the old list was retained. The false signal is that "we updated the ordinance." The harm is that the new uses are prohibited, the innovation is stifled, the ordinance is outdated, and the modernization that should update the ordinance does not.

## Self-Check

- Is the district structure designed with the clear purposes, with the inventory, the consolidation, the purpose statements, and the hierarchy?
- Is the use table organized for the clarity, with the categories, the symbols, the definitions, and the format?
- Are the permissions calibrated to the plan, with the plan alignment, the context, the modern patterns, and the compatibility?
- Are the conditional use standards established, with the use-specific standards, the general criteria, the process, and the findings?
- Is the ordinance restructured for the clarity and the modernization, with the organization, the plain language, the graphics, and the modernization?
- Are the districts clear, or are they too many and confusing?
- Is the table usable, or is it cryptic?
- Are the permissions aligned, or are they misaligned?
- Are the standards specific, or are they vague?
- Is the ordinance modernized, or is it outdated?
