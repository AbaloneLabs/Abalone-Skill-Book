# Military Noise and Aircraft Mishap Zones

The land around the military airfield is subject to the noise (the flight operations that generate the high-decibel sound) and the accident potential (the crash risk that is highest along the flight paths), and the land-use planning in these zones must protect the public safety and the military mission, because the incompatible development (the residential, the noise-sensitive, the assembly uses) in the noise and the mishap zones creates the complaints, the restrictions, and the risk that can compromise the military operations and the community wellbeing. The judgment problem is that the noise and the mishap zones are defined by the military (the AICUZ study), the land-use authority rests with the local government, and the development pressure pushes the incompatible uses into the zones, and the planner must navigate these dimensions to maintain the compatibility. A community that manages the noise and the mishap zones well (the land-use regulations, the disclosure, the acquisition) protects the mission and the safety, while a community that manages them poorly faces the complaints, the restrictions, and the base closure. This skill covers the noise and the mishap zone decisions that determine whether the community and the base coexist or conflict. It complements the broader military-and-defense skill by focusing on the airfield-specific noise and safety dimensions.

## Core Rules

### Use the AICUZ Study as the Regulatory Foundation

The Air Installation Compatible Use Zone (AICUZ) study is the military's analysis of the noise and the accident potential around the airfield, and it defines the noise zones (the contours of the day-night average sound level) and the clear zones and the accident potential zones (the areas of the highest crash risk), and it provides the land-use compatibility recommendations. The rule is to use the AICUZ study as the regulatory foundation, adopting the noise contours and the APZs as the basis for the local land-use regulations, and updating the regulations when the AICUZ is updated (the flight tracks, the aircraft, the operations change). The AICUZ should be adopted by reference in the zoning ordinance, because the adoption provides the legal basis for the regulations, and the unadopted AICUZ is the advisory document that the local regulations do not implement. The AICUZ should be coordinated with the military, because the military provides the data and the expertise, and the coordination ensures the regulations reflect the current operations. The AICUZ should be communicated to the public, because the noise and the mishap zones affect the property owners, and the communication builds the awareness and the acceptance. The AICUZ should be updated when the operations change, because the outdated AICUZ does not reflect the current noise and the accident potential, and the regulations based on the outdated study do not protect the compatibility.

### Prohibit the Incompatible Uses in the High-Risk Zones

The clear zones and the accident potential zones (APZs) are the areas of the highest crash risk, and the incompatible uses (the residential, the assembly, the sensitive) should be prohibited in these zones, because the crash risk is the safety risk that the land-use regulation should prevent. The rule is to prohibit the incompatible uses in the high-risk zones, using the zoning (the clear zone and the APZ overlay that prohibits the residential, the assembly, the institutional uses), the acquisition (the purchase of the incompatible properties, the easement that prevents the development), and the disclosure (the notification to the property owners and the buyers). The prohibition should be comprehensive, because the partial prohibition (the residential allowed in the APZ I but not the APZ II) does not eliminate the risk, and the comprehensive prohibition does. The acquisition should target the existing incompatible uses, because the existing residential in the clear zone is the existing risk, and the acquisition removes the risk. The disclosure should be required for all the properties in the noise and the mishap zones, because the buyers should know the noise and the crash risk, and the disclosure prevents the complaints and the lawsuits. The prohibition should be coordinated with the military, because the military can provide the data and the support, and the coordination strengthens the defense.

### Regulate the Noise-Sensitive Uses in the Noise Zones

The noise zones (the contours of the day-night average sound level) define the areas of the high noise exposure, and the noise-sensitive uses (the residential, the schools, the hospitals, the outdoor assembly) should be regulated in these zones, because the high noise causes the annoyance, the sleep disturbance, and the health effects. The rule is to regulate the noise-sensitive uses in the noise zones, prohibiting the new noise-sensitive uses in the high-noise zone (the 75 dB DNL and above), conditionally allowing the uses in the moderate-noise zone (the 65 to 75 dB DNL) with the noise attenuation, and requiring the disclosure in all the noise zones. The regulation should use the noise attenuation standards (the building construction that reduces the interior noise to the acceptable level), because the attenuation allows the development in the moderate-noise zone while protecting the occupants. The regulation should require the real estate disclosure, because the buyers and the tenants should know the noise exposure, and the disclosure prevents the complaints and the lawsuits. The regulation should address the outdoor noise, because the indoor attenuation does not address the outdoor activity, and the outdoor noise affects the usability of the yards and the outdoor spaces. The regulation should be coordinated with the military, because the military's noise data and the expertise support the regulation, and the coordination ensures the accuracy.

### Prevent the Encroachment That Threatens the Mission

The encroachment (the incompatible development around the base) threatens the military mission, because the noise complaints and the safety concerns lead to the restrictions on the flight operations, and the restrictions (the flight path changes, the hour limitations, the altitude minimums) compromise the training and the readiness. The rule is to prevent the encroachment that threatens the mission, using the land-use regulations (the zoning, the overlay, the density limits), the land conservation (the purchase of the development rights, the conservation easements, the buffer areas), and the military-civilian coordination (the joint planning, the information sharing, the conflict resolution). The land-use regulations should be proactive, because the encroachment that is prevented is the encroachment that does not need to be removed, and the reactive regulation is the late regulation. The land conservation should target the high-risk areas, because the conservation (the agricultural preservation, the open space, the habitat) maintains the compatible use and prevents the incompatible development. The military-civilian coordination should be ongoing, because the encroachment is the ongoing threat, and the coordination identifies and addresses the issues before they become the conflicts. The encroachment prevention should be coordinated with the state and the federal programs (the DoD Readiness and Environmental Protection Integration, the state compatibility programs), because the programs provide the funding and the support.

### Coordinate the Disclosure and the Community Education

The noise and the mishap zones affect the property owners and the residents, and the disclosure and the community education ensure that the affected parties know the exposure and the implications, and the education builds the acceptance and the support for the compatibility. The rule is to coordinate the disclosure and the community education, requiring the real estate disclosure (the notification to the buyers and the tenants), the community education (the information about the noise and the mission), and the complaint management (the process for the noise complaints and the resolution). The disclosure should be required for all the transactions in the noise and the mishap zones, because the buyers and the tenants should know the exposure, and the disclosure prevents the "we did not know" complaints. The community education should explain the military mission and the noise, because the understanding builds the acceptance, and the uninformed community is the complaining community. The complaint management should provide the process for the noise complaints, because the complaints that are heard and addressed are the complaints that do not escalate, and the unaddressed complaints become the political pressure and the restrictions.

## Common Traps

### AICUZ Not Adopted, Regulations Not Based on the Study

The AICUZ study is available but not adopted by the local government, and the land-use regulations are not based on the study, and the noise and the mishap zones are not regulated, because the AICUZ is the advisory document that the local regulations do not implement. The trap is that the unadopted AICUZ does not regulate. The mechanism is that the adoption was not pursued, and the regulations were not updated. The false signal is that "the AICUZ exists." The harm is that the incompatible development proceeds, the noise complaints increase, the crash risk is not managed, the military mission is threatened, and the study that should guide the regulation does not.

### Incompatible Uses Allowed in the High-Risk Zones

The incompatible uses (the residential, the assembly) are allowed in the clear zones and the APZs, and the crash risk is not managed, because the regulations do not prohibit the incompatible uses. The trap is that the allowed incompatible uses create the risk. The mechanism is that the prohibition was not adopted, and the development proceeded. The false signal is that "the zone is regulated." The harm is that the population is exposed to the crash risk, the safety is compromised, the military mission is threatened, and the regulation that should prevent the risk does not.

### Noise-Sensitive Uses Not Attenuated, Complaints Follow

The noise-sensitive uses are developed in the noise zones without the attenuation, and the occupants are exposed to the high noise, and the complaints follow, because the attenuation was not required. The trap is that the unattenuated uses generate the complaints. The mechanism is that the attenuation standards were not adopted, and the development proceeded without the noise reduction. The false signal is that "the development is allowed." The harm is that the occupants are exposed to the noise, the complaints increase, the political pressure builds, the military mission is threatened, and the development that should be attenuated is not.

### Encroachment Not Prevented, Mission Threatened

The encroachment is not prevented, and the incompatible development proceeds, and the military mission is threatened, because the land-use regulations and the conservation were not proactive. The trap is that the unprevented encroachment threatens the mission. The mechanism is that the regulations were not adopted, and the conservation was not pursued. The false signal is that "the base is here." The harm is that the encroachment proceeds, the complaints and the safety concerns increase, the flight restrictions follow, the mission is compromised, and the base may close.

### Disclosure Not Required, Buyers Uninformed

The disclosure is not required for the properties in the noise and the mishap zones, and the buyers and the tenants are uninformed, and the complaints follow, because the disclosure was not implemented. The trap is that the uninformed buyers generate the complaints. The mechanism is that the disclosure was not required, and the transactions proceeded without the notification. The false signal is that "the market works." The harm is that the buyers are surprised by the noise and the risk, the complaints increase, the lawsuits follow, and the disclosure that should inform does not.

## Self-Check

- Is the AICUZ study adopted as the regulatory foundation, with the noise contours and the APZs incorporated into the zoning?
- Are the incompatible uses prohibited in the clear zones and the APZs, with the zoning, the acquisition, and the disclosure?
- Are the noise-sensitive uses regulated in the noise zones, with the prohibition in the high-noise zone, the attenuation in the moderate-noise zone, and the disclosure?
- Is the encroachment prevented, with the land-use regulations, the land conservation, and the military-civilian coordination?
- Is the disclosure and the community education coordinated, with the real estate disclosure, the community education, and the complaint management?
- Is the AICUZ adopted, or is it the advisory document?
- Are the incompatible uses prohibited, or are they allowed in the high-risk zones?
- Are the noise-sensitive uses attenuated, or are the complaints generated?
- Is the encroachment prevented, or is the mission threatened?
- Is the disclosure required, or are the buyers uninformed?
