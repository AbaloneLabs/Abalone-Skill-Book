# Variance Application Evidence and Hardship Analysis

The variance is the relief from the strict application of the zoning ordinance, and it is granted only for the unnecessary hardship or the practical difficulty that is unique to the property, not the hardship that is self-created or shared. The judgment problem is that the variance standard is strict (the unnecessary hardship, the unique circumstances, the minimum relief), the evidence is often thin (the conclusory statements, the no site-specific data), and the self-created hardship is often overlooked (the buyer who knew, the condition that was created), and these failures produce the variance that is granted improperly and the overturned on the appeal. A variance application that is prepared well (the strong evidence, the hardship analysis, the self-created review) is granted and the sustained, while an application that is prepared poorly (the weak evidence, the conclusory hardship, the ignored self-created) is denied or the overturned. This skill covers the variance evidence and the hardship analysis decisions that determine whether the variance is granted and the sustained. It complements the broader variance skill by focusing on the evidence and the hardship dimensions.

## Core Rules

### Establish the Unnecessary Hardship or the Practical Difficulty

The variance standard requires the unnecessary hardship (the use variance) or the practical difficulty (the area variance), and the standard must be established with the specific evidence, because the conclusory claim of the hardship is not the proof, and the proof requires the findings. The rule is to establish the unnecessary hardship or the practical difficulty, using the standard elements (the unique circumstances, the no reasonable use, the no self-created hardship, the minimum relief, the no harm to the public), the evidence (the site analysis, the comparable properties, the financial analysis), and the findings of fact (the specific findings that support each element). The standard elements should be addressed for each variance type, because the use variance requires the unnecessary hardship (the no reasonable return) and the area variance requires the practical difficulty (the significant burden), and the standard differs by the type. The evidence should be specific and the site-based, because the general claim is not the proof, and the site-specific evidence is. The findings of fact should support each element, because the board must make the findings, and the findings support the decision, and the unsupported decision is the overturned decision.

### Assemble the Site-Specific Evidence and the Findings of Fact

The variance evidence should be site-specific and the findings of fact should be detailed, because the variance is granted for the unique property, and the unique property requires the unique evidence, and the findings document the basis. The rule is to assemble the site-specific evidence and the findings of fact, using the site analysis (the topography, the lot shape, the dimensions, the constraints), the comparable properties (the similar lots that comply, the demonstration of the unique burden), the financial analysis (for the use variance, the reasonable return analysis), and the impact analysis (the effect on the neighbors, the public welfare). The site analysis should document the unique physical conditions (the odd shape, the steep slope, the wetland, the rock), because the unique conditions create the hardship, and the documentation supports the claim. The comparable properties should demonstrate that the similar lots comply, because the variance is for the unique lot, and the comparable analysis shows the uniqueness. The financial analysis should demonstrate the no reasonable return (for the use variance), because the use variance requires the financial hardship, and the analysis provides the proof. The impact analysis should demonstrate the no harm to the public, because the variance must not harm the public welfare, and the analysis provides the assurance.

### Evaluate the Self-Created Hardship

The self-created hardship is the bar to the variance, and it must be evaluated, because the hardship that the owner created (by the purchase with the knowledge, by the subdivision, by the construction) is not the basis for the variance, and the self-created hardship denies the relief. The rule is to evaluate the self-created hardship, using the knowledge test (did the owner know of the restriction when the property was acquired), the creation test (did the owner create the hardship by the action), and the exception (the self-created hardship may be granted in the limited circumstances, the balancing of the equities). The knowledge test should assess whether the owner knew of the zoning restriction when the property was acquired, because the buyer who knew of the restriction and the purchased anyway has the self-created hardship, and the knowledge defeats the claim. The creation test should assess whether the owner created the hardship (the subdivision of the lot, the placement of the structure, the failure to seek the variance before the construction), because the owner who created the condition has the self-created hardship, and the creation defeats the claim. The exception should consider the balancing of the equities, because some jurisdictions allow the variance for the self-created hardship when the equities favor the owner, and the balancing provides the limited relief.

### Determine the Minimum Variance and the Minimum Relief

The variance should grant the minimum relief necessary, because the variance is the exception, and the exception should be the minimum, and the excessive variance is the grant that exceeds the hardship. The rule is to determine the minimum variance and the minimum relief, using the relief analysis (the least relief that addresses the hardship), the alternative analysis (the alternatives that avoid the variance), and the condition analysis (the conditions that limit the variance). The relief analysis should identify the least relief that addresses the hardship, because the variance should be the minimum, and the excessive variance is the improper grant. The alternative analysis should explore the alternatives that avoid the variance (the design changes, the setback averaging, the variance for the part), because the variance is the last resort, and the alternatives should be exhausted. The condition analysis should impose the conditions that limit the variance (the time limit, the use restriction, the screening), because the conditions ensure the minimum impact, and the unconditioned variance may exceed the need.

### Structure the Variance Record for the Legal Defensibility

The variance record should be structured for the legal defensibility, because the variance may be appealed, and the appeal reviews the record, and the incomplete record is the overturned variance. The rule is to structure the record for the defensibility, using the application (the complete and the detailed application), the evidence (the site-specific and the documented), the hearing (the testimony, the cross-examination, the public comment), the findings of fact (the specific findings that support each element), and the decision (the written decision with the conditions). The application should be complete and the detailed, because the application is the foundation, and the incomplete application is the weak foundation. The evidence should be site-specific and the documented, because the documented evidence is the proof, and the undocumented claim is not. The hearing should include the testimony and the cross-examination, because the hearing is the due process, and the complete hearing protects the rights. The findings of fact should support each element, because the findings are the basis, and the unsupported decision is the overturned decision. The decision should be written and the conditioned, because the written decision documents the grant, and the conditions limit the impact.

## Common Traps

### Hardship Claimed Conclusorily, Not Proven

The hardship is claimed conclusorily (the statement that the hardship exists), and it is not proven with the evidence, because the site-specific evidence was not assembled. The trap is that the conclusory claim is not the proof. The mechanism is that the evidence was not assembled, and the hardship was stated. The false signal is that "the hardship is obvious." The harm is that the claim is not proven, the findings are not supported, the variance is denied or the overturned, and the hardship that should be proven is merely claimed.

### Evidence Generic, Not Site-Specific

The evidence is generic (the general statements about the zoning), and it is not site-specific, because the site analysis was not conducted. The trap is that the generic evidence does not prove the unique hardship. The mechanism is that the site analysis was not conducted, and the generic evidence was submitted. The false signal is that "the zoning is burdensome." The harm is that the evidence does not address the unique property, the uniqueness is not demonstrated, the variance is denied, and the evidence that should be site-specific is generic.

### Self-Created Hardship Not Evaluated

The self-created hardship is not evaluated, and the variance is granted for the self-created condition, because the knowledge and the creation tests were not applied. The trap is that the unevaluated self-created hardship produces the improper grant. The mechanism is that the self-created tests were not applied, and the hardship was assumed. The false signal is that "the hardship exists." The harm is that the self-created hardship is granted, the grant is improper, the appeal overturns, and the self-created hardship that should bar the variance does not.

### Variance Exceeds the Minimum Relief

The variance grants more relief than necessary, because the minimum relief was not analyzed. The trap is that the excessive variance is the improper grant. The mechanism is that the minimum relief was not analyzed, and the full request was granted. The false signal is that "the variance was granted." The harm is that the variance exceeds the hardship, the impact is greater than necessary, the appeal overturns, and the relief that should be the minimum is the excessive.

### Record Incomplete, Decision Undefended

The variance record is incomplete (the no findings, the no evidence, the no hearing record), and the decision is undefended on the appeal, because the record was not structured for the defensibility. The trap is that the incomplete record produces the overturned variance. The mechanism is that the record was not structured, and the findings were not made. The false signal is that "the variance was granted." The harm is that the record is incomplete, the findings are absent, the appeal overturns, and the record that should defend the decision does not.

## Self-Check

- Is the unnecessary hardship or the practical difficulty established with the standard elements, the evidence, and the findings of fact?
- Is the evidence site-specific, with the site analysis, the comparable properties, the financial analysis, and the impact analysis?
- Is the self-created hardship evaluated, with the knowledge test, the creation test, and the exception?
- Is the variance the minimum relief, with the relief analysis, the alternative analysis, and the condition analysis?
- Is the record structured for the defensibility, with the application, the evidence, the hearing, the findings, and the decision?
- Is the hardship proven, or is it conclusorily claimed?
- Is the evidence site-specific, or is it generic?
- Is the self-created hardship evaluated, or is it overlooked?
- Is the variance the minimum, or does it exceed the relief?
- Is the record complete, or is the decision undefended?
