# Defensible Space and WUI Building Standards

The defensible space and the WUI building standards determine whether the structure survives the wildfire, because the wildfire does not destroy the community by the wall of flame but by the embers and the adjacent ignition, and the defensible space (the managed vegetation around the structure) and the building standards (the ignition-resistant materials and the design) are the structure's defense. The judgment problem is that the defensible space is often inadequate (the no zones, the no maintenance), the building standards are often weak (the no ignition-resistant materials, the vulnerable vents and the decks), and the development is often allowed in the high-risk areas without the standards, and these failures produce the structures that burn. A WUI program that is designed well (the defensible space, the building standards, the development regulation) produces the survivable structures, while a program that is designed poorly (the inadequate space, the weak standards, the unregulated development) produces the vulnerable structures. This skill covers the defensible space and the building standards decisions that determine whether the WUI structures survive the wildfire. It complements the broader wildfire skill by focusing on the defensible space and the building dimensions.

## Core Rules

### Establish the Defensible Space Zones and the Requirements

The defensible space should be established in the zones, because the wildfire defense is the layered defense (the immediate zone, the intermediate zone, the extended zone), and the zones define the management, and the management reduces the ignition. The rule is to establish the defensible space zones, using the immediate zone (the 0 to 5 feet, the non-combustible), the intermediate zone (the 5 to 30 feet, the reduced fuel), the extended zone (the 30 to 100 feet, the managed fuel), and the requirements (the vegetation management, the clearance, the maintenance). The immediate zone (the 0 to 5 feet) should be the non-combustible zone, because the embers land here, and the non-combustible materials (the gravel, the hardscape, the low plants) prevent the ignition, and the zone is the most critical. The intermediate zone (the 5 to 30 feet) should have the reduced fuel, because the fuel here carries the fire, and the reduced fuel (the spaced trees, the low branches, the mowed grass) breaks the path. The extended zone (the 30 to 100 feet) should have the managed fuel, because the fuel here influences the intensity, and the managed fuel (the thinned trees, the broken continuity) reduces it. The requirements should specify the vegetation management, the clearance, and the maintenance, because the defensible space requires the ongoing management, and the requirements ensure it.

### Adopt the WUI Building Standards and the Ignition-Resistant Materials

The WUI building standards should require the ignition-resistant materials and the design, because the embers ignite the vulnerable points (the roof, the vents, the deck, the siding), and the ignition-resistant materials and the design protect them. The rule is to adopt the WUI building standards, using the roof standards (the Class A roof, the no gaps), the vent standards (the ember-resistant vents, the no openings in the vulnerable locations), the deck and the siding standards (the ignition-resistant materials, the enclosed spaces), the window and the door standards (the dual-pane, the tempered glass), and the accessory standards (the fences, the outbuildings, the attachments). The roof standards should require the Class A roof and the no gaps, because the roof is the most vulnerable surface, and the embers ignite it, and the Class A and the no gaps protect it. The vent standards should require the ember-resistant vents, because the vents are the entry points, and the embers enter through them, and the ember-resistant vents block them. The deck and the siding standards should require the ignition-resistant materials and the enclosed spaces, because the decks and the siding ignite, and the materials and the enclosure protect them. The window and the door standards should require the dual-pane and the tempered glass, because the windows break from the heat, and the dual-pane and the tempered glass resist it. The accessory standards should address the fences and the outbuildings, because the attachments ignite the main structure, and the standards break the connection.

### Regulate the Development in the Wildfire Hazard Areas

The development in the wildfire hazard areas should be regulated, because the development in the high-risk areas increases the exposure, and the regulation reduces the risk, and the unregulated development creates the future disasters. The rule is to regulate the development, using the hazard area designation (the high-risk areas mapped), the development standards (the density, the setbacks, the clustering), the access standards (the two routes, the width, the grade, the turnarounds), the water supply (the fire flow, the hydrants, the storage), and the subdivision design (the fuel breaks, the buffer zones, the defensible space). The hazard area designation should map the high-risk areas, because the designation triggers the standards, and the mapping identifies them. The development standards should control the density and the layout, because the high density in the high-risk area increases the exposure, and the standards reduce it. The access standards should require the two routes and the adequate width, because the evacuation and the response require the access, and the standards ensure it. The water supply should provide the fire flow, because the suppression requires the water, and the supply enables it. The subdivision design should include the fuel breaks and the buffers, because the design reduces the risk, and the features implement it.

### Design the Fuel Modification Zones and the Access

The fuel modification zones and the access should be designed, because the zones (the managed vegetation at the community edge) and the access (the routes for the evacuation and the response) are the community-scale defense, and the design determines the effectiveness. The rule is to design the fuel modification and the access, using the fuel modification plan (the zones at the wildland edge, the management standards), the access design (the two routes, the road width, the grade, the turnarounds, the bridges), the evacuation capacity (the routes, the time, the vulnerable populations), and the safe staging (the safety zones, the temporary refuge). The fuel modification plan should establish the zones at the wildland edge, because the edge is the exposure, and the zones reduce it. The access design should provide the two routes and the adequate dimensions, because the wildfire may block one route, and the second route is the backup. The evacuation capacity should assess the routes and the time, because the evacuation requires the capacity and the time, and the assessment identifies the constraints. The safe staging should provide the safety zones, because the evacuation may fail, and the safety zones provide the refuge.

### Review the Site Design and the Landscaping for the Resilience

The site design and the landscaping should be reviewed for the wildfire resilience, because the design (the location, the layout, the materials) and the landscaping (the plants, the placement, the maintenance) determine the structure's vulnerability, and the review ensures the resilience. The rule is to review the site design and the landscaping, using the site assessment (the slope, the aspect, the fuel, the exposure), the design review (the setbacks, the clustering, the materials), the landscaping review (the plant selection, the placement, the defensible space), and the condition (the ongoing maintenance, the inspection). The site assessment should evaluate the slope and the exposure, because the slope and the exposure affect the risk, and the assessment informs the design. The design review should assess the setbacks and the materials, because the setbacks and the materials affect the vulnerability, and the review ensures the resilience. The landscaping review should assess the plant selection and the placement, because the plants and the placement affect the ignition, and the review ensures the defensible space. The condition should require the ongoing maintenance, because the defensible space degrades, and the maintenance preserves it.

## Common Traps

### Defensible Space Inadequate, No Zones

The defensible space is inadequate (the no zones, the no immediate zone), and the structure is vulnerable, because the zones were not established. The trap is that the inadequate space produces the vulnerable structure. The mechanism is that the zones were not established, and the vegetation was not managed. The false signal is that "we have defensible space." The harm is that the immediate zone is combustible, the embers ignite, the structure burns, and the defensible space that should protect the structure does not.

### Building Standards Weak, Vulnerable Points Unprotected

The building standards are weak (the no ignition-resistant materials, the vulnerable vents and the decks), and the vulnerable points are unprotected, because the standards were not adopted. The trap is that the weak standards produce the unprotected points. The mechanism is that the standards were not adopted, and the conventional construction was allowed. The false signal is that "the structure is built to code." The harm is that the roof, the vents, the deck are vulnerable, the embers ignite them, the structure burns, and the standards that should protect the points do not.

### Development Unregulated in the Hazard Areas

The development is unregulated in the hazard areas, and the exposure is increased, because the hazard area regulation was not adopted. The trap is that the unregulated development creates the future disasters. The mechanism is that the regulation was not adopted, and the development was allowed. The false signal is that "the development is approved." The harm is that the high-risk areas are developed, the exposure increases, the future disasters are created, and the regulation that should manage the development does not.

### Access Inadequate, Evacuation and Response Fail

The access is inadequate (the one route, the narrow road, the steep grade), and the evacuation and the response fail, because the access was not designed. The trap is that the inadequate access produces the failed evacuation. The mechanism is that the access was not designed, and the one route was built. The false signal is that "the road exists." The harm is that the route is blocked, the evacuation fails, the response cannot reach, and the access that should enable the evacuation and the response does not.

### Landscaping Unmanaged, Ignition Risk

The landscaping is unmanaged (the combustible plants, the no maintenance), and the ignition risk is high, because the landscaping was not reviewed and the maintenance was not required. The trap is that the unmanaged landscaping produces the high ignition risk. The mechanism is that the landscaping was not reviewed, and the maintenance was not required. The false signal is that "the landscaping is installed." The harm is that the plants are combustible, the defensible space degrades, the ignition risk is high, and the management that should reduce the risk does not.
