# TIPS And Breakeven Analysis

Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities are the most direct inflation hedge in public markets, but they are widely misunderstood. The judgment problem is that investors buy TIPS believing inflation will rise, without realizing that the market has already priced expected inflation into the breakeven spread, so TIPS only outperform nominal Treasuries when inflation exceeds what the market already expects — an inflation surprise, not inflation itself. Agents and investors confuse the inflation hedge with an inflation forecast, buy TIPS when breakevens are already high, and are surprised when realized inflation arrives yet TIPS lag nominals because the surprise was to the downside. The skill is reading breakeven inflation as a signal of what is priced in, and judging TIPS against the bar the market has already set.

This skill is for evaluating TIPS and breakeven inflation. It is not personalized financial advice; yields, tax treatment, and rules vary by jurisdiction and change over time.

## Core Rules

### Understand How TIPS Actually Pay Off

TIPS adjust their principal for inflation and pay a fixed real coupon on the adjusted principal. Their payoff depends on the path of inflation relative to expectations, not on the level of inflation alone.

Mechanics to internalize:

- the principal is adjusted by realized inflation; in deflation, the principal adjusts down (with a floor at par at maturity in most cases);
- the coupon is a fixed real rate applied to the inflation-adjusted principal, so nominal income rises with inflation;
- the real yield at purchase is the return if held to maturity with inflation matching expectations;
- TIPS can have negative real yields, meaning the investor accepts a guaranteed real loss at maturity in exchange for inflation protection.

Know what you are buying: a stream of real cash flows whose nominal value depends on realized inflation, priced at a real yield that may be low or negative.

### Use Breakeven Inflation As The Decision Variable, Not The Inflation Forecast

The breakeven inflation rate is the difference between the nominal Treasury yield and the TIPS real yield for the same maturity. It is the rate of inflation at which TIPS and nominals deliver the same return. TIPS outperform only if realized inflation exceeds the breakeven.

Make breakeven the centerpiece:

- if breakevens are low, TIPS are cheap insurance — only a modest inflation surprise is needed for them to win;
- if breakevens are high, TIPS are expensive — the market already prices in significant inflation, and TIPS need a large upside surprise to outperform;
- compare the breakeven to your own view of likely inflation, but recognize the breakeven is the market's view, which is often a better forecast than any individual's.

The question is never "will inflation rise" but "will inflation exceed what is already priced in." Buying TIPS when breakevens are elevated is buying insurance after the premium has risen.

### Recognize That TIPS Hedge Inflation Surprises, Not Expected Inflation

This is the most common confusion. Expected inflation is already in nominal yields. TIPS add value only when inflation comes in above expectations.

Distinguish:

- expected inflation — already reflected in nominal yields and breakevens; holding TIPS gives no edge if inflation merely meets expectations;
- inflation surprise — inflation above the breakeven; this is what makes TIPS outperform nominals;
- deflation surprise — inflation below the breakeven; here nominals outperform TIPS, though the maturity par floor limits TIPS downside.

A portfolio that needs protection against an unexpected inflation spike is the one TIPS serve. A portfolio that merely expects moderate inflation may be better served by nominals if breakevens are already high.

### Account For Liquidity And Pricing Differences Between TIPS And Nominals

TIPS are less liquid than nominal Treasuries, especially in stress. During the 2008 crisis and other liquidity events, TIPS cheapened sharply relative to breakeven fundamentals because investors sold the less-liquid asset. This means breakevens can overstate true inflation expectations during stress.

Consider:

- breakevens reflect both inflation expectations and a liquidity premium for TIPS, so they can dislocate from fundamentals in stress;
- TIPS funds and ETFs can trade at discounts to NAV during dislocations, and individual TIPS can be expensive to trade in small sizes;
- on-the-run and off-the-run TIPS differ in liquidity and pricing.

Do not treat breakevens as a pure expectations measure during dislocations, and be aware that liquidity-driven cheapening can be an opportunity or a sign of stress depending on the investor's horizon.

### Match TIPS Duration To The Liability Or Spending Horizon

TIPS hedge inflation over the horizon they cover. A short-duration TIPS allocation hedges near-term inflation; long-duration TIPS hedge long-horizon inflation and are more sensitive to changes in real rates.

Match duration to purpose:

- for near-term spending needs, short TIPS or TIPS money market instruments hedge the relevant horizon;
- for long-duration liabilities (retirement spending decades out, pension obligations), long-duration TIPS provide a real-asset match;
- recognize that long TIPS are sensitive to real-rate changes, so their mark-to-market volatility is high even though their real return to maturity is fixed.

A TIPS allocation whose duration does not match the horizon being hedged provides the wrong protection.

### Consider Tax Treatment And Account Location

In some jurisdictions, the inflation adjustment to TIPS principal is taxed annually as income even though it is not received until maturity or sale. This creates a tax drag that can turn a positive pre-tax real return into a negative after-tax real return in taxable accounts.

Locate carefully:

- hold TIPS in tax-advantaged accounts where the inflation adjustment is not taxed annually, where this rule applies;
- in taxable accounts, model the after-tax real return, which can be materially lower than the stated real yield;
- be aware of how TIPS funds distribute and whether they reinvest the inflation adjustment.

Tax treatment can dominate the economics of TIPS for taxable investors and should drive account location.

### Use TIPS As One Tool Within A Broader Inflation Strategy

TIPS provide a clean, low-risk hedge to expected and unexpected inflation over their horizon, but they are a defensive asset with limited upside. They complement, but do not replace, growth assets (equities, real estate) that hedge inflation over long horizons through earnings and cash flow growth. Size TIPS to the precision of hedge needed (matching a liability, protecting a spending stream) and use growth assets for the long-horizon real-return engine.

## Common Traps

### Buying TIPS Because Inflation Will Rise

Expected inflation is already in nominal yields. TIPS outperform only on an inflation surprise, not on inflation itself.

### Ignoring The Breakeven As The Decision Variable

The breakeven is the bar. Buying TIPS when breakevens are high requires a large upside surprise to win.

### Confusing Inflation Hedge With Inflation Forecast

TIPS are insurance against surprise, not a bet on a forecast. The market's breakeven is often a better forecast than any individual view.

### Overlooking Liquidity-Driven Breakeven Dislocation

Breakevens reflect a TIPS liquidity premium and can cheapen in stress, distorting the expectations signal.

### Mismatching TIPS Duration To The Horizon

Short TIPS do not hedge long-horizon inflation; long TIPS carry real-rate volatility. Match duration to the liability.

### Ignoring Tax On The Inflation Adjustment

Annual taxation of the inflation adjustment in taxable accounts can produce a negative after-tax real return despite a positive stated real yield.

### Treating TIPS As A Complete Inflation Solution

TIPS are a precise defensive hedge. Long-horizon real return still requires growth assets whose cash flows rise with inflation.
