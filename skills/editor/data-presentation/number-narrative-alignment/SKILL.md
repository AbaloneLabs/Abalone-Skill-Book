# Number-Narrative Alignment

When documents combine narrative prose with data, the two must align. The claims the text makes should match the numbers presented in tables, charts, or inline figures. The story told about the data should be accurate to what the data shows. Misalignment, where the prose claims one thing and the figures show another, undermines credibility and can mislead readers who do not cross-check. Number-narrative alignment is the discipline of verifying that text and data tell the same story. Editors who read prose and data separately, without cross-referencing, miss the contradictions that arise between them.

Use this skill when editing documents that combine narrative with data to verify alignment. It covers cross-referencing claims to figures, accurate data interpretation, and consistency between prose and presentation. The goal is a document where the narrative and the numbers support each other without contradiction.

## Core Rules

### Cross-Reference Every Numerical Claim To Its Source

Every numerical claim in the prose, a percentage, a total, a comparison, a trend, should trace to a source, whether a table, chart, cited study, or dataset. Claims without traceable sources cannot be verified. Cross-reference.

For each numerical claim in the text, identify its source. Does it come from a table or chart in the document, a cited reference, or original analysis? Flag claims that cannot be traced, which may be invented, misremembered, or misstated. Cross-referencing ensures every number has a basis and allows verification that the claim matches its source. This is tedious but essential, because numerical errors are common and damaging. A single misstated figure can undermine an entire document's credibility. Trace every number to its origin and verify the claim against it.

### Verify Prose Claims Match The Presented Data

When the prose makes claims about data presented in the document, those claims must match the data. A claim that "revenue grew 20 percent" must match a chart or table showing 20 percent growth. Verify matches.

Compare each claim about presented data against the data itself. Does the claim accurately reflect what the table or chart shows? Flag claims that overstate, understate, or contradict the presented data. Common mismatches include claiming a trend that the data does not clearly show, citing a percentage that differs from the figure, or describing a comparison that the data does not support. The prose and the data should agree precisely; any discrepancy is an error that careful readers will catch and that undermines trust. Verify each claim against the figure it describes.

### Check That Data Interpretation Is Accurate

Beyond matching specific numbers, the narrative's interpretation of the data must be accurate. A claim that data shows a causal relationship, a significant effect, or a meaningful pattern must be supportable from the data. Check interpretation.

Assess whether the narrative's interpretation of the data is accurate. Does the data support the conclusions drawn from it? Flag interpretations that go beyond what the data shows, such as inferring causation from correlation, claiming significance without statistical support, or generalizing from limited data. Also flag interpretations that understate what the data shows, missing significant patterns or effects. Accurate interpretation respects the data's limitations and strength; it does not inflate or minimize what the data can support. The narrative should help readers understand the data truly, not spin it toward a desired conclusion.

### Ensure The Narrative Explains Rather Than Repeats The Data

The narrative around data should explain and interpret, adding value beyond what readers could get from the figures alone. Merely repeating the numbers in prose adds no value and wastes space. Ensure value-add.

Assess what the narrative adds to the data. Does it explain the significance, context, implications, or reasons behind the numbers? Or does it merely restate what the table or chart already shows? Flag narrative that repeats data without interpretation, which is redundant. Recommend narrative that helps readers understand what the data means, why it matters, and what to take from it. The narrative and the data should complement: the data provides the evidence, the narrative provides the meaning. A narrative that only repeats fails its interpretive function.

### Verify Consistency Between Summary Statistics And Detail

Documents often present summary statistics in the prose and detailed data in tables or charts. These must be consistent. A summary that does not match the detail misleads. Verify consistency.

Compare summary statistics in the prose, such as averages, totals, or growth rates, against the detailed data in tables or charts. Does the summary accurately represent the detail? Flag summaries that conflict with the underlying data, such as an average that does not match the values averaged or a total that does not match the components. Also check that the summary is appropriate, not misleading by choice of measure; for example, an average that hides significant variation should be noted. Consistency between summary and detail ensures readers can trust both the overview and the specifics.

### Check That Time Periods And Definitions Match

Numerical claims depend on the time period and definitions they use. A claim about "annual revenue" must use the same period and definition as the data it references. Mismatches produce false comparisons. Check periods and definitions.

For each numerical claim, verify the time period and definitions match between prose and data. Does a claim about 2023 revenue draw from 2023 data, or does it mix years? Does a claim about "customers" use the same definition as the data's customer count? Flag mismatches in time periods, units, or definitions that produce inaccurate comparisons or claims. These mismatches are subtle but significant, because they make claims appear supported when they rest on inconsistent bases. Verify that every claim and its data share the same temporal scope and definitional basis.

### Ensure The Narrative Acknowledges Data Limitations

Data has limitations: sampling error, missing values, methodological constraints. The narrative should acknowledge limitations that affect interpretation, not present data as more certain than it is. Acknowledge limitations.

Assess whether the narrative acknowledges relevant data limitations. Where data is uncertain, preliminary, or limited in scope, does the prose note this? Flag narratives that present data with false precision or certainty, ignoring limitations that bear on interpretation. Recommend noting significant limitations, such as small samples, self-reported data, or incomplete coverage, that calibrate how readers should receive the numbers. Honest acknowledgment of limitations strengthens credibility and prevents overconfident decisions based on data that cannot support them.

## Common Traps

### Numerical Claims Without Traceable Sources

Trace every number to its origin. Untraceable claims may be wrong.

### Prose Claims Contradicting Presented Data

Cross-reference claims to figures. They must match precisely.

### Interpretation Beyond What Data Supports

Do not infer causation or significance the data cannot support.

### Narrative That Merely Repeats Data

Explain and interpret, adding value beyond restating the numbers.

### Summary Statistics Inconsistent With Detail

Verify summaries match the underlying data they represent.

### Time Period Or Definition Mismatches

Ensure claims and data share the same temporal scope and definitions.

### False Precision Ignoring Data Limitations

Acknowledge uncertainties and constraints that affect interpretation.

## Self-Check

Before treating number-narrative alignment as verified, confirm:

- Every numerical claim in the prose is traced to a source, whether table, chart, or reference.
- Each claim about presented data matches the data precisely, without overstatement or contradiction.
- The narrative's interpretation of the data is accurate and supported.
- The narrative explains and interprets the data, not merely repeats it.
- Summary statistics in the prose are consistent with detailed data in tables or charts.
- Time periods and definitions match between claims and their data sources.
- Data limitations relevant to interpretation are acknowledged in the narrative.
- No claim presents false precision or certainty the data cannot support.
- The narrative and the data tell the same story without contradiction.
- A reader cross-referencing prose and figures would find them consistent and mutually supporting.
