# Launch Readiness Verification

Launch readiness is the final verification that a work is prepared for publication in every dimension that matters. It is broader than content QA, because publication involves format, distribution, legal, and technical dependencies that content review does not cover. A work can be editorially perfect but fail to launch because the format is wrong for the platform, the metadata is missing, the distribution channels are not prepared, or a technical dependency is unmet. Launch readiness verification confirms that all these dimensions are ready, so publication proceeds without last-minute failures or post-launch problems. Editors who focus only on content readiness publish works that fail at the point of release for reasons content review never examined.

Use this skill as the final check before publication, after content QA and sign-off are complete. It covers verifying readiness across content, format, distribution, legal, and technical dimensions, and confirming no blocking issue remains. The goal is a launch that proceeds smoothly because every dependency has been verified.

## Core Rules

### Verify Readiness Across All Launch Dimensions

Launch readiness spans multiple dimensions, and each must be verified. Content readiness means the work is editorially complete and correct. Format readiness means the work is in the correct format for each publication channel. Distribution readiness means the channels are prepared to receive and publish the work. Legal readiness means all clearances and rights are secured. Technical readiness means systems and dependencies are functional. Verify each dimension.

Systematically check each dimension. For content, confirm the final version is locked and QA is complete. For format, confirm files are produced in required formats with correct specifications. For distribution, confirm channels, schedules, and logistics are arranged. For legal, confirm rights, permissions, and clearances are documented. For technical, confirm platforms, links, and integrations are tested. A launch is only as ready as its least-ready dimension, so all must be verified. Missing a dimension is how launches fail at the last moment.

### Confirm The Final Version Is Locked

Launch requires a locked final version, meaning the version that will be published is fixed and no further changes will be made. Changes after the version is locked introduce risk, because they may not receive full QA or may disrupt dependencies. Confirm the version is locked and that everyone understands no further changes are accepted.

Verify that the final version is identified, locked, and distributed to all parties who need it. Confirm that the locked version is the version that has passed QA and received sign-off. Communicate clearly that the version is locked and that changes are no longer accepted except through controlled change management. Any change after lock requires re-verification of affected dimensions. A locked version is the stable foundation that all other readiness checks depend on; without it, readiness is ambiguous.

### Verify Format And Platform Specifications

Each publication channel has format and platform specifications that must be met. Print requires specific file formats, color profiles, and bleed specifications. Digital platforms require specific file formats, metadata, and accessibility features. Distribution channels have their own requirements. Verify the work meets all applicable specifications.

For each publication channel, identify the required specifications. Verify the final files meet these specifications: file format, resolution, color space, metadata, accessibility features, and any channel-specific requirements. Test files against platform validators where available. Format or specification failures cause rejection at the point of distribution or defects in the published version. Verify specifications before launch, not discover failures at launch.

### Check Distribution And Logistics Readiness

Distribution and logistics must be prepared to receive and publish the work. Print runs must be scheduled, digital platforms must be configured, marketing and announcement plans must be aligned. Verify that distribution and logistics are ready to proceed in coordination with the publication.

Confirm distribution channels are prepared: print schedules, digital platform configurations, distribution partner readiness. Confirm the publication date or window is set and communicated to all parties. Confirm that marketing, announcements, and other launch activities are aligned with the publication schedule. Verify that any dependencies, such as inventory for print or server capacity for digital, are met. Distribution readiness ensures the work reaches its audience when published, rather than launching to an unprepared channel.

### Verify Legal And Rights Readiness

Legal and rights readiness means all necessary permissions, clearances, and rights are secured and documented before publication. Publishing without secured rights creates legal exposure that can force withdrawal or worse. Verify legal readiness as a launch dependency.

Confirm that all third-party content, including quotations, images, and data, has secured permissions or falls under fair use with documented rationale. Confirm that rights held are sufficient for the intended publication channels and territories. Confirm that any required legal reviews are complete and documented. Confirm that privacy, defamation, and other legal risks have been assessed and cleared. Legal readiness is a must-pass dimension; publishing without it is a risk that should never be taken under launch pressure.

### Test Technical Dependencies And Integrations

For digital publications especially, technical dependencies and integrations must be tested before launch. Links must resolve, interactive features must function, integrations with other systems must work, and the work must perform acceptably under expected load. Test technical dependencies to catch failures before they are public.

For digital works, test all links to confirm they resolve correctly. Test interactive features, forms, media, and any functionality. Test integrations with external systems. Verify performance under expected load, especially for high-traffic launches. Test across the platforms and devices the audience will use. For print-digital hybrids, verify any cross-references between formats. Technical failures at launch are highly visible and undermine confidence; testing before launch catches what can be caught.

### Confirm No Blocking Issue Remains

The final readiness determination is whether any blocking issue remains. A blocking issue is one that must be resolved before publication can proceed: a legal gap, a format failure, a missing sign-off, a technical failure. Confirm that no blocking issue remains, or if one does, that the launch decision accounts for it explicitly.

Review the status of all readiness dimensions and outstanding issues. Identify any blocking issues. For each blocking issue, determine whether it can be resolved before launch, requires a launch delay, or requires an explicit decision to launch despite the issue. An explicit decision to launch with a known blocking issue should be made by appropriate authority with full understanding of the risk, and documented. The default is that blocking issues block launch; launching over them is an exception that requires deliberate decision, not momentum.

## Common Traps

### Focusing Only On Content Readiness

Launch spans multiple dimensions. Verify content, format, distribution, legal, and technical readiness.

### Launching Without A Locked Version

Changes after lock introduce risk. Confirm the final version is fixed.

### Missing Format And Platform Specifications

Specification failures cause distribution rejection or defects. Verify against requirements.

### Unprepared Distribution Channels

Channels must be ready to receive and publish. Confirm logistics and schedules.

### Publishing Without Secured Rights

Legal gaps create exposure. Verify permissions and clearances before launch.

### Untested Technical Dependencies

Technical failures at launch are highly visible. Test links, features, and integrations.

### Launching Over Blocking Issues By Momentum

Blocking issues should block. Launching over them requires deliberate, documented decision.

## Self-Check

Before treating launch readiness verification as complete, verify:

- Readiness has been verified across content, format, distribution, legal, and technical dimensions.
- The final version is locked, with no further changes accepted except through controlled process.
- Final files meet the format and platform specifications for each publication channel.
- Distribution channels, schedules, and logistics are prepared and coordinated.
- All permissions, clearances, and rights are secured and documented.
- Legal reviews for privacy, defamation, and other risks are complete.
- Technical dependencies, including links, features, and integrations, have been tested.
- Performance and cross-platform functionality have been verified for digital works.
- Any blocking issues have been identified and either resolved or explicitly decided upon.
- The launch decision is based on verified readiness, with no blocking issue overlooked.
