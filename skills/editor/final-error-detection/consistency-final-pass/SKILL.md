# Consistency Final Pass

A long editing process accumulates changes, and each change creates the possibility of inconsistency. A term standardized early may revert in a late revision. A formatting convention established in chapter one may drift by chapter ten. A cross-reference may break when a section is renumbered. The final consistency pass verifies that all decisions hold across the entire work after every correction has been applied. It is the last opportunity to catch the drift, reversion, and contradiction that survive earlier passes because they were introduced by later changes. Editors who skip the final consistency pass publish works with errors that were created by the editing process itself.

Use this skill as the final check before publication, after all content and corrections are in place. It covers verifying style consistency, terminology consistency, formatting consistency, and cross-reference integrity across the complete work. The goal is a work where every decision holds from first page to last.

## Core Rules

### Verify Style Consistency Across The Entire Work

Style decisions made during copy editing must hold across the entire work, but late changes and long texts create drift. A hyphenation decision, a capitalization choice, a spelling variant, or a punctuation convention established early may not be applied in sections revised later. The final pass verifies that every style decision is applied consistently throughout.

Compile the style sheet with all decisions made during editing. Work through the text verifying each decision holds in every instance. Use search to check variable forms and confirm only the chosen form appears. Pay special attention to sections that were revised late or added last, as these are where drift occurs. Check that the style sheet itself is complete and reflects all decisions, so nothing was decided informally and lost. Style consistency means the work reads as governed by a single standard from start to finish.

### Check Terminology And Naming Consistency

Terminology and naming consistency is a subset of style but warrants dedicated attention because inconsistencies here are highly visible. A term defined one way early may be used differently later. A character or entity name may shift in spelling or form. Competing terms for the same concept may reappear after reconciliation. Verify terminology and naming across the entire work.

Using the glossary or terminology list, verify each term is used consistently with its defined meaning and form. Check that names of people, organizations, places, and entities are spelled and styled consistently throughout. Verify that reconciled terms have not reverted to competing forms. Check that abbreviations are used consistently after their introduction. Terminology drift often occurs because late revisions draw on earlier language that predates reconciliation, so search for superseded forms to catch reversions.

### Verify Formatting And Structural Consistency

Formatting conventions, heading styles, list treatment, block quotations, and other structural elements must be consistent across the work. A heading style used in early chapters may drift in later ones. List formatting may vary. Block quotation treatment may shift. The final pass verifies structural and formatting consistency.

Check that heading levels are styled consistently throughout, with parallel capitalization and formatting. Check that lists are treated consistently, bulleted versus numbered, punctuation, and capitalization. Check that block quotations are formatted consistently and that run-in quotations use consistent quotation mark and punctuation conventions. Check that emphasis, italics, and bold are used consistently for the same purposes. Check front and back matter formatting against body conventions. Formatting consistency gives the work a unified visual and structural surface.

### Check Cross-References And Internal Links

Cross-references and internal links are fragile because they depend on locations that may change during editing. A reference to "chapter 4" may be wrong if chapters were renumbered. A "see page 123" reference may be off if text was added or cut. A figure or table reference may break if numbering changed. The final pass verifies all cross-references resolve correctly.

Compile a list of all cross-references: chapter and section references, page references, figure and table references, footnote and endnote references, and any hyperlinks. Verify each reference points to the correct target. Check that chapter, section, figure, and table numbers match the actual numbering after all revisions. For page references, verify against final pagination if available. Check that hyperlinks resolve and that anchor text is accurate. Broken cross-references are immediately visible to readers who follow them and signal careless production.

### Verify Front And Back Matter Integration

Front and back matter, the title page, table of contents, lists of figures and tables, preface, appendices, index, and other ancillary material, must be consistent with the body and with each other. These elements are often produced separately and may not reflect final changes. The final pass verifies their integration.

Check the table of contents against actual chapter and section titles and numbers. Check lists of figures and tables against the actual figures and tables. Check the title page for correct title, author, and other details. Check running heads and page numbers for consistency. Check that the preface or introduction reflects the final work. Check appendices and supplementary material for consistency with the body. Back matter like the index must reflect final pagination and terminology. Front and back matter integration is often overlooked because these elements feel separate, but inconsistencies here are visible at the work's boundaries.

### Reconcile Corrections With The Whole

Every correction made during editing has the potential to introduce inconsistency elsewhere. A term change should propagate to all mentions. A revised figure should update all references to it. A renumbered section should update all cross-references. The final pass verifies that corrections have been reconciled with the whole work, not just applied locally.

Review the log of corrections made during editing. For each correction that has scope beyond its immediate location, verify the change propagated correctly. Check that a standardized term replaced all instances of the superseded form. Check that a revised statistic updated all mentions. Check that structural changes updated all dependent references. Corrections applied locally but not propagated create the worst kind of inconsistency because the work contradicts itself. Reconciliation ensures the work reflects a single, coherent state.

### Use Systematic Search To Catch Drift

Manual reading catches many inconsistencies, but systematic search catches drift that reading misses. Search for variant forms, superseded terms, and likely error patterns to verify they do not appear. Search is especially powerful for consistency because it is exhaustive in a way reading is not.

For each style or terminology decision, search for variant and superseded forms to confirm they do not appear. Search for common error patterns like double spaces, inconsistent quotation marks, or broken cross-reference patterns. Search for terms known to have been revised to verify all instances updated. Document the searches performed so the verification is auditable. Systematic search complements reading and provides confidence that consistency holds exhaustively, not just in the passages that were read carefully.

## Common Traps

### Skipping The Final Pass After Corrections

Corrections introduce new inconsistencies. Verify the whole after all changes are applied.

### Drift In Late-Revised Sections

Sections revised late may not follow decisions made early. Check late changes especially.

### Reversion To Superseded Forms

Reconciled terms and standardized styles may revert in revisions. Search for superseded forms.

### Broken Cross-References After Renumbering

Structural changes break references. Verify all cross-references resolve to correct targets.

### Overlooking Front And Back Matter

These elements feel separate but must integrate with the body. Check them explicitly.

### Local Corrections Not Propagated

A change applied in one place must update all dependent mentions. Reconcile with the whole.

### Relying Only On Reading

Reading misses drift that search catches. Use systematic search for exhaustive verification.

## Self-Check

Before treating the consistency final pass as complete, verify:

- All style decisions from the style sheet are verified to hold across the entire work.
- Terminology and naming are consistent, with no reversion to superseded forms.
- Formatting and structural elements are consistent throughout, including headings, lists, and quotations.
- All cross-references, including chapter, page, figure, and table references, resolve correctly.
- Front and back matter are integrated and consistent with the body and each other.
- Corrections have been reconciled with the whole, not just applied locally.
- Systematic search has confirmed variant and superseded forms do not appear.
- Late-revised sections have been checked especially for drift from established decisions.
- The style sheet is complete and reflects all decisions made during editing.
- The work reads as governed by a single, coherent standard from first page to last.
