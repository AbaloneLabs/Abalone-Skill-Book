# Tenant Landlord Intake And Lease Fit

Rental work can look simpler than sales because the transaction value is lower, but the legal, fair housing, habitability, deposit, screening, and occupancy risks can be immediate. Agents often collect rent range and move date while missing landlord criteria, tenant rights, property management boundaries, local lease requirements, and discriminatory screening risk. This skill helps the agent qualify rental needs and representation fit before advertising, showing, or drafting lease-related paperwork.

## Core Rules

### Clarify Who The Agent Represents

Identify whether the agent represents the landlord, tenant, both under a permitted arrangement, or only provides listing access. Duties, confidentiality, compensation, and advice boundaries differ. Leasing leads often come through portals or listing inquiries, so agency status can be unclear.

Do not provide strategic advice to both sides without understanding agency rules and brokerage policy. If the agent's role is limited, explain that clearly.

### Define Lease Objectives And Constraints

For tenants, clarify move date, lease length, rent budget, income documentation, credit or screening concerns, occupants, pets or assistance animals, parking, commute, accessibility, utilities, work-from-home needs, storage, and renewal expectations. For landlords, clarify target lease term, occupancy date, rent, included utilities, pet policy, screening criteria, property condition, maintenance responsibilities, HOA rules, and management plan.

Separate lawful objective criteria from preferences that could create discrimination risk. "No children" or "no Section 8" may be unlawful depending on jurisdiction; verify protected categories and source-of-income rules.

### Assess Rent Affordability Without Acting As A Lender

Tenants need to understand rent, deposits, application fees, utilities, insurance, parking, pet fees where lawful, move-in costs, and renewal risk. Landlords need consistent, lawful screening criteria and documentation. The agent can explain process and costs but should avoid making legal conclusions about eligibility or guarantees.

A tenant who can pay first month but not deposits or utilities may not be ready. A landlord with informal criteria may create fair housing risk.

### Identify Property And Habitability Issues Early

Rental properties involve habitability, safety, appliances, heating or cooling where required, locks, smoke or CO detectors where required, pests, water intrusion, utilities, lead-based paint disclosures where applicable, and local inspection or registration requirements. Agents should not promise compliance without verification.

If the property condition is questionable, escalate to landlord, property manager, broker, inspector, or local authority as appropriate. Do not market an unsafe property casually.

### Understand Screening And Application Process

A lawful process should define application steps, required documentation, fees, criteria, decision timeline, privacy handling, and how multiple applicants are treated. Criteria should be applied consistently. Sensitive information should be collected through approved systems.

Do not allow landlords to screen based on protected traits, stereotypes, or informal impressions. If the landlord asks for improper screening, redirect and escalate.

### Match Lease Term To Real Use

Lease length, renewal rights, early termination, subletting, roommates, guests, pets, smoking, parking, storage, maintenance, utilities, landscaping, access, and HOA rules must fit the parties' needs. A tenant planning remote work, frequent guests, or pets may need specific terms. A landlord planning to sell or move back in may need lawful termination planning.

Avoid assuming standard lease terms fit every situation. Local law may limit what can be charged or required.

### Set Property Management Boundaries

Some agents only lease the property; others manage after move-in. Clarify who handles rent collection, repairs, emergencies, notices, renewals, violations, deposits, move-out, and disputes. Tenants and landlords often assume the leasing agent remains responsible.

If the agent is not the property manager, document handoff and contact points before lease start.

### Document Intake And Fit Decisions

Keep records of agency status, criteria, client needs, fair housing redirections, screening process, property condition concerns, lease assumptions, and referrals. Rental disputes can arise quickly, and clear records matter even when commission is small.

Do not treat leasing as informal because the timeline is fast.

## Common Traps

- Treating rental intake as only rent range, bedrooms, and move date.
- Failing to clarify whether the agent represents landlord, tenant, both, or neither.
- Allowing landlord screening preferences that violate fair housing or local source-of-income rules.
- Ignoring deposits, utilities, insurance, parking, and move-in cash needs.
- Marketing a rental without checking habitability or local registration issues.
- Collecting sensitive applicant data through casual email or notes.
- Applying screening criteria inconsistently; forgetting assistance-animal and accommodation issues
- Assuming the leasing agent will manage the property after move-in; using sales transaction habits for leases without checking rental law and forms

## Self-Check

- Is agency status and representation scope clear?
- Are tenant and landlord needs separated into lawful criteria and preferences?
- Are rent, deposits, utilities, insurance, parking, and move-in costs considered?
- Are property condition, habitability, and local compliance issues flagged?
- Is screening process objective, consistent, privacy-conscious, and lawful?
- Are assistance animal and accommodation issues escalated appropriately?
- Do lease terms fit actual occupancy, pets, utilities, maintenance, and renewal needs?
- Are property management responsibilities and handoff contacts clear?
- Are fair housing redirections and client decisions documented?
- Would the rental file support a consistent, lawful leasing process?
