# Move In Condition Deposits And Handoff

Rental move-in is the point where marketing promises become tenancy reality. Disputes often start with unclear condition, missing keys, unresolved repairs, deposit confusion, utility gaps, or no handoff to management. Agents may consider the lease complete once signatures are done, but tenants and landlords still need a controlled possession transfer. This skill helps the agent manage move-in, deposits, condition records, and responsibility handoff.

## Core Rules

### Confirm Lease Execution Before Possession

Before keys or access are released, confirm the lease is fully signed, required payments are received or verified, move-in date is authorized, insurance or utility requirements are met, and any required disclosures or addenda are delivered. If local law or brokerage policy allows exceptions, document approval.

Do not release possession casually because the tenant is waiting with movers. Early access can create liability and enforcement problems.

### Handle Deposits And Fees According To Rules

Security deposits, pet deposits where lawful, application fees, move-in fees, prepaid rent, and cleaning fees may be regulated by amount, timing, account handling, receipts, interest, disclosures, and return deadlines. Requirements vary widely. The agent should follow landlord, property manager, brokerage, trust account, and legal guidance.

Do not hold deposit funds informally or mix them with unrelated money. Deposit errors can become serious compliance issues.

### Document Move-In Condition Thoroughly

Move-in condition should be documented with photos, video where allowed, checklist, utility status, appliance condition, keys, remotes, meters, smoke or CO detectors where applicable, cleanliness, existing damage, and tenant acknowledgments. Tenants should know how and when to report additional issues.

Condition documentation protects both sides. Without it, deposit disputes become memory contests.

### Verify Repairs And Habitability Items

If the landlord promised repairs before move-in, confirm completion, receipts or photos where appropriate, and tenant acceptance if needed. Check that essential services, locks, heat or cooling where required, water, appliances included in lease, and safety items are ready.

If required repairs are incomplete, escalate before possession. Moving a tenant into an unready unit creates immediate conflict.

### Coordinate Keys, Access, And Utilities

Handoff may include keys, mailbox, fobs, gate access, parking, storage, alarm codes, smart locks, laundry cards, amenity passes, trash instructions, HOA rules, and emergency contacts. Confirm who transfers utilities, when service starts, and whether the tenant or landlord is responsible for setup.

Access confusion can make a lawful move-in feel like a failed service experience.

### Clarify Maintenance And Communication Channels

Tenants need to know how to request repairs, report emergencies, pay rent, contact management, handle after-hours issues, and submit notices. Landlords need to know when the leasing agent's role ends and the property manager or owner takes over.

Do not let tenants keep contacting the leasing agent for management issues if that is not the agent's role. Provide a warm handoff.

### Respect Privacy And Entry Rules After Move-In

Once possession transfers, the property is the tenant's home. Showings, inspections, repairs, owner visits, and agent access must follow lease terms and local law. Landlords and agents should not treat retained keys as permission for casual entry.

Explain the boundary to owners who are new landlords.

### Plan Deposit Return Documentation From Day One

Move-in records should support fair move-out evaluation later. Keep lease, deposit receipt, condition report, photos, repair records, tenant notices, and handoff details organized. If a property manager takes over, deliver the complete file.

A clean move-in file reduces deposit disputes months later.

### Confirm Rent Proration And First Payment Period

Move-in often involves prorated rent, mid-month starts, prepaid rent, concessions, free-rent periods, or delayed possession. Confirm the amount, period covered, due date, payment method, ledger entry, and who explains it to the tenant.

Payment confusion at move-in damages trust immediately and can make later ledger disputes harder to resolve.

## Common Traps

- Releasing keys before lease execution and funds are properly handled.
- Misunderstanding local rules for security deposits, fees, receipts, or trust accounts.
- Skipping move-in condition photos and checklist.
- Letting promised repairs remain vague or incomplete.
- Forgetting fobs, mail keys, parking, storage, alarm codes, or utility transfer.
- Failing to tell tenants who handles repairs and emergencies; letting landlords enter after move-in without proper notice or right
- Treating the leasing agent as ongoing property manager without agreement; losing deposit and condition records during handoff
- Ignoring habitability issues because the tenant accepted the keys; starting the tenancy with unclear prorated rent, concession, or first payment records

## Self-Check

- Is the lease fully executed before possession is released?
- Are deposits, fees, receipts, and funds handled according to rules and policy?
- Is move-in condition documented with photos, checklist, and tenant reporting process?
- Are promised repairs and essential habitability items complete or escalated?
- Are keys, fobs, access codes, parking, storage, utilities, and contacts ready?
- Does the tenant know how to request maintenance and report emergencies?
- Does the landlord know the agent's role after move-in?
- Are privacy and entry rules respected once possession transfers?
- Is the move-in file complete for future deposit and condition disputes?
- Would both landlord and tenant understand the handoff responsibilities?; are prorated rent, concessions, and first payment records clear?
