# Escrow Closing And Settlement Coordination

Closing coordination is where many earlier promises become operational. Title, escrow, attorneys, lenders, brokers, buyers, sellers, HOA, payoff departments, insurers, and municipalities must align before ownership changes. Agents can mistakenly treat closing as something handled by title or escrow, then discover late that a payoff, signature, wire, repair receipt, lender condition, or settlement figure is blocking recording. This skill helps the agent keep closing dependencies visible without replacing settlement, legal, or lending professionals.

## Core Rules

### Build A Closing Dependency List

Identify what must be complete before closing: title clearance, lender approval, appraisal, insurance, HOA documents, municipal certificates, repair receipts, final walkthrough, settlement statement, payoff, prorations, seller documents, buyer funds, signatures, recording, possession, keys, utilities, and commission instructions. The exact list depends on jurisdiction and transaction type.

Do not assume that because the closing date is scheduled, every dependency is ready. Closing dates can be aspirational until funding and recording requirements are satisfied.

### Track Title And Payoff Issues Early

Title clearance can be delayed by liens, judgments, unreleased mortgages, probate, trust authority, divorce orders, entity documents, boundary issues, HOA violations, unpaid assessments, solar liens, or incorrect names. Seller payoff statements can also be delayed or inaccurate.

Ask title or escrow for status early and escalate unresolved items. A title issue discovered near closing often forces extensions or emergency legal review.

### Coordinate Lender And Settlement Timelines

Loan closing requires lender conditions, clear-to-close, closing disclosure timing, final employment checks, appraisal completion, insurance binders, title work, and signed documents. The settlement side may need loan package, wire, funding number, or recording authorization.

Agents should not give lending instructions, but should track whether the lender and settlement provider are aligned. A buyer who schedules movers before clear-to-close may face avoidable disruption.

### Review Settlement Figures For Obvious Fit

Clients should review settlement statements, closing disclosures, prorations, credits, commissions, concessions, taxes, HOA fees, repairs, rent-backs, security deposits, and payoffs with the appropriate professional. The agent can help compare figures against contract terms and known amendments, while directing legal, tax, or settlement questions to the right expert.

Look for obvious mismatches: missing credit, wrong commission, omitted repair concession, wrong possession charge, incorrect seller-paid cost, or unexpected fee. Do not certify the statement as legally correct.

### Protect Against Wire And Payment Fraud

Closing involves large funds and high fraud risk. Remind clients to verify wire instructions through trusted, independently confirmed channels. Warn them not to rely on changed instructions by email, text, or suspicious links. Follow brokerage policy for communicating fraud warnings.

If there is any doubt, pause and verify. Fast action after fraud is difficult; prevention is the control.

### Keep Extensions And Delays Formal

If closing cannot occur on time, identify the reason, responsible party, new expected date, possession impact, rate-lock impact, seller payoff impact, moving impact, and whether a written extension is required. Do not rely on informal tolerance if contract rights are affected.

Closing delays can create default risk, per diem charges, expired documents, or failed moves. Treat them as contract issues, not scheduling annoyances.

### Coordinate Final Walk-Through And Repair Proof

Final walk-through should happen close enough to closing to confirm condition, agreed repairs, included items, vacancy or occupancy status, and no new damage. If repairs were negotiated, collect receipts, permits, warranties, or reinspection as agreed. If problems appear, escalate before funds and recording occur where possible.

The agent should not tell the client to ignore unresolved issues to keep the closing on schedule.

### Confirm Recording, Funding, And Possession Sequence

In some jurisdictions, signing is not closing. Funding and recording may happen later. Possession may transfer at recording, at a specific time, after seller rent-back, or under another agreement. Make sure clients know when keys, garage openers, access codes, utilities, and possession actually transfer.

Misunderstanding closing sequence can create lockout, early possession, insurance, or liability problems.

## Common Traps

- Assuming title, escrow, or attorney will catch every contract mismatch.
- Treating scheduled signing as guaranteed closing.
- Missing title, payoff, HOA, municipal, or entity authority issues until late.
- Ignoring settlement statement errors because the numbers look official.
- Failing to warn clients about wire fraud.
- Letting closing delays proceed without written extension or impact analysis.
- Scheduling possession or movers before funding and recording are confirmed; closing before agreed repairs, receipts, or final walk-through issues are resolved
- Giving legal, tax, or settlement advice beyond competence; sending vague updates that hide unresolved closing blockers

## Self-Check

- Is there a closing dependency list for this transaction?
- Are title, payoff, HOA, municipal, and authority issues tracked early?
- Are lender and settlement timelines aligned?
- Has the client reviewed closing figures with obvious contract terms checked?
- Are wire instructions and payment processes verified through safe channels?
- Are delays handled through proper written extension or escalation?
- Is final walk-through timed to verify condition and agreed repairs?
- Are funding, recording, key transfer, and possession timing clear?
- Has the agent avoided replacing title, escrow, lender, attorney, or tax professionals?
- Would all parties know what remains before closing can actually occur?
