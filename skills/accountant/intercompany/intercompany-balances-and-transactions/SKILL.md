# Intercompany Balances And Transactions

In a group of entities, transactions between members are real to each entity but are not real to the group. A sale from a subsidiary to its parent is revenue and cost of sales to the individual entities, but to the consolidated group it is just an internal movement of inventory that produced no external revenue. Intercompany balances, loans, transfers, and pricing must be identified, documented, and eliminated on consolidation, and the elimination must be complete and symmetric: if you eliminate a receivable you must eliminate the payable, and if you eliminate profit you must adjust the asset it is embedded in. Agents often eliminate only one side, leave intercompany interest uneliminated against the corresponding finance income, or forget that intercompany loans create related-party disclosure and tax-transfer-pricing obligations. The result is consolidated accounts that do not actually consolidate.

Use this skill before recording intercompany transactions, reconciling intercompany accounts, preparing consolidation elimination entries, assessing intercompany pricing, or eliminating intercompany profit. The goal is to prevent the agent from treating intercompany activity as ordinary external transactions and missing the elimination, reconciliation, and documentation requirements.

Intercompany and consolidation accounting differs between US GAAP, IFRS, and local GAAP, and intercompany pricing interacts with transfer-pricing tax rules that vary by jurisdiction. This skill gives the general framework; the applicable standard and tax rules govern. For cross-border intercompany loans, transfer pricing, and complex group structures, involve a qualified accountant and tax professional. This is structural guidance, not a determination for a specific group.

## Core Rules

### Identify And Document Every Intercompany Transaction

Intercompany transactions include sales of goods and services between group entities; intercompany loans, interest, and fees; management charges and cost allocations; transfers of assets and intellectual property; dividends and capital contributions between entities; and guarantees or cash-pooling arrangements. Each must be identified, recorded, and documented at the entity level.

Maintain an intercompany register that captures: the transacting entities; the nature of the transaction; the amount and currency; the terms (including pricing basis and settlement terms); the accounting treatment in each entity; and the elimination required on consolidation. Documentation supports both the consolidation and the related-party and transfer-pricing disclosures. Undocumented intercompany activity cannot be reliably eliminated.

### Reconcile Intercompany Balances Before Eliminating

Every intercompany receivable in one entity must equal the corresponding payable in the other, and every intercompany sale must equal the corresponding purchase. Reconcile intercompany accounts each period before consolidation. Differences arise from timing (one entity recorded, the other has not), currency translation (different exchange rates applied), errors, or unrecorded accruals.

Investigate and resolve mismatches before eliminating. Eliminating unmatched balances leaves a residual that distorts the consolidated balance sheet. Common causes: goods in transit recorded by seller but not buyer; interest accrued by one party but not the other; management charges raised but not accepted; and cut-off differences at period-end.

### Eliminate Intercompany Balances Symmetrically

On consolidation, eliminate all intercompany receivables against the corresponding payables, intercompany revenue against the corresponding cost of sales or expense, intercompany interest income against the corresponding interest expense, and intercompany dividends against the corresponding investment income. The elimination must be symmetric and complete.

Eliminate in full for wholly owned subsidiaries. For partially owned subsidiaries, eliminate in full and then allocate the noncontrolling interest's share of any eliminated profit or loss (see the profit elimination skill). Do not eliminate only the parent's share; consolidation eliminates 100 percent of intercompany activity, with noncontrolling interest adjustments made separately.

### Eliminate Intercompany Profit On Unresolved Transactions

When one group entity sells goods or assets to another and those goods or assets are still held within the group at period-end, the profit recorded by the selling entity is not real to the group and must be eliminated. Eliminate the unrealized profit against inventory, fixed assets, or the relevant asset, with a corresponding reduction in the selling entity's profit (see the profit elimination skill for downstream, upstream, and noncontrolling interest mechanics).

The profit elimination applies to inventory, fixed assets, and any asset still held within the group. It also applies to impairment: if an asset acquired from a group entity is impaired, the original intercompany profit must be considered in the impairment calculation.

### Account For Intercompany Loans And Interest

Intercompany loans create a receivable in one entity and a payable in the other, with interest income and expense on each side. Eliminate the principal balances against each other and the interest income against the interest expense on consolidation. If the loan is not at market rate, assess whether it should be measured at fair value or treated as an equity contribution, depending on the substance and the applicable standard.

For long-term intercompany loans in foreign currencies, currency translation differences arise in each entity and must be eliminated or reconciled on consolidation. Document the loan terms, the interest rate, the repayment schedule, and whether the loan is genuinely debt or equity in substance.

### Apply Consistent Treatment To Intercompany Pricing And Cost Allocations

Intercompany management charges, cost allocations, and transfer prices should follow a consistent and supportable basis, ideally aligned with the group's transfer-pricing policy. Charges that are arbitrary, retroactively adjusted, or not supported by documentation create reconciliation difficulties, tax risks, and audit findings.

Document the pricing methodology for each category of intercompany transaction (cost-plus, market-based, negotiated). Apply it consistently across periods. Changes in methodology should be documented and their effect on intercompany balances explained. Inconsistent pricing makes reconciliation and elimination unreliable.

### Distinguish Current Versus Deferred Treatment On Eliminations

Some intercompany items are eliminated as current-period adjustments (revenue, expense, current balances). Others have a deferred or balance-sheet effect (unrealized profit deferred in inventory, intercompany goodwill, fair-value adjustments on combination). Identify which category each elimination falls into, because the timing and the accounts affected differ.

Unrealized profit on inventory is a deferred item that reverses when the inventory is sold externally. Intercompany fair-value adjustments amortize over the useful life of the asset. Match the elimination treatment to the nature of the item rather than applying a single blanket elimination.

### Disclose Related-Party And Intercompany Activity

Disclose related-party transactions and balances, including the identity of related parties, the nature of the relationship, the transaction amounts, outstanding balances, commitments, and guarantees. Intercompany activity is related-party activity and triggers disclosure even though it is eliminated on consolidation.

Users rely on related-party disclosure to understand the group's structure and the dependence on internal transactions. Missing the disclosure, or eliminating the balances without disclosing the underlying activity, leaves the financial statements noncompliant.

## Common Traps

### Eliminating Only One Side Of An Intercompany Balance

Eliminating the receivable without the corresponding payable (or vice versa) leaves a residual on the consolidated balance sheet. Eliminations must be symmetric and reconcile to the underlying accounts.

### Failing To Reconcile Before Eliminating

Eliminating unmatched intercompany balances leaves errors embedded in the consolidated accounts. Reconcile and resolve differences first, then eliminate.

### Forgetting Intercompany Interest On Loans

Intercompany loans generate interest income and expense that must be eliminated against each other. Leaving intercompany interest in the consolidated income statement overstates both income and expense and distorts net finance costs.

### Leaving Unrealized Intercompany Profit In The Group

Profit on sales between group entities where the asset is still held within the group is not real to the group. Failing to eliminate it overstates consolidated profit and the carrying amount of the asset.

### Treating Non-Market Intercompany Loans As Ordinary Debt

An intercompany loan at zero interest or below-market terms may be an equity contribution in substance. Recording it as ordinary debt misclassifies the instrument and misstates interest.

### Inconsistent Or Undocumented Transfer Pricing

Arbitrary or retroactively adjusted intercompany charges cannot be reliably reconciled or eliminated and create transfer-pricing tax risk. Document and apply a consistent methodology.

### Missing Related-Party Disclosure

Eliminating intercompany balances does not remove the related-party disclosure obligation. Failing to disclose related-party transactions and balances leaves the financial statements noncompliant.

## Self-Check

- Is every intercompany transaction identified, documented, and recorded in an intercompany register with entities, nature, amount, terms, and elimination required?
- Are intercompany receivables, payables, revenue, expense, interest, and dividends reconciled between entities before elimination?
- Are intercompany balances and income statement items eliminated symmetrically and in full, with noncontrolling interest adjustments made separately for partially owned subsidiaries?
- Is unrealized intercompany profit on inventory, fixed assets, and other assets still held within the group eliminated against the relevant asset?
- Are intercompany loan principal and interest eliminated, with non-market loans assessed for substance as debt or equity?
- Is intercompany pricing and cost allocation on a consistent, documented basis aligned with the group transfer-pricing policy?
- Are eliminations classified correctly as current-period adjustments or deferred balance-sheet items, with matching treatment?
- Do intercompany accounts reconcile across currencies, with translation differences identified and resolved?
- Are related-party transactions, balances, commitments, and guarantees disclosed despite elimination on consolidation?
- Has a qualified accountant or tax professional reviewed cross-border intercompany loans, transfer pricing, and complex group eliminations?
