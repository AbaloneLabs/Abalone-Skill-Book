# Earnings Per Share And Comprehensive Income

Earnings per share (EPS) and comprehensive income extend the income statement to address two user needs: per-share performance (for shareholders and valuation) and total performance including unrealized items (for a complete view of equity changes). EPS calculation involves the weighted average share count and the complex identification of potentially dilutive instruments (options, warrants, convertibles). Comprehensive income involves classifying items as profit or loss versus OCI, and within OCI as recyclable or non-recyclable. Agents often miscalculate the weighted average shares (failing to adjust for stock splits and dividends retroactively), apply the dilution test incorrectly (including anti-dilutive instruments), or misclassify OCI items.

Use this skill before calculating EPS, identifying dilutive instruments, preparing the statement of comprehensive income, classifying OCI items, or advising on the two-statement versus one-statement approach. The goal is to prevent the agent from producing EPS that is arithmetically wrong or that includes anti-dilutive instruments, and from misclassifying comprehensive income items.

EPS rules are in ASC 260 (US GAAP) and IAS 33 (IFRS); comprehensive income rules are in ASC 220 (US GAAP) and IAS 1 (IFRS). This skill provides the general framework; the applicable standard governs the detail. For entities with complex capital structures, involve a qualified accountant or auditor. This is structural guidance, not a determination for a specific entity.

## Core Rules

### Calculate Basic EPS As Profit Attributable To Ordinary Shareholders Divided By Weighted Average Shares

Basic EPS equals profit or loss attributable to ordinary shareholders (net income minus preference dividends and other amounts attributable to preference shares) divided by the weighted average number of ordinary shares outstanding during the period. The weighted average accounts for shares issued or bought back during the period, weighted by the portion of the period they were outstanding.

Shares issued or repurchased are weighted from the transaction date. However, shares issued in a stock split or stock dividend (or a bonus issue) are adjusted retroactively as if they were outstanding from the beginning of the earliest period presented. This retroactive adjustment is critical — failing to adjust for a split or bonus issue understates the share count and overstates EPS.

### Calculate Diluted EPS By Including All Potentially Dilutive Ordinary Shares

Diluted EPS adjusts both the numerator (adding back amounts related to dilutive potential ordinary shares, such as interest on convertible debt and dividends on convertible preference shares) and the denominator (adding the weighted average dilutive potential ordinary shares). The objective is to show the EPS that would have resulted if all dilutive instruments had been converted.

Apply the treasury stock method for options and warrants (the proceeds from exercise are assumed to buy shares at the average market price, and only the net increase in shares is added to the denominator). Apply the if-converted method for convertible debt and convertible preference shares (assume conversion at the beginning of the period or issuance date, add back the after-tax interest or dividends to the numerator, and add the shares to the denominator).

### Rank And Include Only Dilutive Instruments, Excluding Anti-Dilutive Ones

Each potentially dilutive instrument is tested for dilution: if including it decreases EPS (relative to basic EPS), it is dilutive and included; if it increases EPS, it is anti-dilutive and excluded. Rank instruments from most dilutive to least dilutive and include them in that order, stopping when an instrument becomes anti-dilutive.

Including an anti-dilutive instrument overstates diluted EPS (makes it look better than it should be). The dilution test must be applied to each instrument individually and in sequence. An instrument that is dilutive in one period may be anti-dilutive in another — retest each period.

### Present Basic And Diluted EPS On The Face With Equal Prominence

Basic and diluted EPS are presented on the face of the income statement (or statement of comprehensive income) with equal prominence, for profit or loss from continuing operations and for total profit or loss attributable to ordinary shareholders. Disclose the reconciliation of the numerators and denominators (profit attributable, weighted average shares, dilutive effect) in the notes.

### Prepare Comprehensive Income As Profit Or Loss Plus Other Comprehensive Income

Comprehensive income is the sum of profit or loss for the period and other comprehensive income (OCI) for the period. Present comprehensive income either in a single statement of comprehensive income (with profit or loss and OCI sections) or in two statements (an income statement and a statement of comprehensive income beginning with profit or loss). Under IFRS, the two-statement approach is permitted; under US GAAP, an entity may use a single continuous statement or two separate statements.

Total comprehensive income flows to equity (split between retained earnings for profit or loss and accumulated OCI for OCI items).

### Classify OCI Items As Recyclable Or Non-Recyclable

OCI items are classified as those that may be recycled to profit or loss (reclassified) and those that will not be recycled. Recyclable items include: foreign currency translation adjustments (recycled on disposal of the foreign operation), unrealized gains and losses on available-for-sale debt securities (recycled on sale, under frameworks that maintain this category), and cash flow hedges (recycled when the hedged item affects profit or loss). Non-recyclable items include: remeasurements of defined benefit plans (under IAS 19 and ASC 715) and changes in revaluation surplus for PP&E and intangibles under the revaluation model (IFRS).

Present the two categories separately within OCI. Disclose the amounts and the recycling policy. The recyclable/non-recyclable split helps users understand which OCI items will eventually flow through profit or loss.

### Apply The Tax Effect To OCI Items

OCI items are presented net of related tax. Allocate the tax effect to each OCI item (the tax on the foreign currency translation adjustment, the tax on the pension remeasurement). Present the amounts net of tax on the face, and disclose the pre-tax amount and tax effect in the notes.

Failing to apply tax to OCI items overstates the OCI amount. Apply the entity's tax rate consistently to each item.

## Common Traps

### Failing To Retroactively Adjust Shares For Stock Splits And Dividends

Shares from a split or bonus issue must be adjusted retroactively as if outstanding from the beginning. Omitting this adjustment understates the share count and overstates EPS.

### Including Anti-Dilutive Instruments In Diluted EPS

An instrument that increases EPS is anti-dilutive and must be excluded. Including it overstates diluted EPS. Test each instrument for dilution each period.

### Applying The Wrong Method For Options Versus Convertibles

Use the treasury stock method for options and warrants, and the if-converted method for convertibles. Applying the wrong method misstates the dilutive share count.

### Presenting OCI Items Within Profit Or Loss

OCI items belong below net income, not within profit or loss. Including them inflates reported profit and misstates the realized/unrealized distinction.

### Not Splitting Recyclable And Non-Recyclable OCI

OCI items must be split into recyclable and non-recyclable categories. Presenting them as a single pool obscures which items will eventually affect profit or loss.

### Presenting OCI Before Tax

OCI items are presented net of tax. Presenting them gross of tax overstates the OCI amount and misstates total comprehensive income.

### Omitting The EPS Reconciliation Disclosure

The reconciliation of numerators (profit attributable) and denominators (weighted average shares, dilutive effect) must be disclosed. Omitting it leaves users unable to verify the EPS calculation.

## Self-Check

- Is basic EPS calculated as profit attributable to ordinary shareholders (net income minus preference dividends) divided by the weighted average shares, retroactively adjusted for splits and bonus issues?
- Is diluted EPS calculated using the treasury stock method for options/warrants and the if-converted method for convertibles, with numerators and denominators adjusted?
- Are potentially dilutive instruments tested for dilution and ranked, with only dilutive instruments included and anti-dilutive ones excluded?
- Are basic and diluted EPS presented on the face with equal prominence for continuing operations and total profit or loss?
- Is comprehensive income presented as profit or loss plus OCI, in a single statement or two statements, flowing to retained earnings and accumulated OCI?
- Are OCI items classified as recyclable or non-recyclable, presented in separate categories with the recycling policy disclosed?
- Are OCI items presented net of related tax, with pre-tax amounts and tax effects disclosed in the notes?
- Is the EPS reconciliation (numerator and denominator) disclosed, and has a qualified accountant or auditor reviewed the calculation for complex capital structures?
