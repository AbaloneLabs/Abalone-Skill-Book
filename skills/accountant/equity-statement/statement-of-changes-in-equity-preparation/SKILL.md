# Statement Of Changes In Equity Preparation

The statement of changes in equity, sometimes called the equity rollforward, reconciles opening equity to closing equity by showing every movement in between. It presents the period's profit or loss, other comprehensive income, share issuances and buybacks, dividends, treasury stock movements, and any transfers between equity components. It is the bridge that explains how shareholders' funds changed. The trap is that the statement looks like a simple schedule but depends on every other part of the financial statements being right. A misclassified item in comprehensive income, an unrecorded dividend, or an omitted share movement flows straight into an inaccurate equity statement. Agents often build the rollforward by copying the prior period and plugging the difference, when each line must be independently supported.

Use this skill before preparing a statement of changes in equity, reconciling equity movements, or reviewing the rollforward for accuracy. The goal is to ensure every equity movement is captured, correctly classified by component, and supported so the rollforward ties from opening to closing balance.

This skill covers equity rollforward preparation. The required presentation format, column structure, and disclosure depend on the applicable standard, such as US GAAP or IFRS, and on the entity type. Confirm the governing presentation requirements before preparing the statement.

## Core Rules

### Reconcile Opening To Closing Equity With Every Movement Explained

The statement must reconcile opening equity to closing equity with no plug. Every difference between the two balances must be represented by a specific, supported line item.

The typical movements include:

- profit or loss for the period;
- other comprehensive income for the period;
- share issuances and proceeds;
- share buybacks and treasury stock;
- dividends declared, both cash and stock;
- transfers between equity components, such as reserves to retained earnings;
- prior-period adjustments and restatements;
- equity compensation expense and vesting.

If the rollforward does not tie, the answer is to find the missing movement, not to insert a balancing figure. A plug hides an error in another part of the statements.

### Present Movements By Equity Component

Equity is not a single pool. It is made of components, each with its own rules and restrictions. The statement should present the change in each component separately.

Common components include:

- common stock or ordinary shares at par or stated value;
- additional paid-in capital or share premium;
- retained earnings or accumulated profit;
- accumulated other comprehensive income or loss;
- treasury stock as a contra component;
- non-controlling interests in consolidated statements.

Presenting only a total equity movement hides where the change occurred. A dividend reduces retained earnings. A share issuance increases common stock and paid-in capital. An OCI item moves accumulated OCI. Each must be shown in its own column.

### Tie Each Line To Its Source

Every line in the rollforward must tie to a source outside the statement. The rollforward is a summary, not the origin of the numbers.

Confirm each line ties to:

- the income statement for profit or loss;
- the comprehensive income statement or OCI section for other comprehensive income;
- the share register and capital records for issuances and buybacks;
- the dividend records and board minutes for dividends;
- the equity compensation records for stock-based compensation;
- the prior-period statements for opening balances and restatements.

A line that cannot be tied to a source is unsupported and likely wrong.

### Capture All Comprehensive Income, Not Just Profit

Other comprehensive income is part of the equity movement but is often overlooked because it bypasses the income statement. Items like foreign currency translation adjustments, unrealized gains and losses on certain investments, and pension remeasurements flow through OCI and into accumulated OCI.

Confirm that:

- all OCI items for the period are included;
- OCI is presented net of tax where required;
- accumulated OCI rolls forward correctly;
- reclassifications from OCI to profit are shown where applicable.

Omitting OCI understates the total change in equity and misstates accumulated OCI.

### Include Non-Controlling Interests In Consolidated Statements

In consolidated financial statements, equity includes the non-controlling interest, the portion of subsidiaries not owned by the parent. The statement must show the non-controlling interest separately and reflect its share of profit, additional investments, and distributions.

Failing to present the non-controlling interest, or mixing it with parent equity, misstates the equity attributable to the parent's shareholders.

### Handle Prior-Period Adjustments And Restatements Separately

When a prior-period error is corrected through restatement, the adjustment is shown as a separate line, typically as a restatement of opening balances, not buried in current-period movements.

Present restatements so that:

- the restated opening balance is shown;
- the effect of the restatement is identified by component;
- the comparative period is also restated;
- the disclosure explains the nature of the restatement.

Mixing a restatement into current-period profit or reserves hides the correction and distorts the period result.

### Reconcile The Rollforward To The Balance Sheet

The closing balance of each equity component on the statement of changes in equity must equal the corresponding balance on the balance sheet. This is the ultimate tie-out.

If the rollforward closes to one number and the balance sheet shows another, there is an error in one or the other. Resolve the difference before the statements are finalized.

### Apply Consistent Presentation Across Periods

The presentation of the statement should be consistent across periods so comparatives are meaningful. Adding, removing, or reordering components without explanation undermines comparability.

If a presentation change is justified, apply it retrospectively to comparatives and disclose the change.

## Common Traps

### Plugging The Difference To Make The Rollforward Tie

When opening plus movements does not equal closing, inserting a balancing figure hides the missing movement. The rollforward must tie through identified, supported lines.

### Presenting Only Total Equity Movement

Showing one combined equity change hides which component moved. A dividend, a share issuance, and an OCI item all change equity but belong in different columns.

### Omitting Other Comprehensive Income

OCI bypasses the income statement but still changes equity. Forgetting it understates the equity movement and misstates accumulated OCI.

### Not Tying Lines To External Sources

A rollforward built by copying last period and adjusting the total is unsupported. Each line must tie to the income statement, share records, dividend records, or OCI detail.

### Mixing Restatements Into Current-Period Movements

A prior-period correction shown as current-period profit or reserve movement hides the restatement and distorts the period. Show restatements separately.

### Ignoring Non-Controlling Interests

In consolidation, the non-controlling interest is part of total equity. Omitting it or blending it with parent equity misstates equity attributable to the parent.

### Rollforward Not Tying To The Balance Sheet

If the statement of changes in equity closes to a different total than the balance sheet, one of them is wrong. The two must reconcile.

### Inconsistent Component Presentation Across Periods

Changing the columns or components period to period without retrospective adjustment breaks comparability and confuses readers.

## Self-Check

- Does the statement reconcile opening to closing equity with every movement explained by a specific, supported line and no balancing plug?
- Is each movement presented by equity component, including common stock, paid-in capital, retained earnings, accumulated OCI, treasury stock, and non-controlling interests?
- Does each line tie to an external source, such as the income statement, comprehensive income detail, share register, dividend records, or equity compensation records?
- Are all other comprehensive income items captured and presented net of tax where required, with accumulated OCI rolling forward correctly?
- Are non-controlling interests presented separately in consolidated statements, reflecting their share of profit, investments, and distributions?
- Are prior-period adjustments and restatements shown as separate lines with restated comparatives and disclosure?
- Does the closing balance of each component on the statement equal the corresponding balance sheet equity line?
- Is the presentation consistent across periods, with any justified change applied retrospectively and disclosed?
- Have the applicable presentation standard and entity-type requirements been confirmed before preparing the statement?
