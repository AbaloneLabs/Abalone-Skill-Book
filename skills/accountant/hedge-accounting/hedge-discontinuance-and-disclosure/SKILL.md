# Hedge Discontinuance And Disclosure

Hedge accounting does not last forever, and how it ends is as important as how it begins. A hedging relationship is discontinued prospectively when it no longer qualifies, when the hedging instrument is sold or terminated, when the hedged item is derecognized, or when the forecasted transaction is no longer expected to occur. Each of these triggers has different consequences for the OCI balance already accumulated. Agents frequently discontinue a hedge but leave the OCI balance unexplained, reclassify deferred amounts to the wrong period, or fail to distinguish between a dedesignation (a voluntary or involuntary end to hedge accounting while the derivative continues) and a termination (the derivative itself ends). The disclosure side is equally prone to omission: hedge accounting requires detailed disclosure of the risk management strategy, the amounts in the hedging reserve, and the amounts reclassified to profit or loss, and users rely on this to understand how derivatives affect earnings. Missing or boilerplate disclosure is a common audit and regulatory finding.

Use this skill before discontinuing a hedging relationship, handling the remaining OCI balance, reclassifying deferred amounts on cancellation or disposal, or preparing hedge accounting disclosures. The goal is to prevent the agent from leaving the OCI balance stranded or misclassifying the ending event, and to ensure disclosures give users a real picture of the hedging activity.

Discontinuance triggers and disclosure requirements differ between IFRS 9 and US GAAP (ASC 815), including the treatment of remaining OCI balances and the level of disclosure detail. This skill provides the general framework; the applicable standard governs. For material hedging relationships, involve a qualified accountant and a hedge accounting specialist. This is structural guidance, not a determination for a specific relationship.

## Core Rules

### Identify The Correct Discontinuance Trigger

Hedge accounting ends for different reasons, and the reason determines what happens to the OCI balance. The main triggers are: the relationship no longer meets the qualifying criteria, such as failing effectiveness; the hedging instrument is sold, terminated, or exercised; the hedged item is derecognized; the hedge is dedesignated voluntarily; or the forecasted transaction is no longer expected to occur. Each has a defined consequence.

Identify the trigger precisely before deciding the accounting. A failed effectiveness test leads to prospective discontinuance with the OCI balance retained. A cancelled forecasted transaction leads to immediate reclassification of the OCI balance to profit or loss. Conflating the triggers produces the wrong treatment of the deferred amounts.

### Discontinue Prospectively, Not Retroactively

Discontinuance is prospective. Hedge accounting that was validly applied up to the discontinuance date is not reversed. The derivative, if it continues, is thereafter accounted for at fair value through profit or loss or under the relevant held-for-trading classification, with no offsetting treatment on a hedged item. For a fair value hedge, the basis adjustment on the hedged item is amortized over its remaining life under the framework's rules.

Do not reverse prior-period OCI amounts or restate the hedged item's carrying amount as if hedge accounting never applied. Prospective discontinuance preserves the periods that qualified and changes the treatment only going forward.

### Handle The OCI Balance According To The Trigger

For a cash flow hedge, the OCI balance accumulated while the relationship qualified is handled according to why hedge accounting ended. If the derivative is sold or terminated but the forecasted transaction is still expected to occur, the OCI balance remains deferred and is reclassified when the transaction affects earnings. If the forecasted transaction is no longer expected to occur, the OCI balance is reclassified immediately to profit or loss. If the hedged item is derecognized, the OCI balance is reclassified in the same period.

Apply the trigger-specific rule. Leaving the OCI balance stranded after the forecasted transaction is cancelled misstates both OCI and profit or loss, because the deferral no longer has a future earnings effect to match.

### Distinguish Dedesignation From Termination

A dedesignation ends hedge accounting while the derivative continues to exist; the derivative is then marked to market through profit or loss with no hedge treatment. A termination ends the derivative itself, which may settle the instrument and crystallize a gain or loss. The two are different events with different accounting, and the documentation should make clear which occurred.

A common error is treating a dedesignation as a termination, or vice versa, which misstates the derivative's subsequent accounting and the OCI balance treatment. Confirm whether the derivative continues after hedge accounting ends.

### Disclose The Risk Management Strategy

Hedge accounting disclosure must explain the entity's risk management strategy for each risk category the entity hedges. This is not boilerplate; it must describe how the entity uses hedging to manage risk, the types of hedges, and the risks hedged. Users rely on this to understand why the entity enters derivatives and how they relate to the underlying exposures.

Describe the strategy for each risk, such as interest rate risk, foreign currency risk, commodity price risk, or credit risk. A generic statement that the entity uses derivatives to manage risk is insufficient. The disclosure should let a user understand the exposure being managed and the instruments used.

### Disclose Amounts In OCI And Amounts Reclassified

The disclosure must quantify the hedging activity. Disclose the amount recognized in OCI during the period, the amount reclassified from OCI to profit or loss during the period, and the balance remaining in the hedging reserve. For each, disclose by risk category. Where reclassification affects multiple line items, disclose the line items affected.

Quantify the movement. A disclosure that omits the OCI balance or the reclassification amount leaves users unable to assess the earnings impact of hedging. Reconcile the opening and closing OCI balance for the hedging reserve.

### Disclose Ineffectiveness And Discontinuances

Disclose the amount of any gain or loss recognized in profit or loss as a result of hedge ineffectiveness, and the amount arising from changes in the hedging instrument's fair value that were excluded from the effectiveness assessment. Disclose any hedging relationships discontinued during the period and the reason, particularly where a forecasted transaction is no longer expected to occur.

Ineffectiveness and discontinuance are signals to users that the hedging did not work as intended. Omitting them hides the volatility that hedge accounting was meant to manage and any failure of the strategy.

## Common Traps

### Leaving The OCI Balance Stranded After Discontinuance

When hedge accounting ends, the OCI balance must be handled according to the trigger. Leaving it deferred indefinitely after the forecasted transaction is cancelled misstates OCI and profit or loss.

### Conflating Dedesignation With Termination

A dedesignation ends hedge accounting while the derivative continues; a termination ends the derivative. Treating one as the other misstates the derivative's subsequent accounting and the OCI balance treatment.

### Discontinuing Retroactively Instead Of Prospectively

Discontinuance is prospective. Reversing prior-period OCI amounts or restating the hedged item as if hedge accounting never applied is incorrect.

### Reversing Validly Deferred OCI On A Failed Effectiveness Test

When effectiveness fails but the forecasted transaction is still expected, the OCI balance remains deferred and is reclassified when the transaction affects earnings. Reversing it immediately misstates the period.

### Providing Boilerplate Risk Management Strategy Disclosure

A generic statement that the entity uses derivatives to manage risk does not satisfy the disclosure requirement. The strategy must be described for each risk category, with the instruments and exposures explained.

### Omitting The Ineffectiveness Or Discontinuance Disclosure

Failing to disclose ineffectiveness gains or losses, or discontinuances and their reasons, hides signals that the hedging strategy did not work as intended.

### Failing To Reconcile The Hedging Reserve

The disclosure should reconcile the opening and closing OCI balance for the hedging reserve, showing amounts recognized, reclassified, and the remaining balance by risk category. An unreconciled balance leaves users unable to track the hedging activity.

## Self-Check

- Is the discontinuance trigger identified precisely, distinguishing a failed effectiveness test, instrument sale or termination, hedged item derecognition, dedesignation, and forecasted transaction cancellation?
- Is discontinuance applied prospectively, with no reversal of prior-period OCI amounts or restatement of the hedged item?
- Is the OCI balance handled according to the trigger, retained and reclassified on the hedged item when the forecasted transaction is still expected, or reclassified immediately when it is cancelled?
- Is dedesignation distinguished from termination, with the derivative's subsequent accounting and the OCI balance treatment applied correctly?
- Does the risk management strategy disclosure describe, for each risk category, how the entity uses hedging, the types of hedges, and the exposures managed?
- Are the amounts recognized in OCI, reclassified to profit or loss, and the remaining hedging reserve balance disclosed by risk category, with the line items affected identified?
- Are ineffectiveness gains or losses, excluded component amounts, and discontinued relationships with their reasons disclosed?
- Is the hedging reserve reconciled from opening to closing balance, and has a qualified accountant and hedge accounting specialist reviewed the discontinuance and disclosures?
