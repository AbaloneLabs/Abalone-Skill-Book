# Hedge Relationship Designation And Documentation

Hedge accounting is one of the most documentation-intensive and judgment-heavy areas in financial reporting, because it lets an entity change the normal recognition pattern of derivatives or hedged items only when a strict set of conditions is met. The core misunderstanding is that hedge accounting is a volatility switch that can be turned on to smooth earnings. It is not. Hedge accounting is a conditional permission to align the recognition of gains and losses on the hedging instrument with the hedged item, and that permission exists only while a documented, effective, qualifying relationship is in place. Agents frequently designate a hedge verbally or in a treasury memo but never produce the formal documentation required at inception, designate a relationship that does not meet the qualifying criteria, or fail to specify the risk being hedged precisely enough to test effectiveness later. When documentation is missing or late, hedge accounting is denied retroactively and all the volatility the hedge was meant to manage hits a single period, often producing the very earnings distortion the entity was trying to avoid.

Use this skill before designating a hedging relationship, preparing hedge documentation, classifying a hedge type, defining the hedged item or risk, or assessing whether a relationship qualifies. The goal is to prevent the agent from treating designation as a formality and to make clear that documentation, qualifying criteria, and risk specification are substantive requirements that determine whether hedge accounting is available at all.

Hedge accounting rules differ substantially between IFRS 9 and US GAAP (ASC 815), including documentation timing, effectiveness methods, eligible items, and reclassification. This skill provides the general framework and the judgment the agent should apply; the applicable standard governs the detail. For material or complex hedging strategies, involve a qualified accountant and a hedge accounting or treasury specialist. This is structural guidance, not a determination for a specific hedging relationship.

## Core Rules

### Recognize That Designation Is A Formal Act, Not An Intention

Hedge accounting begins with formal designation of the hedging relationship at inception. Designation is not implied by the existence of a derivative or by management's intent to hedge. An entity can hold a derivative that economically reduces risk but, without designation and documentation, accounts for that derivative at fair value through profit or loss with no offsetting treatment on the hedged item. The economic hedge and the accounting hedge are separate, and only the latter requires the formal machinery.

Treat designation as a formal act by confirming that, at or before inception of the hedging relationship, the entity has documented the relationship, the risk management objective, the hedging instrument, the hedged item, the nature of the risk being hedged, and how effectiveness will be assessed. Designation after the fact, or documentation prepared when the auditors arrive, disqualifies hedge accounting for the period before documentation existed.

### Classify The Hedge Type Correctly

Hedge accounting has three recognized types, and the classification determines the accounting. A fair value hedge hedges the exposure to changes in fair value of a recognized asset or liability or an unrecognized firm commitment, attributable to a particular risk. A cash flow hedge hedges the exposure to variability in cash flows of a recognized asset or liability, a forecasted transaction, or a forecasted intercompany transaction. A net investment hedge hedges the foreign currency exposure of a net investment in a foreign operation.

Classify by identifying the exposure being managed. If the exposure is to changes in fair value of a fixed-rate item, the hedge is a fair value hedge. If the exposure is to variability in future cash flows, such as a forecasted purchase or a floating-rate interest stream, the hedge is a cash flow hedge. If the exposure is the foreign currency translation of a net investment, it is a net investment hedge. Misclassification routes gains and losses to the wrong statement and applies the wrong mechanics.

### Specify The Hedged Item And Hedged Instrument Precisely

The documentation must identify the hedging instrument and the hedged item with enough specificity to test the relationship later. A vague reference to "interest rate swaps" or "forecasted sales" is not sufficient. Specify the instrument, including its notional, terms, counterparty, and the portion designated. Specify the hedged item, including whether it is the entire item, a specific risk component, a layer, or a percentage, and the period or volume it covers.

Under IFRS 9, a component of a non-financial item can be designated as the hedged item only if it is separately identifiable and reliably measurable. Under US GAAP, the rules for components and layers differ by hedge type and have historically been more restrictive, though recent amendments have expanded flexibility. Confirm the applicable framework's rules for what may be designated, because designating an ineligible component or layer disqualifies the relationship.

### Define The Risk Being Hedged Narrowly

The documentation must state the nature of the risk being hedged, and that risk must be specifically identifiable and must affect profit or loss or OCI. Hedging "all risks" is rarely acceptable; the risk must be defined, such as the benchmark interest rate, the foreign currency exchange risk, the credit risk, or the commodity price risk. The defined risk drives the effectiveness assessment, because effectiveness compares changes in the hedging instrument to changes in the hedged item attributable to the hedged risk, not to all changes.

Define the risk narrowly and consistently with the risk management objective. If the entity is hedging benchmark interest rate risk on a fixed-rate bond, document benchmark interest rate risk, not total interest rate risk. If the entity is hedging foreign currency risk on a forecasted sale, document the specific currency pair. A broadly defined risk makes effectiveness testing harder and increases the chance of failing the qualifying criteria.

### Confirm The Qualifying Criteria At Inception

Beyond documentation, the relationship must meet qualifying criteria at inception and throughout its life. The criteria generally include: there is formal designation and documentation; the hedging relationship consists of an eligible hedging instrument and an eligible hedged item; at inception there is a reasonable expectation, or in some cases a high level of assurance, that the relationship will be highly effective; effectiveness can be reliably measured; and the hedge has been and is expected to remain highly effective.

Confirm each criterion before concluding that hedge accounting applies. A common failure is designating a relationship where effectiveness cannot be reliably measured, for example because the hedged item or risk is not separately measurable, or where there is no reasonable expectation of high effectiveness because the instrument and item are mismatched. Document the basis for each criterion, not just the conclusion.

### Apply The Right Effectiveness Assessment Method

Effectiveness must be assessed in a manner consistent with the risk management strategy. Under IFRS 9, effectiveness is assessed through the economic relationship between the hedging instrument and the hedged item, the effect of credit risk, and the definition of the hedge ratio. There is no bright-line quantitative threshold, but the assessment must demonstrate that an economic relationship exists that achieves offsetting of changes in fair value or cash flows. Under US GAAP, the methods and thresholds differ by hedge type and have included the 80-125% bright-line for some legacy hedges, with newer simplified approaches for certain hedges.

Select the method at inception, document it, and apply it consistently. A method chosen after the fact to make a failing hedge look effective is not a valid assessment. Where the relationship is no longer effective, discontinue hedge accounting prospectively; do not retain it by changing the method.

### Time The Documentation To Inception

Documentation must exist at the time of designation, which is at inception of the hedging relationship. This is a hard rule, not a best practice. Documentation prepared after inception, even if it describes the relationship accurately, does not satisfy the requirement for the period before it existed. The consequence is that hedge accounting is unavailable from inception until the documentation date, and the derivative's gains and losses fall into profit or loss with no offsetting treatment.

Build the documentation requirement into the trade process so that designation and documentation happen before or at the same time as the derivative is executed. A post-trade documentation step is too late.

## Common Traps

### Treating A Verbal Or Treasury-Memo Hedge As Designation

A risk management decision or treasury memo that records the intent to hedge is not formal designation. Without the specified documentation elements at inception, hedge accounting is unavailable, and the derivative is marked to market through profit or loss.

### Designating After Inception

Documentation prepared when auditors arrive, or designation backdated to the trade date, does not qualify the relationship from inception. Hedge accounting is denied for the undocumented period, and the resulting volatility cannot be reversed.

### Designating An Ineligible Hedged Item Or Risk

Designating a component of a non-financial item that is not separately identifiable, or designating "all risks" when only a specific risk qualifies, disqualifies the relationship. The eligibility rules for components and layers are framework-specific and must be checked.

### Misclassifying The Hedge Type

Classifying a fair value exposure as a cash flow hedge, or vice versa, routes gains and losses to the wrong statement and applies the wrong deferral or adjustment mechanics. Classification follows the nature of the exposure, not the type of instrument.

### Choosing An Effectiveness Method To Manufacture A Passing Result

Selecting or changing the effectiveness method after observing that the hedge fails, in order to produce a passing result, is not a valid assessment. The method is chosen at inception and applied consistently; a failing relationship is discontinued prospectively.

### Ignoring Credit Risk In The Effectiveness Assessment

Under IFRS 9, the effect of credit risk on the hedging instrument is part of the effectiveness assessment. A relationship that appears effective on a market-risk basis can fail because credit risk dominates the instrument's value changes, particularly for long-dated derivatives.

### Retaining Hedge Accounting After The Relationship Changes

If the hedging instrument is sold, terminated, or restructured, or if the hedged item is derecognized or no longer expected to occur, the relationship no longer qualifies and hedge accounting must be discontinued prospectively. Retaining it misstates the period in which the change occurred.

## Self-Check

- Is there formal designation and complete documentation at or before inception, including the risk management objective, hedging instrument, hedged item, nature of the risk, and effectiveness method?
- Is the hedge classified correctly as fair value, cash flow, or net investment, based on the nature of the exposure being managed?
- Are the hedging instrument and hedged item specified with enough detail, including notional, terms, the portion designated, and the specific component or layer where relevant?
- Is the hedged risk defined narrowly and specifically, consistent with the risk management objective, and does it affect profit or loss or OCI?
- Do all qualifying criteria hold at inception, including reasonable expectation of effectiveness, reliable measurability, and an eligible instrument and item?
- Is the effectiveness assessment method chosen at inception, documented, and applied consistently, with an economic relationship demonstrated rather than a manufactured result?
- Has the effect of credit risk been considered where the framework requires it?
- Is hedge accounting discontinued prospectively when the relationship no longer qualifies, rather than retained or backdated?
- Has a qualified accountant and hedge accounting specialist reviewed material designations, documentation, and effectiveness assessments?
