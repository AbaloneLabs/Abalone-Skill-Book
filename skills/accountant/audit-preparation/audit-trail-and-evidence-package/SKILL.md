# Audit Trail And Evidence Package

An audit trail is the path from a source event, through the accounting records, to the financial statements. An evidence package is the organized set of documents and explanations that lets someone, an auditor, a reviewer, a regulator, or a successor, reconstruct and verify a transaction or balance. These are not formalities. They are the substance of accountability. When the audit trail is broken, a transaction cannot be proven to have occurred, to be accurately recorded, or to belong in the period claimed. When the evidence package is disorganized, even correct accounting looks unsupported and invites doubt.

Use this skill before assembling an audit trail, building an evidence package for a significant transaction or balance, preparing for review or audit, or assessing whether a process can be reconstructed from its records. The goal is to prevent the agent from producing records that are correct but unprovable, or evidence that exists but cannot be followed by an independent reviewer.

## Core Rules

### Build The Trail From Event To Statement

A complete audit trail runs in both directions. From the financial statement caption back to the source event, and from the source event forward to the recorded entry.

Construct the trail to show:

- the originating event or transaction, contract, order, shipment, receipt, payment;
- the source document, invoice, receipt, contract, bank record;
- the authorization or approval, with evidence of who approved and under what authority;
- the recording in the books, the journal entry or system posting;
- the classification in the correct account, period, and entity;
- the accumulation into the trial balance and the financial statement caption.

A break at any point weakens the assertion. The most common breaks are missing approvals, lost source documents, undocumented estimates, and entries posted without explanation.

### Tie Each Significant Balance To Supporting Detail

Every significant balance should be traceable from the financial statement to the underlying detail without gaps.

For each material balance provide:

- the financial statement caption;
- the trial balance account;
- the general ledger detail;
- the subledger or subsidiary detail;
- the reconciliation to the ledger;
- the individual transactions or items composing the balance;
- the source documents supporting the items.

This layered tie-out is what auditors test. If any layer is missing or unreconciled, the balance is unsupported.

### Document Authorizations And Approvals

A transaction that occurred but was not properly authorized is a control deficiency even if the amount is correct. Evidence of authorization is part of the audit trail.

Capture:

- who initiated the transaction;
- who approved it and under what authority or limit;
- the policy or delegation supporting the approval;
- the date and method of approval, signature, system workflow, or email;
- segregation between initiation, approval, and recording.

Approval evidence stored only in informal channels, such as personal chat or verbal confirmation, is weak. Prefer system workflows or signed approvals that are retained with the transaction.

### Preserve Source Documents In A Usable Form

Source documents must be retained in a form that an independent reviewer can read, understand, and trust.

Ensure:

- documents are legible and complete;
- electronic documents are stored in accessible, stable formats;
- documents are linked or indexed to the related transaction;
- documents are protected from unauthorized alteration;
- retention meets the applicable legal, tax, and policy requirements;
- documents can be retrieved within a reasonable time.

A document that exists but cannot be found, or that has been altered without explanation, provides little evidence. Organize retention around retrieval, not just storage.

### Document Estimates And Judgments Explicitly

Estimates and judgments are where audit trails most often fail, because the source is not a document but a reasoning process. That reasoning must be documented.

For each significant estimate document:

- the method or model used;
- the inputs and their source;
- the key assumptions and why they were chosen;
- the sensitivity to changes in assumptions;
- who prepared and who approved the estimate;
- the date and the period the estimate relates to.

An estimate supported only by a final number, with no method or inputs, cannot be audited. Document the reasoning, not just the result.

### Maintain System And Control Evidence

The audit trail includes evidence that controls operated, not just that transactions were recorded. For significant processes, preserve control evidence.

Preserve:

- system-generated logs of postings, edits, and overrides;
- workflow approvals and rejection records;
- reconciliation evidence with reviewer sign-off;
- exception logs and their resolution;
- access and change logs for master data and journal entries.

System evidence is often the only proof that a control ran. Configure systems to retain these logs for the full retention period.

### Make The Package Navigable

An evidence package that no one can follow is little better than no package. Organize it for an independent reviewer.

Organize:

- a summary or index of what the package contains;
- a clear narrative of the transaction or balance;
- documents ordered to follow the process;
- cross-references between the narrative, the ledger, and the documents;
- explanations for any exceptions or unusual items;
- a clear indication of scope and period.

A reviewer should be able to understand the transaction and verify the recording without having to reverse-engineer the package.

### Acknowledge Limits And Escalate

Assembling an audit trail and evidence package supports review and audit, but it does not constitute an audit opinion or a legal determination. Where a transaction is complex, novel, or material, such as a business combination, a fair value measurement, or a tax position, the evidence package should reflect the involvement of qualified professionals. Do not present a self-built evidence package as a substitute for independent audit, professional accounting judgment, or legal review where those are required.

## Common Traps

### Missing Authorization Evidence

A correct amount without evidence of approval is a control deficiency and a finding.

### Broken Link Between Document And Entry

A source document that cannot be tied to the recorded entry, or an entry with no source document, breaks the trail.

### Estimates Documented Only By Result

An estimate with a final number but no method, inputs, or assumptions cannot be audited.

### Disorganized Or Unindexed Packages

Evidence that exists but cannot be navigated wastes reviewer time and invites doubt.

### Weak Retention Or Alterable Records

Documents stored in alterable formats, or retained for too short a period, provide little reliable evidence.

### Informal Approvals

Verbal or chat-based approvals, not captured in a retained system, are weak evidence.

### Overstating The Package's Authority

An evidence package supports verification, but it is not an audit, a legal opinion, or a substitute for professional judgment on complex matters.

## Self-Check

- Does the trail run from the source event through authorization, recording, classification, and accumulation to the financial statement caption?
- Is each significant balance traceable through layered detail, from statement to trial balance to ledger to subledger to source documents?
- Is authorization and approval evidence captured with the transaction, including who, under what authority, and with segregation of duties?
- Are source documents retained in legible, accessible, stable, and protected form for the required period?
- Are significant estimates documented with method, inputs, assumptions, sensitivity, preparer, approver, and date?
- Is system and control evidence, logs, workflows, reconciliations, and exception records, preserved for the full retention period?
- Is the evidence package organized with an index, narrative, ordered documents, and cross-references so an independent reviewer can follow it?
- Does the package acknowledge that it supports review and audit but does not replace independent audit, professional accounting judgment, or legal review where required?
