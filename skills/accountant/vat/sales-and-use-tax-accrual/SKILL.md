# Sales And Use Tax Accrual

Sales tax, value-added tax (VAT), and use tax are transaction taxes assessed on the sale or use of goods and services. Unlike income tax, these are largely pass-through amounts: the business collects tax from customers and remits it to the authority, or pays tax on its own purchases and seeks recovery. The accounting errors here are distinctive. Agents often record collected sales tax as revenue, treat use tax as a deductible expense, accrue tax without checking taxability or nexus, or fail to separate the collection obligation from the remittance liability. Each error misstates revenue, expense, or liabilities and can create real exposure when jurisdictions audit.

Use this skill before accruing sales or use tax, setting up tax collection on products or services, evaluating whether use tax is owed on purchases, classifying output and input tax, or reviewing indirect tax liabilities. The goal is to accrue the correct liability, classify it as a pass-through rather than income or expense, and capture use tax exposure on purchases.

This is an indirect tax domain. Sales tax, VAT, GST, and use tax rules vary enormously by jurisdiction: rates, taxability of products and services, exemption rules, nexus thresholds, registration requirements, and recovery mechanisms all differ across states, countries, and local authorities. There is no single universal rule. Do not apply one jurisdiction's taxability or nexus rules to another. Final determinations on taxability, nexus, and registration should be routed to a qualified indirect tax professional. This skill is operational guidance for the accounting accrual and classification, not professional tax advice, and it carries an explicit limitation that jurisdiction-specific indirect tax rules are not exhaustively covered.

## Core Rules

### Determine Taxability Of Each Product And Service

Taxability is not uniform. Within a single jurisdiction, some products are taxable and others exempt, services are often treated differently from goods, digital products have their own rules, and exemptions exist for specific buyers (resale, manufacturing, government, nonprofit) and specific uses. Before accruing tax on a revenue stream, confirm whether the product or service is taxable in the relevant jurisdiction and whether an exemption applies to the transaction.

Maintain a taxability matrix by product or service and jurisdiction. An accrual built on an assumption that "everything we sell is taxable" will be wrong wherever exemptions or service rules diverge.

### Evaluate Nexus Before Accruing Or Collecting

Tax accrual and collection obligations generally arise only where the business has nexus with the jurisdiction. Nexus rules have expanded (economic nexus thresholds based on sales volume or transaction count) beyond the traditional physical presence standard. Confirm where the business has nexus before accruing sales tax or registering to collect.

Accruing tax without nexus overstates the liability; failing to accrue where nexus exists understates it and creates exposure. Track sales by jurisdiction against economic nexus thresholds and monitor registration obligations.

### Separate Collection From Accrual And Remittance

Sales tax collected from customers is a liability from the moment it is collected, not revenue. The accounting flow is: record the sale net of tax, record the tax collected as a sales tax payable liability, and reduce the liability when remitted to the authority. Do not credit collected tax to revenue and then expense the remittance; that misstates both.

For VAT, output tax on sales is a liability and input tax on purchases is either a recoverable asset or a reduction of the liability, with the net amount payable or recoverable. The net VAT position is a pass-through, not an income or expense item in the ordinary course.

### Accrue Use Tax On Taxable Purchases

Use tax is the mirror of sales tax, owed by the buyer on taxable purchases where the seller did not charge sales tax. It is frequently missed because it depends on accounts payable discipline, not billing. Common sources include online purchases, out-of-state vendors, expense reimbursements, and assets withdrawn from inventory for internal use.

Establish a process to self-assess use tax on taxable purchases at the point of invoice processing, using the same taxability and rate rules as sales tax. Use tax accrued is a liability until remitted; it is not a deductible expense beyond the underlying purchase cost.

### Classify Indirect Tax As A Pass-Through Liability

Output tax collected and use tax accrued are liabilities, classified as current. Input VAT that is recoverable may be presented as a current asset or netted against output VAT per the framework and jurisdictional presentation rules. The remittance to the authority reduces the liability; it is not an expense.

Avoid recording indirect tax in revenue or in operating expense lines except where the tax is genuinely a cost to the business (such as non-recoverable input VAT or use tax that becomes part of asset cost). The default is pass-through liability classification.

### Handle Exemptions, Resale, And Exemption Certificates

Exempt transactions (resale, manufacturing, government, nonprofit) require valid exemption certificates or equivalent documentation to support the non-collection of tax. Without the certificate, the seller may remain liable for the tax. Maintain exemption certificates by customer and jurisdiction, and flag sales made without a certificate on file for review.

Accrual should reflect the tax that is legally due after applying valid exemptions, not a blanket rate on gross sales.

### Reconcile The Indirect Tax Liability To Returns

The accrued sales tax, VAT, and use tax liabilities should reconcile to the returns filed and the amounts remitted. Differences arise from timing (accrual versus cash remittance basis), rounding, rate changes, corrections, and errors. Reconcile each tax type to the return each filing period, and investigate material differences rather than rolling them forward.

## Common Traps

### Recording Collected Sales Tax As Revenue

Crediting tax collected from customers to revenue overstates revenue and creates a matching error when the tax is remitted. Sales tax collected is a liability.

### Treating Use Tax As A Deductible Expense

Use tax is a liability on a taxable purchase, not an operating expense. Recording it as expense misclassifies the amount and often misses the accrual entirely.

### Accruing Tax Without Checking Taxability Or Nexus

Applying a blanket rate to gross sales ignores exemptions, service rules, and nexus limits, overstating the liability in some places and understating it in others.

### Missing Use Tax On Purchases

Use tax depends on invoice review, not billing, so it is easily missed on online, out-of-state, and expense purchases. Without a self-assessment process, exposure accumulates silently.

### Exempt Sales Without Exemption Certificates

Selling without collecting tax and without a valid exemption certificate leaves the seller potentially liable. Certificates must be on file and valid.

### Non-Recoverable Input VAT Recorded As Recoverable

Assuming all input VAT is recoverable ignores partial exemption and blocked-input rules, overstating the recoverable asset. Confirm recoverability per jurisdiction.

### Unreconciled Liability Balances

Letting the accrued indirect tax liability drift away from the returns filed hides errors and timing differences. Reconcile each period.

## Self-Check

- Is taxability of each product and service confirmed by jurisdiction, with a maintained taxability matrix rather than a blanket assumption?
- Has nexus been evaluated for each jurisdiction where sales occur, including economic nexus thresholds and registration obligations?
- Is sales tax collected from customers recorded as a liability, not as revenue, and reduced only when remitted?
- Is use tax self-assessed on taxable purchases through an accounts payable process, and recorded as a liability rather than an expense?
- Are output tax and input tax classified as pass-through items, with non-recoverable input tax or use tax treated as cost only where genuinely incurred?
- Are exempt sales supported by valid exemption certificates on file by customer and jurisdiction?
- Is recoverable input VAT confirmed for recoverability per jurisdictional rules rather than assumed fully recoverable?
- Does the accrued sales tax, VAT, and use tax liability reconcile to the returns filed and amounts remitted each period?
- Are timing differences, rate changes, and corrections investigated rather than rolled forward?
- Is professional referral noted for taxability, nexus, and registration determinations, and is the explicit limitation on jurisdiction-specific rules acknowledged?
