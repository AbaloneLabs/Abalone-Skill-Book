# Uncertain Positions Disclosure And Rollforward

The uncertain tax positions disclosure and rollforward is the public-facing evidence that the recognition and measurement model has been applied. It shows how the liability for unrecognized tax benefits moved during the period and what is expected to change. Agents often produce a rollforward that foots mathematically but is not informative: settlements and statute expirations are mislabeled, the reasonably possible change analysis is boilerplate, or the disclosure omits the policy and balance information users need. A weak disclosure invites auditor challenge and misleads investors about tax risk.

Use this skill before preparing the uncertain positions rollforward, drafting the tax footnote disclosure, reviewing the reasonably possible change analysis, or reconciling the rollforward to the balance sheet and provision. The goal is to produce a rollforward and disclosure that are complete, correctly categorized, reconciled, and decision-useful.

This is a tax accounting domain. Uncertain tax position disclosure requirements are specific to ASC 740 (US GAAP), which mandates a rollforward and reasonably possible change disclosure. IAS 12 (IFRS) and local frameworks have different disclosure requirements and may not require an equivalent rollforward. Do not assume the ASC 740 disclosure format applies universally. Final disclosure and uncertain position judgments should be routed to a qualified tax professional. This skill is operational guidance for preparing and reviewing the rollforward and disclosure, not professional tax advice, and it carries an explicit limitation that framework-specific disclosure rules are not exhaustively covered.

## Core Rules

### Build A Complete Beginning-To-Ending Rollforward

The rollforward reconciles the liability for unrecognized tax benefits from the beginning of the period to the end. It must capture every category of movement. A standard rollforward includes:

- beginning balance;
- gross increases for positions taken or expected to be taken in the current year (additions for prior-year positions and current-year positions);
- gross decreases for positions taken in prior years resulting from settlements, lapse of the statute of limitations, or changes in judgment;
- decreases for positions taken in the current year;
- reductions from settlements with taxing authorities;
- reductions from lapse of the statute of limitations;
- ending balance.

Each line should be populated only with the categories the framework requires, and categories should not be commingled. A settlement is not a statute expiration; a change in judgment is not a settlement.

### Reconcile The Rollforward To The Balance Sheet And Provision

The ending balance of the rollforward must equal the unrecognized tax benefit liability on the balance sheet. The net change in the rollforward must reconcile to the effect recognized in the income tax provision (or equity, where applicable). If either tie fails, a movement was misclassified or omitted. Build the reconciliation into the workpapers so the rollforward is demonstrably correct, not merely formatted.

### Categorize Increases And Decreases Correctly

Mislabeling movements is the most common rollforward error. Use the framework's required categories and apply them consistently:

- increases for current-year positions taken or expected to be taken;
- increases for prior-year positions (newly identified or re-measured upward);
- decreases for current-year positions;
- decreases for prior-year positions from settlements, statute expirations, or changes in judgment;
- reductions from settlements with taxing authorities;
- reductions from statute-of-limitations expirations.

Confirm whether the framework requires separate presentation of settlements and statute expirations, and whether positions taken in the current year are shown gross or net.

### Perform The Reasonably Possible Change Analysis

The disclosure must identify positions for which it is reasonably possible the balance will change significantly within the 12 months following the reporting date, and estimate the range of potential change. This is forward-looking and requires identifying open examinations, positions under administrative appeal, statutes expiring within the year, and positions where new authority is pending.

Do not default to "unknown" or omit the analysis. Identify the specific positions, the event that would change the balance (settlement, statute expiration, audit closure), and the estimated range. If no reasonably possible changes are identified, state that affirmatively with support.

### Disclose The Interest And Penalties Policy And Amounts

The disclosure must include the accounting policy for classifying tax-related interest and penalties and the amounts recognized, consistent with the entity's election. Coordinate this with the interest and penalties schedule so the disclosed amounts tie to the underlying accrual. Where interest and penalties are in the tax line, note the effect on the rate.

### Disclose Open Years, Jurisdictions, And Examination Status

Users need context on tax risk. Disclose the open tax years subject to examination by major jurisdiction, and, where relevant, the status of ongoing examinations. This situates the unrecognized benefit balance in terms of how much is still exposed to challenge and for how long.

### Apply Materiality And Consistency To Presentation

Apply a consistent materiality approach to which movements and positions are separately presented or aggregated. Inconsistent presentation period over period, without disclosure, makes trend analysis misleading. Maintain consistent categorization and disclose any changes in presentation.

## Common Traps

### A Rollforward That Foots But Is Not Informative

Mathematical correctness is necessary but not sufficient. If categories are commingled or settlements are buried in a generic decrease line, the disclosure does not inform users about the nature of movements.

### Mislabeling Settlements As Statute Expirations

A settlement is an agreement with the authority; a statute expiration is the passage of time ending exposure. They have different implications and must be separately categorized per the framework.

### Boilerplate Reasonably Possible Change Analysis

Defaulting to "unknown" or a generic statement fails the requirement. Identify specific positions, triggering events, and estimated ranges, or affirmatively state none exist with support.

### Omitting The Interest And Penalties Policy Disclosure

Failing to disclose the classification policy and amounts leaves users unable to interpret the effective rate and the full tax cost. The policy and amounts are required.

### Rollforward That Does Not Tie To The Balance Sheet

An ending balance that does not equal the liability on the balance sheet indicates a movement was omitted or misclassified. Reconcile before finalizing.

### Inconsistent Categorization Across Periods

Changing which movements are separately presented each year, without disclosure, obscures trends. Apply a consistent materiality and categorization approach.

### Ignoring Open Years And Examination Status

Omitting the open years and jurisdictions leaves the unrecognized benefit balance without context on remaining exposure. Disclose open years by major jurisdiction.

## Self-Check

- Does the rollforward reconcile beginning to ending balance using the framework's required categories for increases, decreases, settlements, and statute expirations?
- Does the ending balance equal the unrecognized tax benefit liability on the balance sheet, and does the net change tie to the provision?
- Are settlements, statute-of-limitations expirations, and changes in judgment separately categorized rather than commingled?
- Are current-year and prior-year position movements presented per the framework's gross or net requirement?
- Does the reasonably possible change analysis identify specific positions, triggering events, and estimated ranges within the next 12 months, or affirmatively state none with support?
- Is the interest and penalties classification policy disclosed, with amounts recognized that tie to the underlying schedule?
- Are open tax years and major jurisdictions subject to examination disclosed to contextualize remaining exposure?
- Is the status of ongoing examinations disclosed where relevant?
- Is presentation materiality and categorization applied consistently period over period, with any changes disclosed?
- Is professional referral noted for uncertain position judgments, and is the explicit limitation on framework-specific disclosure rules acknowledged?
