# Measurement And Derecognition

Once an uncertain tax position passes the recognition threshold, measurement determines how much of the benefit to record, and derecognition determines when a position or its reserve should be removed or adjusted. Measurement under frameworks such as ASC 740 is not the expected value or a probability-weighted average. It is the largest amount of benefit that is more likely than not to be realized upon ultimate settlement, evaluated as a cumulative probability. This produces counterintuitive results that preparers frequently mishandle. Derecognition is similarly nuanced, because it depends on the effective settlement of the position, not merely the passage of time or the filing of a return.

Use this skill before measuring an uncertain tax position, adjusting a reserve, derecognizing a position, or rolling forward balances across periods. The goal is to prevent the agent from measuring positions as expected values, derecognizing too early or too late, or letting reserves drift without reassessment. Measurement and derecognition standards, principally ASC 740 and analogous guidance, are jurisdiction-specific and technically demanding. This skill provides structural judgment, not final determinations. A qualified tax professional, often with counsel, must conclude on any actual measurement or derecognition.

## Core Rules

### Measure At The Largest Amount More Likely Than Not To Be Realized

The measurement standard asks for the largest amount of benefit that is more than fifty percent likely to be realized upon ultimate settlement with the relevant authority. This is a cumulative probability assessment across possible outcomes, not an expected value. If there is a forty percent chance of full benefit, a thirty percent chance of seventy percent benefit, and a thirty percent chance of zero, the measurement is not the weighted average. It is the largest amount for which the cumulative probability exceeds fifty percent.

Work through the outcomes:

- list the possible settlement outcomes and their amounts;
- order them and assess the cumulative probability of realizing at least each amount;
- identify the largest amount for which cumulative probability is more than fifty percent;
- record the benefit at that amount, with the difference from full benefit as the reserve.

### Evaluate Settlement As The Derecognition Trigger

Derecognition occurs when the position is effectively settled through examination, agreement, or expiration of the statute of limitations, depending on the framework. The standard generally requires that the position be effectively settled, meaning the relevant authority has completed its examination and the entity has agreed, or the statute has expired with no examination possible. Do not derecognize merely because a return was filed or a period closed without examination unless the framework's effective settlement test is met.

### Distinguish Settlement From Ongoing Examination

An ongoing examination does not by itself trigger derecognition. The reserve is adjusted when the examination concludes with an effective settlement, or when facts change such that the measurement changes. During examination, reassess the measurement as new information emerges, but do not prematurely release the reserve.

### Adjust Measurement When Facts Or Law Change

Measurement must be reassessed each reporting date for changes in facts, law, interpretation, new authority guidance, examination progress, or settlement. An increase in the realizability of a position increases the recognized benefit and decreases the reserve. A decrease does the opposite. These adjustments flow through the effective tax rate in the period of change, subject to framework-specific presentation rules.

### Handle Interest And Penalties Under A Consistent Policy

Frameworks require a policy election for how to treat interest and penalties related to uncertain tax positions, typically either in the tax provision or as a separate expense. Apply the policy consistently and accrue interest and penalties from the start of the position through settlement. Do not ignore accruals, because they can be material and their treatment is scrutinized.

### Roll Forward Balances With A Clear Schedule

Maintain a rollforward of uncertain tax position balances that shows the beginning reserve, additions for new positions, increases and decreases for remeasurement, settlements, and statute expirations, ending with the closing reserve. This schedule is the primary evidence of movement and is expected by auditors. Without it, reserve changes are unexplained.

Typical rollforward components:

- beginning balance of the reserve;
- gross up of new positions taken in the period;
- increases or decreases from remeasurement of existing positions;
- reductions for settlements with authorities;
- reductions for statute of limitations expirations;
- ending balance reconciled to the balance sheet.

### Treat Statute Expiration Carefully

Expiration of the statute of limitations can support derecognition when the framework requires effective settlement and the statute has run without examination. But the exact rule depends on the jurisdiction and the framework. Confirm whether mere expiration suffices or whether effective settlement requires more, and apply the rule consistently. Track statute expiration dates by position and jurisdiction.

### Reconcile Reserve Movement To The Effective Tax Rate

The net change in uncertain tax position reserves, excluding settlements that are discrete items, flows through the effective tax rate. Large unexplained movements distort the rate and attract scrutiny. Tie reserve changes to specific positions and events so the rate impact is explainable.

## Common Traps

### Measuring As An Expected Value

The most common measurement error is recording the probability-weighted average of outcomes. The standard requires the largest amount more likely than not to be realized, which is usually higher than the expected value. Using expected value understates the recognized benefit and overstates the reserve.

### Derecognizing On Filing Or Period Close

Filing a return or closing a period without examination does not generally meet the effective settlement test. Derecognizing on these events releases reserves prematurely and overstates the benefit.

### Releasing Reserves During Ongoing Examination

An examination in progress is not a settlement. Releasing the reserve because the authority has not yet challenged the position, or because discussions look favorable, is premature. Wait for effective settlement or a change in measurement facts.

### Ignoring Interest And Penalties

Interest and penalties on uncertain positions can be material and accrue over years. Failing to accrue, or applying the policy inconsistently, misstates the provision and breaches the framework.

### Letting The Rollforward Drift Without Reconciliation

A reserve that moves without a documented rollforward is indefensible. Auditors expect a clear schedule from beginning to ending balance with identifiable events. Unexplained movement is a red flag.

### Treating All Statute Expirations As Automatic Derecognition

Statute expiration may support derecognition, but the exact rule varies. Assuming expiration always suffices can release reserves that should remain until effective settlement under the applicable framework.

### Forgetting To Reassess Each Period

Positions are not static. New law, new guidance, examination developments, and changed facts all affect measurement. Failing to reassess leaves stale reserves that misstate the balance.

### Confusing Settlement Accounting With Measurement Changes

A settlement that concludes an examination is a discrete event with specific accounting. A measurement change based on new facts is a different adjustment. Mixing the two misstates the rollforward and the rate impact.

## Self-Check

- Is each position measured at the largest amount more likely than not to be realized upon ultimate settlement, evaluated as a cumulative probability rather than an expected value?
- Is derecognition based on effective settlement, such as completed examination with agreement or statute expiration under the framework, rather than on filing or period close?
- Are reserves maintained during ongoing examination, with measurement reassessed as new information emerges rather than released prematurely?
- Is measurement reassessed each reporting date for changes in facts, law, interpretation, authority guidance, and examination progress?
- Are interest and penalties accrued under a consistently applied policy from the start of the position through settlement?
- Does a rollforward schedule reconcile beginning to ending reserve balances through additions, remeasurement, settlements, and statute expirations?
- Are statute expiration dates tracked by position and jurisdiction, with derecognition applied only where the framework supports it?
- Does the net change in reserves, excluding discrete settlements, reconcile to the effective tax rate with identifiable drivers?
- Are settlement events and measurement changes accounted for separately so the rollforward remains clean?
- Has a qualified tax professional, with counsel where appropriate, concluded on the measurement and derecognition for the relevant jurisdiction and framework?
