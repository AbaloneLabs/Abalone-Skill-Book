# Disclosure And Computation

Uncertain tax position disclosure is where the judgment, measurement, and documentation of the prior steps become visible to financial statement users. The disclosure has two layers. The computation layer is the tabular rollforward of reserves and the classification of balances on the face of the financial statements. The narrative layer is the description of positions, potential changes, and the policy for interest and penalties. Both must be internally consistent with the underlying analysis and with each other. A disclosure that is technically formatted but inconsistent with the reserve schedule, or that omits material positions, misleads users and creates audit and regulatory exposure.

Use this skill before preparing uncertain tax position disclosures, computing the reserve rollforward, classifying balances, drafting footnote narrative, or reviewing the disclosure package. The goal is to prevent the agent from producing a disclosure that looks compliant but disagrees with the underlying computation or hides material positions behind vague language. Disclosure requirements under ASC 740 and analogous frameworks are jurisdiction-specific and detailed. This skill provides structural judgment, not final determinations. A qualified tax professional must review and approve any actual disclosure.

## Core Rules

### Compute The Rollforward From Verified Beginning And Ending Balances

The tabular rollforward reconciles the beginning and ending uncertain tax position reserves for the period. Every component must trace to underlying positions and events. Do not present a rollforward that does not tie to the detailed position schedule. The rollforward is typically presented with categories for gross additions, increases and decreases from remeasurement, settlements, and statute expirations.

Verify:

- the beginning balance agrees with the prior period ending balance;
- additions represent new positions taken in the period;
- increases and decreases reflect remeasurement of existing positions;
- settlements reflect effective settlements with authorities;
- statute expirations reflect positions no longer subject to examination;
- the ending balance agrees to the balance sheet liability.

### Classify Balances As Current Or Noncurrent Consistently

Uncertain tax position liabilities must be classified as current or noncurrent based on when the underlying amounts are expected to be settled, consistent with the entity's balance sheet classification policy. A position expected to settle within a year may be current, while one tied to a multi-year examination may be noncurrent. Apply the classification policy consistently across periods and document the basis. Misclassification distorts working capital and liquidity analysis.

### Disclose Positions That Could Materially Change Within A Year

Frameworks often require disclosure of positions that could significantly change within the next year, such as positions under active examination, in settlement discussions, or nearing statute expiration. Identify these positions and disclose the potential range of change where reasonably estimable. Vague language that all positions could change does not meet the spirit of the requirement when specific material positions are identifiable.

### Describe The Nature Of Material Positions Without Overstatement

The narrative should describe the nature of material uncertain tax positions to the extent practicable, such as the general type of issue or jurisdiction, without overstating certainty or understating risk. Balance transparency with the sensitivity of tax strategy. Avoid language that implies positions are more sustainable than the recognition and measurement analysis supports.

### State The Interest And Penalties Policy

The disclosure must state the entity's accounting policy for interest and penalties related to uncertain tax positions, including where they are classified in the income statement. State the policy clearly and apply it consistently with the accruals. If interest and penalties accrued are material, consider whether separate disclosure of the amounts is required.

### Reconcile The Disclosure To The Provision And Balance Sheet

The uncertain tax position disclosure must reconcile to both the income tax provision and the balance sheet liability. The net change in reserves flows through the effective tax rate. The ending reserve balance equals the liability on the balance sheet. Any inconsistency between the disclosure, the provision, and the balance sheet indicates an error that must be resolved before issuance.

### Maintain Comparability Across Periods

Prior period comparative figures must be presented consistently. If categories or policies change, explain the change and restate comparatives where required. Inconsistent presentation breaks trend analysis and is a common review finding.

### Coordinate Disclosure With Recognition And Measurement Work

The disclosure is the output of the recognition and measurement process. It cannot be drafted in isolation from the underlying analysis. Ensure that the positions described, the amounts shown, and the narrative all reflect the conclusions reached in the recognition and measurement steps. Drafting disclosure that drifts from the analysis creates indefensible positions.

## Common Traps

### Presenting A Rollforward That Does Not Tie To Detail

A rollforward presented without underlying position detail is unsupported. Auditors and reviewers will demand the detail, and a rollforward that does not reconcile to it indicates hidden errors or adjustments.

### Inconsistent Current Or Noncurrent Classification

Classifying balances without a consistent policy, or changing classification period to period without explanation, distorts liquidity metrics and invites questions. Apply and document the policy.

### Vague Language Hiding Material Positions

Generic statements that results are uncertain, without identifying specific material positions that could change soon, understate risk and fail the disclosure objective when specific positions are identifiable.

### Overstating Sustainability In Narrative

Language suggesting positions are well-supported or likely to be sustained, when the recognition analysis is marginal, contradicts the underlying analysis and overstates the benefit. Match narrative tone to the merits assessment.

### Omitting The Interest And Penalties Policy

Forgetting to state the policy, or stating it inconsistently with the accruals, is a basic but common omission. The policy must be disclosed and applied consistently.

### Disclosure Disagreeing With The Provision Or Balance Sheet

If the reserve movement in the disclosure does not match the tax provision impact, or the ending balance does not match the balance sheet, the disclosure is wrong. Reconcile all three before issuance.

### Forgetting Comparative Restatement

When categories or policies change, failing to restate or explain prior period comparatives breaks consistency and comparability.

### Drafting Disclosure Separately From The Analysis

Disclosure written without reference to the recognition and measurement conclusions drifts from the actual positions. Draft disclosure from the analysis, not from a template.

## Self-Check

- Does the reserve rollforward reconcile from a verified beginning balance through additions, remeasurement, settlements, and statute expirations to an ending balance that ties to the balance sheet?
- Are uncertain tax position liabilities classified as current or noncurrent under a consistent, documented policy based on expected settlement timing?
- Are positions that could materially change within the next year identified and, where estimable, accompanied by a potential range of change rather than vague language?
- Does the narrative describe the nature of material positions with a tone that matches the recognition and measurement analysis rather than overstating sustainability?
- Is the interest and penalties accounting policy stated clearly and applied consistently with the amounts accrued?
- Does the disclosure reconcile to both the income tax provision and the balance sheet liability with no unexplained differences?
- Are prior period comparatives presented consistently, with any category or policy changes explained and comparatives restated where required?
- Is the disclosure drafted directly from the recognition and measurement conclusions rather than from a disconnected template?
- Has a qualified tax professional reviewed and approved the disclosure for the relevant jurisdiction and framework before issuance?
