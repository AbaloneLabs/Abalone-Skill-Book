# Affiliate And Influencer Programs

Performance partner programs pay for outcomes, which makes them attractive and also attractive to abuse. They fail when commission structures reward volume over quality, when partners send low-intent or fraudulent conversions, when the program attracts partners who erode brand with aggressive tactics, or when measurement credits outcomes that would have happened anyway. A good program aligns partner incentives with real business value and guards quality as carefully as it guards growth.

Use this skill before building or revising an affiliate or influencer program, setting commission structures, vetting partners, or reviewing program performance. The goal is to prevent the agent from scaling a program that grows conversions while eroding quality and brand.

## Core Rules

### Align Commission To Business Value, Not Just Conversion

The commission structure determines what partners optimize for. Align it to real value.

Align by:

- paying more for higher-quality conversions;
- tiering commissions by partner performance and quality;
- incorporating retention and LTV into payout where possible;
- avoiding structures that reward volume regardless of value;
- clawback or adjustment for early churn or returns.

A flat commission for any conversion rewards partners who send low-intent traffic. Aligned commissions reward partners who send customers who stay and pay.

### Vet Partners For Brand And Quality Fit

Not every partner who can send traffic should be in the program. Vet for fit.

Vet for:

- audience alignment with your target customer;
- content quality and brand safety;
- tactics that respect your brand and terms;
- history of quality performance;
- absence of fraudulent or deceptive practices.

A partner who sends volume through aggressive or misleading tactics damages the brand and attracts the wrong customers. Vetting protects quality.

### Prevent Fraud And Low-Quality Conversions

Performance programs attract fraud. Build controls from the start.

Prevent:

- self-referrals and cookie stuffing;
- brand bidding and trademark abuse;
- incentive and cashback sites capturing organic demand;
- fake or low-quality leads and transactions;
- returns, chargebacks, and early churn patterns.

Fraud and low-quality conversions inflate costs and distort performance data. Monitoring and controls are not optional in performance programs.

### Measure Incrementality, Not Just Attributed Conversion

Partners get credit for conversions, but some would have happened anyway. Measure incrementality.

Measure:

- whether partner-driven conversions are incremental;
- branded search and coupon capture that is not incremental;
- new versus returning customers from partners;
- LTV and retention of partner-acquired customers;
- the true cost per incremental customer.

A partner who captures demand that would have converted organically adds cost without value. Incrementality separates real contribution from capture.

### Set Clear Terms And Enforce Them

Programs need clear rules and consistent enforcement. Define and uphold terms.

Define and enforce:

- allowed and prohibited promotional methods;
- trademark and brand-bidding rules;
- coupon and incentive policies;
- disclosure and compliance requirements;
- consequences for violations.

Ambiguous or unenforced terms invite abuse and brand damage. Clear terms, consistently enforced, protect the program and the brand.

### Distinguish Affiliate From Influencer Dynamics

Affiliates and influencers operate differently and need different management.

Distinguish:

- affiliates optimize for conversion and scale, often transactionally;
- influencers build audience trust and create brand affinity;
- affiliate success is measured in attributed conversions;
- influencer success includes reach, engagement, and brand lift;
- the two require different vetting, contracts, and measurement.

Treating influencers like affiliates, or vice versa, mismanages both. Match the approach to the partner type.

### Nurture High-Quality Partners

The best partners are an asset. Invest in the relationship.

Nurture:

- communicate regularly with top partners;
- provide creative, offers, and support;
- recognize and reward quality performance;
- give top partners exclusives or higher tiers;
- solicit their input on what converts.

High-quality partners can scale if supported. Neglecting them in favor of recruiting new ones leaves value unrealized.

## Common Traps

### Volume Over Quality Commission

Flat commissions for any conversion reward low-intent and low-value traffic.

### Insufficient Partner Vetting

Partners who use aggressive or deceptive tactics damage the brand and attract wrong customers.

### Fraud And Cookie Stuffing

Performance programs attract abuse that inflates costs and distorts data.

### Non-Incremental Capture

Crediting partners for conversions that would have happened organically wastes spend.

### Unenforced Terms

Ambiguous or unenforced rules invite brand bidding, coupon abuse, and misleading tactics.

### Treating Influencers As Affiliates

Applying conversion-only measurement to influencers misses their brand and reach value.

### Recruiting Over Nurturing

Chasing new partners while neglecting high-quality existing ones leaves value unrealized.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Commission structures reward conversion quality and retention, not just volume.
- [ ] Partners are vetted for audience fit, content quality, and brand-safe tactics.
- [ ] Fraud controls prevent self-referrals, cookie stuffing, brand bidding, and fake conversions.
- [ ] Incrementality is measured, separating real contribution from organic demand capture.
- [ ] LTV and retention of partner-acquired customers are tracked, not just conversion counts.
- [ ] Program terms are clear, communicated, and consistently enforced.
- [ ] Affiliate and influencer partners are managed with approaches matched to their type.
- [ ] High-quality partners are nurtured with support, recognition, and exclusives.
- [ ] Coupon, cashback, and incentive partners are evaluated for incrementality, not just volume.
- [ ] The program's cost per incremental, quality customer is understood and monitored.
