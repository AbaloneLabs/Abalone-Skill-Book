# SEM Account Structure And Bidding Strategy

Search engine marketing, paid search, is a channel where structure determines performance. How an account is organized into campaigns and ad groups, how keywords are matched and grouped, how bids and budgets are set, governs whether spend produces profitable acquisition or wasted clicks. The judgment problem is that SEM rewards tight alignment between keywords, ads, and landing pages, and punishes loose, broad structure with low Quality Scores, high costs, and irrelevant traffic. The most common failure is structuring accounts for convenience rather than for relevance, producing ad groups with unrelated keywords, generic ads, and high costs per conversion. The skill is building account structure and bidding strategy that aligns relevance, controls cost, and scales profitably.

Use this skill before building or restructuring a SEM account, before choosing bidding strategies, before scaling spend, or when SEM performance is poor despite investment. The goal is to prevent the agent from building loose account structure, from bidding without attention to Quality Score and relevance, or from scaling spend before profitability is established.

## Core Rules

### [ ] Structure For Relevance, Not Convenience

Account structure determines relevance, and relevance determines Quality Score and cost. Campaigns and ad groups should be organized so that keywords, ads, and landing pages are tightly aligned. Structure built for convenience, few ad groups with many keywords, destroys relevance and raises costs.

- [ ] Organize ad groups around tightly themed keyword clusters.
- [ ] Ensure ads closely match the keywords in their ad group.
- [ ] Send traffic to landing pages matched to the keyword intent.
- [ ] Split ad groups when keywords diverge in intent or theme.

### [ ] Use Match Types Deliberately

Match types, exact, phrase, broad, control how closely a query must match the keyword to trigger an ad. Broad match casts wide but wastes spend on irrelevant queries; exact match is precise but limits volume. Match type strategy balances reach and relevance, with negative keywords managing the gaps.

- [ ] Start with controlled match types and expand based on data.
- [ ] Use exact and phrase match for high-value, proven keywords.
- [ ] Use broad match cautiously, with conversion tracking and negatives.
- [ ] Build comprehensive negative keyword lists to filter irrelevant queries.

### [ ] Optimize Quality Score To Lower Costs

Quality Score, the platform's rating of relevance and experience, directly affects cost per click and ad position. Higher Quality Score means lower cost for the same position. Improving relevance through structure, ad copy, and landing page experience lowers costs without cutting bids.

- [ ] Monitor Quality Score at the keyword level.
- [ ] Improve ad relevance by matching copy to keywords.
- [ ] Improve landing page experience and relevance.
- [ ] Treat Quality Score as a cost lever, not just a diagnostic.

### [ ] Choose Bidding Strategy To Match The Goal

Bidding strategies, manual CPC, maximize clicks, target CPA, target ROAS, maximize conversions, optimize for different goals. The strategy must match the campaign goal and the maturity of conversion data. Mismatched bidding strategies optimize for the wrong outcome.

- [ ] Match bidding strategy to the campaign goal: clicks, conversions, or value.
- [ ] Use automated bidding only with sufficient conversion data.
- [ ] Use target CPA or ROAS to enforce profitability constraints.
- [ ] Revisit bidding strategy as goals and data maturity change.

### [ ] Set Budgets To Capture Profitable Demand

Budgets that are too low cap profitable campaigns and cede demand to competitors; budgets that are too high can overwhelm unprofitable campaigns. Budget allocation should follow performance, feeding profitable campaigns and constraining unproven ones.

- [ ] Allocate budget based on campaign performance and potential.
- [ ] Avoid capping campaigns that are converting profitably.
- [ ] Constrain spend on unproven or unprofitable campaigns.
- [ ] Reallocate budget from poor to strong performers regularly.

### [ ] Track Conversions And Value, Not Just Clicks

Clicks are a cost; conversions and conversion value are the return. Without conversion tracking, SEM optimizes for traffic rather than outcomes, and profitability is invisible. Tracking must capture the actions and values that matter to the business.

- [ ] Implement conversion tracking for all meaningful actions.
- [ ] Track conversion value, not just conversion count, where possible.
- [ ] Feed conversion data into automated bidding strategies.
- [ ] Audit tracking regularly for accuracy and completeness.

### [ ] Write Ads That Match Intent And Earn Clicks

Ad copy must match the keyword's intent and compel the click. Generic copy across ad groups lowers relevance and click-through rate, raising costs. Copy should be specific to the keyword cluster and clearly communicate the value and offer.

- [ ] Write ad copy specific to each ad group's keyword theme.
- [ ] Include the keyword and a clear value proposition.
- [ ] Test multiple ad variations continuously.
- [ ] Use ad extensions to increase visibility and relevance.

### [ ] Manage The Query Report And Negative Keywords

The search terms report reveals what queries actually triggered ads, exposing irrelevant traffic that wastes spend. Mining this report for negative keywords is essential to refining targeting and protecting budget. Without active negative management, broad targeting bleeds spend.

- [ ] Review the search terms report regularly.
- [ ] Add negative keywords for irrelevant queries.
- [ ] Use negative match types to control exclusion scope.
- [ ] Build and maintain negative keyword lists across campaigns.

### [ ] Separate Campaigns By Intent And Funnel Stage

Different keywords serve different funnel stages: branded vs. non-branded, research vs. transactional, competitor vs. category. Mixing them in one campaign obscures performance and misallocates budget. Separating by intent enables clear measurement and targeted strategy.

- [ ] Separate branded and non-branded campaigns.
- [ ] Separate research, consideration, and transactional intent.
- [ ] Allocate budget and strategy by funnel stage.
- [ ] Measure performance by intent segment, not blended.

### [ ] Scale Profitably, Not Just Aggressively

Scaling SEM by pouring more budget into unproven campaigns burns money. Profitable scaling requires expanding what works: proven keywords, successful ad copy, high-converting landing pages, and new keywords validated through testing. Scale follows proof, not precedes it.

- [ ] Scale campaigns that have proven profitability.
- [ ] Expand keyword sets based on query data and conversion evidence.
- [ ] Test new campaigns with controlled budgets before scaling.
- [ ] Maintain profitability constraints, target CPA or ROAS, during scaling.

## Common Traps

### [ ] Loose Account Structure

Ad groups with many unrelated keywords, generic ads, and low relevance.

### [ ] Broad Match Without Negatives

Casting wide without filtering, wasting spend on irrelevant queries.

### [ ] Ignoring Quality Score

Paying more than necessary due to poor relevance signals.

### [ ] Mismatched Bidding Strategy

Optimizing for clicks or using automated bidding without conversion data.

### [ ] Blended Branded And Non-Branded

Mixing high-intent branded traffic with harder non-branded, obscuring performance.

### [ ] Click Tracking Only

Optimizing for clicks without conversion or value tracking.

### [ ] Generic Ad Copy

One-size-fits-all copy that lowers relevance and click-through.

### [ ] Scaling Before Profitability

Pouring budget into unproven campaigns before establishing conversion economics.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Is the account structured for tight relevance between keywords, ads, and landing pages?
- [ ] Are match types chosen deliberately with comprehensive negative keywords?
- [ ] Is Quality Score monitored and improved to lower costs?
- [ ] Does the bidding strategy match the campaign goal and data maturity?
- [ ] Are budgets allocated to capture profitable demand without capping winners?
- [ ] Is conversion and value tracking implemented and accurate?
- [ ] Does ad copy match keyword intent and earn clicks through testing?
- [ ] Is the search terms report mined regularly for negative keywords?
- [ ] Are campaigns separated by intent and funnel stage for clear measurement?
- [ ] Is scaling based on proven profitability rather than aggressive spend?
