# Email Strategy And List Health

Email is one of the few owned channels a marketer truly controls, but its value depends entirely on the quality of the list and the trust of the recipients. Email programs fail when they buy or scrape lists, when they prioritize list size over engagement, when they ignore deliverability fundamentals, when they send the same blast to everyone, or when they treat consent as a legal checkbox rather than a relationship. A degraded list damages deliverability for everyone, including the recipients who actually want the email.

Use this skill before building an email strategy, growing or cleaning a list, setting up lifecycle programs, or diagnosing deliverability or engagement problems. The goal is to prevent the agent from treating email as a free broadcast channel rather than a permission-based relationship that must be protected.

## Core Rules

### Treat Consent As The Foundation

Email only works with people who actually want it. Consent is not a formality; it is the asset.

Establish:

- clear, opt-in consent with proof of how and when it was given;
- expectations set at signup about what subscribers will receive and how often;
- no purchased, rented, or scraped lists that never consented to you;
- easy, working unsubscribe in every message;
- honoring of opt-outs promptly across all systems.

A list built on weak or absent consent produces spam complaints and bounces that degrade deliverability for the entire program.

### Prioritize List Quality Over Size

A large disengaged list is a liability, not an asset. Engagement protects deliverability; disengagement destroys it.

Manage quality:

- focus on engaged subscribers who open, click, and convert;
- remove or suppress hard bounces, spam complaints, and long-term inactives;
- use sunset or re-engagement campaigns before removing inactive subscribers;
- avoid vanity metrics of list size that hide engagement decay;
- segment by engagement to protect the active core.

Deliverability algorithms reward engagement and punish lists full of recipients who ignore the email. Size without engagement is risk.

### Build Deliverability Foundations

Even great content cannot reach the inbox if the technical foundations are broken. Set them up and monitor them.

Ensure:

- proper authentication with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC;
- a clean sender domain and reputation;
- monitoring of spam complaints, bounces, and blocklists;
- warming of new sending domains or IPs;
- compliance with mailbox provider requirements and bulk sender rules.

Deliverability is invisible until it breaks, and when it breaks the entire program stops working. Treat it as infrastructure, not an afterthought.

### Segment Beyond The Blast

Broadcasting the same message to the entire list wastes the medium's greatest strength, relevance. Segment deliberately.

Segment by:

- engagement level, active versus inactive;
- lifecycle stage, prospect, trial, customer, churned;
- behavior, what they clicked, bought, or used;
- attributes, role, industry, plan, or interest;
- consent and preferences, what each subscriber opted into.

Relevance drives engagement, which drives deliverability, which drives results. Irrelevant blasts drive the opposite spiral.

### Balance Lifecycle And Broadcast Email

Different email types serve different purposes. Mix them deliberately, not randomly.

Distinguish:

- lifecycle and triggered email, welcome, onboarding, abandonment, re-engagement, that responds to behavior;
- broadcast and campaign email, newsletters and promotions, sent to many at once;
- transactional email, receipts and confirmations, that must arrive regardless of marketing status;
- the right balance so subscribers are not overwhelmed or neglected.

Lifecycle email usually outperforms broadcast because it is timely and relevant. Build the triggered foundations before chasing more broadcasts.

### Set Cadence And Frequency Deliberately

Sending too much causes fatigue and unsubscribes; sending too little causes forgetting. Cadence is a strategic choice.

Decide:

- the frequency each segment can sustain without fatigue;
- the value each message must deliver to justify the send;
- coordination across programs so a subscriber is not flooded;
- suppression rules so converted or churned users stop receiving irrelevant email;
- signals of fatigue, declining opens, rising unsubscribes or complaints.

Frequency should follow value and engagement, not an arbitrary publishing target.

### Measure Email By Engagement And Outcome

Email metrics are abundant and easy to misread. Measure what indicates a healthy relationship.

Track:

- deliverability and inbox placement, not just sent;
- engagement, opens, clicks, and conversions by segment;
- list growth from genuine sources, not purchases;
- churn, unsubscribes, complaints, and inactivity;
- revenue or pipeline influenced, with honest attribution;
- guardrails such as spam complaints above threshold.

Open rates are increasingly unreliable due to privacy changes. Weight clicks, conversions, and deliverability more heavily than opens.

## Common Traps

### Buying Or Scraping Lists

Purchased lists never consented to you and produce bounces, complaints, and deliverability damage.

### Size Over Engagement

A large disengaged list hurts deliverability and hides that few recipients actually care.

### Ignoring Deliverability

Broken authentication or poor sender reputation silently sends email to spam where no one sees it.

### Blasting Everyone Equally

Sending identical email to the whole list wastes relevance and accelerates disengagement.

### Over-Sending

Sending too frequently to hit a cadence target drives fatigue, unsubscribes, and complaints.

### Relying On Open Rates

Privacy changes have made open rates unreliable; over-optimizing them misleads strategy.

### No Sunset Process

Keeping inactive subscribers indefinitely inflates list size and degrades deliverability and metrics.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Consent is opt-in, documented, and matched to what subscribers actually receive.
- [ ] No purchased, rented, or scraped lists are used.
- [ ] List health is measured by engagement, not size, with inactive subscribers sunsetted.
- [ ] Deliverability foundations of SPF, DKIM, DMARC, and sender reputation are set up and monitored.
- [ ] Segmentation goes beyond the blast, targeting by engagement, lifecycle, behavior, and preference.
- [ ] Lifecycle and triggered email are built alongside, or before, broadcast campaigns.
- [ ] Cadence is set by value and engagement tolerance, with fatigue signals monitored.
- [ ] Unsubscribe is easy, working, and honored promptly across systems.
- [ ] Success is measured by clicks, conversions, deliverability, and revenue, not open rates alone.
- [ ] Spam complaints, bounces, and unsubscribes are tracked as guardrails, not ignored.
