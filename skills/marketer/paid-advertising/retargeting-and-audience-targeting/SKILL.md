# Retargeting And Audience Targeting

Audience targeting is how paid advertising reaches the right people rather than everyone, and retargeting is how it re-engages those who have already shown interest. Together they determine whether ad spend reaches prospects or wastes on irrelevant impressions. The judgment problem is that targeting that is too broad wastes budget on the uninterested, while targeting that is too narrow starves campaigns of scale, and the line shifts as platforms, privacy, and audiences change. The most common failure is either blunt retargeting that bombards all visitors identically, producing fatigue and wasted spend, or over-reliance on platform lookalikes without validation. The skill is designing audience targeting that reaches the right people across the funnel with appropriate frequency and exclusion.

Use this skill before building retargeting or audience targeting, before defining custom and lookalike audiences, before setting frequency and exclusions, or when retargeting is underperforming or causing fatigue. The goal is to prevent the agent from blunt retargeting, from audience definitions that are too broad or narrow, or from neglecting exclusions that waste spend.

## Core Rules

### [ ] Map Audiences To The Full Funnel

Different audiences serve different funnel stages: cold prospecting for awareness, consideration audiences for mid-funnel, retargeting for conversion, and customer audiences for retention and expansion. A targeting strategy must cover the full funnel with audiences matched to each stage's goal.

- [ ] Define audiences for prospecting, consideration, conversion, and retention.
- [ ] Match audience type and message to the funnel stage.
- [ ] Ensure no funnel stage is unsupported.
- [ ] Allocate budget across funnel stages based on goals.

### [ ] Segment Retargeting Audiences By Intent And Recency

All visitors are not equal. Someone who abandoned checkout has high intent; someone who bounced from a blog post has low intent. Segmenting retargeting audiences by behavior and recency allows tailored messaging and spend, rather than identical ads to all.

- [ ] Segment retargeting audiences by depth of engagement, e.g., homepage vs. product vs. checkout.
- [ ] Segment by recency, treating recent visitors differently from older ones.
- [ ] Tailor message and offer to each segment's intent.
- [ ] Allocate more spend to higher-intent segments.

### [ ] Build Custom Audiences From First-Party Data

First-party data, customer lists, site visitors, engagers, is the foundation of precise targeting in a privacy-constrained world. Custom audiences built from first-party data reach known prospects and customers with relevant messaging, and seed lookalikes. Investing in first-party data collection strengthens targeting over time.

- [ ] Build custom audiences from CRM, email, and customer data.
- [ ] Build audiences from site and app engagement.
- [ ] Use first-party audiences for retention, upsell, and suppression.
- [ ] Invest in first-party data collection to reduce reliance on third-party targeting.

### [ ] Use Lookalikes Strategically And Validate Them

Lookalike audiences extend reach by finding people similar to known customers. They are powerful but not all lookalikes perform; their quality depends on the seed audience and the platform. Validate lookalikes against performance rather than assuming they work.

- [ ] Build lookalikes from high-value seed audiences, not broad ones.
- [ ] Test lookalike quality against conversion and value, not reach.
- [ ] Iterate seed audiences based on which lookalikes perform.
- [ ] Do not assume lookalikes perform; prove it.

### [ ] Set Frequency Caps To Prevent Fatigue

Showing the same ad too often causes fatigue, wasted spend, and negative brand sentiment. Frequency caps limit how often an individual sees an ad within a period. Without caps, retargeting in particular can harass audiences and damage the brand.

- [ ] Set frequency caps appropriate to the funnel stage and message.
- [ ] Use tighter caps for retargeting than prospecting.
- [ ] Monitor frequency and adjust when fatigue signs appear.
- [ ] Rotate creative to reduce fatigue within frequency limits.

### [ ] Use Exclusions To Avoid Wasted Spend

Exclusions prevent ads from reaching irrelevant or already-converted audiences: current customers seeing acquisition ads, recent purchasers seeing the same offer, employees seeing ads. Without exclusions, spend wastes on people who should not be targeted.

- [ ] Exclude current customers from acquisition campaigns.
- [ ] Exclude recent purchasers from the offers they just used.
- [ ] Exclude employees, competitors, and irrelevant geographies.
- [ ] Review and update exclusions regularly.

### [ ] Refresh Creative To Combat Fatigue

Audience fatigue is driven by creative repetition as much as frequency. Even well-targeted audiences stop responding to ads they have seen many times. Regular creative refresh sustains performance and prevents decline.

- [ ] Monitor creative fatigue through performance decline.
- [ ] Refresh creative on a regular cadence.
- [ ] Test new creative against proven winners.
- [ ] Retire creative that has fatigued.

### [ ] Adapt To Privacy Changes And Signal Loss

Privacy changes, iOS ATT, cookie deprecation, regulation, are reducing the precision of targeting and attribution. Strategies built on third-party cookies and granular tracking are degrading. Targeting must adapt to a world of less signal through first-party data, contextual targeting, and modeling.

- [ ] Reduce reliance on third-party cookies and granular tracking.
- [ ] Invest in first-party data and consented audiences.
- [ ] Use contextual targeting where behavioral targeting is weakening.
- [ ] Adopt modeled and aggregated measurement where direct tracking is lost.

### [ ] Measure Audience Performance By Quality And Value

Audience performance must be measured on the quality and value of conversions, not just volume and cost. An audience that converts cheaply but produces low-value or churning customers is not profitable. Measure audience-level LTV and quality.

- [ ] Track conversion quality and value by audience.
- [ ] Measure audience-level ROI and payback, not just CPA.
- [ ] Shift spend toward audiences that produce high-value customers.
- [ ] Retire audiences that convert cheaply but produce low value.

### [ ] Coordinate Targeting Across Platforms

Audiences exist across multiple platforms, and uncoordinated targeting can overlap, over-frequency, or contradict. Coordinating audience strategy across platforms manages frequency in aggregate and presents a coherent experience. Siloed platform targeting wastes spend and confuses audiences.

- [ ] Coordinate audience definitions and exclusions across platforms.
- [ ] Manage frequency in aggregate where possible.
- [ ] Align messaging across platforms for the same audience.
- [ ] Deduplicate measurement across platforms.

## Common Traps

### [ ] Blunt Retargeting

Treating all visitors identically regardless of intent or recency.

### [ ] Over-Frequency

Showing the same ad repeatedly, causing fatigue and brand damage.

### [ ] No Exclusions

Wasting spend on current customers, employees, and irrelevant audiences.

### [ ] Lookalike Assumption

Assuming lookalikes perform without validating against quality and value.

### [ ] Third-Party Reliance

Depending on cookies and granular tracking that privacy changes are degrading.

### [ ] Creative Fatigue

Letting performance decline through unrefreshed creative.

### [ ] Volume Over Value

Optimizing audiences for cheap conversions rather than high-value customers.

### [ ] Siloed Platform Targeting

Uncoordinated audiences across platforms causing overlap and incoherence.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Are audiences mapped to the full funnel with stage-appropriate messaging?
- [ ] Are retargeting audiences segmented by intent and recency, not treated as one?
- [ ] Are custom audiences built from first-party data to reduce reliance on third-party?
- [ ] Are lookalikes built from high-value seeds and validated against performance?
- [ ] Are frequency caps set to prevent fatigue, tighter for retargeting?
- [ ] Are exclusions in place to avoid wasting spend on the wrong audiences?
- [ ] Is creative refreshed regularly to combat fatigue?
- [ ] Is the strategy adapting to privacy changes and signal loss?
- [ ] Is audience performance measured on quality and value, not just volume?
- [ ] Is targeting coordinated across platforms to manage frequency and coherence?
