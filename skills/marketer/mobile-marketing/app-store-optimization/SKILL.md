# App Store Optimization

App Store Optimization (ASO) is the practice of making an app discoverable and chosen within the app stores. It fails when it is reduced to keyword stuffing, when the listing is treated as static rather than continuously tested, when ratings are ignored until they damage ranking, or when creative is designed for aesthetics rather than conversion. The store listing is the landing page for every install, and the algorithm that ranks it weighs far more than keywords. An app can be perfectly keyword-optimized and still fail because its screenshots do not communicate value, its rating signals poor quality, or its conversion rate tells the algorithm that users do not want it.

Use this skill before answering questions such as "how do we improve our app store ranking", "what keywords should we target", "how do we get more installs organically", "how do we improve our rating", or "how do we get featured". The goal is to prevent the agent from treating ASO as a one-time keyword task and ignoring the conversion, rating, and algorithmic signals that actually drive visibility.

## Core Rules

### Understand What The Algorithm Actually Rewards

Store ranking algorithms weigh a combination of relevance and quality signals, not keyword density alone. Optimizing only for keywords ignores the signals that sustain ranking.

Account for:

- keyword relevance in title, subtitle, and metadata;
- install velocity and conversion rate from view to install;
- retention and uninstall signals, which indicate quality;
- ratings and review volume and recency;
- engagement and crash or performance metrics.

An app that ranks for a keyword through stuffing but converts and retains poorly will lose that rank as the algorithm detects weak quality signals. ASO must optimize the full signal set, not just text.

### Research Keywords From Real Search Behavior

Keyword selection should be grounded in how users actually search, not in internal vocabulary. The terms a team uses often differ from what users type.

Research keywords by:

- using store keyword tools and competitor analysis to find real search terms;
- prioritizing relevance and intent over raw search volume;
- balancing high-volume competitive terms with specific long-tail terms;
- localizing keywords for each market rather than translating literally;
- revisiting keywords periodically as search behavior and competition shift.

A high-volume keyword that brings the wrong audience produces installs that do not retain, which then damages ranking. Relevance to the actual app experience matters more than volume.

### Optimize Title And Subtitle For Both Algorithm And Human

The title and subtitle are the most heavily weighted text fields and the first thing a human reads. They must serve both.

Optimize by:

- placing the brand name and the most important keyword in the title;
- using the subtitle to add descriptive, keyword-rich context;
- keeping titles readable and not stuffed into incoherence;
- differentiating from competitor titles that look identical in search results.

A title engineered only for the algorithm reads as spam to humans and suppresses conversion. A title engineered only for branding loses keyword relevance. Both must be balanced.

### Design Creative To Communicate Value Before Install

Screenshots, video, and icon determine whether a user who sees the listing installs. They are conversion assets, not decoration.

Design creative by:

- leading the first screenshot with the core value proposition, not a logo;
- showing the app in real use with captions that sell the benefit;
- using video to demonstrate the primary workflow or outcome;
- designing an icon that is recognizable, legible at small sizes, and distinct from competitors;
- localizing creative and messaging for different markets.

Creative that looks beautiful but does not communicate why a user should install suppresses conversion, which in turn suppresses ranking. The first screenshot must answer "what will this do for me".

### Manage Ratings And Reviews As A Ranking And Trust Signal

Ratings and review volume directly influence both ranking and install conversion. They are not a passive outcome; they are a managed asset.

Manage ratings by:

- prompting for ratings at moments of demonstrated satisfaction, not arbitrarily;
- routing dissatisfaction to support before it becomes a public negative review;
- responding to reviews, especially critical ones, professionally and promptly;
- tracking rating trends by version to catch quality regressions;
- earning reviews through genuine satisfaction, not manipulation.

A sudden rating drop after a release is a quality signal that will suppress ranking. Review management must be continuous, not reactive.

### A/B Test The Listing Continuously

Store listings are not set-and-forget. What the team assumes converts is often wrong, and only testing reveals the truth.

Test by:

- using store-provided experiment tools to test icons, screenshots, and text;
- testing one variable at a time to attribute effects clearly;
- measuring view-to-install conversion, not just impressions;
- rolling out winners and continuing to test new hypotheses;
- treating the listing as a living conversion funnel.

### Pursue Featured Placement Through Relationship And Quality

Featuring by the store can drive significant visibility, but it is earned through product quality and relationship, not requested through optimization tricks.

Position for featuring by:

- building a high-quality, well-reviewed app that aligns with store priorities;
- adopting new platform features and guidelines early;
- maintaining a relationship with store editorial and partnership teams;
- timing updates and launches to align with featuring opportunities;
- ensuring the app meets technical and design standards the store rewards.

## Common Traps

### Keyword Stuffing Over Conversion

Cramming keywords into titles and descriptions pleases no human and suppresses the conversion the algorithm rewards.

### Treating The Listing As Static

Failing to test and update creative and keywords leaves conversion and ranking gains unrealized.

### Ignoring Ratings Until They Damage Ranking

Reactive review management allows quality regressions to suppress visibility before they are addressed.

### Creative Designed For Aesthetics Over Value

Beautiful screenshots that do not communicate the core benefit suppress install conversion.

### Chasing High-Volume Irrelevant Keywords

Volume keywords that bring the wrong audience produce installs that do not retain, harming ranking.

### Assuming Cross-Store Equivalence

App Store and Play Store have different algorithms, fields, and behaviors; identical listings underperform.

### Requesting Ratings At Arbitrary Moments

Prompting for ratings without a satisfaction moment produces low ratings and review fatigue.

## Self-Check

- [ ] ASO strategy accounts for relevance, conversion, retention, and rating signals, not keywords alone.
- [ ] Keywords are researched from real search behavior and prioritized by relevance and intent.
- [ ] Title and subtitle balance keyword coverage with human readability and differentiation.
- [ ] First screenshot leads with core value, and creative communicates benefit before install.
- [ ] Ratings are managed continuously, with prompts timed to satisfaction and critical reviews responded to.
- [ ] The listing is A/B tested continuously on creative and text, measuring view-to-install conversion.
- [ ] Rating trends are tracked by version to catch quality regressions early.
- [ ] Keywords and creative are localized per market rather than translated literally.
- [ ] Featuring is pursued through product quality and platform alignment, not optimization tricks.
- [ ] App Store and Play Store strategies are tailored to each platform's algorithm and fields.
