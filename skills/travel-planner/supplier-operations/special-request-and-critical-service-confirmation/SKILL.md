# Special Request And Critical Service Confirmation

Special requests become trip failures when they are treated as notes instead of operational commitments. A hotel may not guarantee connecting rooms, a transfer company may not have the child seat, a restaurant may not understand a severe allergy, or a guide may not know a traveler uses a wheelchair. Agents often add requests to bookings and assume suppliers will handle them. Strong travel servicing identifies which requests are critical, confirms them with the supplier that will actually deliver the service, and gives the traveler a backup plan.

Use this skill when managing accessibility, dietary, medical, family, VIP, celebration, room, guide, transfer, or other special travel requests.

## Core Rules

### Separate Preferences From Critical Needs

Not all requests carry the same risk. High floor, nice view, or early check-in may be preferences. Roll-in shower, adjacent caregiver room, severe allergy, oxygen storage, child restraint, service animal acceptance, medication refrigeration, or late arrival access may be critical.

Classify requests by consequence if unmet. Critical needs require stronger confirmation, written evidence, supplier names, and backup options. Preferences can be requested but should not be promised unless guaranteed.

### Confirm With The Delivering Supplier

A booking platform, wholesaler, DMC, or reservation center may pass notes, but the property, guide, transfer operator, restaurant, airline, or activity provider delivers the service. Confirm critical requests with the actual delivering supplier whenever possible.

Ask for specific confirmation, not generic reassurance. "ADA room confirmed" is weaker than "roll-in shower, two queen beds, elevator access, and shower chair confirmed." Record the name, date, method, and exact details.

### Know What Is Guaranteed Versus Requested

Travel suppliers often distinguish guaranteed services from requests subject to availability. Bedding, connecting rooms, early check-in, late checkout, room location, dietary meals, guide language, and vehicle type may not be guaranteed unless contracted or paid.

Explain this to the traveler. If a request is essential, choose suppliers or rates that can guarantee it, or create backup plans. Do not let critical trip success depend on unconfirmed availability.

### Confirm Timing And Operational Handoffs

Special requests can fail at handoffs: hotel to DMC, transfer dispatcher to driver, restaurant reservationist to kitchen, tour office to guide, airline reservation to airport staff. Reconfirm close to service date and ensure the information reaches the person delivering the service.

For transfers, provide flight details, luggage, child seats, mobility equipment, and contact. For tours, provide pace, accessibility, dietary, and language needs. For hotels, confirm arrival time, bedding, accessibility, and room notes.

### Protect Privacy And Dignity

Special requests may include sensitive health, disability, religion, family, identity, or safety information. Share only the functional details the supplier needs and only with appropriate consent. Avoid unnecessary diagnoses or personal history.

Use respectful language. The goal is to enable service, not expose the traveler. Keep sensitive notes out of broadly shared documents when not needed.

### Build Backup Options For Critical Needs

If a critical request fails, the traveler needs options. Backup may include alternate hotel, different room type, rental equipment provider, nearby pharmacy, different restaurant, private transfer, alternate guide, or emergency contact. For remote destinations, backups must be planned earlier because local options may be limited.

Tell the traveler what to check on arrival and what to do if the request is not met. Keep confirmations accessible offline.

### Reconfirm After Any Change

Schedule changes, supplier substitutions, room changes, rebookings, or date changes can wipe out special requests. Reconfirm critical details after every change. Do not assume notes transfer automatically between systems.

This is especially important for flights, hotel rooms, cruises, group rooming lists, tours, and transfers. A corrected itinerary is incomplete if critical requests are lost.

### Manage Expectations Around Goodwill Requests

Celebrations, upgrades, amenities, view requests, early check-in, late checkout, and room preferences may be fulfilled as goodwill but are often not guaranteed. Present them honestly. If the traveler wants certainty, identify paid options or guaranteed categories.

Overpromising goodwill requests damages trust. A request submitted is not a promise delivered.

## Common Traps

### Calling Every Request Confirmed

Submitted is not confirmed. Confirmed is not always guaranteed. Use precise language.

### Relying On OTA Notes

Third-party notes may not reach the hotel, driver, guide, or restaurant. Confirm critical needs directly.

### Using Generic Accessibility Labels

Specific features matter. Ask for the exact function needed.

### Forgetting To Reconfirm After Changes

Rebookings can erase requests. Reconfirm every critical need after changes.

### Oversharing Sensitive Information

Share functional requirements, not unnecessary medical or personal details.

### Treating Dietary Needs As Preferences

Severe allergies, religious diets, and medical diets require clear confirmation and backup food plans.

### Assuming VIP Or Celebration Amenities Are Guaranteed

Unless paid or contracted, amenities and upgrades may be goodwill only.

### Providing No Arrival Check

Travelers should know what to verify immediately while fixes are still possible.

## Self-Check

- [ ] Are requests classified as critical needs, important preferences, or goodwill requests?
- [ ] Are critical requests confirmed with the actual delivering supplier?
- [ ] Are exact details documented, not generic labels?
- [ ] Is it clear what is guaranteed, paid, requested, or subject to availability?
- [ ] Are handoffs to drivers, guides, kitchens, hotel staff, airline staff, and DMCs confirmed where relevant?
- [ ] Is sensitive traveler information shared only with consent and only as needed?
- [ ] Are backup options planned for critical request failure?
- [ ] Are critical requests reconfirmed after schedule, supplier, room, or date changes?
- [ ] Are traveler-facing instructions clear about what to check on arrival?
- [ ] Does the plan avoid promising goodwill amenities as guaranteed services?
