# Compliance And Audit

Immigration compliance is a continuous operational duty, not a filing event. Employers must verify work authorization at hire, maintain records, reverify when status expires, and withstand audits and site visits with little notice. Agents often treat verification as a formality and miss the record-keeping, reverification, anti-discrimination, and audit-readiness machinery that determines whether the employer can defend its practices under scrutiny. Failures carry fines, debarment, and in some regimes criminal liability.

Use this skill before onboarding workers, designing verification processes, preparing for an audit or site visit, responding to a notice of inspection, or assessing compliance gaps. The goal is to force the agent to connect verification, records, reverification, anti-discrimination limits, audit readiness, and internal controls, and to escalate to immigration counsel for audits and high-stakes matters. This skill is decision support, not legal advice.

## Core Rules

### Establish Right-To-Work Verification At The Right Time

Verification timing and method are prescribed and strict.

Address:

- the required verification system for the jurisdiction (e.g., I-9 in the U.S., right-to-work checks in the U.K., equivalent abroad);
- the deadline for completing verification after hire;
- physical examination of documents versus authorized alternatives;
- acceptable document categories and their handling;
- electronic verification programs (e.g., E-Verify) and their voluntary or mandatory use;
- remote-hire verification procedures;
- anti-discrimination limits on requesting specific documents.

Requesting more or different documents than the law allows, or backdating verification, are common violations. Follow the prescribed process exactly.

### Maintain Records With Required Retention And Accessibility

Record-keeping is independently regulated and audit-tested.

Address:

- what records must be kept and in what form;
- retention periods (often tied to hire or termination dates);
- storage and retrieval for audit;
- segregation of verification records from personnel files;
- confidentiality and data protection of immigration documents;
- indexing by employee and date;
- destruction at the end of the retention period.

Disorganized or over-retained records create audit exposure. Maintain a controlled, retrievable system.

### Manage Reverification And Status Expiration

Work authorization can expire, and the employer must act.

Address:

- tracking of authorization expiration dates;
- timely reverification before expiration;
- handling of automatic extensions and temporary evidence;
- consequences of continued employment after expiration;
- termination or suspension if authorization is not renewed;
- documenting the reverification process.

Employing someone after authorization expires, even inadvertently, can be a violation. Track expirations and act before they lapse.

### Respect Anti-Discrimination Limits In Verification

Verification systems must not become tools for discrimination.

Address:

- documentary abuse (requesting specific or extra documents);
- citizenship or national-origin discrimination in hiring;
- over-verification of certain groups;
- treating authorized workers differently based on citizenship type;
- retaliation for complaints;
- balancing verification duties with anti-discrimination duties.

Aggressive verification aimed at certain nationalities, or demanding permanent residents' specific documents, can violate anti-discrimination provisions even while nominally complying with verification rules.

### Prepare For Audits And Site Visits

Immigration audits and site visits can arrive with little notice.

Establish:

- a designated audit-response team and point of contact;
- procedures for receiving and logging a notice of inspection;
- a process to gather and produce records within deadlines;
- protocols for site visits and inspector access;
- communication controls during an audit;
- counsel involvement from the outset;
- a remediation plan for identified deficiencies.

Panicked or disorganized responses, or producing more than required without review, worsen outcomes. Prepare the playbook before the notice arrives.

### Build Internal Controls And Monitoring

Compliance depends on systems, not individual vigilance.

Implement:

- onboarding workflows that block start without verification;
- automated expiration tracking and alerts;
- periodic self-audits of verification records;
- training for HR and hiring managers;
- vendor and contractor verification where applicable;
- escalation paths for uncertain cases;
- documentation of the compliance program itself.

A compliance program that depends on one person's memory fails when that person leaves. Build durable systems.

### Assess Violations, Penalties, And Mitigation

When deficiencies arise, quantify exposure and mitigation.

Address:

- the types of violations (paperwork, knowing employment of unauthorized workers);
- penalty ranges and aggravating or mitigating factors;
- good-faith compliance as a mitigating factor;
- self-disclosure or correction programs;
- debarment and contract-eligibility consequences;
- criminal liability for knowing violations or pattern of practice;
- immigration consequences for affected workers.

Paperwork violations and knowing-employment violations carry very different consequences. Assess the category before responding, and consider mitigation programs.

### Coordinate With Related Employment Obligations

Immigration compliance intersects with broader employment duties.

Address:

- coordination with payroll and tax verification;
- alignment with contractor classification controls;
- interaction with anti-discrimination and accommodation duties;
- data-protection compliance for immigration records;
- works-council or union notification where required;
- cross-functional escalation for audits affecting multiple areas.

An immigration audit can surface classification, wage, or data issues. Coordinate the response across functions.

## Common Traps

### Verification As A Formality

Verification timing, documents, and method are prescribed. Follow the process exactly.

### Disorganized Or Over-Retained Records

Poor record-keeping creates audit exposure. Maintain a controlled, retrievable system.

### Missing Expiration And Reverification

Employing after authorization expires is a violation. Track and act before lapse.

### Documentary Abuse And Over-Verification

Requesting extra or specific documents can violate anti-discrimination rules even while nominally complying.

### Unprepared Audit Response

Disorganized or over-inclusive responses worsen outcomes. Prepare the playbook before notice.

### Compliance Dependent On One Person

Memory-based compliance fails on turnover. Build durable systems.

### Confusing Paperwork And Knowing-Employment Violations

The categories carry very different penalties. Assess before responding.

## Self-Check

- Is right-to-work verification performed at the prescribed time, with correct documents, approved methods, remote-hire procedures, electronic programs, and anti-discrimination limits?
- Are verification records maintained with required retention, form, accessibility, segregation, confidentiality, indexing, and destruction?
- Are authorization expirations tracked with timely reverification, automatic-extension handling, consequences of lapse, and documentation?
- Are anti-discrimination limits (documentary abuse, citizenship and national-origin discrimination, over-verification, retaliation) respected alongside verification duties?
- Is an audit-response team, notice-handling process, record-production workflow, site-visit protocol, communication control, counsel involvement, and remediation plan established?
- Are internal controls (onboarding blocks, expiration alerts, self-audits, training, vendor verification, escalation, program documentation) durable and system-based?
- Are violations categorized (paperwork versus knowing employment), penalties quantified, mitigating factors assessed, and correction or self-disclosure programs considered?
- Are immigration controls coordinated with payroll, tax, classification, anti-discrimination, data-protection, consultation, and cross-functional audit response?
- Does the output escalate audits, site visits, knowing-employment allegations, and criminal exposure to qualified immigration counsel?
