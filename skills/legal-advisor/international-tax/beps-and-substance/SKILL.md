# BEPS And Substance

The Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS) framework and its domestic implementations have changed how tax authorities view structures that shift profits to low-tax jurisdictions without real activity. A holding company, IP licensing entity, financing vehicle, or distribution hub that produced favorable results a decade ago can now fail economic substance requirements, anti-hybrid rules, controlled foreign corporation (CFC) regimes, the principal purpose test, or transparency reporting duties. Agents often evaluate a structure only against the rate it achieves, ignoring whether the entity has substance, whether profits align with value creation, and whether the arrangement is reportable. The consequence is not just denial of a benefit; it can include penalties, public country-by-country exposure, and reputational harm.

Use this skill before recommending or reviewing a cross-border structure involving low-tax jurisdictions, IP migration, financing, centralization of functions, hybrid instruments, or treaty access. The goal is to force the agent to test the structure against substance, alignment of profits with functions and risks, anti-hybrid rules, CFC regimes, and transparency duties before claiming a result. BEPS implementation is jurisdiction-specific and evolving; any conclusion must be confirmed against the relevant country's enacted rules and current guidance.

## Core Rules

### Align Profits With Functions, Assets, And Risks

BEPS Action 2-10 and the OECD Guidelines insist that profits follow the people functions that create value. A structure where an entity earns high returns without performing the relevant functions, controlling the relevant risks, or holding the relevant assets is vulnerable.

Map for each entity:

- functions performed (manufacturing, distribution, R&D, financing, management);
- assets used and owned (tangible, intangible, financial);
- risks assumed and the capability to control them;
- people functions and key decision-makers;
- contribution to value creation (DEMPE for intangibles);
- contractual allocation vs. actual conduct.

Contractual risk allocation that is not matched by control and financial capacity to bear risk is disregarded. Realign profits to where DEMPE functions occur.

### Satisfy Economic Substance Requirements

Many jurisdictions now require real economic substance for entities claiming local tax benefits or operating in certain sectors.

Verify:

- directed and managed in the jurisdiction (board meetings, quorum, decisions);
- adequate number of qualified employees;
- adequate operating expenditure (OpEx);
- physical office and operations;
- core income-generating activities (CIGA) performed locally;
- specific substance rules for holding companies, IP, financing, shipping, and distribution;
- reporting and sanction for non-compliance.

Substance created only for an audit is weak. Build and document substance contemporaneously, aligned with the income earned.

### Apply Anti-Hybrid Rules

BEPS Action 2 and its implementations neutralize mismatches that produce double deduction or double non-taxation.

Check:

- hybrid instruments (treated as debt in one country, equity in another);
- hybrid entities (transparent in one, opaque in another);
- imported mismatches through chains;
- reverse hybrids and deductible payments to exempt recipients;
- branch mismatches.

Anti-hybrid rules can deny a deduction, treat a payment as a dividend, or disallow a credit. A structure relying on a mismatch is at high risk in jurisdictions that have enacted Action 2.

### Assess CFC And Anti-Deferral Regimes

BEPS Action 3 strengthened CFC rules to tax passive or artificial income of foreign subsidiaries in the parent's hands.

Identify:

- CFC definition and control threshold;
- income covered (passive, tainted, mobile);
- computation method (entity-level, transactional, or substance carve-out);
- exemptions for real activity and low-tax legitimate business;
- GILTI-type regimes and their interaction with foreign tax credits;
- PFIC rules for individuals and certain funds.

A low-tax subsidiary with passive income is likely caught. Model the CFC inclusion and credit interaction before claiming deferral.

### Address Harmful Tax Practices And Preferential Regimes

BEPS Action 5 and the Forum on Harmful Tax Practices scrutinize preferential regimes such as IP boxes, financing, holding, shipping, and fund regimes.

Check:

- nexus approach for IP regimes (income tied to qualifying R&D expenditure);
- substance requirements for the regime;
- transparency and spontaneous exchange of rulings;
- ring-fencing prohibitions;
- whether the regime survives current FHTP review.

An IP box without sufficient nexus, or a ruling that is not exchangeable, is at risk of withdrawal or challenge.

### Apply The Principal Purpose Test And Anti-Abuse

The MLI's PPT and domestic GAARs deny treaty and other benefits where one of the principal purposes is to obtain a tax advantage lacking genuine substance.

Assess:

- whether the arrangement has a genuine commercial purpose;
- whether the tax benefit was a main purpose;
- whether substance matches the claimed treatment;
- holding structures through intermediate jurisdictions;
- timing relative to MLI entry into force;
- domestic GAAR interaction.

PPT is fact-based and broad. Document commercial purpose independent of tax before claiming the benefit.

### Satisfy Transparency And Reporting Duties

BEPS Action 5, 13, and 14 and related rules impose extensive reporting.

Confirm:

- country-by-country reporting (CbCR) thresholds and filing;
- master file and local file transfer pricing documentation;
- spontaneous exchange of cross-border rulings and APAs;
- mandatory disclosure of cross-border arrangements (DAC6-type);
- beneficial ownership registers;
- CRS and FATCA reporting;
- treaty relief reporting and PPT disclosures.

Missing CbCR or mandatory disclosure creates penalties and can trigger audit. Build reporting into the structure's calendar.

### Monitor Dispute Resolution And MAP Effectiveness

BEPS Action 14 pressures countries to resolve treaty disputes through MAP and arbitration. Track:

- MAP availability and arbitration clauses;
- average resolution times;
- domestic time limits that may force litigation;
- suspension of collection during MAP;
- consistency of MAP outcomes across the group.

A structure that creates frequent MAP needs is operationally costly and politically visible.

## Common Traps

### Profits Without People Functions

An entity earning returns without DEMPE functions, risk control, or assets is the core BEPS target. Realign before claiming the result.

### Substance Built Only For The Audit

Substance created after a challenge is disregarded. Build and document contemporaneously.

### Ignoring Anti-Hybrid Mismatches

A structure relying on a debt/equity or transparent/opaque mismatch is vulnerable wherever Action 2 is enacted.

### Assuming CFC Deferral Survives

Low-tax subsidiaries with passive income are commonly caught by CFC or GILTI. Model the inclusion before claiming deferral.

### IP Box Without Nexus

An IP box requires qualifying R&D expenditure. Pure acquisition or licensing income often fails the nexus test.

### Missing CbCR Or Mandatory Disclosure

These are high-frequency, high-penalty filings. Calendar them with the structure.

### Treating PPT As A Formality

PPT is broad and fact-based. A structure whose main purpose is tax access is at risk regardless of formal LOB passes.

### Stale BEPS Implementation

BEPS rules are enacted unevenly and amended often. Confirm the current version for each relevant country.

## Self-Check

- Are profits aligned with functions, assets, risks, and DEMPE people functions for each entity?
- Is economic substance (management, employees, OpEx, CIGA, office) satisfied and contemporaneously documented?
- Are hybrid instruments, hybrid entities, imported mismatches, and branch mismatches tested against anti-hybrid rules?
- Are CFC, GILTI, and PFIC regimes modeled for passive or tainted income of foreign subsidiaries?
- Are preferential regimes (IP box, financing, holding) tested for nexus, substance, and FHTP review?
- Is the principal purpose test applied, with commercial purpose documented independent of tax?
- Are CbCR, master/local file, ruling exchange, mandatory disclosure, beneficial ownership, CRS, and FATCA reporting calendared?
- Is MAP availability and dispute resolution effectiveness considered for the structure?
- Are structures relying on mismatches, conduit flows, or low-tax entities flagged for denial risk?
- Does the output reference the relevant BEPS Action, MLI status, and enacted domestic rules, and recommend confirmation by a qualified international tax advisor?
