# Fair Use And Licensing

Using someone else's copyrighted work legally requires either a license or a defense such as fair use. Agents often treat fair use as a magic word that excuses any reuse, when fair use is a fact-intensive, unpredictable defense that turns on four factors and is decided case by case. Conversely, agents draft or accept licenses without reading their scope, mistaking a Creative Commons attribution license for public domain, or accepting a sync license that covers only one use. The result is either overcautious avoidance of legitimate fair uses or reckless reliance on a defense that fails, exposing the user to statutory damages. Licensing, too, is often done sloppily: broad grants without territory or term, missing warranties, and no mechanism for termination.

Use this skill before relying on fair use, before licensing copyrighted content, before applying a Creative Commons license, before using music in video, or when reviewing a content license for scope and risk. The goal is to force the agent to analyze fair use rigorously across the four factors, to draft and read licenses for what they actually grant, and to manage DRM and anti-circumvention obligations. Fair use and licensing are jurisdiction-specific; significant uses and licenses require qualified copyright counsel.

## Core Rules

### Analyze Fair Use Across All Four Factors

Fair use is an affirmative defense, not a right. It is decided by courts based on four factors, weighed together.

Analyze:

- the purpose and character of the use, including whether it is commercial or nonprofit, educational, or transformative, with transformative uses that add new expression or meaning favored;
- the nature of the copyrighted work, with factual works less protected than creative ones, and published works less protected than unpublished;
- the amount and substantiality of the portion used, considering both quantity and qualitative importance, including the heart of the work;
- the effect on the potential market for or value of the original, including whether the use substitutes for the original or its derivatives.

No factor is decisive, and outcomes are unpredictable. A commercial use can be fair if highly transformative, and a nonprofit use can be infringing if it substitutes for the original. Document the analysis, but recognize that fair use is a litigation defense, not a clearance.

### Distinguish Transformative From Merely Derivative

Transformative use is increasingly central to fair use analysis, but transformation requires more than a new context.

Assess transformation by:

- whether the use adds new expression, meaning, or message;
- whether the use serves a different purpose or audience;
- whether the use is merely repackaging or republishing;
- whether the use comments on, criticizes, or parodies the original;
- the line between transformative fair use and infringing derivative work.

A parody that mocks the original is more likely fair use than a satire that uses the original as a vehicle for unrelated commentary. The distinction matters and is often misjudged.

### Read And Draft Licenses For Actual Scope

A license grants only what it says. Read every license for what it actually permits.

Define or verify:

- the licensed works, including versions and derivatives;
- the rights granted, such as reproduction, distribution, public performance, display, and digital transmission;
- exclusivity versus non-exclusivity;
- the permitted uses, media, and formats;
- the territory;
- the term and renewal or termination;
- sublicense and assignment rights;
- reservation of ungranted rights;
- consideration and royalty terms;
- warranties, indemnities, and limitations of liability;
- audit and reporting rights;
- termination and cure provisions.

A license to reproduce and distribute does not include the right to perform publicly or to make derivatives. A non-exclusive license is not assignable without consent. Read the grant clause first and most carefully.

### Apply Creative Commons And Open Licenses Correctly

Creative Commons licenses are standardized copyright licenses with specific obligations. They are not public domain and are not all alike.

Distinguish:

- CC BY, requiring attribution;
- CC BY-SA, requiring attribution and share-alike of derivatives under the same license;
- CC BY-ND, requiring attribution and no derivatives;
- CC BY-NC, requiring attribution and non-commercial use;
- CC BY-NC-SA and CC BY-NC-ND combinations;
- CC0, a public domain dedication;
- public domain mark for works already in the public domain.

The non-commercial restriction is narrowly interpreted and risky for commercial projects. Share-alike obligations can force derivative works to adopt the same license, creating copyleft-like effects for content. Verify compatibility before mixing licensed materials.

### Handle Music Licensing: Sync, Mechanical, And Performance

Music licensing is fragmented across composition and sound recording rights, each requiring separate licenses.

Clear:

- synchronization licenses from the composition publisher for use in video;
- master use licenses from the sound recording owner for the specific recording;
- mechanical licenses for reproduction and distribution of compositions, with compulsory licensing available in some jurisdictions;
- public performance licenses from performing rights organizations for compositions;
- public performance licenses for sound recordings, including digital transmissions;
- print licenses for sheet music.

Using a song in a video requires both a sync license for the composition and a master license for the recording. Missing either creates infringement. Music clearance is specialized and should use a clearance professional.

### Manage Digital Rights And Anti-Circumvention Rules

Digital works are protected by both copyright and anti-circumvention rules that prohibit bypassing technological protection measures.

Address:

- the prohibition on circumventing access controls, even on works you may otherwise use;
- the prohibition on trafficking in circumvention tools;
- the exceptions and triennial rulemaking exemptions;
- DRM and watermarking obligations in licenses;
- the effect of anti-circumvention on interoperability, security research, and repair;
- the interaction with contract terms that restrict use further.

Anti-circumvention rules can make lawful uses impossible if they require bypassing a technical lock. Factor them into digital content deals.

### Coordinate With Trademark, Publicity, And Contract Rights

Using content can implicate rights beyond copyright. Clear all relevant rights.

Coordinate:

- trademark rights in logos and brands appearing in content;
- rights of publicity for identifiable people;
- contract terms on platforms and services that may restrict reuse;
- location and property release requirements;
- guild and union requirements for talent;
- export and sanctions screening for international distribution.

A video cleared for copyright may still infringe trademark, publicity, or contract rights. Clear comprehensively.

### Document The Analysis And Reliance

Fair use and licensing decisions should be documented to support later defenses and audits.

Document:

- the fair use analysis across the four factors;
- the licenses obtained and their scope;
- the chain of title for owned and licensed content;
- the rationale for relying on fair use versus seeking a license;
- the date and version of the analysis.

## Common Traps

### Treating Fair Use As A Magic Word

Fair use is an unpredictable defense, not a clearance. Analyze the four factors and recognize the risk.

### Confusing Transformation With Derivative Works

A use that merely repackages is not transformative. Add new expression, meaning, or message.

### Misreading License Scope

A license grants only what it says. Read the grant clause and verify rights, exclusivity, territory, term, and sublicense rights.

### Confusing Creative Commons With Public Domain

CC licenses have obligations. Share-alike and non-commercial restrictions create real constraints.

### Forgetting Separate Composition And Recording Licenses

Music requires sync and master licenses. Missing either infringes.

### Ignoring Anti-Circumvention Rules

Bypassing DRM is prohibited even for lawful uses. Factor anti-circumvention into digital deals.

### Overlooking Trademark, Publicity, And Contract Rights

Copyright clearance does not clear other rights. Clear comprehensively.

### Undocumented Fair Use Reliance

Without a documented analysis, defending fair use is harder. Record the four-factor analysis.

## Self-Check

- Is fair use analyzed across all four factors, purpose and character including transformative use, nature of the work, amount and substantiality, and market effect?
- Is transformative use distinguished from merely derivative use, with new expression, meaning, message, purpose, and audience assessed?
- Are licenses read and drafted for actual scope, including works, rights granted, exclusivity, uses, media, territory, term, sublicense and assignment, reservation of rights, consideration, warranties, indemnities, audit, and termination?
- Are Creative Commons and open licenses distinguished by attribution, share-alike, no-derivatives, non-commercial, CC0, and public domain, with compatibility checked?
- Are music licenses cleared for sync, master, mechanical, public performance for composition and sound recording, and print, with compulsory and PRO coverage considered?
- Are anti-circumvention and DRM rules addressed for access controls, trafficking, exceptions, watermarking, interoperability, and contract interaction?
- Are trademark, publicity, contract, location, guild, and sanctions rights coordinated with copyright clearance?
- Is the fair use and licensing analysis documented with four-factor analysis, license scope, chain of title, rationale, date, and version?
- Are platform terms, terms of service, and user-generated content rules checked for reuse restrictions?
- Does the output avoid asserting that a use is fair or licensed and instead recommend qualified copyright counsel for the specific work, use, and jurisdiction?
