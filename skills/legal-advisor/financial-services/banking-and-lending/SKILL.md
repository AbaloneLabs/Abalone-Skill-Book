# Banking And Lending

Banking and lending law combines prudential regulation of institutions with the commercial law of loans, security, and priorities. Agents often treat a loan as a commercial contract and miss the regulatory layer—bank licensing, capital, consumer protection, and reporting—or treat security as automatic and miss the perfection, priority, and enforcement rules that determine whether the lender actually gets paid. The interaction between insolvency, set-off, and security is where lenders most often lose position, and consumer and usury rules can invalidate terms that look commercially reasonable.

Use this skill before advising on bank licensing, loan documentation, security and guarantees, syndicated or intercreditor structures, or consumer lending. The goal is to force the agent to address both the prudential regulation of the institution and the commercial perfection of the loan, and to identify the priority and enforcement risks. Banking and lending law is jurisdiction-specific and detail-critical; conclusions must be confirmed against the applicable statutes and regulations by qualified financial services counsel.

## Core Rules

### Separate Institutional Regulation From Loan Documentation

Banking law has two layers.

Address both:

- institutional regulation (licensing, capital, liquidity, governance, reporting, resolution);
- loan documentation and commercial law (the loan agreement, security, guarantees, priorities, enforcement).

A loan that is commercially sound can still breach institutional regulation (concentration limits, related-party limits, consumer protection). Address both layers.

### Structure Bank Licensing And Capital Requirements

Licensing gates banking activity.

Address:

- the definition of banking and deposit-taking (and who must be licensed);
- the licensing requirements (fitness, capital, governance, risk management, business plan);
- capital and liquidity requirements (basel frameworks and local rules);
- large exposure and concentration limits;
- related-party lending restrictions;
- resolution and recovery planning;
- cross-border branching and subsidiary structures.

Taking deposits or conducting banking business without a license is a serious offense. Confirm the licensing model and the capital requirements.

### Draft The Loan Agreement With Covenants And Events Of Default

The loan agreement allocates risk.

Include:

- the facility, purpose, and drawdown mechanics;
- interest, fees, and the interest rate basis (fixed, floating, benchmark transition);
- representations and warranties (updating and bring-down);
- covenants (financial, affirmative, negative, and information);
- events of default (payment, covenant, insolvency, cross-default, material adverse change);
- remedies (acceleration, set-off, enforcement of security);
- the material adverse change clause and its limits.

Covenants and events of default are the lender's early warning and control. Draft them to capture the real risks and to be objective and measurable where possible.

### Perfect Security Interests And Determine Priority

Security is only as good as its perfection and priority.

Address:

- the types of security (mortgage, pledge, charge, assignment);
- the creation, perfection, and registration requirements;
- the priority rules (registration, the effect of set-off and set-off priority);
- the priority among creditors (secured, unsecured, preferential, subordinated);
- the effect of insolvency on security (stay, avoidance, cramdown);
- cross-border security and the governing law of the asset.

Unperfected security is void against other creditors and in insolvency. Perfect security in the correct registry and confirm priority.

### Structure Guarantees And Suretyship

Guarantees provide recourse to third parties.

Address:

- the distinction between guarantee and indemnity (secondary vs. primary obligation);
- the requirements for a valid guarantee (consideration, writing in some jurisdictions);
- the defenses of the guarantor (suretyship defenses, the effect of variations and releases);
- the enforcement against the guarantor and the notice requirements;
- the effect of the guarantor's insolvency.

Variations to the underlying contract without the guarantor's consent can discharge the guarantee. Preserve the guarantee through consent and parallel debt structures where needed.

### Respect Interest Rate, Usury, And Consumer Protection Limits

Interest and consumer terms are regulated.

Address:

- the interest rate limits and usury rules (and the exceptions for licensed lenders);
- the benchmark transition (LIBOR and its replacements) and the fallback language;
- consumer credit regulation (disclosure, cooling-off, responsible lending, unfair terms);
- predatory and abusive lending prohibitions;
- the effect of usury on the loan (forfeiture of interest, or principal).

Usury and consumer protection rules can invalidate interest and terms. Confirm the rate basis, the disclosures, and the responsible lending assessment.

### Manage Syndicated Lending And Intercreditor Arrangements

Syndicated loans involve multiple lenders with different roles and priorities.

Address:

- the roles of agent, security trustee, and lenders;
- the voting and decision-making mechanics (majority, required lenders, unanimous);
- the sharing and pro rata payment rules;
- the intercreditor agreement and the priority between senior and subordinated debt;
- the standstill and subordination provisions for junior debt;
- the effect of a lender's insolvency or assignment.

Intercreditor priorities determine who gets paid in distress. Draft the intercreditor agreement carefully and confirm the subordination mechanics.

### Handle Set-Off, Combination, And Insolvency Interaction

Set-off is a powerful but fragile remedy.

Address:

- the contractual set-off clause and its scope;
- the conditions for set-off (mutual debts, same parties, due and payable);
- the set-off against contingent and future debts;
- the effect of insolvency on set-off (the insolvency set-off rule and its limits);
- the combination of accounts and the notice requirements;
- the risk of set-off being challenged as a preference or avoidance.

Set-off can be defeated by insolvency avoidance rules. Structure it carefully and confirm the insolvency set-off treatment.

## Common Traps

### Treating A Loan As A Pure Commercial Contract

Institutional regulation (concentration, related-party, consumer) applies alongside the loan. Address both layers.

### Unperfected Security

Security that is not perfected in the correct registry is void against creditors and in insolvency. Perfect it.

### Discharging A Guarantee By Variation

Variations without guarantor consent can discharge the guarantee. Preserve it with consent.

### Breaching Usury Or Consumer Protection

Interest and terms can be invalidated. Confirm the rate basis and disclosures.

### Ignoring Intercreditor Priority

Priority between senior and junior debt determines payment in distress. Draft the intercreditor agreement.

### Assuming Set-Off Survives Insolvency

Insolvency avoidance rules can defeat set-off. Confirm the treatment.

### Overlooking Benchmark Transition

LIBOR replacement and fallback language affect interest. Update the loan documentation.

### Confusing Guarantee With Indemnity

They are primary and secondary obligations with different enforcement. Distinguish them.

## Self-Check

- Are institutional regulation (licensing, capital, liquidity, concentration, related-party, resolution) and loan documentation both addressed?
- Is bank licensing (definition of banking, requirements, capital, cross-border) structured correctly?
- Does the loan agreement include facility, interest, representations, covenants, events of default, remedies, and material adverse change?
- Are security interests created, perfected, registered, and prioritized with insolvency and cross-border treatment?
- Are guarantees (vs. indemnity, defenses, enforcement, insolvency) structured with variation consent?
- Are interest rate, usury, benchmark transition, and consumer credit regulation respected?
- Are syndicated lending (agent, voting, sharing) and intercreditor (priority, subordination, standstill) arrangements managed?
- Is set-off (scope, conditions, insolvency, combination, avoidance) addressed?
- Does the output recommend confirmation against the applicable statutes and regulations by qualified financial services counsel?
