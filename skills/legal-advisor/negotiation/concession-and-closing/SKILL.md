# Concession And Closing

The endgame of a negotiation is where deals are won or lost, and it is where most value leaks. The judgment problem is that concessions and closing are treated as the wind-down, when in fact they are the highest-stakes phase: poorly designed concessions give away value for nothing, an unstructured final offer invites a counter that erases your gains, and a rushed closing locks in terms that were never finalized or that fail to satisfy conditions. Agents often help close deals by summarizing agreement, when the real work is designing concessions as conditional trades, packaging issues to preserve leverage, presenting final offers that credibly commit, and managing the signing and condition satisfaction so the deal actually closes on the agreed terms.

Use this skill before designing concessions, presenting a final offer, closing a negotiation, or managing the signing and condition satisfaction phase of any deal. The goal is to make the agent structure concessions to retain value, package issues strategically, present credible final offers, close cleanly, and handle the conditions and execution that turn agreement into a binding deal. This is strategic support; final deal terms, signing authority, and binding commitments require authorized counsel or a mandated negotiator.

## Core Rules

### Design Concessions As Conditional Trades

Every concession should buy something. Give concessions conditionally, linking each to a reciprocal move, so that value flows both ways. A concession given unconditionally signals weakness and trains the other side to extract more. Identify your tradables, things cheap for you but valuable to them, and use them to obtain your must-haves. Condition language like "if you agree to X, we can move on Y" preserves leverage and creates a sense of exchange. Plan the conditional structure before the endgame so concessions feel deliberate rather than desperate.

### Package Issues To Preserve Leverage

Negotiate issues as packages rather than one at a time, because packaging lets you trade across issues of different value to each side. If you resolve issues sequentially, each side wins some and loses some with no net gain; if you package, you can find trades where both sides gain. Keep several issues open until late in the negotiation so you have tradables to exchange, and avoid closing out issues prematurely, which surrenders leverage. A final package that bundles concessions and gains is harder to chip away than a series of isolated agreements.

### Use Magnitude And Pacing To Signal Limits

The size and pace of your concessions communicate your reservation point. Large, frequent concessions signal that you have far to go and invite more demands; small, diminishing concessions signal that you are approaching your limit. Taper concessions as you near your floor, and slow the pace, so the other side reads that the end is near. An abrupt stop after a string of concessions can also signal a final position. Manage the signal your concessions send, because the other side is reading them as carefully as the substance.

### Present Final Offers To Credibly Commit

A final offer only works if the other side believes it is final. Present it with credibility: state clearly that it is your best and final, tie it to a rationale such as authority limits or objective criteria, and avoid hedging that invites another round. Be prepared to follow through, because a final offer that is bluffing collapses your credibility if called. Use final offers sparingly, since overuse devalues them, and combine them with a package that gives the other side something to claim as a win, making acceptance easier.

### Close Cleanly With A Documented Agreement

Closing is the transition from negotiation to binding commitment, and it must be clean. Summarize the agreed terms in writing promptly, before memories diverge or side understandings emerge, and confirm that the summary captures every issue including the packaged trades. Distinguish agreed terms from open items, and either resolve open items or defer them explicitly with a process. Avoid letting the closing drift into renegotiation of settled points, which erodes the deal. A clean close locks in the value negotiated.

### Manage Signing, Authority, And Execution

Signing is not the same as closing. Confirm who has authority to sign on each side, whether internal approvals, board consents, or regulator clearances are required, and whether the signing is conditional on those approvals. Coordinate the form of the final agreement, the counterparts, the effective date, and any conditions precedent. A deal that is signed by someone without authority, or that skips a required approval, can be unenforceable or voidable. Verify authority and approvals before signature.

### Handle Conditions Precedent And Satisfaction

Many deals close subject to conditions, such as due diligence, financing, regulatory approval, or third-party consents. Identify every condition, the party responsible for satisfying it, the timeline, and the consequences of failure. Build in reasonable efforts obligations so a party cannot quietly let a condition fail, and plan for what happens if a condition is not met, including extensions, termination rights, and break fees. Unmanaged conditions are a common reason deals collapse after agreement in principle.

### Plan For The Walk-Away And Preserve The Relationship

Know when to walk away and do it cleanly if the terms fall outside your reservation point. A credible walk-away is itself a negotiating tool, and a party known to walk gains respect. At the same time, preserve the relationship where the deal is ongoing or future deals matter, by being firm on substance but respectful in manner, and by leaving the door open for future engagement. Walking away rudely or stringing the other along both damage future value.

### Watch For Closing Manipulation And Last-Minute Demands

The endgame invites last-minute demands, when one side tries to extract more by threatening to walk at the moment of greatest sunk cost. Anticipate this tactic, decide in advance how you will respond, and avoid being pressured by the desire to close. Conversely, do not deploy last-minute demands yourself unless you are willing to lose the deal, because they can trigger collapse. Keep the closing disciplined and the agreed terms stable.

## Common Traps

### Unconditional Concessions

Giving without getting signals weakness. Make every concession conditional.

### Resolving Issues Sequentially Instead Of Packaging

One-at-a-time resolution surrenders leverage. Package issues to find value-creating trades.

### Large Or Frequent Concessions Signaling Weakness

Big, rapid concessions invite more demands. Taper and slow them near your limit.

### A Final Offer That Is Obviously Bluffing

A bluff called destroys credibility. Make final offers credibly and follow through.

### Drift Into Renegotiation At Closing

Reopening settled points erodes the deal. Lock in agreed terms in writing promptly.

### Signing Without Authority Or Approvals

A signature without authority or required approvals can be voidable. Verify before signing.

### Unmanaged Conditions Precedent

Conditions no one is responsible for cause deals to collapse. Assign responsibility and plan for failure.

### Succumbing To Last-Minute Demands

Pressure to close can force bad terms. Decide your response to endgame demands in advance.

## Self-Check

- Are concessions designed as conditional trades, with each linked to a reciprocal move?
- Are issues packaged rather than resolved sequentially, keeping tradables open until late?
- Does the magnitude and pacing of concessions taper to signal approach to your reservation point?
- Is the final offer presented credibly, with rationale and willingness to follow through?
- Is the closing clean, with agreed terms summarized promptly and open items resolved or deferred?
- Are signing authority, internal approvals, board consents, and regulator clearances verified before signature?
- Are conditions precedent identified, with responsibility, timeline, efforts obligations, and failure consequences planned?
- Is the walk-away point known, and is the relationship preserved where future value matters?
- Are closing manipulation and last-minute demands anticipated, with responses decided in advance?
- Does the output flag that final deal terms, signing authority, and binding commitments require authorized counsel?
