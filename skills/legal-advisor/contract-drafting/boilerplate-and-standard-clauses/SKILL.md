# Boilerplate And Standard Clauses

Boilerplate clauses are called boilerplate because they look routine, but they are the clauses that most often decide disputes. The judgment problem is that parties skip over governing law, notice, assignment, waiver, entire agreement, and severability clauses as standard, when in fact each can change the outcome: a notice clause can invalidate a termination, an assignment clause can block a transaction, an entire agreement clause can defeat a fraud claim, and a waiver clause can strip rights through inaction. Agents often copy boilerplate from a template without checking whether the standard language fits the deal, the jurisdiction, or the parties' actual intent. "Standard" is not the same as "correct for this contract."

Use this skill before drafting, reviewing, or negotiating any boilerplate or standard clause in a contract. The goal is to make the agent read each boilerplate clause as operative law, tailor it to the deal and jurisdiction, and check it for interaction with the rest of the agreement. This is drafting support; final boilerplate packages, especially for cross-border, regulated, or high-value transactions, require review by qualified counsel.

## Core Rules

### Treat Boilerplate As Operative, Not Cosmetic

Every boilerplate clause has a job. Governing law chooses the legal framework. Forum and arbitration choose the battlefield. Notices determine whether communications are effective. Assignment controls whether rights and duties can transfer. Amendment and waiver control how the contract changes and how rights are lost or preserved. Entire agreement limits what extrinsic evidence applies. Severability determines whether one bad clause sinks the rest. Read each clause asking what it actually does in this contract, not what it usually does in general. A boilerplate clause that does not fit can quietly override the parties' intent.

### Tailor Governing Law And Forum To The Deal

Governing law and forum selection are choices with consequences, and a template's default may be wrong. Confirm the chosen law actually has a connection to the parties or transaction, because some jurisdictions scrutinize or invalidate choice of law that lacks a reasonable relationship. Check whether the forum clause is mandatory or permissive, whether arbitration is required, and whether consumer or employee protections override the choice. Coordinate governing law with forum so the chosen forum will apply the chosen law, and consider enforceability of judgments across borders. A mismatch between law and forum can make the choice ineffective.

### Draft Notice Clauses So Communications Actually Work

Notice clauses determine when a communication is legally effective, and a defective notice can invalidate termination, renewal, or claim steps. Specify permitted methods, addresses, deemed-receipt timing, and how to update addresses. Consider whether email or electronic notice is permitted and what counts as delivery. A notice clause that requires registered mail to a stale address can block a party from acting. Test the clause against the likely communications, including urgent ones, and make sure the process is usable under time pressure.

### Control Assignment And Change Of Control Deliberately

Assignment clauses determine whether a party can transfer its rights or obligations, and they are high-stakes in mergers, financings, and restructurings. Decide whether consent is required for assignment, whether consent can be unreasonably withheld, whether assignment is allowed to affiliates, whether a change of control triggers consent, and what happens on an automatic assignment. Check the interaction with change of control provisions elsewhere in the deal. A boilerplate anti-assignment clause can block a corporate transaction and trigger breach, so tailor it to the commercial intent.

### Make Amendment And Waiver Clauses Match Governance Reality

Amendment clauses control how the contract changes, and waiver clauses control how rights are lost or preserved by conduct. Specify who must consent to amendments, whether writing is required, and whether course of conduct can amend. For waiver, state that failure to enforce is not a waiver and that waiver must be writing, because otherwise a party can lose rights through inaction or informal accommodation. These clauses protect against inadvertent loss of rights and against informal side deals overriding the written agreement.

### Use Entire Agreement And No-Reliance Clauses Purposefully

Entire agreement clauses state that the written contract supersedes prior agreements and negotiations, and they are often paired with non-reliance clauses stating the parties did not rely on extrinsic statements. These clauses limit fraud and extrinsic evidence claims, but their effectiveness varies by jurisdiction and they cannot disclaim fraud in many places. Draft them to capture the intended scope, coordinate them with reps and warranties, and confirm they are enforceable under the governing law. An entire agreement clause that omits non-reliance may not defeat fraud claims based on pre-contract statements.

### Draft Severability To Preserve The Deal

Severability clauses state that if one provision is unenforceable, the rest survives, often with a mechanism to substitute a valid approximation. Without severability, one invalid clause can sometimes sink the whole contract or a major part of it. Draft the clause to sever narrowly, preserve the remainder, and, where useful, authorize a court or the parties to reform the invalid provision to approximate the original intent. Check the governing law's approach to severability, because some jurisdictions reform aggressively and others do not.

### Handle Waiver Of Rights, Jury Waiver, And Class Waiver Carefully

Clauses that waive rights carry enforceability risk. Jury trial waivers, class action waivers, and waivers of statutory rights are often restricted, especially in consumer and employment contexts, and may require specific procedures like initialing or conspicuous presentation. Confirm the governing law permits the waiver and follow the required formalities. A waiver clause that is unenforceable leaves the party without the protection they thought they had. Never assume a waiver clause is valid without checking the jurisdiction's rules.

### Check Force Majeure, Time Of Essence, And Survival

Several other standard clauses deserve tailored attention. Force majeure should define the qualifying events, the notice and mitigation duties, and the termination right if the event persists, because generic force majeure may not cover the intended scenarios. A "time is of the essence" clause makes deadlines strict and can change termination rights. Survival clauses determine which obligations continue after termination. Read each in the context of the deal, because their standard form may not match the commercial reality.

## Common Traps

### Copying Boilerplate Without Tailoring

Template boilerplate can misfit the deal, jurisdiction, or parties. Read each clause as operative law.

### Governing Law And Forum That Do Not Match

A forum that will not apply the chosen law makes the choice ineffective. Coordinate them.

### Notice Clauses That Block Urgent Action

Rigid notice methods or stale addresses can invalidate termination or claims. Make notice usable.

### Anti-Assignment Boilerplate That Blocks A Transaction

A standard consent-to-assign clause can derail mergers and financings. Tailor it to commercial intent.

### Entire Agreement Without Non-Reliance

Without non-reliance, fraud claims based on pre-contract statements may survive. Pair them where intended.

### Severability That Lets One Clause Sink The Deal

Without a robust severability and reformation mechanism, an invalid clause can take more with it. Draft narrowly.

### Waiver Of Rights That Is Unenforceable

Jury, class, and statutory waivers often require formalities and may be void in some contexts. Check the jurisdiction.

## Self-Check

- Is each boilerplate clause read as operative law tailored to this deal, rather than copied as standard?
- Are governing law and forum selected with a real connection, coordinated with each other, and checked against consumer, employee, and cross-border enforceability?
- Does the notice clause specify methods, addresses, deemed receipt, updates, and electronic notice usable under time pressure?
- Does the assignment clause address consent, reasonableness, affiliates, change of control, and interaction with other change of control provisions?
- Do amendment and waiver clauses require writing, protect against inadvertent loss of rights, and prevent informal conduct from overriding the agreement?
- Are entire agreement and non-reliance clauses drafted to capture the intended scope and coordinated with reps and warranties?
- Does severability sever narrowly, preserve the remainder, and authorize reformation where useful under the governing law?
- Are jury, class, and statutory waivers checked for enforceability and required formalities in the relevant jurisdiction?
- Are force majeure, time of essence, and survival clauses tailored to the deal rather than left in standard form?
- Does the output flag that final boilerplate packages for cross-border, regulated, or high-value deals require qualified counsel review?
