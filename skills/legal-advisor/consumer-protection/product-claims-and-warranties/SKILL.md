# Product Claims And Warranties

Product claims and warranties sit at the intersection of consumer protection, product liability, and contract law. A claim about what a product does, a warranty about its performance, and the terms of return and remedy each create enforceable expectations and regulatory exposure. Agents often treat warranties as boilerplate and miss that an express warranty can arise from any description or affirmation, that disclaimers are tightly regulated, that refund and recall duties are mandatory in many cases, and that class actions target the gap between promise and performance.

Use this skill before making product claims, drafting warranties and disclaimers, setting return and refund terms, managing a recall, or assessing product-marketing exposure. The goal is to force the agent to connect claims, warranty creation and disclaimer, refund and recall duties, and class-action risk, and to escalate to product-liability and regulatory counsel for high-stakes matters. This skill is decision support, not legal advice.

## Core Rules

### Recognize How Express Warranties Are Created

An express warranty can arise without the word "warranty."

Identify:

- any affirmation of fact or promise about the goods;
- any description of the goods;
- any sample or model;
- the basis-of-the-bargain requirement;
- the distinction between puffing (non-actionable opinion) and affirmation of fact.

Marketing copy, spec sheets, demonstrations, and packaging can all create express warranties. Review product communications for unintended warranty creation.

### Understand Implied Warranties And Their Limits

Implied warranties attach by operation of law unless properly disclaimed.

Address:

- the implied warranty of merchantability (fit for ordinary purposes);
- the implied warranty of fitness for a particular purpose (when the seller recommends for a specific use);
- title and against-infringement warranties;
- disclaimer requirements (conspicuous, specific language, sometimes "as is");
- limits on disclaiming in consumer transactions in some jurisdictions;
- mandatory warranty rules for specific products (e.g., Magnuson-Moss in the U.S.).

Disclaimers that are inconspicuous or use the wrong language may be ineffective, and some consumer transactions do not permit disclaimer at all. Confirm the disclaimer is valid for the product and jurisdiction.

### Comply With Written-Warranty Rules

Where a written warranty is offered to consumers, specific rules apply.

Address:

- availability before purchase (display of warranty text);
- required content (what is covered, duration, remedy, how to obtain);
- designation of full versus limited warranty;
- pre-sale availability methods (posting, binder, online);
- state minimum standards for service contracts;
- language and readability.

Offering a written warranty triggers disclosure and remedy obligations that informal promises do not. Decide deliberately whether to offer one and comply with its rules.

### Match Product Claims To Performance And Evidence

Claims about performance create warranty and deceptive-practices exposure.

For each claim:

- confirm the product performs as claimed under ordinary use;
- maintain substantiation (testing, data);
- disclose material limitations and conditions;
- avoid overstatement in durability, compatibility, or results;
- update claims when formulation or design changes;
- coordinate claims across packaging, web, and support.

A claim the product cannot consistently meet is both a warranty breach and a deceptive practice. Align claims to tested performance.

### Structure Refund, Return, And Remedy Terms Fairly

Return and refund terms are regulated and scrutinized.

Address:

- mandatory refund or return rights in some jurisdictions regardless of policy;
- cooling-off periods for specific sales (door-to-door, online distance selling);
- clarity and prominence of return terms before purchase;
- restocking fees and their disclosure;
- warranty remedy hierarchy (repair, replace, refund);
- the duty to honor stated policies;
- subscription and recurring-charge cancellation ease.

A "no refunds" policy that conflicts with mandatory consumer rights is unenforceable and a deceptive practice. Align policy with legal floors.

### Manage Recall, Safety, And Reporting Duties

Product safety triggers mandatory duties that cannot be contracted away.

Address:

- reporting known defects and safety hazards to regulators within deadlines;
- recall planning and execution;
- consumer notification and remedy (repair, replacement, refund);
- coordination with safety regulators;
- record-keeping of complaints and incidents;
- supply-chain traceability;
- post-sale monitoring and incident analysis.

Failing to report a known safety defect is a serious violation independent of the defect itself. Build monitoring and escalation so safety issues surface and are reported on time.

### Assess Class-Action And Mass-Claim Exposure

Product claims that underperform at scale attract class actions.

Address:

- the gap between promise and performance across the customer base;
- economic loss and warranty theories;
- consumer-protection statutory claims;
- aggregation through class or mass arbitration;
- exposure to statutory damages and fee shifting;
- insurance coverage for product claims.

A small per-customer shortfall, multiplied across a customer base, can be material. Evaluate claims for aggregate exposure, not just individual complaints.

### Coordinate With Product Liability And Safety Law

Warranty and claims interact with tort liability for injury.

Address:

- design and manufacturing defect exposure;
- failure-to-warn duties;
- strict liability for defective products;
- the effect of disclaimers on personal-injury claims (often limited);
- compliance with safety standards as a floor, not a ceiling.

A warranty disclaimer rarely shields from product-liability claims for injury. Treat safety compliance and warnings as primary, with warranties as secondary.

### Localize Warranty And Consumer Rights For Each Market

Consumer-protection floors differ by jurisdiction.

Address:

- mandatory warranty durations and remedies;
- non-disclaimable consumer rights;
- distance-selling and cooling-off rules;
- language and disclosure requirements;
- recall and reporting regimes;
- restricted-claim rules for specific products.

A warranty valid in one country may be unenforceable or non-compliant in another. Localize for each market.

## Common Traps

### Unintended Express Warranties From Marketing

Descriptions and affirmations create warranties. Review product communications.

### Ineffective Warranty Disclaimers

Inconspicuous or wrongly-worded disclaimers may fail, and some consumer transactions bar disclaimer.

### Claims The Product Cannot Consistently Meet

Overstated performance is both a warranty breach and a deceptive practice.

### "No Refunds" Policies Conflicting With Mandatory Rights

Such policies are unenforceable and deceptive. Align with legal floors.

### Failing To Report Known Safety Defects

Non-reporting is a serious independent violation. Build monitoring and escalation.

### Ignoring Aggregate Class-Action Exposure

Small per-customer gaps multiply. Evaluate claims for mass exposure.

### Assuming Disclaimers Shield Product-Liability Injury Claims

They generally do not. Treat safety and warnings as primary.

### One-Market Warranty Applied Everywhere

Consumer rights differ. Localize per jurisdiction.

## Self-Check

- Are express warranties recognized from affirmations, descriptions, samples, and models, with puffing distinguished from actionable fact?
- Are implied warranties (merchantability, fitness, title, infringement) addressed with conspicuous, valid disclaimers checked against mandatory consumer-transaction limits?
- Do written warranties comply with pre-sale availability, required content, full versus limited designation, and readability rules?
- Do product claims match tested performance, with substantiation, disclosed limitations, no overstatement, updates on change, and cross-channel consistency?
- Are refund, return, cooling-off, restocking, warranty remedy, policy-honoring, and subscription-cancellation terms aligned with mandatory consumer rights?
- Are recall, safety-defect reporting, consumer notification, remedy, regulator coordination, complaint record-keeping, traceability, and post-sale monitoring managed with deadlines?
- Is class-action and mass-claim exposure assessed for the gap between promise and performance, economic loss, statutory claims, aggregation, damages, and insurance?
- Are product-liability and safety-law interactions (defects, warnings, strict liability, disclaimer limits, safety standards) coordinated?
- Are warranty, disclaimer, consumer-rights, distance-selling, recall, and restricted-claim rules localized for each market?
- Does the output escalate safety defects, recalls, class-action exposure, and cross-border warranty design to qualified product-liability or regulatory counsel?
