# Tax Planning And Structuring

Tax planning is not arithmetic. A structure that looks efficient on a spreadsheet can be recharacterized, denied, penalized, or treated as evasion once the tax authority applies substance-over-form, anti-avoidance, general anti-avoidance rule (GAAR), controlled foreign corporation (CFC), transfer pricing, beneficial ownership, or economic substance tests. Agents often copy a structure that worked in one jurisdiction or one year and present it as generally safe, while ignoring residency transitions, permanent establishment exposure, step transactions, anti-conduit rules, and the difference between deferral and elimination of tax.

Use this skill before recommending a holding structure, an intercompany flow, a residency change, a restructuring, an asset vs. share deal, a loss utilization plan, a deduction or credit position, or any arrangement whose main value is tax reduction. The goal is to force the agent to test the structure against substance, purpose, disclosure, and anti-avoidance rules before presenting it as a plan. Tax law is jurisdiction-specific and changes often; any conclusion that depends on a specific rate, treaty article, or rule must be confirmed by a qualified tax advisor for the relevant jurisdiction and year.

## Core Rules

### Separate Lawful Planning, Avoidance, And Evasion

Not every tax reduction is legitimate, and the line is jurisdiction-dependent. Distinguish three categories before recommending anything.

- Lawful tax planning uses choices the law expressly provides, such as entity form, permitted elections, treaty benefits the taxpayer genuinely qualifies for, timing of recognized events, and deductions the statute allows.
- Tax avoidance pushes against the edges of the law, often relying on technical reading of text against evident purpose; many jurisdictions override this through GAAR, statutory anti-avoidance, judicial doctrines (substance over form, business purpose, step transaction, sham), and targeted anti-avoidance rules.
- Tax evasion involves concealment, false statements, omitted income, or fabricated deductions and is criminal.

A structure that depends on no real business purpose, circular flows, back-to-back financing through conduit entities, or treaty shopping without substance is high-risk even if each step is facially legal. Document the business purpose and economic substance before claiming the tax result.

### Determine Tax Residency Of Each Person And Entity

Residency drives worldwide vs. source taxation, treaty access, and CFC/GILTI-type exposure. Do not assume residency from incorporation alone.

For individuals, capture:

- physical presence day counts and half-year rules;
- domicile and its abandonment requirements;
- center of vital interests, habitual abode, nationality tie-breaker under treaty;
- split-year treatment and exit tax on departure.

For entities, capture:

- place of incorporation;
- place of management and control (central management and control, effective management);
- place of effective management under treaty tie-breaker;
- dual residency risk and mutual agreement procedure exposure;
- incorporation-only "shell" with no local substance.

A residency change that looks clean on paper can trigger departure or exit tax, deemed disposition, deferred income acceleration, or CFC inclusion. Map each transition event.

### Characterize Income Correctly

Tax rates, withholding, deductions, and treaty articles depend on income classification. Mischaracterization is a common audit finding.

Classify each income stream:

- ordinary business income;
- capital gains (short vs. long term, participation exemption, holding period);
- dividend (and dividend equivalent, deemed dividend);
- interest (and contingent, convertible, profit-participating);
- royalty;
- rental;
- service income and where it is performed;
- employment income vs. contractor income;
- digital, platform, or data-driven revenue;
- passive vs. active for CFC purposes.

Hybrid instruments and cross-border structures can be classified differently in two countries, creating double deduction, double non-taxation, or later denial. Check classification in every relevant jurisdiction, not just one.

### Test Deductions, Credits, And Losses Against Limits

A deduction on the income statement is not automatically a deduction for tax. Common limits include:

- transfer pricing on related-party charges (interest, royalties, service fees);
- thin capitalization and interest limitation rules (earnings stripping, fixed ratio, group ratio);
- anti-hybrid rules denying deductions for duplicated losses or hybrid instruments;
- non-deductible items (fines, bribes, entertainment, certain taxes);
- at-risk and passive activity loss rules;
- capital loss offset limits;
- net operating loss carryback/carryforward, change-of-ownership limits (Section 382-type), and expiration;
- research and development credit substantiation and nexus;
- foreign tax credit eligibility, sourcing, and basket limits.

Loss buying, loss consolidation through mergers, or artificial loss creation are heavily restricted. Confirm the loss survives the transaction and that utilization is not blocked by ownership change rules.

### Evaluate Deferral, Not Elimination

Many structures defer tax rather than eliminate it. Deferral has value but also risks.

Ask:

- Is the deferral based on non-realization, reinvestment, or a CFC exception?
- What event accelerates it (sale, distribution, residency change, death, change of use)?
- Is there an anti-deferral regime (CFC, GILTI, PFIC, passive income rules) that overrides the deferral?
- Is the deferred amount measured and disclosed?
- Does deferral create a higher future rate or recapture?

Present deferral honestly as timing, not as a permanent saving, and quantify the acceleration triggers.

### Stress-Test The Structure Against Anti-Avoidance

Before recommending a structure, run it against the anti-avoidance toolkit likely to apply.

Check:

- general anti-avoidance rule or judicial sham/substance doctrine;
- specific anti-avoidance rules for the relevant transaction (anti-conduit, anti-treaty-shopping, CFC, PFIC, GILTI, MLI principal purpose test);
- economic substance and real activities requirements;
- beneficial ownership and limitation on benefits tests;
- step transaction and series-of-steps doctrines;
- transfer pricing arm's length and business purpose;
- mandatory disclosure of aggressive arrangements (DAC6-type, reportable transaction rules, promoter rules).

If the structure fails any of these in a plausible audit, do not present it as safe. Disclose the risk and the conditions needed to defend it.

### Document Substance And Business Purpose

A defensible structure needs contemporaneous evidence of substance, not after-the-fact narrative.

Maintain:

- board minutes and decision records in the jurisdiction of management;
- qualified local directors with actual authority;
- office, employees, payroll, and decision-making location;
- bank accounts controlled locally, not by remote affiliates;
- commercial rationale independent of tax;
- intercompany agreements consistent with conduct;
- transfer pricing documentation aligned with the structure;
- financing with genuine repayment ability and terms.

Substance created only after an audit starts is weak and can be disregarded.

### Confirm Disclosure And Reporting Duties

A lawful structure can still trigger penalties if reporting is missed.

Identify:

- country-by-country reporting thresholds;
- master and local file obligations;
- FATCA, CRS, and beneficial ownership registers;
- mandatory disclosure of cross-border arrangements;
- CFC, PFIC, GILTI, and foreign trust reporting;
- form 5471/5472-type ownership and transaction reporting;
- state and local nexus and apportionment filings;
- stamp duty, transfer tax, and real estate withholding.

Calendar each filing with owner, jurisdiction, and deadline.

## Common Traps

### Treating A Rate Table As A Plan

Low statutory rates are not a plan. Substance, anti-avoidance, residency, and reporting rules determine whether the rate applies.

### Copying A Structure Across Jurisdictions

A holding-company or licensing structure that works in one country can create permanent establishment, CFC, or anti-hybrid problems in another. Re-test each jurisdiction.

### Ignoring Residency Transitions

Moving residency, incorporating offshore, or relocating management can trigger exit tax, deemed disposition, or treaty loss. Plan the transition, not only the end state.

### Calling Deferral Elimination

Deferral is timing. If a later event accelerates the tax, the "saving" disappears. Disclose acceleration triggers.

### Relying On No Business Purpose

A structure whose only rationale is tax reduction is vulnerable to GAAR, sham, step transaction, and substance doctrines. Build and document a real commercial purpose.

### Forgetting Loss Ownership Change Limits

Acquiring a loss company to absorb profits is frequently blocked by ownership-change rules. Confirm the loss is usable post-transaction.

### Overlooking State, Local, And Indirect Tax

Income tax planning that ignores VAT/GST, sales tax, stamp duty, transfer tax, customs, and state apportionment can move the cost rather than reduce it.

### Presenting Aggressive Positions As Settled

Aggressive positions should be flagged with uncertainty, disclosure duty, and penalty exposure, not stated as safe conclusions.

## Self-Check

- Is the arrangement classified as lawful planning, avoidance, or evasion, with the basis for that classification stated?
- Are tax residencies of all persons and entities mapped, including dual-residency and exit-tax triggers?
- Is each income stream characterized for every relevant jurisdiction, with hybrid or mismatch risks identified?
- Are deductions, credits, and losses tested against transfer pricing, interest limitation, anti-hybrid, at-risk, and ownership-change limits?
- Is deferral distinguished from elimination, with acceleration triggers identified and quantified?
- Has the structure been stress-tested against GAAR, CFC, PFIC, GILTI, treaty shopping, substance, and mandatory disclosure rules?
- Is there contemporaneous evidence of substance, business purpose, local management, and genuine financing?
- Are all disclosure and reporting duties (CbCR, master/local file, FATCA, CRS, DAC6-type, foreign ownership forms) calendared with owner and deadline?
- Are state, local, indirect, stamp, and transfer taxes considered, not only income tax?
- Does the output flag aggressive positions, recommend qualified tax advisor confirmation, and avoid presenting jurisdiction-specific rules as universal?
