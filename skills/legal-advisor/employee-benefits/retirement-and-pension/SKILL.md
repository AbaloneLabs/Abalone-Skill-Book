# Retirement And Pension

Retirement plans sit at the intersection of tax qualification, fiduciary duty, employment law, and financial regulation. A plan that loses its tax-qualified status, breaches fiduciary duty, underfunds its obligations, or mishandles a transfer can create liability for directors, fiduciaries, and the sponsor, and can deprive participants of benefits they relied on for decades. Agents often treat a pension as an HR policy item and miss the fiduciary, funding, reporting, and tax-qualification machinery that determines whether the plan is lawful and solvent.

Use this skill before adopting or amending a retirement plan, changing contribution or benefit formulas, terminating or merging a plan, handling a corporate transaction with pension obligations, responding to a regulator inquiry, or advising on fiduciary governance. The goal is to force the agent to connect plan type, funding, vesting, fiduciary duties, tax qualification, reporting, and termination or transfer mechanics, and to escalate to benefits, tax, and ERISA or equivalent counsel for high-stakes matters. This skill is decision support, not legal or tax advice.

## Core Rules

### Distinguish Plan Types And Their Legal Regimes

The plan type determines the entire regulatory framework.

Common structures:

- defined benefit plans (employer promises a benefit, bears investment and longevity risk);
- defined contribution plans (employer and often employee contribute, employee bears investment risk);
- cash balance and hybrid plans (defined benefit with notional account features);
- statutory or mandatory state pensions and occupational schemes;
- individual retirement accounts and personal pensions;
- 401(k), 403(b), and similar employer-sponsored defined contribution plans;
- employee stock ownership plans (ESOPs);
- supplemental executive retirement plans (SERPs) and top-hat plans.

Each carries distinct funding, fiduciary, vesting, reporting, and tax rules. Do not assume a 401(k) framework applies to a defined benefit plan or to a foreign occupational scheme.

### Establish Fiduciary Duties And Governance

Retirement plans are typically subject to fiduciary duties that are personal, enforceable, and not waivable by contract.

Address:

- identification of named fiduciaries and their scope;
- duty of loyalty to plan participants and beneficiaries;
- duty of prudence in decisions, investments, and vendor selection;
- duty to diversify investments to minimize large losses;
- duty to follow plan documents consistent with law;
- prohibition on self-dealing and conflicts;
- delegation to prudent experts with monitoring;
- fiduciary liability insurance and indemnification limits;
- committee structure, training, and meeting cadence;
- documentation of decisions and rationale.

Fiduciary breaches can be brought by participants, regulators, and successors, and damages can be personal to the fiduciary. Governance documentation is the primary defense.

### Manage Funding, Contributions, And Minimum Requirements

Underfunding and missed contributions trigger penalties, liens, and regulator intervention.

For defined benefit plans:

- actuarial valuation and funding target;
- minimum required contribution and deadlines;
- funding deficiency and excise taxes;
- PBGC or equivalent guarantee premiums and reporting;
- maximum deductible contribution limits;
- amortization of shortfalls;
- impact of interest rates and asset returns.

For defined contribution plans:

- employer matching and non-elective contributions;
- nondiscrimination testing and corrective distributions;
- safe-harbor structures and notices;
- plan-level and per-participant contribution limits;
- timing of deposits (employee deferrals must be deposited promptly).

Late deposit of employee deferrals is a fiduciary breach and a prohibited transaction in many regimes. Treat deposit timing as a hard control.

### Handle Vesting, Accrued Benefits, And Participant Rights

Participants have enforceable rights to accrued benefits regardless of plan changes.

Define:

- vesting schedules and statutory minimums;
- accrued benefit calculation and protected status;
- anti-cutback rules (amendments cannot reduce accrued benefits);
- eligibility and service-crediting rules;
- treatment of breaks in service;
- distribution options and required minimum distributions;
- loans and hardship withdrawals and their limits;
- rollover and portability rights;
- qualified domestic relations orders (QDROs) or equivalent.

Amending a plan to reduce future benefits may be permissible; reducing accrued benefits generally is not. Distinguish prospective changes from protected accrued rights.

### Maintain Tax Qualification And Reporting

Tax-favored status depends on ongoing compliance with qualification rules and reporting.

Address:

- written plan document and restatement cycles;
- determination or opinion letter where available;
- annual filings (e.g., Form 5500 or equivalent);
- participant disclosures and summaries of material modifications;
- summary plan descriptions and timing;
- nondiscrimination and coverage testing;
- operational compliance with the plan's own terms;
- correction programs for failures (e.g., self-correction or voluntary compliance programs).

Operational drift (administering the plan differently from its written terms) is a leading cause of disqualification. Reconcile administration to the document regularly.

### Navigate Plan Termination, Merger, And Transfer

Plan changes at the end of life or in transactions carry concentrated risk.

For terminations:

- board and fiduciary approval;
- regulator notice or approval (e.g., PBGC, tax authority);
- full funding of benefit obligations;
- distribution or annuity purchase for participants;
- selection of an annuity provider with prudence process;
- residual asset handling;
- participant notices and elections;
- final reporting and audit.

For mergers and transfers:

- asset and liability transfer mechanics;
- protection of accrued benefits;
- fiduciary duties on both sides;
- regulator approval where required;
- communication to affected participants.

Terminating a defined benefit plan without full funding and a prudent annuity-provider process is a fiduciary minefield.

### Address Corporate Transactions And Pension Risk

Mergers, acquisitions, and restructurings interact with pension obligations in complex ways.

Check:

- successor liability for underfunded plans;
- withdrawal liability from multiemployer plans;
- representations, warranties, and indemnities in the deal;
- due diligence on plan status, funding, and litigation;
- post-closing integration or spinoff;
- works-council or union consultation;
- bond and escrow for contribution shortfalls.

Pension underfunding discovered late in a deal can shift the economics significantly. Scope it in diligence.

### Manage Cross-BBorder And Mobile Employee Pensions

Employees working across borders create pension complexity.

Address:

- coverage and contribution when working in multiple countries;
- totalization or social security agreements;
- transfer of accrued benefits across jurisdictions;
- local mandatory occupational schemes;
- taxation of cross-border distributions;
- reporting and withholding for non-residents;
- currency and investment restrictions.

A domestic pension extended to overseas employees without localization can be over-taxed, unenforceable, or non-compliant with local mandatory schemes.

## Common Traps

### Treating The Plan As An HR Policy Item

Retirement plans carry fiduciary, tax, funding, and securities obligations that HR alone cannot manage. Involve fiduciaries, finance, and counsel.

### Late Deposit Of Employee Deferrals

Holding employee contributions too long is a fiduciary breach and prohibited transaction. Deposit promptly and monitor.

### Operational Drift From The Plan Document

Administering the plan differently from its written terms jeopardizes qualification. Reconcile regularly.

### Amending To Cut Accrued Benefits

Anti-cutback rules protect accrued benefits. Limit changes to future accruals unless the law clearly permits otherwise.

### Terminating Without Full Funding Or A Prudent Annuity Process

Shortcuts in termination create fiduciary liability and regulator action.

### Ignoring Withdrawal Liability In Multiemployer Plans

Withdrawing from a multiemployer plan can trigger large, immediate liability. Assess before any restructuring.

### Overlooking Cross-Border Mandatory Schemes

Local occupational pension requirements may override a global plan. Localize for each country.

## Self-Check

- Is the plan type (defined benefit, defined contribution, hybrid, statutory, ESOP, SERP) identified with its funding, fiduciary, vesting, reporting, and tax regime?
- Are named fiduciaries, duties of loyalty and prudence, diversification, plan-document compliance, conflict avoidance, delegation, monitoring, and documentation established?
- Are funding, minimum contributions, deficiencies, premiums, deductibility, nondiscrimination testing, safe harbors, contribution limits, and deposit timing addressed?
- Are vesting, accrued benefits, anti-cutback protection, eligibility, breaks in service, distribution options, loans, rollovers, and QDRO handling defined?
- Is tax qualification maintained through written plan documents, restatements, annual filings, participant disclosures, testing, operational compliance, and correction programs?
- Are plan termination, merger, and transfer handled with approvals, full funding, prudent annuity selection, participant notices, regulator approval, and final reporting?
- Are corporate transactions scoped for successor and withdrawal liability, diligence, representations, consultation, and integration?
- Are cross-border and mobile employees handled with coverage, totalization, transfer, local mandatory schemes, taxation, and reporting?
- Does the output escalate plan adoption, amendment, termination, fiduciary governance, and tax qualification to qualified benefits, tax, and ERISA or equivalent counsel?
