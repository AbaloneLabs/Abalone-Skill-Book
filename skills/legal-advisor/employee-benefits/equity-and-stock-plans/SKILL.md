# Equity And Stock Plans

Equity compensation is a convergence of employment, tax, securities, corporate, and cross-border law. A grant that looks like a simple retention bonus can trigger securities-registration exposure, immediate tax, unfavorable withholding, dilution accounting, or cross-border reporting that the company did not anticipate. Agents often copy a plan or grant agreement from a U.S. startup and assume the tax, vesting, and securities treatment travels to other jurisdictions, employee categories, or exit scenarios. It does not.

Use this skill before adopting or amending an equity plan, issuing grants, structuring vesting, handling exercise or settlement, managing equity on termination or exit, or extending a plan to employees in a new country. The goal is to force the agent to connect instrument type, vesting, taxation, securities exemptions, dilution, change-of-control, and cross-border treatment, and to escalate to tax, securities, and employment counsel for high-stakes matters. This skill is decision support, not legal or tax advice.

## Core Rules

### Choose The Instrument For The Right Purpose

Each equity instrument has distinct legal, tax, and accounting consequences.

Common instruments:

- incentive stock options (favorable U.S. tax treatment with holding-period conditions and limits);
- non-qualified stock options (flexible, broader eligibility, ordinary income on exercise);
- restricted stock units (RSUs) (promise of shares or cash at settlement, vesting-based);
- restricted stock awards (actual shares subject to forfeiture and vesting);
- phantom stock and stock appreciation rights (cash or share-settled value, no actual ownership);
- employee stock purchase plans (qualified or non-qualified, with discounts and lookbacks);
- profit interests (LLC/partnership equity tied to future appreciation);
- performance shares tied to metrics.

Match the instrument to the goal (alignment, retention, cash conservation, ownership, exit participation). Do not assume options and RSUs are interchangeable; their tax timing, securities treatment, and employee perception differ.

### Confirm Securities-Law Compliance For The Grant

Issuing equity is usually a securities offering. Exemptions and filings are required.

Address:

- registration or exemption for the plan and grants;
- U.S. state blue-sky notices and filings;
- applicable non-U.S. securities exemptions and prospectus requirements;
- plan share reserve and authorization under corporate law;
- disclosure documents for participants;
- holding-period and resale restrictions;
- Rule 144 or equivalent resale conditions;
- public-company disclosure and insider-trading controls if listed.

Granting equity without securities compliance can create rescission rights and regulator exposure. Build the exemption analysis into plan adoption, not after the first grant.

### Structure Vesting And Forfeiture Deliberately

Vesting terms drive retention, accounting, and tax.

Define:

- time-based vesting schedule (cliff, monthly, quarterly, annual);
- performance-based vesting metrics and measurement;
- acceleration on change of control (single trigger, double trigger);
- acceleration on termination without cause or good reason;
- treatment on death, disability, or retirement;
- treatment on voluntary resignation, for-cause termination, or layoff;
- post-termination exercise period for options (and its limits);
- clawback or malus provisions;
- minimum required post-termination exercise periods under local law.

Long post-termination exercise windows can extend securities and tax exposure; some jurisdictions cap them or require minimums that protect employees.

### Map Tax Timing, Withholding, And Reporting

Equity tax is event-driven and jurisdiction-specific.

Identify taxable or reporting events:

- grant (some jurisdictions tax at grant);
- vesting (RSUs and restricted stock often taxable at vest);
- exercise (options often taxable at exercise);
- settlement or sale (capital gains);
- cancellation or cashout;
- cross-border mobile employees (allocation of gain across work jurisdictions).

For each event, address:

- character of income (ordinary, capital, employment);
- withholding obligations and deposit deadlines;
- social security or payroll-tax treatment;
- employer tax deductions and reporting (e.g., W-2, equivalent foreign reporting);
- employee reporting obligations;
- 409A or equivalent deferred-comp compliance for discounts, deferrals, and severance equity;
- mobile-employee tracking and split-payroll withholding.

Withholding failures can leave the employer liable for the employee's tax plus penalties. Mobile employees create split-jurisdiction allocation and reporting obligations that must be tracked from grant forward.

### Handle Cross-Border And Mobile Employees

Equity for employees who work in multiple countries or move during the vesting period is a frequent compliance gap.

Address:

- plan eligibility and local-law amendments for each country;
- local tax, securities, exchange-control, and labor-law requirements;
- localized grant agreements and translations where required;
- tracking of workdays by country for tax allocation;
- withholding in the correct jurisdiction;
- data-transfer and privacy notices for participant data;
- exchange-control or approval requirements in restricted currencies;
- mandatory employer contributions or socialization with works councils.

A U.S. plan offered to employees in France, Germany, China, or India without localization can be unenforceable, over-taxed, or unlawful.

### Define Change-Of-Control And Exit Treatment

Exit events are where equity disputes concentrate.

Specify:

- treatment of unvested awards (acceleration, assumption, cashout, rollover);
- treatment of vested options (cashout, net exercise, rollover);
- treatment of RSUs and restricted stock;
- treatment of underwater options;
- good-reason and double-trigger protections for employees terminated after a deal;
- treatment of performance awards on change of control;
- escrow, holdback, and clawback interaction;
- lock-up agreements for employees.

Ambiguous change-of-control language causes litigation at exactly the moment the company is most distracted. Draft it precisely at plan adoption.

### Manage Administration, Records, And Cap Table

Operational discipline protects both the company and participants.

Maintain:

- board and shareholder approvals for the plan and share reserve;
- grant approvals and written award agreements;
- cap-table accuracy and reconciliation;
- vesting and exercise records;
- withholding deposits and reporting;
- participant communications and tax documents;
- insider-trading windows and blackout procedures;
- plan-level compliance calendar for filings and deadlines.

Cap-table drift and undocumented grants create securities and tax exposure that surfaces during diligence or audit.

### Respect Employment-Law Limits On Equity

Equity tied to employment is often treated as compensation with non-waivable protections.

Check:

- whether equity is "wages" or compensation under local law;
- vesting and forfeiture enforceability;
- treatment of accrued or vested equity on termination;
- mandatory minimum post-termination exercise periods;
- works-council or collective-agreement interaction;
- discrimination or equal-pay exposure in grant patterns.

In some jurisdictions, vesting that forfeits without cause, or exercise windows shorter than statutory minimums, is unenforceable.

## Common Traps

### Treating Options And RSUs As Interchangeable

They differ in tax timing, withholding, securities treatment, and employee economics. Choose deliberately.

### Ignoring Securities Compliance On Grants

Equity grants are securities offerings. Missing exemptions or filings creates rescission and regulator risk.

### Withholding Gaps For Mobile Employees

Failing to track workdays by country and withhold correctly leaves the employer liable and creates employee surprise tax bills.

### Overbroad Forfeiture Or Short Exercise Windows

Forfeiture or exercise terms that violate local employment law may be unenforceable and generate claims.

### Ambiguous Change-Of-Control Language

Vague acceleration or cashout terms cause disputes at the worst time. Draft precisely at adoption.

### 409A Or Deferred-Comp Defects

Discounted options, severance equity, or deferral elections that violate deferred-comp rules can trigger immediate tax plus penalties.

### Copying A U.S. Plan Internationally Without Localization

Local tax, securities, exchange-control, and labor rules differ. Localize grants country by country.

## Self-Check

- Is the instrument (ISO, NSO, RSU, restricted stock, phantom, ESPP, profit interest, performance share) chosen for the right purpose with its tax, securities, and accounting consequences understood?
- Are securities exemptions, blue-sky or foreign filings, plan authorization, participant disclosures, and resale restrictions addressed?
- Does vesting cover time, performance, acceleration triggers, termination scenarios, death or disability, post-termination exercise periods, and clawback?
- Are taxable events (grant, vest, exercise, settlement, sale, cashout), withholding, social security, employer deductions, reporting, and 409A or equivalent compliance mapped?
- Are cross-border and mobile employees handled with localization, workday tracking, correct withholding, privacy notices, and exchange-control checks?
- Is change-of-control and exit treatment (unvested awards, vested options, underwater options, double trigger, performance awards, escrow, lock-up) defined precisely?
- Are plan approvals, grant records, cap-table reconciliation, vesting and exercise records, withholding, reporting, and insider-trading controls maintained?
- Are employment-law limits on equity as wages, forfeiture, minimum exercise windows, works-council interaction, and grant-pattern discrimination checked?
- Does the output escalate plan adoption, cross-border extension, tax structuring, and securities matters to qualified tax, securities, and employment counsel?
