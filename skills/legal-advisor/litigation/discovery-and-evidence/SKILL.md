# Discovery And Evidence

Discovery is where most litigation cost, risk, and outcome are determined. The party that develops the factual record through discovery usually controls the case, because summary judgment, settlement value, and trial outcomes all rest on the evidence. Agents often focus on legal arguments while treating discovery as an administrative task, when in fact the scope of requests, the completeness of responses, the handling of privilege, the conduct of depositions, and the preservation of evidence are the decisions that win or lose cases.

Discovery is also a zone of high sanctions risk. Incomplete production, overbroad withholding, destroyed evidence, or evasive answers can lead to adverse inference, monetary sanctions, default, or separate spoliation liability. Use this skill before issuing or responding to discovery, before preparing for depositions, before handling privilege and work product, before litigating discovery disputes, or before advising on evidence preservation. The goal is to help the agent manage discovery strategically and defensibly, not to provide legal advice, which requires qualified counsel in the governing forum.

## Core Rules

### Build A Discovery Plan Around The Elements And Defenses

Discovery should be driven by what must be proven or defended, not by a generic checklist. Map the discovery to the elements of each claim and defense.

Plan:

- the facts needed to prove or defeat each element;
- the documents that establish those facts;
- the witnesses with knowledge;
- the damages evidence and calculations;
- the defenses and the evidence to rebut them;
- the authentication and foundation needed for key exhibits;
- the sequencing of written discovery, depositions, and experts.

A discovery plan tied to the elements avoids both overbroad fishing expeditions and critical gaps. Prioritize the dispositive issues first.

### Use The Right Discovery Tools For Each Purpose

Each discovery device has strengths and limits. Matching the tool to the purpose improves results and reduces objection.

Match tools:

- interrogatories for contentions, fact identification, and damages breakdowns;
- requests for production for documents, electronically stored information, and tangible things;
- requests for admission to narrow issues and establish authenticity;
- depositions for testimony, impeachment, and locking down positions;
- subpoenas to non-parties for documents and testimony;
- expert discovery for opinions, methodology, and qualifications;
- physical or mental examinations where permitted.

Requests for admission can streamline trial by establishing documents and narrowing disputes. Depositions lock testimony but are expensive; reserve them for key witnesses.

### Draft Proportionate And Defensible Requests

Overbroad requests invite motions to protect and produce little. Proportionality is a limiting principle in most forums.

Draft requests that:

- are tied to the claims and defenses;
- specify time periods, custodians, and subject matter;
- define key terms to avoid ambiguity;
- are proportionate to the amount in controversy and burden;
- request electronically stored information in a usable form;
- avoid cumulative or duplicative demands;
- anticipate the likely objections.

A request that asks for "all communications" over ten years will be challenged and may yield less than a targeted request for specific custodians and topics.

### Respond Completely, Specifically, And On Time

Discovery responses create their own record. Evasive, incomplete, or late responses lead to motions to compel and sanctions.

In responses:

- meet the deadline or obtain an extension;
- state and substantiate each objection specifically;
- produce all non-privileged responsive documents;
- identify what is being withheld and why;
- use a privilege log where withholding for privilege;
- avoid boilerplate general objections that courts disregard;
- supplement responses as new information emerges;
- certify the response as complete after reasonable inquiry.

Boilerplate objections such as "overbroad, unduly burdensome, and vague" without specifics are routinely overruled. Substantiate every objection.

### Protect Privilege And Work Product Deliberately

Privilege and work product are the main bases for withholding, but they are easily waived by careless handling. Withholding without a log, or producing privileged material, both create risk.

Manage privilege:

- identify attorney-client communications and attorney work product;
- withhold only genuinely privileged material;
- maintain a privilege log with sender, recipient, date, subject, and basis;
- avoid subject-matter waiver by careful review of what is produced;
- handle inadvertent production under the governing rule's clawback;
- protect common-interest and joint-defense privileges where applicable;
- separate factual business communications from legal advice.

Marking a document "privileged" does not make it so. The communication must fit the legal requirements, and overclaiming weakens credibility.

### Prepare Depositions To Lock Testimony And Build Impeachment

Depositions are the highest-value and highest-risk discovery events. Preparation determines whether they help or hurt.

Prepare:

- the outline organized by topic and element;
- the key documents marked as exhibits;
- the questions that lock down favorable testimony;
- the impeachment material for unfavorable witnesses;
- the areas to avoid that could help the opponent;
- the witness's own preparation, including document review;
- the handling of privilege calls during the deposition;
- the follow-up written discovery needed after the deposition.

A deposition that elicits a damaging admission cannot be undone. Prepare the witness and the outline, and know the impeachment record.

### Handle Experts, Daubert, And Disclosure Deadlines

Expert discovery has its own rules and deadlines. Missing them can preclude expert testimony and cripple a case.

Manage experts:

- the disclosure deadlines for initial and rebuttal experts;
- the required content of expert reports;
- the qualifications and methodology standards;
- the Daubert or equivalent challenge to opposing experts;
- the deposition of experts;
- the coordination between liability and damages experts;
- the cost of experts against the value of the case.

An expert whose methodology is flawed can be excluded, eliminating the claim. Vet methodology before designation.

### Litigate Discovery Disputes With Meet-And-Confer And Proportionality

Courts dislike discovery disputes. Most forums require a meet-and-confer before motion practice, and favor proportionality.

Before moving to compel:

- conduct a meaningful meet-and-confer;
- identify the specific deficiencies;
- document the efforts to resolve;
- frame the dispute around proportionality;
- request the right relief, production, or sanctions;
- anticipate the cost and benefit of the motion.

A motion to compel without a genuine meet-and-confer may be denied on that basis alone. Document good-faith efforts.

### Address Spoliation And Preservation Proactively

The duty to preserve arises when litigation is reasonably anticipated. Failure to preserve can lead to sanctions and separate liability.

Address:

- the trigger date for the preservation duty;
- the scope of relevant evidence;
- the litigation hold and its implementation;
- the suspension of routine deletion;
- the preservation of electronically stored information;
- the documentation of preservation efforts;
- the response to alleged spoliation by the other side.

Silent loss of evidence after the duty attaches is the most dangerous discovery failure. Implement and document the hold.

## Common Traps

### Boilerplate Objections

General objections without specifics are overruled and damage credibility. Substantiate each objection.

### Overbroad Requests That Invite Protection

"All communications" requests are challenged and yield little. Target custodians, topics, and periods.

### Waiving Privilege By Careless Production

Producing privileged material, or mixing legal advice with broad business communications, can waive privilege. Review carefully.

### Failing To Lock Testimony At Deposition

A deposition that does not lock down favorable testimony or elicits damaging admissions hurts the case. Prepare the outline and the witness.

### Missing Expert Deadlines

Late or incomplete expert disclosures can preclude testimony. Track and meet expert deadlines.

### Skipping Meet-And-Confer

Moving to compel without a genuine meet-and-confer wastes the motion. Document good-faith efforts first.

### Silent Loss Of Evidence After The Duty Attaches

Failing to implement a litigation hold after litigation is foreseeable is spoliation. Implement and document the hold immediately.

## Self-Check

- Is the discovery plan mapped to the elements and defenses, with facts, documents, witnesses, damages evidence, rebuttal, authentication, and sequencing identified?
- Are the right tools matched to each purpose, including interrogatories, production requests, admissions, depositions, subpoenas, and expert discovery?
- Are requests proportionate, tied to claims and defenses, specific as to custodians and periods, with defined terms and anticipated objections?
- Are responses complete, specific, timely, with substantiated objections, privilege logs, supplementation, and certification after reasonable inquiry?
- Is privilege managed with identification of attorney-client and work product, proper logs, avoidance of subject-matter waiver, clawback handling, and separation of business and legal communications?
- Are depositions prepared with outlines, exhibits, locking questions, impeachment material, witness preparation, privilege-call handling, and follow-up discovery?
- Are expert deadlines, report content, qualifications, methodology, Daubert challenges, depositions, and cost tracked?
- Are discovery disputes handled with meet-and-confer, documented deficiencies, proportionality framing, and the right relief?
- Is spoliation and preservation addressed with trigger date, scope, litigation hold, deletion suspension, ESI preservation, documentation, and response to allegations?
- Does the output escalate to qualified counsel for discovery strategy, motions, depositions, privilege, and sanctions?
