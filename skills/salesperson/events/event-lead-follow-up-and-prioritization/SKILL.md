# Event Lead Follow Up And Prioritization

Event leads decay quickly, but fast generic follow-up is still poor follow-up. The value of an event is not lead volume; it is relevant conversations converted into the right next action. Agents often treat all event leads alike or fail to separate high-intent conversations from passive attendance. This skill helps the agent prioritize event follow-up using fit, intent, context, and ownership.

## Core Rules

### Sort by fit and intent

Prioritize leads using ICP fit, account tier, role, conversation quality, stated pain, timing, authority, current opportunity status, customer status, and requested follow-up. A high-fit target account with a specific issue should outrank a generic badge scan.

### Preserve event context and route before outreach

Use notes from booth conversations, questions, sessions attended, poll answers, meeting topics, and requested resources. Follow-up should prove the seller remembers why the interaction happened.

Check account owner, territory, partner involvement, customer success owner, active opportunities, channel rules, and duplicate records. Bad routing creates confusion and buyer fatigue.

### Set follow-up SLA by priority and use source attribution carefully

High-intent target leads should receive fast human follow-up; lower-intent leads may enter nurture; disqualified contacts may need no sales follow-up. Define timing by priority rather than treating every lead as urgent.

Record event name, session, booth conversation, campaign, partner source, and interaction type. Attribution helps measure event value and prevents future confusion about why the person was contacted.

### De-duplicate with judgment and build account-level follow-up plays

Multiple scans or attendees from one account may indicate account-level intent, but they can also create duplicate tasks. Merge records carefully while preserving individual roles and questions.

When several contacts from the same account engage, consider coordinated account outreach instead of separate individual follow-ups. Account-level plays should reference the common theme and route through the right owner.

### Close the event loop and preserve handoff to sales owners

After follow-up window, review which leads converted, which were disqualified, which accounts warmed, which messages worked, and which event investments should change. Event learning should affect future spend.

When leads are routed, include event context, qualification notes, urgency, requested asset, suggested next action, and any sensitivity. Handing over only contact data forces the owner to restart discovery.

### Avoid post-event over-contacting and match follow-up offer to signal

Events can create multiple touches from SDRs, AEs, marketing, partners, and customer success. Coordinate outreach so the buyer does not receive repetitive or conflicting messages.

High-intent leads may need a meeting, technical workshop, executive follow-up, or proposal discussion. Low-intent leads may need content, nurture, or future trigger monitoring. Do not force meetings from weak signals.

### Move quickly on high-priority leads and avoid false personalization

For strong signals, follow up while the event is fresh. Reference the specific conversation and propose a buyer-relevant next action. Speed matters most when paired with relevance.

Do not pretend a deep conversation happened if only a scan occurred. Be honest about the context: "You stopped by our booth" or "You registered for the session" can be enough.

### Handle customers separately and look for account-level signals

Existing customers may need CS follow-up, renewal context, support routing, or expansion discovery. Do not drop them into new-logo sequences.

Multiple attendees from the same account, repeated session engagement, or cross-functional interest may matter more than any single lead. Aggregate signals at account level.

### Define disqualification and nurture paths and measure quality, not only speed

Some leads are students, vendors, competitors, partners, job seekers, poor-fit companies, or too early. Route them correctly rather than leaving them in active sales follow-up.

Track meeting conversion, opportunity creation, account engagement, pipeline quality, customer actions, and disqualification accuracy. Fast follow-up with low relevance is not success.

## Common Traps

- Following up with every event lead the same way.
- Prioritizing scan volume over fit and intent; losing conversation context
- Creating duplicate outreach because routing was skipped; forcing meetings from weak engagement
- Waiting too long on high-intent leads; pretending more personalization than the interaction supports
- Treating customers like prospects; missing account-level signal across multiple attendees
- Leaving poor-fit leads in active follow-up; measuring only response speed
- Ignoring partner or channel ownership; using one follow-up SLA for all event leads; losing attribution details that explain contact source; de-duping records in a way that loses role-specific context
- Missing account-level plays from multiple individual signals; failing to review event ROI and learning after follow-up; routing leads without context or suggested action; over-contacting attendees through uncoordinated teams

## Self-Check

- Are leads prioritized by fit and intent?
- Is event context preserved in follow-up?; are account ownership, customer status, partner rules, and duplicates checked?
- Does follow-up offer match signal strength?; are high-priority leads handled quickly and relevantly?
- Is personalization honest?; are customers routed separately?
- Are account-level signals aggregated?; are disqualification and nurture paths defined?
- Are quality metrics tracked?; is partner or channel ownership respected?
- Does follow-up convert event investment into meaningful sales evidence?; is follow-up SLA matched to priority?
- Is source attribution complete?; are duplicates merged without losing individual context?
- Are account-level plays used where multiple contacts engaged?; is event learning reviewed after the follow-up window?
- Does routed handoff include context, urgency, and recommended next action?; is outreach coordinated to avoid repetitive contact?
