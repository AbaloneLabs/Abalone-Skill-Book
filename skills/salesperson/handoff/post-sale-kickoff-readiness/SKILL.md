# Post Sale Kickoff Readiness

Kickoff is not the first implementation discovery call. It should confirm shared understanding, owners, goals, prerequisites, and immediate actions. Agents often treat kickoff as a scheduling task after close, while unresolved sales assumptions appear in front of the customer. This skill helps the agent make kickoff a clean transition from promise to execution.

## Core Rules

### Confirm handoff completeness before scheduling

Kickoff should not proceed if scope, contract, buyer contacts, implementation owner, success criteria, special terms, and risks are missing. Scheduling a meeting without readiness turns kickoff into rediscovery.

### Identify required attendees

Include buyer project owner, admin, technical owner, executive sponsor where appropriate, implementation lead, customer success owner, and any specialist needed for immediate decisions. A kickoff without decision-capable attendees may only create follow-up.

### Align on goals and success criteria and review scope, assumptions, and exclusions

Restate why the buyer purchased, what outcomes matter, what success metrics will be used, and what timeline is expected. If sales value was vague, clarify before promising measurement.

Confirm included products, services, integrations, data, training, support, timeline, buyer responsibilities, and exclusions. Kickoff should reduce ambiguity, not reopen negotiation.

### Check prerequisites and run internal alignment before customer kickoff

Before kickoff, verify access, data, security approvals, procurement completion, technical contacts, implementation documents, admin availability, and internal project readiness. Missing prerequisites should be named, not discovered live.

Implementation, customer success, support, sales, and any specialist should align internally on scope, risks, customer sensitivities, agenda, and owner roles before the customer meeting. The customer should not watch internal teams discover disagreements live.

### Define kickoff acceptance and exit criteria and prepare customer-ready materials

Clarify what must be true by the end of kickoff: project plan accepted, owners confirmed, data path agreed, timeline validated, risks acknowledged, communication cadence set, and next tasks assigned. Kickoff without exit criteria can feel productive while leaving ambiguity.

Use final and approved materials: project overview, implementation plan, responsibility matrix, prerequisite checklist, success criteria, and contact path. Do not reuse sales decks that imply unapproved scope or value.

### Plan for missing attendees or prerequisites and send a pre-kickoff expectation recap

If a required buyer owner misses kickoff or a prerequisite is incomplete, decide whether to proceed, reschedule, or narrow agenda. Continuing as if everything is fine can create false agreement.

Before kickoff, send or prepare a concise recap of scope, goals, attendees, prerequisites, decisions needed, and preparation required. This gives the buyer a chance to correct misunderstandings before the live meeting and reduces surprise.

### Protect the first customer experience and prepare an agenda with decisions

The first post-sale interaction sets tone. Avoid internal confusion, repeated discovery, unclear ownership, and visible disagreement. If the team is not ready to present a coordinated path, fix readiness before putting the customer in the room.

A good kickoff agenda includes decisions and owners: project plan, responsibilities, data access, integration path, communication cadence, risks, and next milestones. Avoid a generic welcome call.

### Surface sales context carefully and define communication cadence

Share useful context from sales: champion, concerns, promised value, risks, sensitivities, and constraints. Avoid internal commentary that would be inappropriate or irrelevant to the customer.

Set meeting rhythm, status format, escalation path, decision process, and owner for updates. Poor communication cadence creates avoidable anxiety.

### Address early risk openly and confirm next actions immediately

If data, timeline, scope, adoption, or resources are at risk, state the issue and mitigation early. Surprising the customer later is worse.

Kickoff should end with owners, dates, artifacts, and decision points. If everyone leaves with only enthusiasm, implementation risk remains.

## Common Traps

- Scheduling kickoff before handoff is complete; inviting attendees who cannot make required decisions
- Treating kickoff as ceremonial instead of operational; repeating sales value without measurable success criteria
- Failing to confirm scope and exclusions; discovering missing prerequisites during the call
- Using a generic agenda with no decisions; sharing irrelevant or sensitive sales commentary
- Leaving communication cadence undefined; hiding early risks to keep kickoff positive
- Ending without owners and dates; making the customer repeat discovery from the sales process
- Skipping internal alignment before customer kickoff; treating kickoff as successful without defined exit criteria
- Using sales materials that imply the wrong implementation scope; proceeding normally when required attendees or prerequisites are missing; skipping a pre-kickoff recap that could catch misunderstanding; treating internal confusion as acceptable during the first customer experience

## Self-Check

- Is handoff complete enough to schedule kickoff?
- Are decision-capable buyer and seller attendees identified?
- Are goals and success criteria clear?; are scope, assumptions, exclusions, and buyer responsibilities confirmed?
- Are prerequisites checked before the call?; does the agenda include decisions, owners, and milestones?
- Is sales context useful and appropriate?; is communication cadence defined?
- Are early risks named with mitigation?; are next actions assigned with dates?
- Will kickoff feel like execution, not rediscovery?; is the customer's first post-sale experience consistent with what was sold?
- Has internal alignment happened before customer kickoff?; are kickoff exit criteria defined?
- Are customer-ready materials final and scope-safe?; is there a plan for missing attendees or prerequisites?
- Is a pre-kickoff expectation recap prepared?; will the first post-sale interaction feel coordinated and credible?
