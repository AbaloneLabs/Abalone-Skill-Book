# Reseller Distributor Handoff

Channel handoff is not forwarding a lead. Resellers and distributors may own pricing, procurement, local relationship, implementation, support, billing, or compliance steps. Weak handoff creates buyer confusion, partner frustration, and lost deal context. This skill helps the agent transfer enough information for the partner to continue cleanly.

## Core Rules

### Define handoff purpose and confirm partner capability and authority

Clarify whether the handoff is for resale, local procurement, quote, implementation, support, renewal, expansion, or partner qualification. Different purposes require different context and owners.

Check whether the partner can sell, price, implement, support, localize, contract, invoice, and represent the product for this buyer and region. Do not assume every partner can handle every use case.

### Transfer buyer context and align commercial boundaries

Share buyer problem, stakeholders, decision criteria, timeline, commercial status, technical needs, risks, objections, and next action. The partner should not restart discovery unless needed.

Clarify list price, discounts, margin, quote ownership, taxes, currency, payment terms, marketplace fees, expiration, and approval rules. Buyer-facing commercial confusion damages trust.

### Provide a handoff acceptance checklist and control quote and version history

The partner should confirm they have buyer context, commercial path, next action, quote rules, scope boundaries, support expectations, and escalation contacts. A checklist prevents the handoff from being treated as an email forward.

If partner creates or modifies quotes, define version ownership, expiration dates, discount approval, and who can send buyer-facing pricing. Conflicting quote versions create trust and margin problems.

### Confirm partner enablement and define escalation and fallback

Before expecting the partner to sell or implement, confirm they understand the product, value story, scope limits, support process, and objection handling. A partner may have commercial rights but weak enablement.

If partner does not follow up, misstates scope, mishandles pricing, or lacks technical capability, know who intervenes and how. Buyer experience should not depend on unmonitored partner execution.

### Include renewal and support ownership and align buyer introduction language

Handoff should clarify who owns renewal, expansion, first-line support, customer success, and issue escalation. Channel confusion often appears after the initial sale.

When introducing the partner or distributor, explain why the buyer is being routed that way and what the partner will handle. This prevents the buyer from thinking they are being passed off without reason.

### Track partner response SLA and confirm data sharing permission

Set expectations for partner follow-up speed, quote turnaround, and escalation. If partner response is slow, the vendor may still own buyer trust. Monitor the handoff until the buyer is actively served.

Only share buyer information with the partner under appropriate consent, agreement, and policy. Channel handoff should not expose confidential buyer context casually.

### Preserve promise boundaries and define communication roles

Tell the partner what was promised, what was not promised, what requires approval, and what assumptions exist. Partners should not unknowingly inherit unapproved commitments.

Decide who communicates with buyer, who attends meetings, who sends quote, who handles technical questions, who updates CRM, and who escalates. Undefined roles create duplicate or missing follow-up.

### Include implementation and support context and track handoff acceptance

If the partner delivers services or first-line support, include scope, readiness, buyer responsibilities, escalation path, and post-sale owner. Delivery gaps are channel risk.

The partner should acknowledge receipt, owner, next action, and timeline. Handoff is not complete until accepted.

### Keep internal records updated and monitor buyer experience after handoff

CRM should show partner owner, handoff date, context sent, commercial path, risks, and buyer status. Channel deals need inspectable records.

Check whether the buyer received timely, accurate, and aligned follow-up. Partner handoff should improve local execution, not reduce accountability.

## Common Traps

- Treating handoff as lead forwarding; assuming partner capability without checking
- Sending no buyer context; creating pricing or discount confusion
- Letting partners inherit unclear promises; leaving buyer communication roles undefined
- Omitting implementation and support expectations; considering handoff complete before partner acceptance
- Losing channel details from CRM; ignoring buyer experience after handoff
- Using partner route to avoid direct sales responsibility; failing to escalate when partner response is slow
- Treating handoff as accepted without a checklist; allowing multiple quote versions with unclear ownership; assuming partner enablement is sufficient; having no fallback if partner execution is weak
- Forgetting renewal and support ownership; introducing a partner without explaining buyer value; ignoring partner response SLA after handoff; sharing buyer data without appropriate permission or agreement

## Self-Check

- Is handoff purpose clear?; is partner capability and authority confirmed?
- Is buyer context complete enough?; are commercial boundaries aligned?
- Are promises, assumptions, and exclusions clear?; are communication roles defined?
- Is implementation and support context included?; has partner accepted the handoff?
- Is CRM updated?; is buyer experience monitored after handoff?
- Are slow or misaligned partner actions escalated?; would the buyer experience one coordinated commercial path?
- Has the partner accepted a clear handoff checklist?; are quote versions and pricing authority controlled?
- Is partner enablement sufficient for the task?; is escalation and fallback path defined?
- Are renewal and support ownership clear?; is buyer introduction language clear?
- Is partner response SLA monitored?; is data sharing permission confirmed?
