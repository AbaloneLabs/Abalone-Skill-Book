# Assessment Platform And Vendor Governance

Recruiting vendors and assessment platforms can shape who gets hired before humans review the evidence. Tools may score personality, cognitive ability, coding skill, video responses, language, or job simulations. Agents often accept vendor promises and miss validation, accessibility, job relevance, data protection, and candidate impact. This skill helps the agent evaluate recruiting tools as governance decisions, not convenience purchases.

## Core Rules

### Define the job signal the tool must measure

Before selecting a tool, identify the capability it should assess and why existing methods are insufficient. A cognitive test, coding platform, video tool, or personality assessment should map to role requirements.

Do not buy tools because they sound modern or reduce recruiter workload.

### Ask for validation evidence

Vendors should provide evidence that the assessment predicts relevant job performance for comparable roles and populations. Understand limitations, sample sizes, adverse impact analysis, and how scores should be interpreted.

Marketing claims are not validation.

### Review accessibility and accommodation support

Tools should support assistive technology, alternative formats, extra time where approved, language needs, mobile access where relevant, and clear candidate instructions. Candidates should not be rejected because the platform is inaccessible.

Accessibility is a core vendor requirement.

### Protect candidate data

Confirm data processing, retention, deletion, subprocessors, cross-border transfer, security controls, AI use, recording, and candidate notice. Assessment data can be sensitive and should have controlled access.

Do not let vendor convenience create uncontrolled candidate profiles.

### Understand scoring and human review

Recruiters and hiring teams should know what scores mean, what they do not mean, and how humans review exceptions. Avoid black-box rejection where no one can explain the job-related basis.

Scores should inform decisions, not become unquestioned gates.

### Pilot before broad rollout

Test completion rate, candidate feedback, pass-through, accessibility, interviewer usefulness, scoring consistency, and operational burden. Compare results with existing process and downstream outcomes.

A tool that looks efficient in demo may fail in real candidate flow.

### Monitor adverse patterns over time and keep ownership clear

Review pass rates by source, role, location, candidate type, and approved demographic analysis where lawful. Watch for drop-off, complaints, and score patterns that do not align with interview or performance evidence.

Tool governance continues after purchase.

Decide who owns vendor relationship, configuration, candidate support, accommodations, data requests, score interpretation, audits, and renewal decisions. Without ownership, tools drift.

Recruiting technology needs operational governance.

### Review candidate burden before rollout and prevent score misuse outside intended scope

Assessment platforms can add logins, browser requirements, camera use, time pressure, identity checks, or unfamiliar interfaces. Evaluate whether burden is proportional to role risk and stage. A technically valid tool can still cause candidate drop-off if the experience is poor.

Pilot feedback should include candidate effort, not only recruiter convenience.

A tool validated for one role, level, geography, or language may not be valid for another. Hiring teams may try to reuse scores broadly because they look scientific. Define where scores can and cannot be used.

Misuse of assessment scores creates false precision and fairness risk.

### Review vendor change management and plan candidate support ownership

Vendors may update scoring models, question banks, interfaces, proctoring rules, or AI features without recruiters noticing. Require notice for material changes and retest the candidate experience and score interpretation after updates.

A tool that was acceptable at purchase can become risky after vendor changes.

Candidates may face login issues, accessibility problems, lost submissions, browser incompatibility, or confusing instructions. Decide who supports them and how quickly. Vendor support should not leave candidates stranded during assessment deadlines.

Assessment governance includes operational support, not only procurement review.

## Common Traps

- Selecting tools mainly to reduce recruiter workload.
- Accepting vendor claims without validation evidence.
- Using assessments that do not match job requirements; ignoring accessibility until candidates complain
- Allowing black-box scores to reject candidates; failing to pilot with real candidate workflows
- Storing assessment data without retention or deletion controls; not monitoring adverse patterns after rollout
- Letting hiring teams misuse scores beyond their meaning; renewing vendors without reviewing quality and candidate impact
- Ignoring candidate burden created by assessment platform requirements; reusing scores outside the role or population for which the tool was reviewed
- Missing vendor model, interface, or scoring changes after rollout; leaving candidates without support for platform failures

## Self-Check

- Is the tool tied to a defined job signal?
- Is vendor validation evidence reviewed and understood?
- Are accessibility and accommodation features sufficient?
- Are data processing, retention, deletion, security, and AI use approved?
- Can recruiters explain what scores mean and how humans review them?
- Was the tool piloted before broad rollout?
- Are candidate feedback, completion, pass-through, and downstream outcomes monitored?; are adverse patterns reviewed through approved methods?
- Are ownership and support responsibilities clear?; would the tool decision be defensible to candidates, HR, legal, and hiring teams?
- Is candidate burden proportional to role risk and process stage?; are score-use boundaries documented and enforced?
- Are vendor changes reviewed and retested before they affect candidates?; is candidate support ownership clear for platform issues?
